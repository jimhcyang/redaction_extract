4. MOSCOW MOVES TO DISCOURAGE POLISH ECONOMIC TIES WITH WEST

Comment on:

The American embassy in Warsaw believes that a recent increase in Soviet funds available to Poland for large-scale purchases in the USSR will almost certainly arrest the trend of growing trade between Poland and the West. Poland has obtained an estimated 200,000,000 rubles as a result of Soviet agreement in September to pay for transit facilities used from 1946 to 1954. Settlement of this issue is presumably a part of the Soviet effort to lure Poland back into closer economic ties with the bloc.

Polish consumers prefer Western goods, but payment difficulties, the exhaustion of Western short-term credit possibilities, and reduced prospects for Polish exports will force Poland to turn to the bloc for needed consumer goods.
