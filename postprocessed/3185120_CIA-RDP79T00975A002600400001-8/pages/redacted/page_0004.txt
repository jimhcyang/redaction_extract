1. REVOLT REPORTEDLY PLANNED IN PERU FOR
16 JULY

military
and civilian elements there are planning to stage a revolt on 16 July to nullify the results of last month's presidential and congressional elections, according to the American armed forces attachés in Lima. The attachés note that the military commander in Arequipa is known to be close to Minister of War Nindacochea who was scheduled to arrive in Arequipa on 15 July, purportedly to dedicate a new army barracks.

The attachés believe that any attempted revolt is not likely to receive widespread support.

Comment            Success of a revolt at this time probably depends on the attitude of Peru's all-military cabinet. These officers have reportedly been in control of the government for the past three to four weeks while hospitalized President Odria has become a mere figurehead.

Many senior military officers, including at least some cabinet members, are currently concerned over the apparent good relations between conservative president-elect Manuel Prado and the outlawed leftist but non-Communist APRA party which gained significant strength in the recent congressional election. Consequently, the cabinet may be fomenting a revolt as an excuse for continuing in power. Recent reports have suggested, however, that the present cabinet intends to permit the installation of the new government, as scheduled, on 28 July and that the military will act only in the event of subsequent unfavorable developments.
