6. INDIA REMAINS OPPOSED TO A KASHMIR PLEBISCITE

Both Krishna Menon in New York and Raghavan Pillai, secretary general of the Indian Ministry of External Affairs in New Delhi, have bluntly informed American diplomats that India has no intention of giving up its portion of Kashmir.

Both men were also cool toward the idea of another visit to the area by a UN representative seeking to arrange for a plebiscite.

Menon indicated his government would reluctantly accept a Soviet veto if this was the only way to avoid UN Security Council action.

Menon said he understood that Nehru and President Eisenhower had agreed the Kashmir situation should not be stirred up, and expressed his hope that the UN would take no action on the case.
