9. Buying wave sparked by Radio Free Europe sweeps Prague:

A buying wave is sweeping Prague, set off, according to the American Embassy, by speculations on the possibility of a currency reform broadcast over Radio Free Europe. Purchases are so far selective, and buyers have concentrated on textiles, gold and diamonds sold on the free market. The Embassy feels that the broadcasts may provide the Czech regime with a scapegoat upon which to pin the necessity for more stringent economic measures against the population.

Comment: Rumors of an impending currency reform have circulated for several months in Czechoslovakia. A buying panic swept Budapest earlier this month. Economic conditions in both Hungary and Czechoslovakia make a future currency reform probable.
