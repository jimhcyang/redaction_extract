CENTRAL INTELLIGENCE BULLETIN
21 July 1960

DAILY BRIEF

I. THE COMMUNIST BLOC

East Germany: Moscow is at least acquiescing in the exertion of greater pressure by the East Germans on the Western military liaison missions in East Germany in an effort to extort an acknowledgment of some degree of East German jurisdiction. East German party boss Ulbricht in his press conference of 19 July charged that the US and British missions had been caught spying. East German officials are spreading the word that the USSR no longer feels its own liaison missions in West Germany are important.
