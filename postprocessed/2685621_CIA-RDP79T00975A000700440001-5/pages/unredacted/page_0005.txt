3. Large rubber shipment from Indonesia offered to Communist China:

An offer of 20,000 tons of rubber was transmitted during May. Half of the rubber was to be shipped within 45 days after the opening of the letter of credit; the remainder was to be delivered 45 days after the first shipment. The Indonesians agreed to a barter ratio of one ton of rubber for ten tons of rice.

Comment: The amount offered exceeds all Communist Chinese foreign rubber purchases since the first of the year.

The same barter arrangement also offered a guarantee of the shipment "by the Indonesian Government." This guarantee was made despite Indonesia’s promise to honor the UN-sponsored embargo on strategic materials to China.

SOUTH ASIA

4. Pakistani interest in the Middle East Command reported:

Comment: Pakistani officials have in the past displayed an interest in joining a Middle East defense organization. They have stated, however, that participation in such an organization would depend upon prior solution of the Kashmir question which at present engages the full attention of Pakistan’s armed forces.
