Paraguayan Revolutionary Pressures

A group of prominent Paraguayans, including some clergymen, military officials, and student leaders, gave President Stroessner a memorandum on 22 June urging him to turn over power to a transitional junta composed of representatives of the political parties and the armed forces.

The junta would guarantee Stroessner's personal safety to remain in or leave the country and would hold elections at the end of the year. The group planned to wait for a short while, and if no reply were forthcoming, to begin a "campaign of resistance."

Presentation of the memorandum is another reflection of the increasing pressures on the Stroessner dictatorship and offers a possibility of avoiding the bloodshed threatened by the four principal groups which have recently stepped up their revolutionary activities against the regime. These activities have thus far featured small guerrilla action inside Paraguay and small invasions from border points, but there are reports that the rebels plan large-scale fighting in the near future.

Stroessner's military support now is strong but would not be deaf to alternative propositions in the face of a strong revolutionary tide.
