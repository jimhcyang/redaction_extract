Announcement of the formal unification of Somalia and former British Somaliland, which became independent on 26 June, has been delayed because negotiators from each territory insist on naming the prime minister and are unable to reach agreement over the allocation of ministerial portfolios. Representatives from the Somaliland region of the republic, having supported the election of Aden Abdullah Osman--former president of Somalia's Legislative Assembly--for chief of state, now insist on naming the prime minister and at least three of the other ten ministers envisaged in the new government. If these demands are not met, they may return to Hargeisa without ratifying the Act of Union. Somalis from the eastern (Mogadiscio) region, however, seek to retain the office of prime minister for one of their own members, although they are unable to agree on a candidate.
