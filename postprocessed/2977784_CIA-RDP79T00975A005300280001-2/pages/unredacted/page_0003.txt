3 OCTOBER 1960

I. THE COMMUNIST BLOC
Peiping using National Day celebrations to make show of "socialist unity," but propaganda continues hard line in Sino-Soviet dispute.

II. ASIA-AFRICA
Jordan's recognition of Iraq probably stems from resentment of UAR subversive activities.
Resumption of Israeli dredging in demilitarized zone may cause UAR protest to armistice commission.
Lumumba asks Cairo for arms, aircraft, and helicopters.
Laos--Tentative steps toward army reunification may be undone by reported dismissal of Phoumi and other officers.

III. THE WEST
East Germany reacts cautiously to Bonn's denunciation on 1961 interzonal trade agreement.
Gaitskell faces uphill fight to defeat nuclear disarmament resolution at labor party conference.
