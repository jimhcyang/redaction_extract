  Text: France May Plan Military Intervention in Guinea The French representative to the tripartite talks on Africa stated that France takes an "extremely serious view" of recent developments in Guinea and implied that France may undertake military action. members of the French Community which are neighbors of Guinea are "most anxious" regarding Conakry's intentions. Following the arrival in Conakry last month of Czech military and other material, a Polish vessel unloaded further "gifts" from Eastern Europe on 16 April. Soviet ambassador Gerasimov, with a staff of seven, arrived in Conakry by air on 16 April. A Czech charge presented his credentials on 11 April and a Bulgarian ambassador has been in residence in Conakry since late January. In view of De Gaulle's insistence on the need for US-UK-French consultation and cooperation on world-wide defense planning, with Africa as a special sphere of French responsibility, he may make his handling of the Guinea problem the immediate test case of his own version of the meaning of Western solidarity
