WESTERN EUROPE

9. East German trade now oriented 75 percent toward Orbit:

About 75 percent of East German exports in the first three quarters of 1952 went to the Soviet bloc, 40 percent of all exports went to the USSR, 15 percent to Poland, 7 percent to Czechoslovakia, 5 percent to Hungary and 5 percent to China. The West German Federal Republic ranked highest among Western countries, with only 2.5 percent.

Comment: These figures, compared with those of 1950, show a marked decline in East Germany's trade with West Germany, its principal trade partner outside of the Orbit.
