CONSTRUCTION ON EAST CHINA RAILROAD MOVES TOWARD COMPLETION The Chinese Communists have completed at least five railway marshalling yards, four of which are on the northern third of the line. (See map, page 6.) The railroad now appears operational from Yingtan to Hsiayang, a distance of 165 miles. Bridging is nearly finished on the next 40-mile section from Hsiayang to Shahshien, while from the latter point south to Amoy, the roadbed is well developed. Bridges and tunnels on this section, however, are incomplete. Comment The line will probably be completely operational late this year. The Chinese Communists have announced that they plan to reopen the major deep-water port of Amoy, and earlier photography shows that the line will extend to the Amoy dock area. The previous estimates of the line's capacity--4,000-4,800 tons each way per day--are supported by the photographic evidence of four important railway marshalling and passing yards on the northern third of the line. If this pattern of development continues, the line will be able to serve both the future commercial ocean shipping activity at Amoy and the military-economic requirements of the East China area.
