With the increase of pre-election political activity aimed at the approach of the 29 October deadline for national elections, and with the approaching conclusion of the political trials of the leaders of the ousted government, the military regime will probably become more sensitive to popular criticism and will deal ruthlessly with demonstrations opposing the programs of the regime or favoring the leaders of the former regime who face possible death sentences. In the event of any major demonstration of pro-Democratic strength, the CNU might decide to postpone elections, although such action would probably arouse opposition from all political elements. All elements have so far restrained their criticism in an effort to assure the early resumption of civilian government.
