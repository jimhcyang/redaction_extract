CENTRAL INTELLIGENCE BULLETIN
20 March 1961

DAILY BRIEF

*Laos: (Information available as of 0400 EST) The government's blocking positions on Route 13--located about 25 air miles south of Luang Prabang and 15 miles north of Vang Vieng--have held so far against probing attacks by Kong Le - Pathet Lao forces. These positions, however, are vulnerable to enemy flanking movements. Government units in southern Xieng Khouang Province have been continuing their withdrawal toward Tha Thom. The chief of the US military mission in Laos reported on 18 March that General Phoumi is now rather desperate and cannot be persuaded to go on the offensive on any front. Phoumi, lacking trained reserves, is committing newly formed units in an effort to hold his defensive positions.
Bloc airlift operations into Laos continue.
Souvanna Phouma on 18 March arrived in India, where he hopes to obtain support for an immediate 14-nation conference on Laos. During his brief visit to Rangoon, Souvanna won Burmese Premier U Nu's agreement to participate in such a conference. (Backup, Page 1) (Map)

Congo: Implementation of the Tananarive conference resolutions concerning the future structure of the Congo is likely to prove difficult, as almost every participant has a different idea as to what was decided. Ambassador Timberlake in Leopoldville also believes that many of these leaders fail to appreciate the need for obtaining international recognition and that among those who do, there is disagreement as to the steps necessary to achieve it.
