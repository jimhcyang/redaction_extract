30 MAY 1960

I. THE COMMUNIST BLOC

Khrushchev's 28 May speech continues effort to reconcile his arguments for a policy of detente with his performance in Paris.

North Korea steps up number of planes scrambled against possible intruders into air space.

II. ASIA-AFRICA

Gursel promises elections in Turkey as soon as possible but believes he will need from one to three months beforehand to restore situation to normal.

Ethiopia, in expectation of additional US military aid, reportedly plans not to accept Soviet arms offer at this time.

Cameroun's foreign minister claims terrorist leader Moumie plans to overthrow government after country's security agreement with France ends next month.

III. THE WEST

Debre stresses De Gaulle's desire for close Western Big Three cooperation on nuclear weapons and Africa.

Cuban regime emphasizes cultural ties with Communist China; may soon proceed with establishment of diplomatic relations.
