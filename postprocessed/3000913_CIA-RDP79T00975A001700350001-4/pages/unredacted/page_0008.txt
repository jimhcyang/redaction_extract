Mohammad Ali and Iskander Mirza, governor of East Pakistan, left Pakistan on 22 and 21 September for state visits and medical treatment respectively, while General Ayub, commander in chief of the Pakistani army, is scheduled to leave for the United States on 7 October. WESTERN EUROPE

8. German coalition leaders see Mendes-France plan as "intentionally unacceptable":

West German coalition leaders interpret the German rearmament proposals of French premier Mendes-France as "intentionally unacceptable," and aimed at placing the blame for a breakdown of the London conference on Germany or Britain. They insist that if the conference fails, Britain and the United States must act immediately to rearm Germany. Failure to do this, they say, would discredit Chancellor Adenauer, and result in the de facto neutralization of the Federal Republic.

West German defense officials specifically criticize the fact that under the French plan, Germany would be the only Brussels Pact member whose entire armed force would be subject to control. German officials contend that some form of German NATO membership is a military and political necessity.
