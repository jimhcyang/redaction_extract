CENTRAL INTELLIGENCE BULLETIN

25 April 1961

DAILY BRIEF

*France-Algeria (Information available as of 0430 EST) 6
The strength and impact of the French military insurrection appear to be weakening slightly in view of reports of gaps in its control in Algeria, the passing of another night without an attack on metropolitan France, and further vigorous countermeasures by Paris. While popular response in France to De Gaulle's appeal for support has been enthusiastic, some of his supporters have expressed fears that the Communists stand to gain from the government's arming of citizens for local defense. In Tunisia and Morocco, the governments have taken precautionary measures in an effort to confine the insurrection to Algeria. (Backup, Page 1)
