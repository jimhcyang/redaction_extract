The Sudanese Situation

The possibility of a sudden move to change or overthrow the Abboud military regime in the Sudan is growing. Dissen- sion within the Supreme Military Council--the top governing body--has become acute, as each of its several factions tries to line up army and civilian support.

Influential political and religious leaders have sustained their pressures for a rapid return to civilian government, and the Supreme Council members have been unable to agree on how to deal with them.
The head of the Ansar religious sect, which could probably muster several thousand partially armed tribesmen in an emergency, has warned the gov- ernment that action against him would force "drastic counter- measures."

It seems doubtful that the regime can count on support of all five regional army commanders in the event of a full-scale show- down.
