2. EGYPT MAY OBTAIN SUEZ PILOTS FROM USSR

An Egyptian newspaper on 22 August offered Soviet pilots for the canal should British and French pilots resign.

Cairo reportedly recognizes that the pilot shortage is its most serious immediate problem in operating the canal, and almost certainly would utilize Soviet personnel if other nationalities are not available.

New pilots must have second-mate papers and eight to ten years' experience. The Suez Canal Company previously required captain's papers and ten years' experience.
