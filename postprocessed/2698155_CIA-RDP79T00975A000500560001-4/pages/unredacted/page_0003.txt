FAR EAST

Soviet air defense unit probably participating in Korean war:
about mid-November 1951 an air defense commander subordinate to the Soviet 5th Fleet moved from the Port Arthur Naval Base to Antung, which controls Communist jet combat operations in northwestern Korea.
At the same time, aircraft of a 5th Fleet fighter division appear to have moved from Port Arthur into the combat area and to have begun
Beginning shortly thereafter, there were frequent Chinese Communist references to flights of "Soviet" MIG-15's in the combat area.

Comment: This is the first indication of the direct participation of an air defense organization of the Soviet Armed Forces in Korean air operations.
The 5th Fleet air defense officer in question apparently continues to communicate with his Soviet superiors. It is probable that his presence in the Antung area represents either an attempt to give him and his personnel combat experience, or a measure to establish closer Soviet control over that portion of the combat operations which are considered a Soviet responsibility.

Bombers of Chinese 8th Air Division leaving Manchuria:
Eleven TU-2 light bombers of the Chinese 8th Air Division were scheduled to make a "transfer move" from Mukden to Pan fou 100 miles northwest of Nanking,53 The 8th Division, equipped with at least 34 TU-2's, has been based at the Mukden West airfield.
