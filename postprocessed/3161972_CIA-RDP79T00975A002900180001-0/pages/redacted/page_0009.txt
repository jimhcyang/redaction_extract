5. COMMUNIST INFLUENCE GAINS AMONG CHINESE
IN CAMBODIA

The Communist take-over of powerful Overseas Chinese social organizations as well as Chinese schools in Cambodia has accelerated in recent weeks, the American embassy in Phnom Penh reports. Elections held recently by several of these groups throughout Cambodia have resulted in Communist control, usually by landslide votes. The influence of Chinese Nationalist adherents in Cambodia has declined to a new low, although a leader of the anti-Communist groups believes that approximately 30 percent of the local Chinese students could still be denied to Communist control if appropriate textbooks were made available.

Comment
The Communists are exploiting Prince Sihanouk's deference toward Peiping in order to circumvent the Cambodian government's attempt to suppress political activity among the Overseas Chinese.

The "hero's welcome" accorded Chou En-lai last November gave strong impetus to the pro-Peiping drift among the approximately 250,000 Overseas Chinese in Cambodia. The increasing economic ties between Phnom Penh and Peiping are also a significant factor in the drift toward the Communists.
