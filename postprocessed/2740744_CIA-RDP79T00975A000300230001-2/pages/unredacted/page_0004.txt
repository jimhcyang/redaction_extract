FAR EAST

3. Enemy in Korea can attack with little or no additional warning:

The US Eighth Army warns that the enemy is now capable of moving his reserves into position 24 hours prior to an attack, and that even this movement may be accomplished without detection under present weather conditions. Furthermore, so long as the tactical situation remains almost static there may be no noticeable change in the pattern of enemy activity.

Comment: In the past, the five-day period preceeding the launching of an offensive provided numerous indications of the probable date of attack. As yet there is no firm indication of an intent to resume the offensive.
