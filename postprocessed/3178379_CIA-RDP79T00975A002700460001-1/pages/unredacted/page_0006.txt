3. SUEZ CRISIS ADVERSELY AFFECTING BLOC TRADE WITH CHINA

Comment
As of 12 September at least nine non-bloc freighters normally involved in the China trade had been withdrawn from charter operations involving a Suez transit. Such vessels, mainly British, carried approximately 25 percent of bloc shipments to China in 1955. An even greater proportion of Chinese shipments to Eastern Europe is carried in chartered non-bloc ships.

Peiping's dependence on these vessels has grown this year as bloc ships on Far East trade routes are becoming increasingly involved in carrying cargoes under barter agreements with South Asian countries.
