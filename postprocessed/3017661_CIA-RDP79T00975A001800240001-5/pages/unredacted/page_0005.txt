4. South Korea reported prepared to eject Communist members of UN inspection teams:
South Korea will attempt positive action to eject Polish and Czech members of the UN inspection teams.

General Won Yong-tok's threat to take "positive measures" if the Communists fail to respond favorably by 29 November to his request that they withdraw from Korea manifests a sincere hope to force the withdrawal of the teams and must be taken seriously.

South Korea will start with a campaign to incite public opinion against the Communists' presence in South Korea, and follow it with public demonstrations, mob violence, and government-directed covert acts of violence against individual Communist team members.

Comment: A government-directed campaign against the teams in August included sniping at team members and the throwing of crude bombs into the NNIT compounds. At that time the Eighth Army commander warned that it was impossible to ensure the teams' absolute security. Only a firm stand by American authorities caused a temporary halt in the Korean campaign.

Won Yong-tok was preparing a group of former Chinese prisoners of war to take action against the Communist members.
