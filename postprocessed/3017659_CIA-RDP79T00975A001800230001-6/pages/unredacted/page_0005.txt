SOUTH ASIA

4. Comment on probable talks between India and Pakistan:

Indian-Pakistani talks, to which Prime Minister Nehru agreed on 21 November, are not likely to make much headway toward a settlement of outstanding political differences between the two countries, for example the Kashmir dispute, although there may be some progress toward better economic relations, especially in trade matters. India has not been willing to make any major concessions in previous bilateral negotiations. Pakistan, which proposed the new talks, has been prepared to go further in the past in seeking agreement with India.
