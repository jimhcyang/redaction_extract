7. Comment on Egyptian situation:

The Revolutionary Command Council's announcement canceling the June elections of a constituent assembly and its own dissolution on 24 July is a major victory for Colonel Nasr and his supporters in their month-long struggle for power with General Nagib.

The council's determination to reassert its authority, by force if necessary, followed its realization that drastic action was required to preserve its position.

The announcement, following three days of confused political maneuvering and conferences between the two factions, re-establishes the council as the supreme authority in Egypt and represents a setback to the forces demanding an early return to parliamentary government.
