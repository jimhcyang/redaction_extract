NEAR EAST - AFRICA

5. Signs of increased political stability reported in Iran:

Ambassador Henderson reports from Tehran that the downward trend in the prestige of Prime Minister Zahedi's government has been reversed, at least temporarily. The government's greater self-confidence is reflected in Zahedi's recent cabinet changes.

The public appears impressed by the strict order maintained during the 12 November demonstrations and the apparent solution of pressing financial problems. The initial impact of Mossadeq's demagogic behavior at his trial has been dissipated.

Apparently the shah is now less preoccupied with seeking a potential alternative to Zahedi as prime minister. Effective propaganda is expected to result from wide distribution of a photograph showing the shah and Zahedi smiling at each other.

Comment: This relatively favorable report of Iranian political stability is encouraging but probably not indicative of long-term stability.
