EASTERN EUROPE

10. Scandinavian diplomats anticipate easier trade negotiations with Poland:
Chiefs of the Swedish, Finnish and Danish missions in Warsaw consider that Poland is finding payment for badly needed imports increasingly difficult because of the strained domestic economic situation and the lowering of coal prices in the West. The heads of the Danish and Swedish missions do not anticipate that their countries will have to make important concessions to Poland in trade negotiations scheduled to begin soon in Warsaw, despite their need for Polish exports.
