However, faction leaders within the LDP as well as the opposition Socialists are maneuvering to force him to come to grips shortly with major issues. LDP leaders agree that the issue--particularly the questions of a government-to-government trade agreement and admission of Communist China to the UN--is more political than economic, particularly since poor economic conditions in mainland China have decreased the interest of large Japanese industries in the Chinese market. However, some of Ikeda's conservative rivals may try to improve their own political positions by appealing to small producers still interested in trade with the mainland and by exploiting the popular desire for an independent Japanese policy toward Peiping. Many conservatives as well as the Socialists oppose repayment, on grounds that the Japanese believed the aid was a grant rather than a loan. The Japanese press reported on 6 April that Finance Minister Mizuta hopes to hold repayments below $500,000,000--a figure $50,000,000 below Japan's lowest informal offer.
