Iran: The nine new members of Prime Minister Ali Amini's cabinet appear generally less experienced and capable than their immediate predecessors. All have had some government experience, mostly at a rather low level. Two of them have past histories of association with the Communists, although not in recent years. Four ministers were held over from the previous cabinet, two of them, the ministers of war and interior, at the insistence of the Shah. Two posts remain unfilled. The new appointees, however, probably owe their primary allegiance to the prime minister rather than to the Shah.

*On 9 May the Shah dissolved both houses of parliament and called for new elections under revised electoral laws.
