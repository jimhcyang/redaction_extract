CENTRAL INTELLIGENCE BULLETIN
15 January 1958

DAILY BRIEF

I. THE COMMUNIST BLOC

Khrushchev's absence from Moscow for a short rest has been officially announced. He has not appeared in public since 2 January, although there have been several functions he would normally have attended. Khrushchev took a six-week vacation in early fall, and his present absence may have been caused by illness.

The Gomulka regime during 1958 will try to preserve its position within the Communist bloc and stay in power without returning to harsh Stalinist practices. Polish leaders have only limited confidence in the economic prospects for the coming year and they are determined to maintain an opening to the West, not only for material benefits, but also in the hope of using international recognition as a protection against Soviet encroachments.
