7. BURMESE BORDER DISPUTE SETTLEMENT EXPECTED SOON

An early signing of the Sino-Burmese border agreement, under negotiation for some months, appears likely.

a special meeting of the Kachin State Council on 14 February is expected to approve the cession of three Kachin villages to Communist China.

Despite a formal settlement which would appear to remove a major source of friction between the two governments, tension in the border area is likely to develop from time to time as the result of local incidents.
