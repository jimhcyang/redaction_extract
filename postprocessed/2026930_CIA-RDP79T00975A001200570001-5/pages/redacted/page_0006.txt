NEAR EAST - AFRICA

8. Latest Anglo-Egyptian meeting reveals sharp conflict:

A sharp conflict between the British and Egyptians over the question of the duration of any Anglo-Egyptian agreement on the

Suez base emerged at the informal meeting of representatives of the two countries on 24 August, according to the American embassy in Cairo.

The British are proposing a ten-year agreement, with 4,000 technicians remaining at the base for five years and the number tapering off thereafter. Britain insists that all duration periods must start after withdrawal of British troops has been completed. Eighteen months to two years is considered the minimum for evacuation.

The Egyptian counterproposal was a five-year agreement, with technicians to remain three years only. The Egyptian negotiators maintain that the duration period must start from the date of the signing of the agreement.

Comment: Anglo-Egyptian negotiations on the Suez base were suspended on 6 May. Informal meetings between the delegates have been taking place in Cairo since 28 July in an effort to establish sufficient agreement for the resumption of formal talks.
