5. FINNS UNMOVED BY SOVIET WARNINGS

Comment on:

The Finns generally regard the 26 March Izvestia warning against growing militarism in Finland as a periodic reminder that Moscow continues to keep a close watch on their activities, according to the American embassy in Helsinki. The Russians have on several recent occasions expressed distrust of Finnish military leaders, and the Finnish defense minister was warned on 22 March against allowing Finland to "become a bridge against the USSR in the event of war." The Finns do not regard themselves as singled out for a special attack, since the Scandinavian countries have recently been subjected to criticism from Moscow too.

President Kekkonen has ordered a brigade of troops permanently stationed in Finnish Lapland to show Finland's determination to defend the area against both East and West. Finland will probably use the exchange of high-level delegations on the anniversary in early April of the 1948 Soviet-Finnish treaty of friendship and mutual assistance as an occasion for reaffirming its policy of strict neutrality.
