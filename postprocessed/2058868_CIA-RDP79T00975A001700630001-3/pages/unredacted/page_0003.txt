FAR EAST

1. Agents may have directed Communist fire on Quemoys:

The Chinese Communist artillery bombardment of the Quemoy Islands on 3 September was apparently directed by Communist agents on the islands, light signals were directed during the night to Communist-held Amoy from a hilly area in the southwest part of the larger island.

between 3 and 6 September three 155mm guns in the same part of the island were moved three times to new positions. Each time, artillery in the Amoy area registered on them in a manner suggesting that the Communists knew the precise location of the guns.

Comment: An American officer who recently returned from the Quemoys reported that Communist agents were believed to be present and to be very well informed.

NEAR EAST - AFRICA

2. Comment on Iraqi policy on Middle East defense:

A recent conversation between Iraqi prime minister Nuri Said and American chargé Ireland strongly suggests that Iraq is willing to follow Egypt's lead in reference to Western plans for Middle East defense and do nothing on the matter for the present.

Nuri told Ireland that he had no confirmation of reports of differences between Iraq and Egypt on their current proposal for modifying the Arab League Collective Security pact to provide for non-Arab participation. Nuri stated that Egypt is presently reluctant to take any potentially unpopular step, such as joining a defense pact with the West, but that all it needed was a little time.
