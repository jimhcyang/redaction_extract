8. SOUTH VIETNAM INCENSED OVER ANTI-DIEM FORCES IN CAMBODIA

The apparent conviction of Saigon leaders that the recent attempted assassination of President Diem is attributable to anti-Diem Vietnamese in Phnom Penh may provoke reprisals against Cambodia. Diem himself believes that the growing Communist influence in Cambodia as a result of Prince Sihanouk's policies fosters the activities of these elements and poses a serious subversive threat to South Vietnam.

Saigon has long been frustrated by its inability to suppress harassing attacks by dissident sects and Viet Minh bands using Cambodian territory as a base of operations.
