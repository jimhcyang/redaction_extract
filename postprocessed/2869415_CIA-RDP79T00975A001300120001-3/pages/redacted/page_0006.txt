6. Tunisian Communists open country-wide youth campaign:

A recently organized Communist cell at the Grand Mosque school in Tunis is recruiting Moslem youth to form the nucleus of groups which they plan to establish in secondary schools throughout Tunisia, according to an official at the Grand Mosque. Leaders of this campaign reportedly hope to have cells in operation by early October, the beginning of the school year.

Comment: Communism, though legal, has not enjoyed notable success in Tunisia, and most of its top leaders have been imprisoned since early 1952. Organizers may operate without police interference so long as they do not disturb the national "peace and security."

The prolonged tension and dissatisfaction which prevails in Tunisia provides a receptive atmosphere for Communist propaganda. Organizers have been increasingly active in education and cultural institutions since last March, when political controls were beginning to be somewhat relaxed.
