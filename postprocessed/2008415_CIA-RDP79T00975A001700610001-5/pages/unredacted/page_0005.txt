Comment: This plan closely follows German Socialist thinking, and is apparently based upon the assumption that its main support in Germany would come from the Socialists. It also would have a definite appeal to the French neutralist elements on whose advice Mendes-France has consistently acted.

The proposed four-power agreement implies willingness to engage in four-power discussions without prior Soviet commitments. The USSR could be expected to encourage these proposals as they can be exploited to cause dissension among the Western allies.
