EASTERN EUROPE

7. Trieste Military Commander opposed to election postponement:

The British Commander of the Allied Military Government is opposed in principle to postponing the Trieste elections. He feels that an indefinite postponement would allow the Italians to undermine AMG's authority and sees no genuine Italian effort to compromise with the Yugoslavs. The commander indicates that he will risk postponement to the end of the year, however, if there is any real prospect of opening negotiations for a Trieste settlement.

Comment: In view of the currently inflamed state of Italian public opinion on Trieste, it is unlikely that the Italian Government would make sufficient concessions at this time to effect a settlement.
