1. CHINESE NATIONALIST EVACUATION OF WUCHIU
ISLANDS REPORTED IMMINENT

The American naval attaché in Taipei believes Chiang Kai-shek will approve Chinese Nationalist plans for an "imminent" evacuation of the 480-man guerrilla force from the Wuchiu Islands, midway between Matsu and Quemoy.

The Nationalist source who reported plans for the evacuation says an evacuation of the Yinshan Islands, 30 miles northeast of Matsu, has also been discussed. The Yinshans will be held, however, since they lie well offshore and it would require a major Chinese Communist amphibious effort to take them.

The attaché's source also reported that the White Dog Islands, 10 miles south of Matsu, are a "real worry" and that the Nationalists "never should have attempted to defend" them.

Comment

The leaking of this information suggests that Taipei is probing for American reaction to withdrawals from the smaller islands. Without an American commitment to help defend the islands, the Nationalists may eventually abandon both the Yinshan and White Dog groups, in addition to the Wuchius.

While withdrawals of Nationalist garrisons from any of the offshore islands will be exploited to the maximum by Communist propaganda, the Yinshans, White Dogs and Wuchius are highly vulnerable to Communist strikes and are of little military value to the Nationalists.
