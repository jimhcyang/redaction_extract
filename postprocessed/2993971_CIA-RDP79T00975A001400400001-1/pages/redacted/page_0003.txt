FAR EAST

2. South Korea planning to demand criminal jurisdiction over UN troops:
Public statements by Foreign Minister Pyun in the past few days confirm that the Rhee government is now drafting an administrative agreement covering the status of UN forces in South Korea and giving the Rhee government criminal jurisdiction over them, according to the American embassy in Seoul. The draft aims at arrangements comparable to those provided under the NATO, US-Japan and US-Philippines agreements.

Comment: Some UN members probably will prefer to withdraw their troops from Korea rather than to subject them to Korean justice.
