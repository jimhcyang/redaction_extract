Japan-Korea: The Japanese Government has lifted restrictions on trade with North Korea. A Japanese Foreign Ministry official told the US Embassy that Japan could not continue to be the only free world country, other than the United States, to maintain such restrictions. North Korea has sought to have these restrictions lifted for some time, and Pyongyang's propaganda has claimed that trade could be expanded considerably if Tokyo would agree to exchange--preferably on a long-term basis--certain high-priority industrial equipment for North Korean iron ore.

South Korea instructed Foreign Minister Chong, now attending the UN General Assembly session in New York, to seek US pressure on Japan. (Backup, Page 5)
