5. Peiping hints at five-year plan goals:

Communist China's Ministry of Fuel Industry has calculated that 80 percent more coal "will be needed" in China by 1957.

Comment: Peiping has released very little information on the goals of the present five-year plan which ends in 1957. Tentative intelligence estimates of the planned industrial expansion up to now have ranged from 60 to 100 percent.

Expansion of the coal industry can be a rough indication of plans for industrial expansion as a whole, because coal is generally used for fuel in China's industries, power plants and railroads. Therefore, a more nearly precise estimate of 1957 industrial prospects is now possible.
