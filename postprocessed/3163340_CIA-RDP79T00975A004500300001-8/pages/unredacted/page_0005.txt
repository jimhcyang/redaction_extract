Yemen: Unrest, probably related to the uncertain Yemeni succession problem, has again developed into violence in Yemen. "the situation is very serious" in Taiz, Yemen's southern capital, where fighting involving soldiers and civilians was in progress. Government forces quelled an earlier army uprising in Sana, the northern capital, on 18 May.

(In Rome, it is improbable that the Imam will ever be able to rule effectively again. Physicians have characterized his treatment for drug addiction as "endless and probably hopeless." Crown Prince Badr apparently hopes to keep the Imam in Italy so that he can consolidate his position as the Imam's designated successor in Yemen.)

Guinea: (President Sekou Touré of Guinea may be planning the "liberation" of neighboring Portuguese Guinea through a campaign of propaganda and civil disobedience. He apparently is organizing a network of sympathizers there among tribesmen related to those in the Republic of Guinea. Although Touré has reportedly stated that Guinea would offer armed aid in case of an insurrection in the Portuguese territory, such a development appears unlikely in the near future.) (Page 4)
