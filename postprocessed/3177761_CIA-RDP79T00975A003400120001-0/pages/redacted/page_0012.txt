9. COMMUNISTS MAY MAKE POLITICAL GAINS IN GUATEMALA

Comment on:

Anti-Communist political and military forces in Guatemala have yet to recover from the divisive effect of the violence which led to the annulment of last month's elections. New elections, for which candidates have not yet been named, are set for 19 January, and the Communists are likely to gain politically if the leftist Revolutionary party (PR), which they have infiltrated and which is probably the country's strongest single party, wins significant representation in congress. The party candidate may even win the presidency.

Ambassador Hill in Mexico emphasizes that the return of a pro-Communist regime to Guatemala would be especially damaging to the United States' position in Mexico, where a large and influential pro-Communist element is becoming more active.
