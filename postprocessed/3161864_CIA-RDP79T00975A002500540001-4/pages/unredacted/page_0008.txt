THE ARAB-ISRAELI SITUATION
(Information as of 1700, 25 May)

The Soviet military attache in Syria stated in a conversation with the American army attache that while the T-34 medium tank was suitable for use in Syria, he was opposed to Syria receiving JS-3 heavy tanks and had so recommended. He added that the Syrians would be better off fighting on camels than in tanks. The Soviet assistant military attache also declared that even the new military equipment which Syria was receiving would deteriorate rapidly because of Syria's improper maintenance of tanks and vehicles.

Egyptian armored forces now include two companies of JS-3 heavy tanks, each with 17 tanks mounting 122-mm guns; three companies of SU-100 assault guns, each with 17 100-mm self-propelled guns; a regiment of T-34 medium tanks organized into three companies of 17 tanks each, mounting 85-mm guns; a similar regiment in which each company is reported equipped with 17 T-34's mounting 100-mm guns; and a regiment of Centurions organized into two companies of 17 tanks each. Existence of a T-34 with a 100-mm gun has not been confirmed; however, the new Soviet medium tank, the T-54, mounting a 100-mm gun, has been issued in large quantities to Soviet forces in Germany. No T-54 has previously been reported in connection with the Soviet bloc arms shipments to Egypt. It is not known whether the above units are part of Egypt's newly formed tank division.

According to Beirut radio, Syria will shortly follow Egypt's example in strengthening its navy by equipping it with modern naval units. At present Syria's naval combat forces consist of three small ex-French submarine chasers. (Press)
