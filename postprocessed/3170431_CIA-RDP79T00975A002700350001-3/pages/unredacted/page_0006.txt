3. SOME BRITISH SHIPPING BEING DIVERTED TO AVOID SUEZ CANAL

Comment on:

British shipowners have begun to route around Africa passenger ships normally passing through Suez. Diversions of cargo ships will presumably be ordered if delays occur in transit of ships through the canal under Egyptian management. London shipping rates to the Far East have been increased 15 percent in anticipation of the cost of delays at the canal or possible rerouting.

The American naval attache in Colombo reports that 11 passenger ships have already been rerouted. Two British passenger lines were reported on 14 September to have said that all their ships would avoid the canal.

19 Sept 56 Current Intelligence Bulletin Page 5
