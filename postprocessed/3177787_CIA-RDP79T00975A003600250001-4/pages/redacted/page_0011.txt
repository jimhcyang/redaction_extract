Lebanese Political Situation

Lebanese President Chamoun's maneuvers to ensure parliament's approval of a constitutional amendment allowing him to run for re-election are meeting increasing public opposition. A manifesto issued on 27 March by the President's opponents exhorted the populace to rise up and force him to resign should he try to tamper with the constitution. As a first move against Chamoun, his enemies plan to call a general strike and lead a street demonstration which they believe would lead to disorders necessitating army intervention. The group is confident that army commander General Shihab would refuse to back Chamoun in the face of public disorders, thus forcing the President out of office.

Shihab is being propelled by events into the position of a possible contender for the presidency. In the present circumstances, it is uncertain if the security forces would be willing to maintain public order in the interests of the present pro-Western government. The chiefs of the gendarmerie and police believe their forces are capable of ensuring security in Beirut, but not in the countryside.

Psychological warfare by both Chamoun and his opponents, including veiled threats and undercover activities, are aggravating the already delicate situation.
