SOVIET UNION

1. Molotov may be retired as Soviet foreign minister before October conference:

Bulganin said at Geneva that "perhaps it will be necessary to get rid of Molotov as foreign minister before the October conference,"

It is improbable that there are any serious policy disagreements between Molotov and the other Soviet leaders and he will probably, therefore, retain his position on the party presidium.

Gromyko, as the senior first deputy foreign minister, would appear the most likely candidate to succeed Molotov. If the USSR wishes to repudiate the "hard" approach to foreign relations, however, Gromyko might be passed over for someone more "reasonable" and conciliatory, such as Molotov's other first deputy, V. V. Kuznetsov.

FAR EAST

2. Resignation of Sun Li-jen may weaken Chinese Nationalist regime:

Sun said that "confessions" implicating him had been extracted by torture from young officers seized when their plan was discovered.
