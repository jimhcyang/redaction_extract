CENTRAL INTELLIGENCE BULLETIN
14 January 1961

DAILY BRIEF

I. THE COMMUNIST BLOC

II. ASIA-AFRICA

Indonesia: President Sukarno has appointed a high proportion of leftists, including the Communist party chairman, to executive bodies in the National Front, which Sukarno heads and which he probably intends to develop as Indonesia's single political party. The army's representation is fairly submerged in the Front hierarchy. Sukarno appears to have shelved temporarily his efforts to include Communists in the cabinet; instead, he is promoting them in other government organizations in the hope of gradually working them into the direct line of government, despite army objections.
