WESTERN EUROPE

7. French point out advantages of five-power talks:

The most important point in the Soviet note of 28 September was the suggestion of five-power talks to ease world tensions, in the opinion of French vice premier Paul Reynaud, who told Ambassador Dillon that the cabinet had shown general sympathy for his views. Reynaud has little hope that such negotiations would be successful but thinks they would "unmask the real intentions of Communist China in the same manner that the Russians' intentions regarding Germany had now been unmasked."
