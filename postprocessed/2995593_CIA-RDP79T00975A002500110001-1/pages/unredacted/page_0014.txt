Four Soviet bloc mine craft and a Polish merchant vessel, suspected of being en route to Egypt, have been reported west of Ireland. The mine craft probably are ex-Soviet T-43 class mine sweepers known to have been ordered under the Egyptian-Soviet bloc arms deal. They are equipped for both mine sweeping and laying, and often operate as escort vessels as well.

The Egyptian naval attaché in Poland has stated that, in addition to some 200 Egyptian naval personnel who have received training in Poland, approximately the same number are training in Czechoslovakia and the USSR.

the Egyptian list of requirements for additional Soviet bloc arms includes 55,000 rifles and 25,000 submachine guns as well as the previously reported 40 MIG fighter aircraft and at least 20 light jet bombers. A new program is being worked out for the training in Czechoslovakia of Egyptian technical personnel in the maintenance and servicing of bloc aircraft.
