10. Possible Soviet moves in Berlin examined:

Although American officials in Berlin believe that recent reports of a Russian plan for another Berlin blockade were planted by the USSR as part of a "scare campaign," they anticipate increased harassing in Berlin at the time of West Germany's signing or ratification of the contractual agreements.

These officials doubt that the Russians will step up interference in Berlin at the present time, however, particularly while they are trying to attract West German sympathy for their unity and peace proposals.

Comment: Soviet harassing measures in Berlin have slackened in recent months, although the USSR is still causing some inconvenience by delaying the approval of export permits and by the occasional closing of some canal locks on the routes to Berlin.

11. Some influential Adenauer supporters favor delay in Allied-German negotiations:

Although Chancellor Adenauer wants the current negotiations on the contractual agreements and the European Defense Treaty to be concluded speedily, some of his coalition supporters, including right-wing Free Democrats and left-wing Christian Democrats, now want these discussions delayed.
