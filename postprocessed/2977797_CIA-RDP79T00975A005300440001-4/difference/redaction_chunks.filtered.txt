  Text: Communist China Over Trade Prospects Peiping is postponing decisions on the extent and nature of its future trade with the rest of the bloc. Peiping, in the process of conducting a survey of its economy, feels it is not in a position to estimate its 1961 export capabilities. The Chinese have delayed signing new contracts and have canceled some import orders, which will affect the level of trade this year. The cancellation of imports applies only to orders which have not been formalized in contracts; formal contracts signed earlier by the Chinese are still in effect, and deliveries to China are continuing as usual. Export contracts are apparently still being honored, but Peiping is delaying any new commitments. Peiping's indecisiveness about trade plans for next year is probably attributable in part to the regime's recognition of its internal economic difficulties. The Chinese have been uncommonly vague about economic accomplishments this year and have been especially worried about agriculture. Difficulties of maintaining agricultural production because of drought and flood this year throw China's trade potential into doubt. Agricultural products, both raw and processed, make up approximately two thirds of China's exports to the bloc.
