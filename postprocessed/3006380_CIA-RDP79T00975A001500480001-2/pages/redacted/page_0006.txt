EASTERN EUROPE

7. Soviet offer of MIG-15's to Finland reported:

The Finnish government is considering a Soviet offer to supply the Finnish air force with MIG-15 aircraft.

The number of MIG-15's offered is not known. The cost of the planes would be deducted from the existing Finnish trade credit.

Comment: The sale of aircraft to a non-Orbit country would be a departure from Soviet practice. The USSR might be willing, however, to export older type MIG-15's--now being replaced by newer models--since no aircraft secrets would be revealed to the West.

the commander of the Finnish air force is opposed to the purchase of MIG-15's because of the training problem. Finnish officials feel certain that Moscow would not permit Finnish instructors to receive training in the USSR, and they are opposed to having Soviet instructors in Finland.
