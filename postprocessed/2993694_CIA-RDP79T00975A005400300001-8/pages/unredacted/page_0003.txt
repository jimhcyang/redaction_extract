5 DECEMBER 1960

I. THE COMMUNIST BLOC
USSR, through contacts at UN, offers Bolivia substantial economic assistance.

II. ASIA-AFRICA
South Vietnamese President, concerned over reports of planned North Vietnamese aggression, decides to mobilize additional forces.
Sudanese regime moves toward closer ties with UAR and neutrality between East and West following Nasir visit.
Laos--Phoumi's troops reportedly gaining advantage over Kong Le's Vientiane forces; proposed National Assembly meeting unlikely to be held in near future.
Congo--Lumumba's followers in Stanleyville move toward "secession" of Orientale Province; apparently hope to extend their influence further in Congo interior with help of "foreign powers."

III. THE WEST
West German cabinet sharply divided on question of handling trade negotiations with East Germany.
