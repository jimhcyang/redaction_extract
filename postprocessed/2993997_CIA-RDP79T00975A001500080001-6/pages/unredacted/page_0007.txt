LATIN AMERICA
12. Argentina may support anti-Communist resolution at Caracas:

Comment: The anti-Communist resolution was passed in committee on 13 March by a 17-to-1 vote. Argentina and Mexico abstained, however, insisting that the text did not provide adequate safeguards against foreign intervention in national affairs. Guatemala cast the only negative vote.

The Argentine chief of protocol told Ambassador Nufer on 17 March that Argentina might support the resolution in plenary session. At that time any country may note reservations to its approval of the resolution. Mexican support, even with reservations, remains highly doubtful.
