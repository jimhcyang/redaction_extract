GENERAL

India strongly opposed to Thai UN appeal:

The Indians are "really getting their steam up" over Thailand's appeal to the UN Security Council, according to the British acting high commissioner in New Delhi. He told Ambassador Allen on 3 June that Indian officials believe the Thai move is an effort to by-pass, impede or belittle the Geneva negotiations on Indochina.

On 2 June the secretary general of the Indian Foreign Ministry summarily refused the Thai ambassador's request that India support the appeal.

Comment: Vehement Indian opposition could severely hamper the success of Thailand's request that the UN Peace Observation Commission observe Communist action in Indochina as a threat to Thai security, since India has been envisaged as a leading participant in the subcommittee which would be set up for this purpose.

New Delhi can also be expected to oppose any other move which it regards as tending to distract attention from Geneva or is strongly objected to by the Communists.
