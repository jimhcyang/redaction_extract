29 OCTOBER 1959

I. THE COMMUNIST BLOC
Evidence that CPSU received little advance information on Chinese commune program suggests lack of close Sino-Soviet consultation on doctrinal matters.
Khrushchev says he is convinced West would not have gone to war had he foreclosed Western occupation of Berlin.
Peiping reportedly sends sharp protest to Djakarta over its action against local Chinese businessmen.
GMAIC comments on recent Tyura Tam firings.

II. ASIA—AFRICA
Nehru said to consider withdrawal of Indian diplomats from Peiping if Chinese continue aggressive acts; diplomatic break unlikely.
Soviet official states USSR absolutely opposed to continued UN presence in Laos.
Shake-up of Libyan Government appears imminent.
Way opened for repatriation of Koreans in Japan to North Korea.
