WESTERN EUROPE

3. Britain considering modification in NATO defense goals:

The British government is reconsidering its approach to Western European defense requirements and NATO force plans, according to a Defense Ministry official. Britain will recommend shifting emphasis from NATO's "forward" defensive strategy to a more modest "deterrent" force, the size of which would be within the present capabilities of NATO members and West Germany.

One purpose of this change would be the elimination of NATO's concern caused by the gap between theoretical requirements and existing forces.

Comment: There is little prospect of any substantial increase in the NATO defense effort for 1954. The proposed modification of strategy would be a further step in the process, begun during last year's annual review, in which the NATO members' political and economic capabilities were given precedence over the military requirements for the security of the entire North Atlantic area.

4. Allied and West German officials agree on plans for food program:

In a meeting of Allied representatives with West German and Berlin authorities, it was tentatively agreed that the second round of food distribution would begin as planned on 27 August but would taper off with the approach of the harvest season on about 1 October.
