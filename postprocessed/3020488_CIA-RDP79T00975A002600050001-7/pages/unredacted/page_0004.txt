1. ARAB AID TO ALGERIAN REBELS

The Arab states appear to be moving promptly to implement a mid-May Arab League Political Committee resolution, which calls for a "continuation and increase of material and military assistance to the Algerian rebels in the manner which each country deems appropriate."

12 tons of arms and ammunition promised the rebels some time earlier by the Syrian army were to be collected in Syria and transported by air, presumably to Egypt but possibly direct to Algeria.

Iraq has vigorously supported the Algerian nationalists.
