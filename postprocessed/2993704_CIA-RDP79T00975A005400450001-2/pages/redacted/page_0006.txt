It appears unlikely that the Communist bloc has decided to intervene openly with its own identifiable military forces at this time. A decision on its part to do so in the future, however, is not precluded and probably would be dependent in part on the bloc's estimate of its chances of success in achieving a military or political settlement acceptable to the Communists.
