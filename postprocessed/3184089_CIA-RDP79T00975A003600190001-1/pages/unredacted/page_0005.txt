CENTRAL INTELLIGENCE BULLETIN
22 March 1958

DAILY BRIEF

I. THE COMMUNIST BLOC

Latest Soviet nuclear tests: Two new Soviet nuclear
explosions have been reported by the US Atomic Energy
Detection System. The first occurred at the Semipalatinsk
proving ground on 20 March and the second in the Novaya
Zemlya area on 21 March.
(Page 1)

Soviet missile activity:
Tyura Tam launchings are intended to impact in the waters adjacent
to Kamchatka, and that the USSR may have acquired some
re-entry data from the 30 January firing.
(Page 2)

II. ASIA -AFRICA

Indonesia: The Indonesian army G-3 states an attack
on the dissident center of Padang is planned for the second
week of April. A dissident warning that an attempt will be
made to sabotage Caltex and Stanvac installations in Central
Sumatra rather than permit Djakarta to benefit from re-
newed operations is causing concern to company officials.
In North Sumatra, government forces claim to be success-
fully dispersing the dissident forces which withdrew from
Medan on 17 March.
(Page 3) (Map)
