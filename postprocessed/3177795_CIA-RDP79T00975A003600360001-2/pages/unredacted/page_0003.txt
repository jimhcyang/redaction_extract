11 APRIL 1958

I. THE COMMUNIST BLOC

Soviet reply--later withdrawn--to 31 March Western proposals suggested ambassadors in Moscow limit discussion of pre-summit foreign ministers' meeting to time, place, and composition.

Khrushchev apparently disturbed by indifference of public during his Hungarian visit.

Soviet Union increases air defense capabilities in Central Arctic.

Soviet Air Force begins practice for Moscow May Day flyby.

Peiping apparently agrees to negotiate with New Delhi on one of disputed territories near Tibetan border, but will probably avoid quick settlement.

II. ASIA-AFRICA

Indonesia - Bloc countries and Yugoslavia offer Djakarta further economic and military assistance.

Taipei accepts Japanese assurances regarding Communist Chinese trade mission; plans to resume normal trade relations with Tokyo.

Japanese officials anticipate talks with South Korea for an over-all settlement to begin on 15 April. Negotiations likely to be difficult.

United Arab Republic - Some elements in Syrian Army reported dissatisfied over Nasir's reorganization moves.

III. THE WEST

Gaillard calls US-UK good offices proposals for reopening French-Tunisian negotiations unacceptable.

Cuba - Failure of Castro's general strike removes threat to Batista's control at this time.
