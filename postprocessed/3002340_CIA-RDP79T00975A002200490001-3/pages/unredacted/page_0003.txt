1. POLITICAL IMPLICATIONS OF ADENAUER'S ILLNESS

Comment on:

If the chancellor dies at this time, the
president would ask the Christian Democratic Party to
form a new government. In the struggle for the succession,
Finance Minister Schaeffer and Foreign Minister von
Brentano would be leading candidates.

Should Adenauer merely be prevented
from resuming full conduct of his office for several weeks
more, no grave impairment of the government's functioning would be likely. Since his is largely a personal government, a longer absence would probably have serious
consequences regarding such critical issues as the Saar,
rearmament legislation, and relations with the Soviet
Orbit.

At present, Adenauer's personal aide,
Hans Globke, is probably assuming a major role in the
daily decisions of the chancellery. Vice Chancellor
Bluecher's political influence is waning and presumably
does not permit him to speak with any real authority.
(Concurred in by OSI)
