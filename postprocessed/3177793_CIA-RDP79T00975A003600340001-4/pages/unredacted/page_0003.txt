9 APRIL 1958

I. THE COMMUNIST BLOC

East German, Polish, and Czech foreign ministers meet in Prague, presumably to plan campaign against West German nuclear armament.

Soviet submarines may be active in Atlantic as far west as Newfoundland and in Pacific off US west coast.

Peiping's purge of rightist officials seems to be over for time being.

II. ASIA-AFRICA

Israelis apparently resume drainage operations in Hula area with minor adjustments in conforming with UN survey.

Iran exploring with Pakistan and Turkey advisability of Middle East non-Arab federation to counter Egyptian influence; Afghanistan might also be invited.

IBRD President Black believes Nasir has "real desire" to settle with Suez Canal shareholders soon.

III. THE WEST

Cuba - Batista and Trujillo renew charge that Castro is instrument of international Communism. Charge is largely unsubstantiated.

West Germany appears to have modified repatriation demands in entering trade, repatriation, and consular agreement with USSR.

Italy - Change in Italian Communist leadership unlikely to affect Communist chances in 25 May elections.
