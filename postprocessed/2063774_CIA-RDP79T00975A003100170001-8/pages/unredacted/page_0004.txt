1. SITUATION IN INDONESIA

Comment on:

The central government of Indonesia and military leaders in the dissident provinces are continuing to strengthen their forces and there are no indications that either side has any intention of capitulating. Arrests of antigovernment civil and military personnel continue in Djakarta. The government stated on 12 May that it "cannot allow the existence of movements which endanger the unity of the state and the Indonesian people."

In East Indonesia, Lt. Col. Samual is said to have refused to relinquish his command as ordered by Sukarno, to have demanded the resignation of the cabinet and threatened to arrest any minister coming to his territory. His forces and those of Col. Sudirman, who commands a special task force in the area and is loyal to the central government, are about equal in strength and reportedly are in battle position.

Outstanding anti-Communist national leaders are reported either to be in Central Sumatra or planning to convene there soon. These include former vice president Hatta, Masjumi chairman Natsir, national chief of police Sukanto, and the sultan of Jogjakarta.
