5. French losing support of Thai tribesmen in western Tonkin:

Some guerrillas of the Thai tribes in
western Tonkin, who have long been
"useful allies of the French, are now
going over the Viet Minh with their
materiel and supplies,

Comment: Consolidation of the Thai
territory by the Viet Minh would put an end to the reconnaissance
and harassing activity carried out by the French in conjunction
with the Thai tribesmen, and would facilitate the flow of Chinese
Communist supplies to the Viet Minh from Yunnan.

6. French postpone fall offensive plans in Indochina:

The 1 September progress report on the
Navarre plan from the chief of the Military Assistance Advisory Group in Saigon
states that the French are now planning to
counterattack in the event of a Viet Minh attack on the delta in late
September or early October. If the Viet Minh does not attack in
the delta, the French will undertake a diversionary action against
enemy forces north of that area.

Comment: This planning differs from
the original Navarre program, which called for an "offensive to
forestall the enemy attack."
