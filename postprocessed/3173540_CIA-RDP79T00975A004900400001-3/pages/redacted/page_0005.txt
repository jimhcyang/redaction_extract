President Stroessner reportedly feels confident he can prevent any movement of revolutionaries into the interior, and the US army attache comments that Stroessner has the solid backing of the armed forces.

Cuba: The Cuban foreign minister has reportedly been instructed to examine the agreement under which the US Naval Base at Guantanamo Bay is leased from Cuba, with a view to drafting a note to the US for revision of that agreement. Castro officials have in the past complained that the unilateral power of the US to terminate the agreement is objectionable to Cuba and probably have waited to raise the issue until they considered the timing most useful for purposes of bargaining and propaganda.

Watch Committee Conclusion: The following developments are susceptible of direct exploitation by Soviet/Communist hostile action which could jeopardize the security of the US in the immediate future: None.
