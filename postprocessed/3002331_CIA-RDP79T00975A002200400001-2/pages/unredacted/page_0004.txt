1. REACTIONS TO ESTABLISHMENT OF MOROCCAN
COUNCIL OF THE THRONE

The installation of a 4-member Council of the Throne on 17 October to exercise the functions of the Moroccan sultan is unlikely to lessen Moroccan tension or resolve the two-year-old impasse. The dominant nationalist organization, Istiqlal, immediately denounced the council as a violation of the understanding reached with Premier Faure at Aix-les-Bains in August, and has refused to participate in the new government, which is to be headed by Fatmi ben Slimane. The nationalists want France to recognize Moroccan independence and negotiate a new treaty to replace the protectorate treaty of 1912 under which France governs Morocco.

The organization of reactionary settlers, Presence Francaise, has also indicated its dissatisfaction with the council, claiming that its establishment violates an agreement with Resident General Boyer de Latour.
