6. DALAI LAMA STALLING DEPARTURE FROM INDIA

The Dalai Lama asked permission on 13 December to remain in India a few months longer to visit Buddhist shrines and attend to other religious duties,

As of 15 December, the Indian government had not replied to this request, but Nehru has invited the Dalai Lama to his home in January for further talks.

The Chinese Communists are continuing their pressure on the Dalai Lama to accompany Chou En-lai to Nepal. The Dalai Lama's advisers are urging him not to go, for fear that the Nepalese government is too weak to keep the Communists from forcing him back to Tibet. The advisers consider that the Dalai Lama's refusal to make the trip would probably lead to his breaking with the Communists.

Comment

the Dalai Lama planned to seek asylum while in India. Nehru's reaction would be a key factor in the Dalai Lama's final decision. Nehru would not wish to offend Peiping by granting formal asylum, but he could hardly refuse to an extension of stay on religious grounds. He might also attempt to avoid embarrassment by urging the Dalai Lama to seek asylum in some other country.
