Urging Roberto to have faith in him ("I am leading Africa down the path to freedom and socialism."), Nkrumah also warned the Angolan against any contact with "the Americans," whom he accused of assassinating Lumumba and of refusing to "help me in my cause." When Roberto expressed hope of receiving help from the 20 moderate Monrovia conference states, Nkrumah condemned the Monrovia conferees as "tools of imperialism." Roberto had earlier asked the participants at Monrovia for material and financial support to supplement and offset the UPA's present dependence on Ghana.
