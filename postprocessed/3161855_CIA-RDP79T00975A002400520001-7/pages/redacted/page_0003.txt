1. PINEAU SUGGESTS TRIPARTITE APPEAL TO USSR
ON NEAR EAST ARMS

French foreign minister Pineau told Ambassador Dillon on 19 March he felt there should be a French-British-American ministerial meeting in the near future to consider the Near East arms race. Pineau wondered if the time had not come for the three Western foreign ministers to make a joint public appeal to the Soviet Union to agree to end the arms race, although he expressed himself as opposed to discussing a Near East "settlement" with the USSR.

Pineau indicated his thinking had been spurred by figures he had seen in Cairo on the size of the Soviet arms program for Egypt. He said an additional 200 MIG's were scheduled to follow the first 200 now being delivered.

Comment
The need to reach some kind of agreement on Near Eastern matters has been a recurrent note in French diplomacy since the consummation of Egypt's arms deal with the Soviet bloc last fall. Moscow probably would welcome an approach from the tripartite powers, but it is unlikely that the USSR would agree to limit discussion to the arms problem.

The Israeli ambassador to the United States told Ambassador Lodge on 19 March that Egypt had 110 MIG's and "would soon have 200 more."
