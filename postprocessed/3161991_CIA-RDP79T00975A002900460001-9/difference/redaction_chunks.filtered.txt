In the interest of getting the talks started, Britain is no longer insisting on an agreed agenda--thus giving the Yemenis an opportunity to assert their claim to the entire Aden Protectorate--and proposes to allow the Emir of Beihan to speak for the other Western Protectorate rulers without the presence of a British representative of the Aden government. The Foreign Office states that London is considering making public the fact that Soviet bloc arms are being distributed to Yemeni army training centers. Comment The British earlier indicated their intention to try direct negotiation before turning to other approaches, such as recourse to the UN.
