8 APRIL 1960

I. THE COMMUNIST BLOC

II. ASIA-AFRICA

South Africa--White population, unconcerned at growing international criticism, remains confident natives can be suppressed.

Belgian officials foresee "economic and political turmoil" in the Congo if large-scale financial aid not available before 30 June independence date.

British Somaliland--British fear outright rejection of demands for independence by 1 July would lead to breakdown in security.

Iraq--Army continues tough measures against Communists on eve of Mikoyan's visit.

Indonesia--Anti-Sukarno plotting goes on, but without essential army backing.

Laos--Communists warn of "extremely dangerous situation" if "reactionaries" win elections on 24 April.

III. THE WEST

Communists, with support of labor minister, trying to take control of Cuban Labor Confederation before May Day rally.
