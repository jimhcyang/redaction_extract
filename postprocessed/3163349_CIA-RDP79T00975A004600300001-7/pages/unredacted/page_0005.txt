Morocco: King Mohamed V's hasty departure from France on 2 August without meeting President de Gaulle as planned points up a further deterioration of the prolonged instability within Morocco. Against a background of mounting financial crisis and insecurity along the Algerian border, the anti-monarchist faction within the Moroccan Government is reported to be attempting to undermine the King's authority. (Page 3)

Yemen: Crown Prince Badr and his Egyptian supporters are increasingly concerned that the Imam will countermand the changes instituted during the Imam's absence by which Badr hoped to strengthen his chances for succession. Badr fears the Imam, now en route home from Italy, will not visit the UAR, where Nasir would seek to reassure him about Egyptian intentions in supporting Badr. UAR reports that the tribal chiefs, apparently anticipating the Imam's return, have refused to provide manpower for Badr's enlarged personal military force, on which the prince hoped to base his political ambitions. (Page 4)

Iraq: UAR intelligence reports the Qasim regime has uncovered another Communist plot, this one set for 6 August. The plotters are said to have included the commander of the Iraqi Air Force and an army brigade commander. It is expected that Qasim will announce publicly the Communist party's treachery. Qasim's allegedly pro-Communist aide, Col. Wasfi Tahir, was named as the agent used to expose the conspiracy. This information is not confirmed.

Ceylon: The government of Prime Minister Bandaranaike remains highly vulnerable in view of its narrow majority and the generally unsettled conditions in Ceylon, despite Parliament's approval of the budget. In the critical 31 July vote, defections which conservative elements have been trying to engineer among Bandaranaike's supporters again failed to materialize. (Page 5)
