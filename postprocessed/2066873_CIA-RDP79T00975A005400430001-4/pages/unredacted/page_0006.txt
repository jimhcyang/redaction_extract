There is as yet no confirmation that the USSR has obtained permission to overfly for flights to Stanleyville. UN representative in Leopoldville has stated that three Soviet-type aircraft landed at Stanleyville between 10 and 14 December, but the UN "believes" them to have been UAR aircraft.

III. THE WEST

Cuba: In his effort to dissuade the Castro regime from establishing diplomatic relations with East Germany, the West German ambassador said the acting Cuban foreign minister that West Germany has nothing against the Cuban Government and is neutral insofar as the "Cuban-US quarrel is concerned." The American charge notes that there is probably not a single foreign chief of mission in Havana, with the exception of certain Latin American ambassadors, who has condemned to high Cuban officials the violation of human rights by the Castro regime or criticized its rapprochement with the Sino-Soviet bloc.
