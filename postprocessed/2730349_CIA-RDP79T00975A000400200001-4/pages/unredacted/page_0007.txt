7. US-Spanish military talks may be reaching stalemate:
Latest reports from the US Ambassador in Madrid indicate that US-Spanish military talks may be approaching an impasse because Franco fears that US aid will be of a limited military nature only and that appreciable economic aid will not be forthcoming. In a recent statement to an official of a US news agency, Franco is reported to have mentioned $200 million as a satisfactory beginning for the economic assistance he wants.

Comment: Franco's determination to require substantial economic aid as a quid pro quo for a US-Spanish military arrangement has been reported before and there is no indication that he will alter his views.
