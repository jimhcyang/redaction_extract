5. Saudi Arabian King favors moderation toward West Germany on Israeli reparations:
Comment: King Ibn Saud traditionally exerts considerable influence in the Arab world. His moderate attitude, which is similar to the conciliatory position unofficially adopted by Egyptian leaders, may partly account for the fact that Bonn and Arab representatives are making some progress in their current trade negotiations.

Barring a violent popular reaction to the expected Bundestag ratification, Arab League opposition to the West German action will probably result only in a face-saving boycott against certain German firms dealing with Israel, rather than in a general Arab boycott of West Germany.
