Diem is purging some locally disliked rural officials and is permitting some controlled opposition political activity, but he maintains that no real liberalization is possible under present circumstances. However, unless tangible improvement is demonstrated, the prospect of prolonged insecurity and continued harsh controls may encourage new plotting against Diem or his government and the growth of sentiment for political accommodation with the Communists.
