6. EGYPTIAN FOREIGN MINISTER REPORTS "UNREST" IN ARMY CIRCLES

it was "apparent" that there was "unrest" in the Egyptian army. while President Nasr formerly visited the army divisions he now seldom calls on the army and in fact "visits hardly anyone." Hussein responded that one of his colleagues had heard a broadcast to the effect that army commander in chief Amer and certain other generals now in a majority in "the Revolutionary Command Council" favored "a treaty with the USSR" but were opposed in this by Nasr.

Comment  Recriminations among officers of the Egyptian forces have been reported frequently since the Egyptian military debacle in Sinai. Strong differences of opinion among Nasr's military supporters, who formerly composed the Revolutionary Command Council, have not been unusual in the past, but there has been no good evidence that such disagreements have gone further than debate.
