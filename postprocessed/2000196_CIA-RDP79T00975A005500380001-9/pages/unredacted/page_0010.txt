As long as the USSR remains in the UN, it will have to conduct routine business with the Secretariat, the staff of which is predominantly loyal to Hammarskjold. The USSR vetoed the reappointment of Hammarskjold's predecessor, Trygve Lie, in October 1950, linking him to the UN action in Korea. The General Assembly later that year extended Lie's appointment for three years. The Soviet bloc responded by officially ignoring Lie as secretary general for the ensuing two years. This pressure finally forced him to resign in 1953.
