15 February 1961
CENTRAL INTELLIGENCE BULLETIN

CONTENTS

1. Congo: Moscow's 14 February statement foreshadows probable increase of pressure on Sudan to allow transit of aid to Gizenga and possible formal recognition of Gizenga by USSR. (Page 1)

2. Laos: Moscow hints desire to avoid crisis in Laos. (Page 4)

3. Berlin: East Germans tell Bonn they do not intend to honor their previous commitment to relax restrictions on West Germans traveling to East Berlin. (Page 10)

4. India: Nehru's release of official reports on Sino-Indian border talks underscores deadlock between New Delhi and Peiping. (Page 6)

5. Albania: Hoxha blames US, Greece, and Yugoslavia for attempted coup against his regime; our evidence suggests coup instigated by pro-Soviet elements within Albania. (Page 8)

6. Turkey: Cabinet changes and undefined role of political parties newly approved by ruling junta have again aroused public concern. (Page 10)

7. Angola: Lisbon considers the situation in Angola extremely bad. (Page 12)

8. Cuba: Communists describe their alliance with Castro regime as "exceedingly close." (Page 14)
