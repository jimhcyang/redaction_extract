8. MAGLOIRE FACTION IN HAITIAN ARMY MAY
CONTROL NEW PRESIDENT

A faction of Haitian army officers, led by men representing the interests of ousted dictator Paul Magloire, has gained the upper hand in the army and is determined to retain control of the government. This group intends to make a puppet of President-elect Francois Duvalier, scheduled to be inaugurated in mid-October, and will remove him from office if he proves intractable. Duvalier is believed to be aware of this plan and will probably conform with the wishes of the army, at least for the present.

A new outbreak of civil disorder is probable if the army should attempt a postinauguration move against Duvalier. Popular reaction to an extension of army rule, which of late has become excessively harsh and arbitrary, would probably be violent. Racial bitterness, which recently resulted in increased persecution of mulattoes, and further plotting by opposition elements have already aggravated the situation.
