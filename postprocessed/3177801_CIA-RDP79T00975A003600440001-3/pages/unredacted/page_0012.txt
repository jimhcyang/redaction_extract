Recent dissident air attacks on Makassar and Balikpapan led the central government's chief of staff for the navy on 15 April to order the dispersal of ships at the Surabaya naval base.

Indonesia and Communist China have exchanged notes whereby Indonesia will receive $11,400,000 worth of rice and textiles on a credit which will run for ten years at 2.5-percent interest. The use of an exchange of notes to cover this transaction rather than a formal agreement is believed to be a device to defer the necessity for parliamentary ratification.
