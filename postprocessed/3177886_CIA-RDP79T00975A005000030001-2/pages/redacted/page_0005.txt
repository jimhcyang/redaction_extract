Afghanistan: Foreign Minister Naim, in a press interview
a few days prior to Khrushchev's arrival, said that President
Eisenhower's visit to Kabul had not resulted in any greater un-
derstanding on the part of the West. He also accused the United
States of tying its military aid to membership in military pacts,
a condition unacceptable to Afghanistan. The Foreign Minister
expressed appreciation for American economic aid. However,
he reiterated Kabul's determination on the Pushtoonistan issue
and his intention to discuss economic aid with Khrushchev.
Naim seemed to reflect the opinion that the US should be able
to exercise some control over Pakistani propaganda.
