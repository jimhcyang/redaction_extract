LATE ITEM

USSR: The TASS announcement on 7 January that flight tests of a more powerful rocket booster for space vehicles would be undertaken between 15 January and 15 February 1960 is the first public Soviet announcement that such tests would be made within specified dates. The reported impact area covers about 45,000 square miles of the Pacific with the center about 550 miles south of Johnston Island and 400 miles west-northwest of Palmyra Island. The USSR says Soviet ships will be in the impact area, and asks other governments to refrain from sending ships or aircraft into the area during the specified time period.

On 7 January, three of the four Soviet Sibir-class instrumentation ships were observed stopped in the water about 400 miles southwest of Midway, some 1,200 miles northwest of the reported impact area. The fourth ship was located about 560 miles west-northwest of Midway. These ships participated in a practice exercise with the Tyura Tam rangehead on 6 January.

The validity of the Soviet statement that the projected tests will be in development of a more powerful rocket system for space vehicles cannot be determined at this time. The center of the impact area is about 6,500 miles from the Tyura Tam rangehead. Current estimates give the Soviet ICBM a range of 5,500 to 7,500 miles depending on the weight of the nosecone. The Soviets are considered capable of testing to this range during the time period specified, or before, if they so choose.
