CENTRAL INTELLIGENCE BULLETIN
24 March 1958

DAILY BRIEF

Laos: The Communists could make a good showing in the 4 May elections for 21 new seats in the National Assembly. The two leading conservative parties have been unable to resolve their differences and still have 53 candidates in the field. The Communists, in contrast, have been working hard, avoiding controversial issues, and are posing as the champions of Buddhism, the crown, and established Laotian traditions.
