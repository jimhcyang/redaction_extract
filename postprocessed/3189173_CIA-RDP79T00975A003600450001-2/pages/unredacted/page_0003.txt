22 APRIL 1958

I. THE COMMUNIST BLOC

Gomulka planning trip to other satellite countries in further Polish move to assert bloc solidarity.

Soviet ambassador renews general aid offer to Lebanon in attempt to weaken Chamoun government.

Peiping introduces new complicating proposal in border dispute with Burma.

II. ASIA-AFRICA

Indonesia - Government troops continue advance toward Bukittinggi, last dissident stronghold in Sumatra. Dissident leader reported planning guerrilla warfare.

Tension in Jerusalem rising as both Jordan and Israel deploy armor; serious incidents likely.

Majority of Suez Canal Company shareholders are likely to approve Nasir's compensation offer of $65,000,000.

III. THE WEST

Cyprus - British authorize Turkish Youth Day demonstrations for 23 April despite dangers of violence.
