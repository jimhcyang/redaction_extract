DAILY BRIEF

I. THE COMMUNIST BLOC

USSR: Red Army Major General Nikolai A. Talensky, a noted writer on Soviet military doctrine, has condemned the idea that war might be justified if it meant the end of capital- ism. In the magazine "International Life," Talensky said that after another war, which "could not fail" to be a world war, "the earth's population would be cut in half, and it would be the most active and most cultured part of mankind that would disap- pear." He concluded that a mass surprise attack cannot bring victory for the attacker because the retaliation could still destroy all his vital centers.

The article, which has been given added stature by a re- view by TASS, is a direct attack on the Chinese Communist attitude that the dangers of nuclear war have been overrated. Talensky is also in effect reiterating to the Chinese--who con- tinue to clamor for Taiwan--that the concept of limited war is invalid because it would risk full-scale war.
