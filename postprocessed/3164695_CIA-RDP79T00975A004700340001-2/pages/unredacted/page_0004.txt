CENTRAL INTELLIGENCE BULLETIN
9 October 1959

DAILY BRIEF

I. THE COMMUNIST BLOC

USSR: The counselor of the Soviet Embassy in Paris told an American official if the United States rebuffs Khrushchev's efforts toward a relaxation of world tensions, those forces in the USSR opposed to a rapprochement--now "10 to 20 percent of the population"--would "expand rapidly." As part of Moscow's persistent effort to make the West more amenable to Soviet views, Soviet officials have in the past suggested that strong Western opposition to foreign policy initiatives identified with Khrushchev would strengthen elements in the USSR which favor a "tougher" foreign policy. The Soviet diplomat again suggested that agreement could easily be reached on cessation of nuclear tests and expansion of US-Soviet trade, and that Moscow might be willing to pave the way for increased trade by agreeing to a lend-lease settlement of around $500,000,000 to $600,000,000.
