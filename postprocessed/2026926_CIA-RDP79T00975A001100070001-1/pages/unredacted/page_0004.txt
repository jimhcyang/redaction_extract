3. Merchant shipping to Communist China reaches high point in February:
During the month of February, 45 merchant ships totaling 297,000 gross tons arrived in Communist China from overseas. This is the largest number of such arrivals observed during any one month since the May 1951 embargo on shipment of strategic goods to China.

About half of these vessels were of non-Communist registry, mainly British and Greek. The Communist ships were Russian and Polish.

Comment: The tonnage arriving in Communist China from overseas during February was more than twice the monthly average in 1952. There has been no corresponding increase in shipping arriving at Chinese Communist ports from Hong Kong. The shipping figures reflect Peiping's progress in decreasing its dependence on Hong Kong middlemen by establishing direct contact with European sources of supply.
