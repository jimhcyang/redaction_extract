2. COMMENT ON CZECH PLANS TO REDUCE TROOP
STRENGTH

The Czech government's announcement of 24 August that it intends to cut its armed forces by 34,000 men before the end of the year is probably designed to strengthen the propaganda value of the recently announced Soviet troop reduction plan. This Czech move may soon be followed by similar announcements from the other Eastern European Satellites.

While the planned reduction would mean an 18-percent cut in Czech armed strength, it would not appreciably diminish the Soviet bloc's overall military capabilities.

Czechoslovakia has long been faced with a labor shortage, especially in agriculture, and the release of 34,000 troops from military service may ease this problem.
