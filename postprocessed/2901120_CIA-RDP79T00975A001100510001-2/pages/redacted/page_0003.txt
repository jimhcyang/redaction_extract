GENERAL

1. Comment on Soviet refusal to resume Austrian treaty talks:

The Soviet rejection of a bid for four-power discussion of an Austrian treaty appears to be primarily a delaying action. By emphasizing the futility of further discussion on the deputies' level, the USSR may be attempting to retain its bargaining position for higher-level talks where broader issues can be negotiated. The USSR may also be hoping through a prolonged exchange of notes to promote further dissension among the Allies.

Pravda recently stressed that the Soviet Union could not accept any preconditions to discussions of major world issues. The Soviet refusal, therefore, also reflects the new government's sensitivity to being "forced" into negotiations.

The Kremlin may consider that it can conduct its present "peace offensive" by a continuation of minor conciliatory gestures, and through the Korean negotiations, without making major concessions in Europe.
