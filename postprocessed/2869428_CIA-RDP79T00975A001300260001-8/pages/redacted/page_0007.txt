7. Comment on Syrian elections:

The rigged election on 9 October of a Syrian parliament at least superficially completes the restoration of constitutional rule promised by General Shishakli when he seized power in December 1951. He now possesses the prestige and constitutional machinery necessary for implementing domestic reforms and foreign policies.

He may be expected to push through parliament a series of agricultural, commercial and financial reforms. He is also likely to renew efforts to obtain sizable economic and military assistance from the United States, as well as a loan from the World Bank, for which legislative approval is needed. He will probably now resume his efforts to get higher oil revenues from the Iraq Petroleum Company.

The majority of Syrians are relatively indifferent to President Shishakli's dictatorship, but the disgruntled politicians and extremist elements, currently checked by his police controls, are a constant threat to his position.
