Ghanaian President Nkrumah plans to devote a large part of his address to the General Assembly--probably late this week--to Ghana's position in recognizing the Gizenga regime. He intends to draw a parallel between US and UK recognition of the Belgian Government in exile during World War II. Nkrumah asked for detailed information on how this had been done, adding that "a direct quote from a US Secretary of State or from a British foreign minister would be of value."
