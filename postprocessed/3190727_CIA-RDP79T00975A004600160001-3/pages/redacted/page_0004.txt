Algeria: The 14 July attack by about 500 Algerian rebels against a French position near the Tunisian border was one of the largest rebel operations to date. This attack by the rebels, who subsequently withdrew into Tunisia, may give French officers who favor a major thrust into Tunisia a pretext for pursuing the rebels into Tunisian territory in greater force than in the past.

West Africa: The West African "summit" meeting in Liberia of the leaders of Guinea, Ghana, and Liberia has apparently begun in a chilly atmosphere reflecting the basically different political outlooks of President Tubman of Liberia and the more militant nationalist leaders of Guinea and Ghana. The agenda reportedly includes a discussion of the form any future West African union should take, as well as the Algerian and Cameroon situations. Tubman, however, has said that all talks on African unity must be exploratory and that no decisions must be made until other African territories--Cameroun, Togo, and Nigeria--become independent next year.
