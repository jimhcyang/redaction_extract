9. Top Sudanese leader predicts disturbances if Cairo talks fail:
The Mahdi, top Sudanese leader, has informed the American Liaison Officer in Khartoum that if no agreement is reached between Egypt and Britain, disturbances will take place simultaneously in Egypt and the Sudan.

The Mahdi said he had learned that the Governor General was planning to call Sudanese party readers together to consider elections and to decide the country's future. He warned, however, that this approach "would not work" because of the agreement already reached between Egyptian and Sudanese leaders.

It is not necessary, the Mahdi stated, for any Sudanese to participate in the Anglo-Egyptian talks in Cairo because "Egypt is representing the Sudan."

Comment: The Mahdi's statement strengthens Egypt's position in the current talks with Britain since Mirghani, the other important leader in Khartoum, has already indicated his support for the agreement which Cairo worked out with the Sudanese leaders.
