Laos: Armed Forces Commander General Ouane is now in Luang Prabang laying plans for a dual effort against the Pathet Lao and Captain Kong Le. Ouane appears to have made his decision to work against Kong Le independently of General Phoumi. He plans, however, to concert his moves with Phoumi's Savannakhet Revolutionary Committee. Ouane's departure from Vientiane on Saturday has already aroused Kong Le's suspicions, and it is possible that Kong Le will move quickly to neutralize troops loyal to Ouane in Vientiane in an effort to establish full control of the capital.

Premier Souvanna Phouma, in a quick trip to Luang Prabang on 16 October, professes full approval of Ouane's plans. He was careful to qualify a promise eventually to identify himself openly with Ouane's scheme, however, which may indicate that he has not yet abandoned hope of finding a formula more to his liking for a solution to the crisis in Laos.

III. THE WEST

Austria-Italy: Austria's determination to introduce into the UN General Assembly's Special Political Committee on 18 October a resolution demanding full regional autonomy for the South Tirol has already exacerbated the bad feeling between Italy and Austria on this question and may lead to outbreaks of violence in the Tirol. Several Western European UN members have deplored the Austrian resolution as "bad" and likely to do the West no good. The Italians, as expected, have indicated they will strongly oppose any such resolution.

Cuba: The summary execution last week of more than 20 men--including three Americans--for counterrevolutionary activities is a serious blow to the chances of a genuine revolt against the Castro regime.
