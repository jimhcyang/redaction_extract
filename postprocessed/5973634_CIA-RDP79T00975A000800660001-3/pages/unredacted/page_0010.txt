10. French cabinet approval for UN discussion of Tunisia seen in doubt:

French Foreign Minister Schuman fears that cabinet approval for inclusion of the Tunisian question in the UN General Assembly agenda may be prejudiced if there is public discussion of the American position favoring General Assembly consideration of this issue.

Schuman had understood that the United States would not tell other UN delegations of its position and expressed his concern when he learned that the chief Pakistani delegate had been so informed.

Comment: French refusal to approve the General Assembly discussion of the Tunisian issue would mean considerable diplomatic embarrassment for the United States. Schuman previously anticipated that at its 7 October meeting the cabinet would decide not to oppose placing the question on the agenda.
