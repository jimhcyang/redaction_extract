Saud presumably desires to continue his
efforts to exert a moderating influence on the Syrian leaders
in the hope of preventing them from moving too close to the
USSR. At the same time, he may be endeavoring to provide
jobs outside of Syria for moderate officers who were ousted
in the August "coup." He has employed for the past five
years Col. Fawzi Silaw, former chief of state under Syrian
dictator Shishakli. Bizri and Sarraj probably are seeking to
enhance their own positions by appearing to be in King Saud's
good graces.

King Hussayn on 5 October sent his aide-
de-camp and a palace official to Riyadh on a previously sched-
uled trip to present Hussayn's views on the Syrian situation and
to warn King Saud against the course of events in Syria. Whether
Saud timed the Bizri-Sarraj visit to coincide with that of Hus-
sayn's emissaries is not known, but Saud may feel that a personal
meeting between them would be a step toward bettering Arab
unity.
