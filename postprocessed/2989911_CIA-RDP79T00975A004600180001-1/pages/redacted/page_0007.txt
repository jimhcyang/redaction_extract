The cancellation leaves Moscow free to indulge in the tactic of a bid for an early summit meeting--possibly during Vice President Nixon's forthcoming visit to the USSR. Although the Soviet leaders would probably expect the Western powers to reject a bid for a mid-August meeting on the grounds of insufficient time for preparation, they may believe that such a move would increase pressure in the West, particularly on London during this crucial pre-election period, for agreement to hold a top-level meeting in the near future.

The Soviet note expressed the hope, however, that future conditions would allow the trip to be rescheduled, "perhaps in a year."
