4 APRIL 1958

I. THE COMMUNIST BLOC
Peiping suggests Japanese failure to implement flag rights may void trade agreement.
Ulbricht maintains firm control in East Germany despite continued opposition within party.

II. ASIA-AFRICA
Indonesia - Orders for paratroopers now committed in Sumatra to regroup in Djakarta suggest new action by central government in near future.
Algerian rebels' plans for provisional government reported ready.
Saudi Government fears possible subversive activities by Egyptian nationals remaining in Saudi Arabia.
Pakistani leaders suggest consideration of neutral foreign policy.

Cuba - Government forces still in control in Santiago.

III. THE WEST
France - Gaillard insists neutral observers must operate on both sides of Algerian-Tunisian border.
Paraguay - Stroessner's position weakened despite suppression of 1 April revolt.
