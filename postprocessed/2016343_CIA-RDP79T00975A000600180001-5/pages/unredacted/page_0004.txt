NEAR EAST - AFRICA

3. Mossadeq again rejects possible use of British technicians:
Prime Minister Mossadeq has stated emphatically to Ambassador Henderson that he would never agree to the re-entry of British oil technicians into Iran. If an agreement with the International Bank were contingent upon the return of the British, however, he would retire rather than block a settlement. Someone else, Mossadeq added, could take the responsibility for Iran's future.

Comment: One of the main points blocking an agreement between Iran and the International Bank is Iranian insistence that no British technicians should be employed. The Bank holds that it should be free to hire any personnel necessary.

4. Iraqi Prime Minister may soon resign:
Iraqi Prime Minister Nuri Said is about to resign. Since Parliament has ratified the new oil agreement with the Iraq Petroleum Company and since the Middle East Command issue is being "soft-pedalled for the present," Nuri, who has held the premiership for over a year, is reported eager to let another Iraqi leader dissolve Parliament and supervise the holding of new elections.
