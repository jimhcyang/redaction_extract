Comment: The resolution of the Vietnamese Congress rejected membership in the French Union "in its present form."

Assembly opponents of Laniel's program have succeeded in scheduling a debate on Indochina for 23 October. The government will probably try to parry criticism by seeking support until the results of Navarre's campaign can be assessed, stressing that the Vietnamese Congress had weakened its original rejection of French Union status.

6. Bidault to force integration issue in French Assembly:

French foreign minister Bidault told Secretary Dulles in London that he would resign unless the National Assembly takes a "positive stand" on both the EDC and the proposed political community before the meeting of foreign ministers at The Hague now scheduled for late November. He expects the next six weeks to be difficult, but reiterated his determination to push ahead with the EDC and the Indochina war.

Comment: For internal political reasons the Laniel government has scheduled a general debate on foreign policy in November although planning to delay a vote on the EDC until early next year. This strategy fails to take advantage of the present momentum favorable to EDC.
