7. JAPAN PLANNING EXCHANGE OF STEEL PRODUCTS
FOR CHINESE COMMUNIST IRON ORE

Japan is planning future exports of steel goods to Communist China under the exceptions procedure in return for iron ore. current deal for Communist coking coal, Tokyo desired American approval for the export of wooden ships instead of steel.

Comment

Japanese steelmakers want additional iron ore as well as cheaper Chinese coking coal to meet the expanding needs of their steel industry. In April, Japanese businessmen decided to negotiate for 300,000 tons of iron ore from Hainan, and they hope eventually to obtain 500,000 tons from this source.

The Japanese have noted British use of CHINCOM exceptions procedures in shipping limited quantities of steel products to Communist China and London's announcement that British colonies could relax differential trade restrictions against Peiping. These moves will increase existing pressures in Japan for expanding trade with Communist China in embargoed items. (Concurred in by ORR)
