5. Egyptian palace group seeks to replace Prime Minister Hilali:

American Ambassador Caffery in Cairo reports an effort among the palace group to replace Prime Minister Hilali with Maraghi Pasha, who is both Minister of the Interior and of War and Marine. The Ambassador believes that this plot may also have some support from the Wafd, and that the palace group seeks to postpone the autumn elections by having Maraghi become Prime Minister.

Comment: Maraghi, a political independent, has frequently been mentioned as a possible successor to Hilali. As Minister of the Interior, Maraghi was responsible for the effective reorganization of the police force following the January riots. In his capacity of Minister of War and Marine, he is reportedly reorganizing the top command of the Egyptian Armed Forces.

6. Possible speedup in Hungarian frontier deportations reported:

The American Legation in Budapest believes that deportations from Hungarian frontier areas may have been speeded up during the past four weeks. The current expulsions are chiefly from the western and southern border areas. All property left behind has been confiscated.
