2. COMMENT ON NENNI'S VISIT TO MOSCOW AND PEIPING

The current trip of Pietro Nenni, head
of the Italian Socialist Party, to Moscow
and Peiping appears to have become a
source of embarrassment both to the
Italian government and to the Italian
Communist Party. The trip may enhance Nenni's political position, particularly as a "mediator" in relaxing
East-West tensions.

the [[CLEAN_REDACTION_1]]trip may further accentuate the reported differences developing between
Nenni's party and the Communists.

The[[/CLEAN_REDACTION_1]] leftist press in Italy has been playing up the visit as a semi-official exploration of the possible establishment of diplomatic relations with Communist
China despite an official foreign ministry denial. Moreoever,
a high foreign ministry official has expressed fear to an
American diplomat that Nenni may make a statement in Peiping proclaiming that China is the "Eldorado" of the modern
world and that the Italian government is completely ignoring
the possibility of trade relations because it follows the American line.

Nenni's visit is not expected to lead to
any great expansion of Italian exports to China, now in the
neighborhood of $5,000,000 annually, but it may have considerable effect on domestic politics. Nenni will probably
take advantage of his seeming respectability vis-a-vis
the government and his independence from his Communist
allies to exploit popular interest in a further relaxation in
East-West relations and to renew his pressure for a political "opening to the left." ([[CLEAN_REDACTION_2]]Concurred in by ORR[[/CLEAN_REDACTION_2]])
