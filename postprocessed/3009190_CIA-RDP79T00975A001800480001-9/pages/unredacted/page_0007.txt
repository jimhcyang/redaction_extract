Comment: The appearance in this case of Pritt, known internationally as a pro-Communist lawyer, suggests that the defeated Moslem League dissidents are now willing to enlist Communist support in seeking revenge. The Communists may therefore for the first time in Pakistan's history be able to capitalize on the intimate knowledge of government operations provided by a former prime minister and several ministers and parliamentarians.

Pritt, who received a Stalin Peace Prize on 21 December 1954, defended Indian Communists on trial in 1950. He helped to defend Kenya Mau Mau leader Jomo Kenyatta in 1952. In November 1954 he defended the pro-Communist Ceylonese publication Trine in a case involving alleged defamation of Governor General Goonetilleke.
