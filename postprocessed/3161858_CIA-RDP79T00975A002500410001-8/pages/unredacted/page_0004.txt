1. SOVIETS DISPLAY JET HEAVY BOMBERS EQUIPPED FOR IN-FLIGHT REFUELING

Photographs of BISONS participating in rehearsals for the annual May Day air show in Moscow show two aircraft equipped with nose booms. The booms, similar to one seen during the Soviet Air Force Day Show on 3 July last year, are about one foot in diameter and about 13 feet long.

Comment
This equipment is probably the probe element of a probe-and-drogue in-flight refueling system. With one in-flight refueling from a compatible tanker, i.e., a tanker with similar performance characteristics, the combat radius of the BISON could be extended from 2,450 to 3,300 nautical miles. (See map, p. 4.)

The absence of squadron markings on both probe-equipped aircraft, which were interspersed among about 20 other BISONS when observed, suggests they are still under the control of the Ministry of Aviation Industry rather than the Long Range Air Force.

The aerial tanker element of the refueling system has not been identified. Of known Soviet aircraft, another BISON or a BEAR would be best suited for this role.
