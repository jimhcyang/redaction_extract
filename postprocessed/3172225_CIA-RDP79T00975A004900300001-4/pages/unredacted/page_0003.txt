6 FEBRUARY 1960

I. THE COMMUNIST BLOC

Soviet census reveals that fewer people will enter working- and military-age groups during 1959-65 than we had estimated.

Absence of Chinese Communists at bloc agricultural conference may have been caused by a desire to avoid criticism on communes.

Bloc sponsored military training program for Guinea appears to be under way.

II. ASIA-AFRICA

Sudan, perturbed over limitations of US aid and World Bank "stalling," says it will apply for Soviet aid.

Khrushchev expected to offer substantial credits for Indian Third Five Year Plan.

III. THE WEST

French cabinet changes show De Gaulle's determination to implement his Algerian policy.

Fidel Castro will probably accept Mikoyan's invitation to visit Moscow; resumption of diplomatic relations between USSR and Cuba also appears likely.

Cuba notifies UN of its candidacy for a seat on Security Council.
