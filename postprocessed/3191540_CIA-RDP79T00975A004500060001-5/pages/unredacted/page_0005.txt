USSR: A Northern Fleet submarine detachment, probably larger than those recently engaged in similar activity, is continuing nonexercise operations and is estimated to be in the northeastern Atlantic--southern Norwegian Sea area. One Albanian-based unit is believed to be in the Mediterranean, and one or more submarines are operating beyond local areas in the Pacific.

II. ASIA-AFRICA

UAR-Iraq: The UAR is making further efforts to widen the breach between Qasim and the Iraqi Communist party. The Pakistani ambassador in Baghdad on 11 May set the stage for the UAR chargé to meet with the Iraqi foreign minister--and possibly Qasim himself--to convey UAR assurances of support for Qasim should he take action against the Communists. Since 16 May the Cairo press and radio have sharpened their propaganda attacks against the Iraqi Communist party.
(Page 4)

UK--Yemen: The talks opening in Aden on 18 May between local British and Yemeni representatives regarding border incidents and subversion across the borders are unlikely to result in any significant improvement in British--Yemeni relations despite the fact that Crown Prince Badr has recently veered away from a pro-Soviet line. London seeks a period of quiet to facilitate development of the Aden Protectorate Federation and will propose creating a permanent border investigating commission. Badr also seems interested in keeping the border undisturbed but is maintaining his capability for arousing disaffected tribesmen within the protectorate.
