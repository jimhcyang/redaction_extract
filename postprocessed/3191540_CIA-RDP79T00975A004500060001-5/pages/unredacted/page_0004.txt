CENTRAL INTELLIGENCE BULLETIN
DAILY BRIEF
18 May 1959

I. THE COMMUNIST BLOC

USSR: An attempt was made to launch a vehicle of undetermined nature at the Tyura Tam Missile Test Range (TTMTR) on 16 May 1959. The countdown operation began with "X-8" hours at 0855 EDST and reached "X-4" at 1100 EDST. The operation was canceled shortly thereafter, possibly because of communications difficulties between the range elements.

One possibly significant variation from all previous TTMTR launchings was noted during this operation; the time intervals between the X-8 hour announcement and subsequent announcements down to X-4 were considerably shortened, being respectively 16, 16, 36, and 57 minutes. While no firm reason for this time compression can currently be given, it is possible that this may presage for selected operations at TTMTR a considerable decrease in real time consumed in the countdown procedure.

USSR: The Soviet Union is seeking to exploit any differences between the US and Britain over Macmillan's proposal, endorsed by Khrushchev, for a predetermined number of annual on-site inspections of suspected nuclear explosions. Khrushchev's letters of 16 May to President Eisenhower and Macmillan and Gromyko's statements at Geneva are designed to create the impression that the way has been opened for an early signing of a test-cessation agreement. Khrushchev probably believes these moves will place the British Government under increasing pressure to conclude such an agreement.
