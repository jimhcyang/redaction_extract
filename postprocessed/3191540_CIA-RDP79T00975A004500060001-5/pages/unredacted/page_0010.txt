II. ASIA-AFRICA

UAR Moving to Back Qasim Against Iraqi Communists

The UAR Government, conscious of the breach which has opened between Iraqi Prime Minister Qasim and the Iraqi Communist party and aware of the present weakness of pro-UAR elements in Iraq, has begun efforts to support Qasim against the Communists.

Beginning on 16 May, Cairo sharpened its press and radio attacks on the Iraqi Communist party. It charged that the Communist party's insistence on participation in the Iraqi Government had unveiled its "sinister plans and real objectives" and had alienated Iraqi public opinion. It claimed that the party, now faced with disaffection of opportunist elements, was preparing "to go underground," prior to staging a sudden uprising to seize power by force.

Within Iraq, the dispute persists between Qasim and Communist party leaders on the issue of legalizing political parties and appointing acknowledged Communist party representatives to the cabinet. Communist-controlled newspapers and mass organizations continue to press these demands, despite Qasim's reiteration in a 14 May speech that "the time had not yet come" for a return to political parties.
