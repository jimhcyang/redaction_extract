31 JANUARY 1959

I. THE COMMUNIST BLOC

Guided Missile Intelligence Committee statement about Soviet guided-missile submarines.

UAR instructs ambassador in Peiping to reject Communist China's offer of arms "with thanks".

North Vietnamese message explains communist intention in Taiwan Strait issue as promoting split between US and Taiwan, not immediate capture of island.

Yugoslavs respond to Khrushchev's statements at party congress with severe personal press attacks on Soviet leader.

Soviet delegates at Geneva state privately that negotiations should continue until treaty is signed.

II. ASIA-AFRICA

Chinese Nationalists order interception of Communist shipping in Taiwan Strait area.

Shah of Iran says he intends to wait one week before deciding on non-aggression pact with USSR.

Libyan military mission now in Cairo reported discussing acquisition of arms from the USSR.

Cambodia - Dap Chhuon reported to have approached Thai authorities for support in plot against Sihanouk.

Japanese - South Korean relations will worsen as a result of Japanese decision to permit Korean residents to depart Japan for North Korea.

III. THE WEST

Paraguay - Attempt to oust President Stroessner may be imminent.
