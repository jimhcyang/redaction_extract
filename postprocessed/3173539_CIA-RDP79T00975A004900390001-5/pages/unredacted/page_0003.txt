17 FEBRUARY 1960

I. THE COMMUNIST BLOC

Khrushchev's formula for solution of German problem calls for signing of separate treaties with two German states; Communists claim it would eliminate "objections" to signing of single treaty.

Chinese Communists and Nationalists in air clash above Taiwan Strait; Communists showing increased confidence in ability to engage Nationalists.

II. ASIA-AFRICA

Israel's boycott of UN Mixed Armistice Commission meeting concerning fighting along Israeli-Syrian border may lead to UN Security Council consideration of border dispute.

Belgian Congo--Brussels’ announcement of 30 June independence for the Congo may lead to substantial exodus of Congo's 100,000 Europeans.

Communists in Iraq receive another jolt in cabinet shake-up.

III. THE WEST

US Embassy sees Castro's agreements with Mikoyan as long step toward Cuba's joining Soviet camp.

LATE ITEM

New Soviet proposal for temporary standards for detecting underground nuclear tests is designed to undercut US proposal for limited test ban; USSR still aims at ban on all tests.
