3. Taipei presses for immediate security pact with United States:

The Chinese Nationalist foreign minister told the American chargé on 24 February that this is the psychological moment for concluding a mutual security pact between his country and the United States. He argued that since the forthcoming Geneva conference is regarded by many Chinese Nationalists as a step toward admitting Communist China to the United Nations, the signing of the pact would provide strong reassurance that the United States will "not let free China down."

Comment: The Nationalists have long been seeking such a pact to formalize the existing American commitment to the defense of Formosa. They also have an abiding suspicion, stimulated by the agreement on the Geneva conference, that the United States may be induced by its major allies to compromise with the Chinese Communists at Nationalist expense.
