Saudi Arabia: The continuing dispute with Aramco and Tapline over profit-sharing, by delaying production increases has probably cost Saudi Arabia its position as second largest oil producer in the Middle East. Saudi crude oil is moving through Tapline to the Mediterranean at less than half of full capacity, and over-all Saudi oil production has not increased appreciably over 1956 levels. Production in adjoining states has increased substantially, and Iran now holds second place behind Kuwait. By spring Iraq also may surpass Saudi production.
