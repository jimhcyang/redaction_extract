In Amman the speech was hailed by the press as logical and "watertight."

Moscow reacted promptly to the Nasr statement. In its Near Eastern broadcast it praised Nasr and defended the legality of his position. Referring to Nasr's proposal for an expanded conference, Moscow insisted that "no reasonable person . . . would hesitate to agree with this Egyptian proposal." The Yugoslav press has also come out in support of Nasr's position.
