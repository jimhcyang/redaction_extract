3. Soviet jets pursue B-29 inland over Japan:
Far East Command reports that a B-29 on a surveillance mission over the Sea of Japan encountered six Soviet jet aircraft west of the northern tip of Hokkaido. On reaching the Hokkaido coast two of these planes followed the B-29 inland for a distance of ten miles.

In an encounter over the Japan Sea on the Soviet aircraft turned back before reaching the Japanese coast.

4. Chinese Nationalists in Burma still receiving outside aid:
General Li Mi is still in contact with Taipei and has recently received arms from Nationalist authorities there. Thai authorities are involved with the movement between Thailand and Formosa of Chinese Nationalists who are connected with Li Mi's operations.

Comment: Li Mi still commands the Nationalist troops in Burma and that both Thai and Formosan authorities are supporting him, despite their denials. Such support is probably due to the control exercised by Li Mi's forces over the profitable opium trade in the area. The situation suggests that the Nationalist troops will not voluntarily agree to the plan of repatriation to Formosa which has recently been proposed by the Burmese Defense Minister.
