Under the proposed law, foreign banks would be required to maintain capital reserves amounting to 20 percent of deposits, while Brazilian-owned banks would need reserves of only 10 percent. Ambassador Cabot believes that the measure is unlikely to be beaten in Congress. Closing of foreign banks would give new momentum to a long-standing proposal for expropriation of foreign electric companies, owned principally by US and Canadian interests.
