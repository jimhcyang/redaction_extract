CENTRAL INTELLIGENCE BULLETIN
15 April 1959

DAILY BRIEF

I. THE COMMUNIST BLOC

USSR-Poland: Commenting on the recent party congress in Warsaw,
stated that Khrushchev's grudging endorsement of both Gomulka and Poland's special brand of Communism is, in time, bound to have an impact on the Communist world. In the long run, Moscow may be faced eventually with the alternative of crushing Gomulka or permitting other satellites the same degree of autonomy as Poland, which would weaken Soviet control.
