SOUTH ASIA

9. East Pakistan government's position threatened:

Following the riots on 15 May in East Pakistan, the situation is "distinctly ominous" and serious tension in five major cities is reported by the American consulate general in Dacca. The army commander in the province has strongly urged the imposition of martial law.

The provincial government seems unable to control the situation and apparently is trying to place the blame for the riots on the mill owners and the central government in Karachi. The consulate general believes this may encourage Communist exploitation of the situation.

One provincial minister, a strong pro-Communist, has helped spread a rumor--probably false--that refugees from India plotted the massacre and that most of the 360 dead were native Bengalis.
