BIWEEKLY SUMMARY
(4 October-17 October 1956)

THE TAIWAN STRAIT

Report of the IAC Current Intelligence Group
for the Taiwan Strait Problem

1. There were no significant combat operations in the area during the period.

2. The Chinese Communists in recent weeks have reiterated Peiping's willingness to negotiate a Taiwan settlement with the Nationalists. Chou En-lai has been quoted as stating that Chiang Kai-shek would be permitted to remain on Taiwan and retain his armed forces under Communist control. In another interview, Chou professed confidence that Chiang was "willing to concede." The Chinese Nationalists state they have rejected a recent covert Communist overture for negotiations made through Hong Kong.

Peiping intends "to open negotiations" with Washington for a settlement of the Taiwan question after the American elections.
