Gregor states that total Czech trade with the USSR will be 25 percent higher this year than in 1951. The overall value of such trade in 1952 will probably be 540 to 580 million dollars, or more than double that of 1948. Should this rate of development continue the total trade between the two countries will be considerably larger than the levels set in the four-year agreement of November 1950.

Indications are that the commodity pattern will remain the same, with the Soviet Union exporting raw materials and semi-finished products and Czechoslovakia providing finished goods.
