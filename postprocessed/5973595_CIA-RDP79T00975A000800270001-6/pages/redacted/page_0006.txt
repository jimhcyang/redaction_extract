WESTERN EUROPE

6. Comment on the West Berlin coal situation:

Despite current efforts to double the West Berlin coal stockpile in preparation for a possible blockade, the stockpiles may by next spring be only slightly larger than now if the Rothensee canal lock remains closed and Soviet authorities refuse to grant compensating transport facilities to the city.

Prospects for increasing the coal stocks over the winter months may improve, however, as a result of the new interzonal trade agreement which provides for the delivery of nearly 650,000 tons of brown coal from East Germany. Deliveries should begin next week if the East German Government intends to fulfil the agreement.

The Soviet contention that the canal lock actually needed repairs, and that its closure was not merely a harassment measure. The lock is being worked on at this time, and Soviet authorities may intend to reopen it in the next few weeks.
