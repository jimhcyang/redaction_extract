A general atmosphere of public calm prevails in Israel, although there is obvious intense concern for the immediate future of Jordan and the eventual possibility that an Egyptian-dominated ring may close around the country. A check by the American military attache at all mobilization points and freight stations in the vicinity of Tel Aviv revealed no unusual activity. Prime Minister Ben-Gurion has stated, however, that Israel is now prepared to ask the United States for arms, planes, and antisubmarine weapons since, unless the Iraqi coup is crushed Israel will be in "mortal danger."
