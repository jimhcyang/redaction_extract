Britain-USSR: Britain's trade mission in Moscow is finding the Soviet negotiators "unexpectedly difficult" largely because of demands that the UK provide for increased purchases of Soviet goods by granting the USSR most-favored-nation status in regard to British import quotas. London apparently believes that despite the USSR's sweeping demands, an agreement will be reached providing for the modest increases in trade which Britain seeks.
