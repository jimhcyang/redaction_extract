5. TITO-NEHRU-NASR COMMUNIQUE

The vagueness of the Tito-Nehru-Nasr communiqué of 20 July reflected the desire of all three leaders to avoid antagonizing either the West or the Sino-Soviet bloc. It stressed the need to work through the United Nations and personal diplomacy to relax world tension.

Nasr's acceptance of Nehru's proposals for a cease-fire in Algeria to be followed by direct negotiations is a departure from the Arabs' previous stand that the issue should be brought to the UN.

The direct reference to the "great importance of removing embargoes and obstacles to the normal flow and extension of international trade" is an obvious slap at Western strategic trade controls. Nasr presumably does not intend his statement to refer to the Arab boycott of Israel, however.
