1. VETO THREATENED ON UN MEMBERSHIP ISSUE

The Chinese Nationalist government has threatened to use its veto in the UN Security Council if the admission of Outer Mongolia to the United Nations is brought to a vote. In this event, Taipei says it will vote against the four Eastern European Satellites.

Comment

A great majority of UN members expect a solution to the membership problem at this General Assembly session and would resent any action preventing a solution.

The British delegate has declared that Taipei's veto might lead to a violent reaction against Nationalist China and reopen the whole question of Chinese representation in the UN. The Chinese Nationalists apparently estimate that damage to their position in the UN resulting from an unpopular veto would be little worse than the long-run effect of admitting Outer Mongolia.
