SOVIET UNION

1. Wrangel Island construction requirements may explain Soviet airlift:

Comment: The Directorate of Special Construction, which has been associated with airfield construction or improvement at seven other Arctic locations, may be planning such activity on Wrangel Island. This possibility is supported by indications that since last November aircraft which formerly landed at Rogers Bay have been using an alternate airfield on the west side of the island.

An "airlift" involving 12 military transports from Far East bases and 84 from Europe has been under way in northeast Siberia since late February. Wrangel Island appears to be the final destination of the cargo or personnel involved, since over 100 shuttle flights from mainland fields have terminated there since the "airlift" began.

FAR EAST

2. Peiping maintains pressure for farm collectivization:

The spring cultivation directive issued on 31 March by Peiping's Government Administration Council informed rural officials that "agricultural production and mutual aid and cooperation are a whole task which is unified and cannot be separated. They are the center of all work in the rural areas."
