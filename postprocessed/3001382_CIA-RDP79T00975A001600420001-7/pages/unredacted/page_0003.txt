GENERAL

1. Mendes-France sees possible last-minute agreement at Geneva:

Mendes-France looks for a possible last-minute agreement at Geneva on 19 July,

Comment: The Communists are expected to offer Mendes-France a settlement on Indochina, in advance of his 20 July deadline, which the French premier will find it difficult to refuse.

The offer is expected to include a cease-fire proposal and an outline for a political settlement requiring further negotiations. Ambiguities in the Communist proposals would be designed to make agreement easier.

The French were mistaken in believing that Chou En-lai had withdrawn Communist demands on behalf of the "resistance movements" in Laos and Cambodia. It is believed that Communist proposals will involve the partitioning of Vietnam on terms very favorable to the Communists, Communist control of some part of Laos, and some form of recognition for Communist forces in Cambodia.
