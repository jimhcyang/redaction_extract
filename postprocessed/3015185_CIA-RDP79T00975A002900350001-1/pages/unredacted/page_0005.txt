2. NEW SAUDI ARABIAN POLICY ON OIL CONCESSIONS UNDER CONSIDERATION

The Saudi Arabian government is considering adopting a new policy on development of its oil resources.

Saudi Arabia had made a "huge mistake" originally to deal solely with the "big syndicates," because it had now "lost control of its crude output and was caught in an inflexible operation!" The only lands which Saudi Arabia could offer for new concessions at present were too far inland and appeared to have too little potential yield to be operated without great expense.

Saudi Arabia desired its own fleet of oil tankers, which could be built at the lowest cost in Japan. Tariqi believed that since Japan is a potentially important customer for oil, it might be interested in an agreement on petroleum development.
