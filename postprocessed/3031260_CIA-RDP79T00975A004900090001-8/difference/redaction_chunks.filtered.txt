It could happen either before the Iranian new year begins on 21 March or while the Shah is out of the country. The Shah is planning to visit Pakistan in mid-February and Europe in May. The conspirators claim they can control Tehran and believe Bakhtiar can prevent commanders in the provinces from moving against the new regime. During the past year, Bakhtiar has indicated his concern over what might happen in Iran if anything happens to the Shah. Bakhtiar, age 45, is probably one of the closest military officers to the Shah and, after the Shah, may be the most powerful man in Iran. In recent months, the Shah seems to have been placing still greater trust in him. He is noted for having speedily moved his troops to Tehran in 1953 to support the Shah during the disturbances surrounding the fall of Premier Mossadeq.
