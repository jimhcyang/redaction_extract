Japan: The Japanese Government, despite recent requests from US officials for cooperation in relieving the American dollar outflow, is raising further obstacles to settling its obligation to the United States for postwar aid. Although the Japanese have been requested to repay less than one third of the $2 billion total obligation, Foreign Minister Kosaka has asked the US to provide legal justification for its claim in order to facilitate passage in the Diet. Congo: The 12 December formation in Leopoldville of a "liberation committee" to bring Katanga back into the Congo may further strain relations between the Kasavubu government and the secessionist regime of Moise Tshombé. Despite their mutual antipathy to Lumumba, the Leopoldville and Elisabethville governments have failed to resolve differences concerning a Congo confederation to include Katanga. Reports that the Katanga liberation committee was formed with Kasavubu's and Mobutu's concurrence will further impair prospects for a reconciliation. The committee is dominated by representatives of the Baluba tribesmen now in revolt against Tshombé in northern Katanga.

Algeria-France: An uneasy calm prevails in Algiers and Oran after four days of rioting. Army commanders have made plain their determination to put down any further disorders, and De Gaulle reportedly intends to punish severely the European leaders of the riots.
