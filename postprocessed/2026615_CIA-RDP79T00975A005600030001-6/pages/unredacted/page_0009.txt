aircraft had wing racks for training bombs and rockets, the gov-
ernment of Katanga has no armament nor any prospects of re-
ceiving any. He admitted that the "bombs" dropped near Manono
on 30 January had been made from pipes filled with TNT, and had
been employed at the insistence of the minister of defense "to scare
the natives." The commander concluded that these aircraft were of
little use other than to increase the prestige of the Katangan gov-
ernment. The rest of Katanga's air force consists of six Piper
cubs, nine other assorted propeller-driven planes, and five hel-
icopters.
