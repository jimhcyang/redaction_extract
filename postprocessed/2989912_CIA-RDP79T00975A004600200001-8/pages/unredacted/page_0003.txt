23 JULY 1959

I. THE COMMUNIST BLOC

In personal message to Macmillan and Lloyd, Khrushchev is adamant on Soviet position that any Berlin solution must be tied to establishment of all-German committee.

East German customs police board US military train en route from Berlin; further such incidents likely.

Peiping likely to resent Khrushchev's remarks in Poland on communes.

Communist China sends strong protest to Pakistan.

II. ASIA-AFRICA

Tunisia announces attacks on its border posts by "unidentified Algerian elements."

UAR reopens its frontier with Jordan.

Turkey still facing serious economic problems.

Japan negative toward new ICRC plan

III. THE WEST

Cuba--Anti-US incidents possible during 26 July demonstrations.

Argentine naval crisis worsening.
