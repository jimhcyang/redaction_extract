FAR EAST

3. Comment on recent defections from Rhee's Liberal Party:

The defection of 12 South Korean legislators from the Liberal Party on 9 December resulted from long-standing antagonism in the legislature because of Rhee's constitutional amendments, which impair the already truncated authority of the legislature, and because of the meager patronage and cabinet representation permitted Liberal Party members of the assembly. Defection of from eight to ten additional Liberal assemblymen is anticipated during the next few weeks.

The dispute basically arises out of Rhee's practice of favoring nonassembly members of his Liberal Party in the top party posts, as well as his bringing nonparty members into his cabinet. Rhee does this in order to perpetuate his domination of Korean politics through personal control of patronage and of the ministries.
