4. Pakistan eager to participate in SEATO arrangements:

Ambassador Hildreth sees increasing indications that Pakistan is willing not only to join SEATO but even to take the initiative in persuading Ceylon to do so. He notes that if Pakistan is encouraged to take this kind of initiative, it will expect returns in the form of additional American political, military and economic support.

Comment: Karachi's eagerness to volunteer support for SEATO has probably been based from the outset on the calculation that it will thereby have stronger claim for more American aid. Such aid would serve in turn to quiet the government's domestic opposition.

Pakistani leaders hope to seize this opportunity to gain material benefits and a larger voice in international affairs. They have repeatedly expressed chagrin that they missed such an opportunity during the Korean war.
