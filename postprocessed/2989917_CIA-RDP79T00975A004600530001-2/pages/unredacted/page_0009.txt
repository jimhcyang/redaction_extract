On 29 August these two planes were scheduled to fly from Hanoi to Dien Bien Phu with at least one shuttle flight between Dien Bien Phu and Na San before returning to Hanoi. There were no load descriptions but these aircraft might be carrying gasoline and oil to Na San and Dien Bien Phu.

The Indian External Affairs Ministry around 23 August proposed to the Laotian ambassador that India and Laos exchange military missions, but stressed that any such Indian group would function only as a "good-will mission" and would not be concerned with the present hostilities. New Delhi's proposal is probably designed to bolster Indian influence in the area and is indicative of New Delhi's recent more sympathetic attitude toward Vientiane.

Both Hanoi and Peiping on 29 August commented on American aid to Laos. A North Vietnamese Foreign Ministry statement strongly protested the American action, calling it a violation of the Geneva Agreement. The statement concluded with a call for "emergency measures" to reactivate the International Control Commission in Laos. A Peiping press commentary accuses the United States of seeking a "pretext for stepped up interference in Laos." The commentary denounced UN or SEATO intervention as part of an American plot to extend the Laotian "civil war." Both statements asserted that the United States must bear full responsibility for the consequences of its activities.
