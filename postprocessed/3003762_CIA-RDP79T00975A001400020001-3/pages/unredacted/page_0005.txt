4. USSR may seek German participation at proposed Berlin meeting:

The 16 December statement by Walter Ulbricht, acting East German premier, welcoming Western acceptance of a Berlin conference, indicates East German anticipation that the USSR will agree to the meeting.

Ulbricht's remarks suggest continued Soviet insistence that a united Germany could follow an independent foreign policy but could never be incorporated in a Western military pact. The speech further indicates that the USSR may urge that East and West German representatives be invited to the Berlin conference. Another East German spokesman has proposed that a mixed German commission prepare a program for the meeting.
