Iraq: Prime Minister Qasim intends to appoint to his cabinet in early July four retired generals now serving in important provincial posts. At least three of the generals are believed to be anti-Communist. Such a move would strengthen Qasim's claim that his government stands above political parties and would tend to inhibit any Communist criticism, which has so far stopped short of overt propaganda attacks on Iraqi Army leaders.
