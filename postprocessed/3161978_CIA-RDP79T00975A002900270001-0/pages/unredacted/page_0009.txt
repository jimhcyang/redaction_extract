France has expressed readiness to share in the cost of construction. The principal obstacle to the Israeli pipeline projects, however, is an assured source of crude oil. The Arab states would almost certainly not allow oil produced in Arab countries to transit Israel. The only likely source of supply would be Iran. Iran has shipped about 340,000 barrels daily through the Suez Canal and at least some of this could be diverted to pipelines through Israel. While the Iranian government itself would have no objections, it would be subjected to strong Arab pressure, particularly from Egypt, which would lose sizable canal revenues.
