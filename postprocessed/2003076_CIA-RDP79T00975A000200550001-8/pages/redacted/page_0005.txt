NEAR EAST

6. Iranian tribal leaders offer support to UK:

The US Embassy in Tehran reports that two senior chiefs of the Qashqai tribe called secretly on the UK Ambassador on 19 June, expressing concern over the trend of events in Iran and forecasting disturbances and a possible breakdown of authority, especially in the southern oil area. They saw no promise of an early improvement in the situation, and added that only the USSR profits from the existing weakness and confusion in Iran. The tribal leaders reportedly stated that the British could count on the Qashqais in the event of serious disturbances.

Comment: The Qashqais, one of the major tribal groups in Iran, are located in the mountainous area to the east of Abadan, where the oil installations are located. The Iranian Government has been concerned over rumors that the UK was agitating among the tribes, and several tribal leaders recently felt it necessary to reaffirm their loyalty to the Shah. Although UK has disavowed any machinations among the tribes, it is generally considered that in the event of a direct conflict between the UK and Iran, the UK would obtain some tribal support. This is, however, the first firm indication of tribal cooperation that could aid the British in maintaining their position in the area. Qashqai support for the British would presumably be aimed at achieving tribal autonomy.
