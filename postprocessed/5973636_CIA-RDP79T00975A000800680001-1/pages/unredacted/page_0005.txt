SOUTHEAST ASIA

5. Burma interested in acquiring US petroleum:
Comment: The Burmah Oil Company, a subsidiary of Anglo-Iranian, owns most of the oil installations in Burma. Negotiations between the company and the Burmese Government for the joint development of petroleum resources are at an impasse because of Burma's constitutional provision that at least 60 percent of the capital for any enterprise affecting the nation's resources must be owned by Burmese citizens.

6. Cabinet crisis threatens in Indonesia:
The Indonesian Government is threatened with an imminent cabinet crisis, Demands by elements of the two largest political parties that certain officials in the Defense Ministry and the army be purged are likely to result in the resignation of the Minister of Defense, and possibly in the fall of the cabinet.

President Sukarno has not revealed his position, but is believed to be displeased with the present government.
