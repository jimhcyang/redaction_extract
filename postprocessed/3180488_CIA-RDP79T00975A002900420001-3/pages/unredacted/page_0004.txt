2. NEW TENSION IN SYRIAN-ISRAELI DEMILITARIZED ZONE

Comment on:

Israel is "slowly, deliberately and thoroughly" taking over the entire demilitarized area between Syria and Israel, and if the UN General Assembly should call for stationing of UNEF forces in these zones, there could be trouble, in the opinion of the chief of the Israeli-Syrian Mixed Armistice Commission on 29 January.

On the same day, the Syrian Front Command reported that Israeli forces had occupied new positions in one of the demilitarized zones, according to an Egyptian message from Damascus to Cairo. The Syrians appear apprehensive over the situation in the demilitarized areas, part of which was quietly occupied by Israeli forces during hostilities in Sinai. On 1 February, a UN investigator reported considerable Syrian effort to keep him away from the Syrian front, where he observed heavier troop concentrations than those noted on previous visits. He believed that the Syrians feared an attack, since they were deployed defensively.

The Syrians also probably fear that Israel may shortly begin work in the demilitarized zone above Lake Tiberias to divert waters of the Jordan River for long-deferred irrigation and power projects. Syria gave notice last summer that it would open fire on the Israelis if such operations were undertaken in the demilitarized zone.
