FAR EAST

1. South Korea demands Communist withdrawal from south of 38th parallel:

The South Korean government's declaration on 1 August that it would attempt to restore its authority over Communist-occupied territory south of the 38th parallel by ordering the Communists to withdraw is President Rhee's latest attempt to counter what he fears is a rapprochement between the United States and Communist China.

South Korea is not in a position to implement such an order, since two American divisions are positioned astride the main route to the lost areas and Rhee's forces remain under operational control of the UN Command.

SOUTHEAST ASIA

2. Magsaysay-Recto break seen irreconcilable:

President Magsaysay is apparently determined to destroy Senator Claro Recto and is publicly committed to blocking his renomination for the Senate on the Nacionalista Party ticket, according to the American embassy in Manila, which believes a reconciliation is now virtually impossible. A senator close to the president has advised him to seek Recto's expulsion by the Nacionalista executive committee prior to the party's nominating convention, which is less than three weeks off.
