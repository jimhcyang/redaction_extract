30 APRIL 1958

I. THE COMMUNIST BLOC
Soviet bloc trying to smooth over differences with Yugoslavia, but recurring outbursts are most likely.
Chou En-lai raises issue of Burmese insurgency with Rangoon.

II. ASIA-AFRICA
Lebanese ambassador in Washington instructed to work "night and day" for more American aid.
General Shihab still considering running for president.
Indonesia - Some dissident leaders in Sumatra may escape to North Celebes. Dissident air attacks cause fresh alert in central government navy.
Tunisia - Bourguiba wants continuation of good offices mission; will probably demand French military evacuation of Bizerte.
India orders military patrols to investigate reported Chinese Communist incursions into Kashmir.

III. THE WEST
Pleven banking on Socialist support and vague statement on Algeria in investiture effort on 5 or 6 May. His chances are rated poor.
Argentina - Frondizi will be inaugurated on 1 May; will then seek American private capital. He alleges Soviet Government has offered help.
