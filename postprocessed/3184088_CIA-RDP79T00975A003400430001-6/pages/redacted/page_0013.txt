The new rail line will give Yunnan, traditionally one of the most isolated Chinese provinces, direct line access through North Vietnam to the rest of China and will make possible the export of products of Southwest China--including tin from Kochiu, the most important tin-producing area in the Sino-Soviet bloc--through the port of Haiphong. It will also help the Communists to consolidate their control over the Thai-Meo Autonomous Zone, an area where the Hanoi regime has encountered difficulties with a restless minority population.
