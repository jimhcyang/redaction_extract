WESTERN EUROPE

7. French see East German "reforms" as turning point:

The reorganization measures approved by the East German Parliament on 23 July marked the end of that "semi-bourgeois phase" during which "the Russians had not wanted to do anything which would exclude the possibility of a reunification of Germany."

Comment: Communist propaganda emphasis has shifted temporarily from the unity campaign, the immediate objective of which was to block Western defense plans, to the "socialization" of East Germany. The Soviet Union has not abandoned its ultimate objective of reuniting Germany on Soviet terms, however, and East German leaders expressly hope that the Bonn government can be upset from a Communist base in East Germany.
