2. NEW EGYPTIAN ARMS SHIPMENT BELIEVED EN ROUTE
TO ALGERIANS

Libya has agreed that "the goods" may pass through his country. the shipment would be ready for delivery in "al-Salum"--presumably the port city on the Egyptian-Libyan border--on 31 January and that "a reliable group" should be sent from Tunisia to receive it there in order to guarantee its arrival in Tunis.

Comment

The "goods" are probably additional arms to support the military offensive being planned by the Algerian rebels.

Arms consignments to the Algerians via Libya reportedly have been stepped up since 1 December with the Libyan government facilitating their transshipment. These reports suggest that Tunisian authorities usually take charge of consignments at the Libyan-Tunisian border and make the actual deliveries.
