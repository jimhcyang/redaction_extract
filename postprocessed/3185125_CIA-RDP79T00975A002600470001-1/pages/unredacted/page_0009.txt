THE ARAB-ISRAELI SITUATION
(Information as of 1700, 23 July)

Prior to his departure from the Near East on 23 July, UN secretary general Hammarskjold announced plans to return to the area in October. (Press)

The death of Lt. Col. Mustafa, Egyptian military attaché in Jordan and director of terrorist activity against Israel from Jordan, moved the Cairo press to call for revenge. Although evidence remains circumstantial, Egypt considers Israel responsible for the recent killing of Egyptian terrorist leaders with bombs sent in parcels through the mail.

Israel charged that infiltrators from Jordan penetrated an Israeli settlement west of Jerusalem at daybreak, tossing a hand grenade into a school and firing on a bus. No casualties were reported. (Press)
