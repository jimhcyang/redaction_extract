2. MOLOTOV SAYS ZORIN RECALLED BECAUSE OF STALEMATE IN SOVIET-WEST GERMAN RELATIONS

Molotov recently told the Swedish [[PRED_REDACTION_6]]ambassador[[/PRED_REDACTION_6]] that Moscow had recalled Ambassador Zorin from Bonn because there are to be no particular [[PRED_REDACTION_7]]developments in relations[[/PRED_REDACTION_7]] with West Germany at present and a man of Zorin's experience in foreign affairs could not be wasted there. Molotov was evasive about Zorin's [[PRED_REDACTION_8]]possible[[/PRED_REDACTION_8]] successor and left the impression that it might be some time before one is named.

Ambassador Bohlen believes that [[PRED_REDACTION_9]]Molotov's[[/PRED_REDACTION_9]] explanation has some credibility. However, Bohlen thinks the West German ambassador is also correct in connecting Zorin's recall with the East German leaders' visit to Moscow and the USSR's desire to reassure them that it will not negotiate with Bonn.

Comment. The recall of Zorin will probably be followed by Soviet propaganda charges that Bonn, by its cool treatment of Zorin, has shown its lack of interest in progress toward unification.

5. SINGAPORE TRADE MISSION TO COMMUNIST CHINA

A 60-man Singapore trade mission, [[PRED_REDACTION_13]]including[[/PRED_REDACTION_13]] 50 Chinese businessmen, is scheduled to visit Communist China the second or third week of August in [[PRED_REDACTION_14]]response[[/PRED_REDACTION_14]] to an invitation from the [[PRED_REDACTION_15]]Communists[[/PRED_REDACTION_15]] in March. The mission is sponsored by wealthy, [[PRED_REDACTION_16]]pro-Peiping[[/PRED_REDACTION_16]] rubber merchants.

The American consul general in Singapore believes the delegates will be offered impressive, politically inspired business deals. While local businessmen hope to develop a mainland market for rubber, tin and other exports, the consul general believes that actually any increased trade would result mainly in greater exports by the Chinese, further widening the present trade gap in favor of Communist China.

Widespread support from the Singapore Chinese community, together with former chief minister Marshall's decision to head the delegation, has prevented the Singapore Labor Front government from officially [[PRED_REDACTION_17]]discouraging[[/PRED_REDACTION_17]] the mission.

A 40-man delegation from the Federation of Malaya will also visit Communist China in August.

THE ARAB-ISRAELI SITUATION
(Information as of 1700, 23 July)

Prior to his departure from the Near East on 23 July, UN secretary general Hammarskjold [[PRED_REDACTION_18]]announced[[/PRED_REDACTION_18]] plans to return to the area in October. ([[PRED_REDACTION_19]]Press)

The death of Lt. Col. Mustafa, Egyptian military attaché in Jordan and director of terrorist activity against Israel from Jordan, moved the Cairo press to call for revenge. Although evidence remains circumstantial, Egypt considers Israel responsible for the recent killing of Egyptian terrorist leaders with bombs sent in parcels through the mail[[/PRED_REDACTION_19]].

Israel charged that infiltrators from [[PRED_REDACTION_20]]Jordan[[/PRED_REDACTION_20]] penetrated an Israeli settlement west of Jerusalem at [[PRED_REDACTION_21]]daybreak[[/PRED_REDACTION_21]], tossing a hand grenade into a school and firing on a bus. No casualties were reported. ([[PRED_REDACTION_22]]Press[[/PRED_REDACTION_22]])
