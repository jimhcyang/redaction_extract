II. ASIA-AFRICA

Indonesia: The Indonesian Government apparently has begun a military campaign in the hope of achieving a quick knockout of the dissident movement in Central Sumatra. Army forces, said to number 300 men, have landed at Bengkalis in east Central Sumatra. The Indonesian G-3 says a total of 4 battalions is available in the Riau Islands area to stage an attack on Pakanbaru, 120 miles up the Siak River from Bengkalis. This may coincide with an air and sea attack which previous reports indicated was planned against the west coast. At the urgent request of the Indonesian Government, Stanvac and Caltex employees and dependents in Central Sumatra have been ordered to evacuate to Singapore or to designated safe havens. Meanwhile, the Hatta-Sukarno talks have been indefinitely suspended and Sukarno has started on a one-week tour of Java and East Indonesia during which he can be expected to attempt to mobilize public support for the antidissident campaign.
