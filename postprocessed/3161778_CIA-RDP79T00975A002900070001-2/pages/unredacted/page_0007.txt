The apparent postponement may have stemmed in part from disagreements within the hierarchy over the future course of economic policy, and may also be connected with recent rumors that changes in the leadership would be approved by the plenum.

The changes in economic plans implied by these reports would have the effect of increasing labor productivity by increasing incentives, and do not necessitate abandonment of the major economic goal of the Sixth Five-Year Plan--a high rate of overall economic growth with continued emphasis on heavy industry.
