Situation in South Vietnam

The Diem government has hoped that holding presidential elections on schedule in spite of the increase in internal dissidence would increase its popular support. The government has sought to maintain the appearance of fully democratic elections by guaranteeing equal facilities to all three competing tickets; however, Diem's control of the press, of mass organizations, and of government machinery has heavily slanted the campaign in his favor and will undoubtedly provoke Communist charges of fraud.

A prominent opposition figure, Phan Huy Quat, recently commented that, despite Diem's unnecessary "rigging," he regarded the election as a step forward in South Vietnam's "inevitable" democratization. He added, however, that Diem must follow up by relaxing police and censorship controls.

In a 29 March broadcast the Communist "Liberation News Agency" of the National Liberation Front in South Vietnam announced that the "self-defense armed forces in the southern area" have been reorganized as the "Liberation Army of South Vietnam." With formal establishment of this military arm of the National Liberation Front, the Communists have taken another step in the development of their subversive apparatus in the South, attempting to create the impression of an indigenous movement which unites both paramilitary and political elements seeking the overthrow of the Diem government.

President Diem repeated his intention to make changes in his cabinet following the election, stating that he would include some National Assembly members but no prominent opposition figures. Diem complained particularly that competent officials were reluctant to be appointed to positions in rural areas, where the Communists have conducted an assassination campaign.
