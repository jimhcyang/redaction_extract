3. Withdrawal of two Chinese Communist armies from North Korea indicated:

Over 55,000 Chinese Communist troops left North Korea between 19 and 27 February,

If the present rate of redeployment is maintained through 5 March, as is expected, the equivalent of about two Chinese Communist armies will have withdrawn from North Korea in the current movement.

Comment: The withdrawal of two Chinese armies would leave only seven in North Korea, where 19 were stationed at the time of the armistice.
