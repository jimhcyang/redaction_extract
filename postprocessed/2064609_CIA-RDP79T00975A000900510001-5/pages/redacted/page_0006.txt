6. Comment on the abolition of the Egyptian constitution:

General Nagib's announcement of 9 December abolishing the Egyptian constitution is a significant step in expanding the army group's control of the country. Just prior to abolishing the constitution, the Prime Minister reshuffled and strengthened his cabinet.

This action, together with the reported formation of a "national bloc" to sponsor a new constitution, represents an effort by the military to strengthen its position through a popular measure. Nagib apparently hopes to remove the last vestiges of the "corrupt" monarchy and provide a constitutional basis for the regime and its reform program.
