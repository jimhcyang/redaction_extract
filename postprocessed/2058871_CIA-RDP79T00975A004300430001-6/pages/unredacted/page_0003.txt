23 JANUARY 1959

I. THE COMMUNIST BLOC
Khrushchev meeting with Finnish President Kekkonen in Leningrad.
East German Premier Grotewohl's visit to India unproductive.

II. ASIA-AFRICA
Israel has probably received high performance jet fighters from France recently.
Arab countries setting up tanker company to carry Middle East oil.
Four of the seven autonomous republics in French West-Africa draft constitution for federal regime.
Japan - Prime Minister Kishi's re-election on 24 January as Liberal-Democratic party president virtually assured, but his position remains weak.
US ambassador thinks Indonesian Government may be shifting away from dependence on pro-Communist and Communist groups.

III. THE WEST
Austria - USSR warns that bilateral association with the European Common Market would violate Austria's neutrality.
Argentina - Industrial strikes will probably evoke revision of government's labor policies and stronger action against Peronistas and Communists.
Panama - Some antiadministration elements plan civil disorders to weaken National Guard as first step in coup attempt.
Cuba will probably grant aid sought by Haitian rebels in invasion attempt.
