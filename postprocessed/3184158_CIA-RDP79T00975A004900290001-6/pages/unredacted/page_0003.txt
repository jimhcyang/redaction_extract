5 FEBRUARY 1960

I. THE COMMUNIST BLOC
Warsaw Pact meeting provides demonstration of Bloc solidarity prior to summit; Khrushchev's speech, not yet released, may give more specific indications of Bloc plans.

II. ASIA-AFRICA
Voroshilov's 18-day visit to India makes little impact.
Indian cabinet committee approves Nehru's proposal to meet Chou En-lai in mid-March.
Burma-Party of former Premier Nu expected to win elections on 6 February; Western relations with new civil government may be somewhat more difficult than with military regime under Ne Win.
UAR, Jordan, and Lebanon to co-ordinate demands for increased revenue from Tapline.
