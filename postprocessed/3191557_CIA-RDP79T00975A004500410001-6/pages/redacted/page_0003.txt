CENTRAL INTELLIGENCE BULLETIN
27 June 1959

DAILY BRIEF

I. THE COMMUNIST BLOC

USSR-Ethiopia:
The Emperor, who wishes to stress his independence of the United States and associate Ethiopia more closely with Pan-Africanism, can be expected to accept some Soviet aid as a demonstration of this policy.

II. ASIA-AFRICA

Ceylon: Governor General Goonetilleke on 26 June issued a general mobilization order calling out Ceylon's armed forces to prevent the outbreak of civil disturbances and to maintain essential services disrupted by a month-long wave of leftist-led strikes. These strikes were designed to weaken Prime Minister Bandaranaike's government in the period before Parliament reconvenes on 30 June. At the moment, however, extreme leftist parties plan to boycott the opening session of Parliament, apparently with the intention of creating more political and economic confusion before directly challenging Bandaranaike's control with a no-confidence motion.

Morocco: The government plans to make a formal request of the United States early next week for military equipment to modernize Morocco's 31,000-man army.
