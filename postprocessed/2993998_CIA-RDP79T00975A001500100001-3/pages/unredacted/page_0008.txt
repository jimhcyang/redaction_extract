9. Moroccan nationalist appointed to Indonesian UN delegation:

A member of the French Moroccan nationalist party, Istiqlal, has been appointed "technical counselor" to the Indonesian delegation to the United Nations.

The French Foreign Ministry ordered its ambassador in Djakarta on 13 February to protest the appointment and request that it be rescinded.

Comment: Indonesia apparently ignored the French demarche. President Sukarno promised the secretary general of the Tunisian Neo-Destour party on 16 March that his country would continue to support the "Tunisian people's independence struggle."

Pakistan was the first member of the anti-colonial Arab-Asian bloc to include a North African nationalist in its UN delegation. Anticolonialism is one of the few issues in the UN on which the Moslem and Asian peoples are generally agreed.
