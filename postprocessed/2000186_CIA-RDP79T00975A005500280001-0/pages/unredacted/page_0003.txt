3 February 1961
CENTRAL INTELLIGENCE BULLETIN

CONTENTS

1. USSR: Launching on Tyura Tam Missile Test Range yesterday results in in-flight failure. (Page i)

2. Laos: Souvanna Phouma appoints "acting premier" of his "government" in rebel-held Xieng Khouang. (Page i)

3. Chinese UN Representation: Peiping's supporters may attempt to reopen the moratorium issue at UN General Assembly reconvening in March. (Page ii)

4. Congo: Gizenga suggests to Nasir that arms aid from UAR transit Sudan under guise of "humanitarian assistance." (Page ii)

5. Iraq: Several reports indicate recent shipment of Soviet arms included MIG-19 jet fighters. (Page iii)

6. Arab League: Tunisia, Iraq, and Jordan are attempting in current league meeting to curtail Nasir's influence. (Page iii)

7. Ethiopia: Haile Selassie delays naming new government; appears more concerned with pressing charges against the rebels. (Page iv)

8. Bolivia: President Paz Estenssoro believes acceptance of bloc aid will not endanger further aid from US. (Page iv)

9. Venezuela: Army units alerted against possible action by rightist Venezuelan exiles in Colombian border areas. (Page v)
