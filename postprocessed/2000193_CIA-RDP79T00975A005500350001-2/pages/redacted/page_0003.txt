11 February 1961

CENTRAL INTELLIGENCE BULLETIN

CONTENTS

1. Communist China: Peiping selling silver bullion to help pay for recent grain purchases. (Page i)

2. Laos: Vientiane government to hold special cabinet meeting with King Savang today; may propose negotiations with Souvanna Phouma as tactical gesture. (Page i)

3. France-Algeria: De Gaulle says preliminary talks with Algerian rebels under way in Tunis. (Page ii)

5. Sudan: Opposition groups active against Abboud government. (Page iii)

6. Iran: Attempt to foment general strikes today expected to have only limited success. (Page iii)

7. British Cameroons: Disorders likely during self-determination plebiscites on 11 and 12 February. (Page iii)
