A TASS correspondent in Geneva suggested that the foreign ministers could agree in principle on a time period of two or three years and that the precise date could be established at a summit meeting. He agreed that an all-German committee would imply substantial Western recognition of East Germany, and added that "it would seem to be one way of making recognition more palatable."
