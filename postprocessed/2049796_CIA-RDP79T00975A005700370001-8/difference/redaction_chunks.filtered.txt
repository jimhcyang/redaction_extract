Quasi-political groups which might also take part in the merger are the Castro/Communist-controlled labor, student, youth, and women's organizations, the revolutionary militia, and the "committees of defense of the revolution," according to Che Guevara. The Communists heretofore have preferred to cultivate the impression that the PSP supports the general revolutionary program of the Castro movement but remains politically separate from it. Party leaders have previously acknowledged, however, that the amalgamation of all existing political groups into a single party is a fundamental Communist goal and a prerequisite for the organization of the Cuban state along "socialist" lines.
