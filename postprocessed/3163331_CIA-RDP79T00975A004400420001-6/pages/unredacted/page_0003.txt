1 MAY 1959

I. THE COMMUNIST BLOC
USSR-Nepal - Recent economic and diplomatic agreements provide opportunities for greater Soviet influence, and will increase India's concern over its northern frontier.

II. ASIA-AFRICA
Afro-Asian Economic Conference committee decides against Soviet membership.
Chinese Nationalists offer training and equipment to Karen dissidents in Burma.
South Vietnam - Rumors persist that Communists planning violence, possibly today, involving US, French, and Chinese Nationalist embassies.
President Rhee closes second largest South Korean newspaper for attacks on government.
Jordan - Prime Minister Rifai will demand removal of Army Chief of Staff Shara.
More incidents involving French military action across the Algerian-Tunisian border lead Bourguiba to make new request for American arms.

III. THE WEST
The Netherlands - Lack of effective government likely to continue for some time.
Cuba - Raul Castro may be undertaking purge of Cuban military.
Panama - Reinforcements for invaders may be en route.
