Chinese Nationalist Assistance to Karen Insurgents in Burma

Chinese Nationalists are prepared to give the Karens communications equipment and to train 30 young Karens on Taiwan, possibly as a beginning of a covert assistance program.

Chinese Nationalist guerrillas collaborated with the Karens for a short time in 1953. Since then Taipei has maintained liaison contacts with the Karens, but has given no significant aid. A policy of renewed encouragement of the Karens would be in line with the apparent revival of Chinese Nationalist interest in strengthening anti-Communist dissident groups in Burma. The Nationalists recently have flown several airdrop missions to the Kuomintang irregulars located near the Burma-Thai-Laos border. The Tibet revolt apparently has induced Taipei to seek to maintain friendly guerrilla forces close to China's borders.
