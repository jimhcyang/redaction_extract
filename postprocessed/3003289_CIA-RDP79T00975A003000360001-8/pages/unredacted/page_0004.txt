1. THE SITUATION IN JORDAN

Comment on:

Prime Minister Nabulsi and his cabinet
have not yet submitted their resignations
to King Hussain, despite their decision
on 31 March to do so in an effort to force
a showdown.

The cabinet apparently continues to hope
that it can through intimidation prevent the king from carrying
out a series of political moves to arrest Jordan's leftist drift.
The cabinet also objects,
to Hussain's opposition to its efforts to reorganize the government and the electoral machinery, and to "his constant contacts with America without reference to the government."

Nabulsi and his cabinet appear to believe
that without representatives of the parties in the present cabinet,
Hussain could not form a government with sufficient popular support to maintain order. They are reportedly prepared to support their stand with the threat of calling out leftist mobs.. Nabulsi is reported to have appealed personally to Jordan's Communist Party "to stand by and be ready to fight the king,"
