6. NASR CALLS CONFERENCE OF ARAB LEADERS ON JORDAN

Comment
The Saudis have previously suggested that Arab states should replace the British subsidy to Jordan, which amounts to about $33,000,000 annually, but have never made a concrete offer.

It is unlikely that the Saudis would live up to such a commitment. However, if the present political unrest in Jordan continues, Hussain may feel that the safety of his throne depends on weakening his ties with Britain.
