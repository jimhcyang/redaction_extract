7. Comment on Benelux reaction to French arms pool plan:

A spokesman for the Belgian Foreign Ministry, in criticizing the French proposal for an arms pool as "complicated" and contradictory, stated, however, that it is more satisfactory than had been anticipated. A high-level Dutch Foreign Ministry official told Ambassador Matthews on 7 January that the French proposal is unsatisfactory and does not provide adequate national safeguards. He revealed that his government is sounding out industrialists on the possibility of the Netherlands staying out of the pool even if all other WEU countries join.

The Benelux countries will probably be greatly antagonized because Mendes-France has not included them in the itinerary of his current trips to Italy and West Germany to discuss the Paris proposal for an arms pool.

Representatives of the Western European Union countries are scheduled to meet on 17 January to discuss the French arms pool plan, and the success of this meeting may be jeopardized if the Benelux countries are convinced that France, Italy and Germany intend to impose their views.
