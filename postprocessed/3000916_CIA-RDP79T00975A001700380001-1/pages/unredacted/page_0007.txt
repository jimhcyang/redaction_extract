The semiofficial French Press Agency is being reorganized, reportedly to reflect a progovernment orientation. The reported director-designate is Jean Marin, a conservative Gaullist and pro-American but also a close friend of Interior Minister Mitterrand, who is now under fire for alleged Communist sympathies.

French premier reported planning to take uncompromising stand in London:

Premier Mendes-France will deliberately take an unacceptable stand at the London conference on German rearmament. Then, if the United States urges West Germany to rearm, France expects to join Great Britain, the Scandinavian countries, Yugoslavia and the German Socialists in demanding a new East-West four-power conference. By that time the French allegedly hope that the American elections will have forced a modification of American foreign policy.

Comment: Although Mendes-France has repeatedly stated that the London conference must not be "another Brussels," his latest proposals may have the effect of making it so. His reported suggestion that Britain be included in an organization having many of the supranational features of EDC will probably be unacceptable to the British.
