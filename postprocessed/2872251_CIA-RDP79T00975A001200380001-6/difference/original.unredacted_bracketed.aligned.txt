SOVIET UNION

2. Soviet Union may have new-type bomber:

On 30 July, observed on a field near Moscow a plane with a [[PRED_REDACTION_5]]fuselage[[/PRED_REDACTION_5]] and tail section resembling those of the B-47 jet medium bomber. show the plane to be half again as large as a nearby TU-4 conventional medium bomber. The plane may have had swept-back wings.

The engine type could not be determined, but a swept-wing aircraft could be expected to be jet-propelled.

Comment: If the suggested jet-type [[PRED_REDACTION_6]]propulsion[[/PRED_REDACTION_6]] is substantiated, this would be the first evidence that the USSR has constructed a prototype jet which is at least a large medium and possibly a heavy bomber.
[PAGE 04]
Other information has already indicated that a smaller jet medium bomber, designed by German scientists, has been flight-tested in the USSR and may be intended for serial production.

[[PRED_REDACTION_7]]SOUTHEAST ASIA

3. Sharp governmental divisions foreseen in Indonesia:

Communist-front ministers in the new Indonesian cabinet will meet "solid opposition" from their staffs, in the opinion of Sudjatmoko, editor of a Djakarta Socialist newspaper. He states that Prime Minister Ali will "rebel" if leftist elements try to push through measures to which he is opposed. He believes that provincial military and civil authorities may give lip service to the new government, but that they will not follow its dictates.

Comment: Sudjatmoko, a leftist Socialist, has in recent years served as counselor in Indonesia's embassy in Washington, as charge in London, and with the Indonesian UN delegation.

As yet there is no clear indication whether the opposition is sufficiently organized to be effective or whether the expected divisions in all branches of the government will only lead to further confusion[[/PRED_REDACTION_7]].

WESTERN EUROPE

4. French official doubts if his government seeks victory in Indochina:

Despite the French policy announced on 3 July, there appears to be no real [[PRED_REDACTION_8]]intention[[/PRED_REDACTION_8]] to accompany liberal political [[PRED_REDACTION_9]]concessions[[/PRED_REDACTION_9]] with effective measures to win the war in Indochina, according to the French political adviser to the
[PAGE 05]
Vietnam government. From talks with Deputy Premier Reynaud, Foreign Minister Bidault, and others, he got the impression that the French government hopes for sufficient victories to enable it to make some sort of deal with the Chinese Communists or the Viet Minh.

Comment: The French have long hoped to eliminate Chinese Communist aid to the Viet Minh through [[PRED_REDACTION_10]]negotiations[[/PRED_REDACTION_10]]. The French government denies any intention to negotiate with the Viet Minh.
