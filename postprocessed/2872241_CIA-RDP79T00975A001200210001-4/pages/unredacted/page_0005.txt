SOUTHEAST ASIA
5. Alliance between Burmese Communists and Karen insurgents believed limited:

The American embassy in Rangoon comments that press reports of an alliance between Karen insurgents and the Communists in Burma, which has been mooted for two years, are probably true insofar as they apply to the Karens in southern Burma. It sees no indication, however, that the more important Karen forces in eastern Burma are involved. Their position is not as desperate as that of those in the south.

The embassy points out that most Karen leaders are ardent Christians and that an agreement with the Communists is likely to cause serious dissension among the leaders in the south as well as to further isolate them from the eastern group.

Comment: Karen insurgents in Burma are believed to total about 4,000, of whom perhaps 1,000 are in south Burma.
