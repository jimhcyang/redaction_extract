IV. CONCLUSIONS OF SPECIAL USIB COMMITTEE
ON BERLIN SITUATION

The USIB has taken note of the following conclusions reached by its special committee on the Berlin situation. The committee pointed out that its conclusions are to be used in context with all SNIEs relevant to the Berlin situation.

1. The USSR continues to maintain its hard position on Berlin and has attempted to increase pressure on the Western governments for further concessions--for example, by the Soviet-Polish communique. At Geneva, however, Gromyko is trying to keep the door open for negotiations, and no major developments are likely until the results of the Nixon visit have been evaluated by the USSR.

2. So long as the Soviet leaders estimate that the prospects for negotiation with the West over Berlin are favorable to them, they will not conclude a separate peace treaty with the GDR or turn over access control to the East Germans. The USSR will continue to use the threats of such actions as a form of pressure on the West. The actual physical transfer of controls could be accomplished with little or no warning.
