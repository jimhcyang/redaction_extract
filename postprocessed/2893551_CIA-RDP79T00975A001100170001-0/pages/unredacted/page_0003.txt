GENERAL

1. Molotov agrees to clarify position of Italian prisoners:
Soviet Foreign Minister Molotov has assured the Italian Ambassador that the recent amnesty in the USSR also applied to foreigners and that he would take steps to clarify the position of Italian prisoners sentenced as war criminals. The overture was in response to the Ambassador's inquiry during a recent courtesy call.

Comment: The USSR has repeatedly asserted that it held only war criminals, not prisoners of war. Molotov's statement may foreshadow a token release of German, French, Spanish and Japanese war criminals without compromising the previous Soviet position.

Although this is the first official notice that the amnesty decree applies to foreigners, a British subject already has been released under the decree.
