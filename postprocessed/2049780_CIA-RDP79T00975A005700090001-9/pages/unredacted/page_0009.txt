Communist China - USSR: While Sino-Soviet trade talks were concluded with the signing of the annual trade protocol on 7 April, discussions begun in February involving Soviet long-term assistance and technical aid for China are still dragging on, and do not appear to be going smoothly. Technicians are not likely to return unless some position agreement is reached in these talks.

India: A bumper rice harvest, combined with an increase in other food-grain crops, has resulted in a new high in Indian food-grain production during 1960-61. While these results reflect in part New Delhi's strenuous efforts to expand acreage and yields, the key factor appears to have been generally favorable weather.
