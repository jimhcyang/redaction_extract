7. British see Indochina drifting toward disaster:

The American embassy in London reports that British officials at the forthcoming tripartite talks in Washington will take a pessimistic view of the Indochina situation, both military and political. They believe the French proposals of 3 July were probably too late and that, in any case, they were merely an offer to negotiate what was promised more than four years ago. They foresee only further deterioration and perhaps disaster if the situation is allowed to drift much longer.
