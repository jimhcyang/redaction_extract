9. Czech government rumored preparing to allow private businesses to reopen:

Rumors are circulating in Prague that the Czech government recently offered to advance credit and material to permit former retail store owners and small-scale consumer goods manufacturers to reopen their businesses.

The embassy believes that the Kremlin undoubtedly prompted the repeal on 6 July of the week-old decree specifying stringent measures to combat labor absenteeism. This action may presage further relaxations.

Comment: The reopening of private retail stores and consumer goods manufacturing plants would mark a significant reversal of the Czech regime's socialization program and would reflect the critical problems it faces in fulfilling the need for consumer goods. Virtually all Czech domestic trade has been nationalized since 1951.

WESTERN EUROPE

10. Churches believed unable to handle food shipments from West to East Germany:

Commenting on Chancellor Adenauer's draft request to President Eisenhower for American aid in the program of food shipments to East Germany, Ambassador Conant expresses the view that the churches do not have adequate facilities or funds to handle shipment and distribution. The could, however, participate in a general supervisory capacity.
