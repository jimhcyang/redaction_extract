Lebanon: President Shihab is apparently in the process
of replacing the Karame cabinet with a government of techni-
cians. He has asked Nizam al-Akkari, a semiprofessional
civil servant whose background suggests that he probably fa-
vors the Moslem rebel side, to act as formateur. A politically
neutral government of civil servants and military officers would
be a stopgap until a compromise between the militant Chris-
tian and Moslem elements can be worked out.
(Page 6)

Tunisia: President Bourguiba reiterated on 8 October to
the French and American ambassadors that he could not accept
arms of French manufacture on any basis, including those sup-
plied by the United States under offshore procurement, so long
as the French continue their attempts to suppress the Algerian
rebels. He declared that although acquisition of French arms
might strengthen him militarily, they "might easily destroy"
him politically.
