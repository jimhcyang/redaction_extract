LATE ITEM

Laos: The shaky compromise government of Souvanna Phouma has declared a state of emergency in Laos to meet the rebellion launched by General Phoumi and Prince Boun Oum on 10 September. From their headquarters in southern Laos the rebels have "abolished the constitution" and declared martial law.

Souvanna apparently hopes to retrieve the situation short of civil war. After Souvanna conferred with the king on 11 September, the palace invited the rebels to come to Luang Prabang for consultations. Phoumi posed a condition that he first be allowed to airlift one of his battalions to Luang Prabang, but this has been refused. Further efforts to arrange a conference are in progress.

Phoumi claims to have the support of three of the four military regions outside of Vientiane. Despite his apparent military superiority, Phoumi has indicated that he is open to a new compromise; his objectives probably include resumption of control over the Laotian army.

Saigon is working closely with Phoumi and is providing, at the minimum, political advice.

Premier Sarit in Thailand told an American observer that while he has not encouraged Phoumi's rebellion, he might, depending on its progress, later extend covert support. There is the danger that Laos' Communist neighbors, North Vietnam and Communist China, would react to such intervention by stepping up their support to the Pathet Lao. (Page 9)
