Kekhia also reportedly expressed dissatisfaction with American policy toward the Arab states, as against Israel. He criticized the rigid stand of the United States on jurisdiction provisions in its negotiations with Libya and remarked that "Americans seemed to think that their wealth and power could dictate" to smaller countries.

Comment: There are other indications that the proposed $2,000,000 annual payment, double what Washington initially offered two years ago, will not now satisfy Libya. Kekhia's criticism of American policy reflects both Egyptian activity in Libya and the influence of the Arab League.
