1. JORDANIAN KING ASKS FOR IRAQI TROOPS

Comment  The American embassy in Amman on 10 January described the situation as steadily deteriorating and stated that "law and order extends only within range of the Legionnaire's rifle."

Hussain probably would call in Iraqi troops only as a last resort. Their use in Jordan would be interpreted by the already aroused Jordanians, as well as by Egypt, Syria, and Saudi Arabia, as confirmation that Britain and Iraq were determined to force Jordan's adherence to the Baghdad pact. Violent conflicts between Iraqi units and Jordanian extremists would be almost certain, and attempts might be made to assassinate Hussain and Iraqi premier Nuri Said.
