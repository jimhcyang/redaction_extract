Comment: This is the first indication that the Pleven government faces an immediate and serious challenge on a major foreign policy issue in addition to the challenge on basic economic problems.

The Socialists, who are basically opposed to German rearmament in any form, initially gave only reluctant approval to the EDF plan. A year ago in Washington former Defense Minister Moch, a Socialist, stubbornly held to his party's views by ruling out the creation of German divisions, and the French Government's recent concessions on this point have apparently antagonized the Socialists.
