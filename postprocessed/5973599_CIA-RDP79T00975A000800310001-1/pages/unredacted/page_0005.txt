NEAR EAST - AFRICA

3. Tudeh seeks tribal assistance in campaign to expel US Military Mission:
Tudeh leaders have approached National Front Deputy and Qashqai tribal leader Khosro Khan for assistance in the current Tudeh campaign to oust the American Military Mission from Iran. Khosro Khan refused, stating that the Tudeh's alignment with Soviet policy prevented it from being truly nationalistic.

Comment: This approach is consistent with current Communist efforts to obtain National Front agreement to Tudeh collaboration. It was apparently made on the basis of the Qashqai's known hostility to the army and on their desire to regain sole control of the tribe's territory in oil-rich southwestern Iran. The Qashqai have reportedly been urging the National Front to purge the army and place officers sympathetic to the tribe in key positions.

4. Iranian Government requests more legal information on nationalizing industries:
The newly-appointed Iranian Foreign Minister has instructed legations and embassies to air mail copies of all local laws on the nationalization of industries and other establishments.

Comment: National Front supporters have advocated extensive measures in addition to the nationalization of oil. The Foreign Minister's request may foreshadow further nationalization efforts.

The Iranian-Soviet fisheries contract expires within a few months, and the government has assured Parliament that it will "solve the problem" of the Caspian Sea fisheries.
