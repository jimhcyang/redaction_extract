CENTRAL INTELLIGENCE BULLETIN
19 March 1958

DAILY BRIEF

Japan: Japan's largest labor federation, Sohyo, has decided to invite labor representatives from Communist China and five other countries to meet in Tokyo on 15 May as a preparatory committee for an Asian-African labor conference. The American ambassador in Tokyo comments that the inclusion of Chinese Communist representatives is a departure from Sohyo's previous attitudes and may indicate a weakening of the moderate elements in the federation.

III. THE WEST

France: Gaillard's 282-196 victory in the 18 March confidence vote on constitutional reform has not resolved the basic differences within his coalition, which is increasingly split over the North African situation. Speculation on the possible return of General de Gaulle to power continues.
