CENTRAL INTELLIGENCE BULLETIN
19 March 1958

DAILY BRIEF

Egypt - Israel: The Egyptian commander in chief on 7 March authorized dispatch of intelligence parties into Israel to gather information on Israeli military activity. Egyptian intelligence missions into Israel were ordered halted in June 1957. Egypt's willingness to undertake infiltration of Israel appears to reflect concern over Israel's reaction to increasing Egyptian activity in the Gaza strip and eastern Sinai. Egyptian intelligence appreciation of Israeli military activity as recently as 17 March, however, remained calm.

Japan: Japan's largest labor federation, Sohyo, has decided to invite labor representatives from Communist China and five other countries to meet in Tokyo on 15 May as a preparatory committee for an Asian-African labor conference. The American ambassador in Tokyo comments that the inclusion of Chinese Communist representatives is a departure from Sohyo's previous attitudes and may indicate a weakening of the moderate elements in the federation. (Page 5)

III. THE WEST

France: Gaillard's 282-196 victory in the 18 March confidence vote on constitutional reform has not resolved the basic differences within his coalition, which is increasingly split over the North African situation. Speculation on the possible return of General de Gaulle to power continues. (Page 6)
