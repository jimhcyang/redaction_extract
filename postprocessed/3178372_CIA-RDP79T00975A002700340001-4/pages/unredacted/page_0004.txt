2. SOVIET OFFICIAL ASSURES THAT WAR IS UNLIKELY

Comment on:

Grigory T. Zaitsev, chief of the Middle East division of the Soviet Foreign Ministry, agreed with the view that the Suez crisis will not lead to war against Egypt because the West "knows the seriousness of it in the atomic age". Zaitsev said the unwillingness of a majority of NATO members to approve anything which would lead to war and the disagreement within the British government itself were added deterrents.

Zaitsev's remarks indicate that Moscow continues to believe that negotiations can be prolonged as a means of forestalling military action against Egypt. The Soviet statement suggested that Moscow might take the initiative in bringing the problem before the UN Security Council.
