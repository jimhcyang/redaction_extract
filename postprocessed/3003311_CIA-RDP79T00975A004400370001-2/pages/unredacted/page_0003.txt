25 APRIL 1959

I. THE COMMUNIST BLOC
Khrushchev's letter to Nasir.

II. ASIA-AFRICA
Iranians protest against Soviet overflights; their count believed exaggerated.
Yemen - Crown Prince Badr tightens security measures.
Power struggle in Sudanese military government appears to be approaching showdown.
Morocco to resume negotiations on US air bases; will press for further steps toward evacuation.
Britain decides to sell heavy weapons to Iraq; delivery will not take place before 1960.
Afro-Asian Economic Conference to take up question of admitting USSR.
Burmese Army using Tibet situation to stimulate sentiment against Communist China.
Japanese elections disclose no major shift in party strengths.

III. THE WEST
West German public opinion poll.
Britain - Deferral of elections will tend to put Macmillan under pressure to compromise in East-West negotiations.
