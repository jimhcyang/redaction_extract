2 NOVEMBER 1960

I. THE COMMUNIST BLOC
Peiping continues to stand firm in dispute with Moscow; latest issue of China's Red Flag implicitly criticizes Khrushchev's "gradualist" strategy.

II. ASIA-AFRICA
New effort to get France and Algerian rebels together in direct talks decided on at Abidjan meeting last week of leaders of former French territories in Black Africa.
Chinese Nationalist Air Force said to have averaged two flights a month this year supplying Nationalist irregulars in Burma-Thailand border area.
Indonesia's new petroleum law will require foreign oil companies to operate under contract rather than on present concession basis.
Situation in Laos.

III. THE WEST
Opposition elements in Guatemala encouraged by Salvadoran coup.
Some possibility seen of anti-US violence during Panamanian independence celebrations this week.
