2. Soviet "peaceful coexistence" policy considered likely:
The US Embassy in London suggests the possibility that the USSR will now seek a period of "peaceful coexistence" in which to increase its own strength while the West is weakened by unpreparedness, complacency, or economic crises. Recalling the precedent of Soviet policies in the 1920's and 1930's, the embassy characterizes the coexistence policy as a middle road between a "genuine general retreat" and an imminent showdown with the West.

The USSR may even, for the time being, refrain from further local aggressions through Satellites, since the risk of thereby precipitating a general conflict may be greater than the USSR cares to incur.
