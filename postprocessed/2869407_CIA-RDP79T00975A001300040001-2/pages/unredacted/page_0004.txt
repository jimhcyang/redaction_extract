SOVIET UNION

3. Moscow ministerial reorganization designed to raise Soviet living standards:

Division of the three economic ministries previously concerned with agriculture, trade and the production of food and consumer goods into six ministries is a definitive government action aimed at carrying out the promise of the Malenkov regime to improve the material well-being of the Soviet citizen.

A. I. Mikoyan, a member of the party presidium and fourth-ranking man in the governmental hierarchy, has been named minister of internal trade, thereby focusing the attention of the USSR's top expert in foreign and domestic trade on internal problems alone. For the first time a Ministry of the Consumer Goods Industry has been established and a former politburo member, A. N. Kosygin, named as head.

WESTERN EUROPE

4. French Communists seen improving their position:

The French government may be preparing to abandon its anti-Communist program.

Left-wing leaders of the Christian Workers Confederation have implied to American embassy officials that the recent release of imprisoned Communist leaders resulted from a deal between the government and the Communist Party.

The embassy believes that the progress made by the Communist-led General Labor Confederation in its unity-of-action program during the August strikes has strengthened the position of the French Communist Party.
