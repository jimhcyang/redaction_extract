CENTRAL INTELLIGENCE BULLETIN
17 April 1961
DAILY BRIEF

Laos: Government forces on 15 April evacuated Nhommarat, 28 miles northeast of Thakhek, after receiving enemy 105 mm. howitzer and 120 mm. mortar fire. On the previous day, an attempted government attack eastward from the town had been repulsed by heavy enemy mortar fire. Recently arrived reinforcements have brought the government's strength in this area to over 1,000 men, but the troops' morale is low and their quality poor. Nhommarat was the government's key blocking position against the threat of an attack on the Mekong River port town of Thakhek.

An enemy force estimated at two battalions has taken the strongpoint at Ban Pha, about 15 miles southwest of Xieng Khouang town.
