Both Hanoi and the Pathet Lao have expressed opposition to Western formulations that would freeze the present military lines in Laos. On 31 March the Pathet Lao termed a "deceitful trick" the idea of having a cease-fire precede a conference. On the same day, Hanoi charged the US with an attempt to "gain time" to bolster Vientiane forces and "prevent the expansion of patriotic forces."

In a conversation on 29 March with Ambassador Stevenson, Soviet Foreign Minister Gromyko repeated the generally moderate line he had taken in his previous discussion with President Kennedy. Gromyko urged "patience" from both sides and voiced his hope that neither the US nor the Soviet Union would take measures to aggravate the situation in Laos.

Deputy Foreign Minister Winiewicz of Poland recently told Ambassador Stevenson that while he was certain the Soviet Union wanted a detente with the West, the position of the Chinese Communists was causing acute difficulties.
