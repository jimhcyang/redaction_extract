2. The Soviet memorandum of 17 February to Bonn, published
by the USSR on 4 March, was intended to remind the West that the
USSR still insists on an early solution of the Berlin situation through
negotiations. The memorandum reinforced Khrushchev's earlier
assertions that the USSR could not agree to postponing discussions
beyond the West German elections set for this September.

3. The East Germans have somewhat relaxed their implementation
of controls of the Berlin intersector boundary without altering their
claims of sovereignty in this regard.

4. While maintaining trade with the West at the level of previous
years, East Germany has initiated some of the long-range moves
necessary to make its economy independent of Western imports and
thereby deprive the West of this leverage in future negotiations over
Berlin.
