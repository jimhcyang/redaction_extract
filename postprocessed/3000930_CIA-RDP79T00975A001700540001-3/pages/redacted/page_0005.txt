Although the premier is willing to negotiate on his proposals, the Belgian representative on the EDC Interim Commission doubts that enough of the proposals can be salvaged at the Brussels meeting scheduled for 19 August to prevent the appearance of serious defeat for Mendes-France.

Comment: The changes proposed by Mendes-France reveal the hand of EDC opponents in his entourage and can be expected to exasperate France's partners, who have spent two years in delicate negotiations on these and similar issues. While many of the proposals could be handled under the treaty as it stands, several of them, specifically those aimed at giving France special privileges and discriminating against Germany, will require textual alterations in the treaty and in any event will not be acceptable to the other EDC countries.

In view of Mendes-France's conviction that an early French decision on EDC is imperative, he can be expected to modify his proposed changes at Brussels.
