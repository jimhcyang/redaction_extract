5. Peiping plans to exploit capture of American pilot near Manchuria:

The British Charge in Peiping reports that the Chinese Communist press is playing up the case of an American pilot, allegedly shot down over Chinese territory, as "living evidence" of American aggression against China.

The Charge believes that the Chinese intend to publicize the case "to the utmost," probably with a "confession" from the pilot.

According to Far East Air Force records, the pilot was shot down over North Korea near the Manchurian border.

Comment: A major theme of Chinese Communist propaganda has been the violation of Chinese territory by American planes. A "confession" from this pilot could be used by Peiping to buttress claims of this type.

SOUTH ASIA

6. Appointment of new general secretary of Indian Communist Party reported:

Comment: A change in Communist Party leadership was foreshadowed by a Politburo meeting in Madras from 11 to 19 August, at which Dange was recommended for the post of general secretary. The Politburo decided at the same time to continue the use of parliamentary methods but to increase agitation among peasant, labor, and student groups, and to make plans for going underground if the party should be banned.

Dange, a Politburo member and veteran Communist leader with wide experience in both political and organizational fields, is probably better equipped to coordinate these activities than any other candidate.
