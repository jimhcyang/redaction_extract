NEAR EAST - AFRICA

6. Israeli Foreign Minister believes Israeli-Syrian border accord likely:

The Israeli Foreign Minister believes that current Israeli-Syrian boundary discussions will result in an agreement.

He told the American Ambassador in Tel Aviv that the Israeli Cabinet has approved in principle a proposal for liquidating the demilitarized zone between the two countries, even though it entails giving up some territory.

Comment: The Syrian dictator, Colonel Shishakli, is also interested in reaching a settlement, although he may become more difficult as the negotiations progress. On the other hand, Jordan, because of its border difficulties with Israel, may endeavor to prevent any Israeli-Syrian agreement by exploiting popular Arab opposition to direct negotiations with Israel.
