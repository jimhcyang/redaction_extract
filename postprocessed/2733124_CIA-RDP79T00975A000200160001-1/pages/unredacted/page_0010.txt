FAR EAST
13. Burmese set three-day deadline before appeal to UN:
On 5 May, after conferring with Prime Minister Thakin Nu concerning Chinese Nationalist troops in Burma, the Burmese Foreign Minister told Ambassador Key that Burma would not defer an appeal to the UN for more than three days unless, in the meantime, the US could suggest an alternative solution. The Minister added that the Chinese Communist Ambassador had inquired earlier in the day regarding reports that large numbers of Chinese Nationalist troops had recently entered Kengtung from Thailand and asked what action Burma intended to take in the matter. He was told that the intruders were Vietnamese refugees and was assured that Burma would take action to terminate the Nationalist problem in Kengtung.

Ambassador Key comments that the Burmese Government is "fearful almost to the point of hysteria that unless it acts quickly the Chinese Communists may intervene."
