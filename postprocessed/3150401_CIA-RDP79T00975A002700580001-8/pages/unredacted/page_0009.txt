6. AUSTRIAN SOCIALISTS DOUBT THEY CAN BLOCK ACCEPTANCE OF SOVIET LOAN

Austrian Socialists feel they will be unable to prevent acceptance of a Soviet loan which First Deputy Premier Mikoyan may offer when he arrives in Austria on 2 November, the Socialist parliamentary leader informed Ambassador Thompson on 12 October.

The Soviet embassy in Vienna is trying to influence the conservative People's Party as well as the Socialists--the two parties comprising the coalition government--to accept a loan to modernize Vienna's public transportation system, including the construction of a subway, and to assist the industrial complex formerly controlled by the USSR.

Comment

Last year a Soviet proposal for a substantial loan to the Lower Austrian provincial government, where most of the formerly Soviet-controlled industries are located, was turned down largely as a result of Socialist efforts. The Socialists are now in a poor position to block Soviet loan offers since they lost ground to the People's Party in the May national election. The People's Party now controls the Austrian parliament.
