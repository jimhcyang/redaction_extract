27 AUGUST 1960

I. THE COMMUNIST BLOC

II. ASIA-AFRICA
Japanese foreign minister's visit to South Korea next month intended to serve as demonstration of desire of both Tokyo and Seoul to improve relations.
Indonesian resentment mounts sharply over Dutch plans for Karel Doorman, and may lead to new retaliatory moves by Djakarta.
Turkish cabinet dismissals not regarded as reflecting split or new uneasiness in National Unity Council, which remains the real government.
Mali Premier Keita's hopes for holding federation together apparently diminishing; Hammarskjold planning no formal action at this time on either Keita's or Senegal's pleas for UN action.
The situation in the Congo.

III. THE WEST
Trujillo's official press announces negotiations with New China News Agency; this follows contract by Radio Caribe with TASS.
