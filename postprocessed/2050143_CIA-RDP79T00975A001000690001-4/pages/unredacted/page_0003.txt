GENERAL

Soviet submarine believed sighted off South Africa:
The submarine sighted by a South African pilot
off Natal on 12 March is believed to have been
a Soviet "L" Class type, according to a Union
intelligence officer.

He also states that on 24 February a Soviet tanker
sailed from Cape Town, allegedly to refuel Russian whaling vessels in the
Antarctic.

Comment: The circumstances of this sighting
suggest that it is reliable. Soviet submarines of the "L" Class are capable of long-range operations; they do not normally operate without
some kind of support.

FAR EAST

South Korea seeks substantial American aid to offset budget deficit:
Acting Prime Minister Paik Tu-chin, in a note
delivered to the American Embassy on 21 March,
declares that substantial outside aid will be required to cover Korea's 1953-54 budget deficit
of 43 billion hwan. The South Korean official states that his country's
financial situation is so desperate that "it is difficult, if not impossible,
for us to support our armies in the field." The note indicates that the
South Korean Government can meet its ordinary nonmilitary expenditures
plus only about 25 percent of planned military commitments.

Comment: The South Korean budget is predicated
on an increase in the armed forces from 443,000 to 600,000 men. At the
official exchange rate, the budget deficit would amount to $715,000,000;
in terms of actual purchasing power it amounts to $238,500,000.
