5. IMPACT OF NEW SOVIET POLICY STATEMENTS ON INDONESIAN ELITE

Ambassador Cumming in Djakarta is concerned over the potential impact of recent Soviet statements on the circles which form opinion and policy in Indonesia. He cites as illustrative of a trend a conversation with an intelligent, widely traveled and anti-Communist Socialist official in which the latter stressed the importance of the olive branch the Soviets had extended to Socialists throughout the world.

Comment
The relatively poor showing of the anti-Communist parties in the Indonesian elections last fall has in itself brought a leftward reorientation in Indonesian political thinking.
