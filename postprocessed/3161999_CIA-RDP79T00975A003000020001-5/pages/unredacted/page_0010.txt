5. RENEWED OPPOSITION TO CHINA DIFFERENTIAL

Comment on:

Statements made to the Japanese CHINCOM representative in Paris by the representatives of Britain, France, and Norway indicate that these powers plan to give strongly negative replies to the American proposal of 27 December for maintaining the China trade control differential and for further restricting strategic trade with East Europe.

Britain's representative is quoted as saying London will strongly oppose measures for implementing the US recommendation. The French representative stated that "France will insist that there is no reason to impose stricter control on trade with China than with Russia and that the levels of controls should be equalized." The Norwegian representative likewise indicated opposition.

Parallel intimations of growing opposition from other countries include consideration of trade missions by West Germany and Japan, which have in the past been relatively co-operative in supporting the China differential.
