3. British concerned over impact of Malayan Communists' peace proposal:

The British authorities in Malaya are publicly treating the Communists' recent offer to negotiate a settlement of the seven-year-old terrorist campaign as a sign of weakness, but privately they are dismayed by it. The British consider the proposal, which would appear to a large number of Malayans, admirably timed to promote the Communists' objective of dividing the country before its first elections, on 27 July, and recognize that their rebuff of the bid can be used by the Communists to promote anti-British sentiment.
