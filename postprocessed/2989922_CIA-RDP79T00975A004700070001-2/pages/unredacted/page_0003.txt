8 SEPTEMBER 1959

I. THE COMMUNIST BLOC

American officials in Hong Kong feel basic Communist motive in Sino-Indian border and Laotian situations is to reverse setbacks of the past year.

Soviet ambassador returning to Tehran; USSR may have decided to relax propaganda pressure on Iran.

Taiwan Strait--Chinese Communists may have initiated alert in Amoy area.

II. ASIA-AFRICA

India issues White Paper on border issues; Nehru states no Chinese Communist border intrusions since 26 August.

Indonesia--Peiping protests Djakarta's treatment of Chinese nationals.

Japan--Kishi fears effects of visit to Peiping by former Prime Minister Ishibashi.

Iraq--Coup attempt reportedly now set for 9 September.

UAR propaganda charges Moscow with interference in UAR internal affairs.

Algerian rebels investigating possibility of hospitalizing their wounded in the US.

III. THE WEST

Belgian Government weakened by dispute over Congo policy.

LATE ITEMS

USSR--Tyura Tam Missile Test Range activity on 6 and 8 September.

Laos--Communist pressure continues in Sam Neua and Phong Saly provinces; South Vietnam and Thailand not yet prepared to intervene openly in Laos.
