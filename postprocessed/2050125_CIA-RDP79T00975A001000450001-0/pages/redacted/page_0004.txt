SOUTHEAST ASIA

3. Burmese Communist penetration of Li Mi's forces alleged by Rangoon official:

A Burmese War Office spokesman, reiterating charges of Chinese Communist penetration of Li Mi's Nationalist forces in Burma, claims evidence that Burmese Communists as well have infiltrated the Nationalists and in even greater numbers. In addition, rebellious Karen forces have been heavily infiltrated by Burmese Communists, and all three groups are now allied against the government.

The War Office estimates Nationalist strength in Burma at 18,000 men and believes that they are under the effective control of Li Mi.

Comment: Burmese Communist infiltration of both Nationalists and Karens, if it resulted in large-scale coordinated military efforts, would seriously strain government forces, which have been hard pressed to conduct a concerted campaign against even one insurgent group.

The number of Nationalists in Burma is believed to be far below 18,000, and there is evidence that Li Mi does not control the bulk of these forces.
