CENTRAL INTELLIGENCE BULLETIN
22 October 1960

DAILY BRIEF

I. THE COMMUNIST BLOC

II. ASIA-AFRICA

Republic of the Congo: Increased friction between Mobutu and the UN Command in the Congo, stemming from UN representative Dayal's efforts to check Mobutu's anti-Lumumba activities, could lead to clashes between Mobutu's followers and UN troops. There are unconfirmed reports Mobutu is reinforcing Congolese Army troop strength in Leopoldville.

Elements favoring Lumumba again appear politically active in his old stronghold in Orientale Province, an area viewed by some of his adherents as a possible seat for a rival government. The UAR ambassador, however, has advised Lumumba not to leave Leopoldville. Dayal told the UAR ambassador that Antoine Gizenga, Lumumba's leftist vice premier, has caused a "great amount of trouble" in the Orientale capital of Stanleyville, and that he had delivered a "revolutionary speech" to troops and people in the area.

Mali: Tension between anti-French extremists and less radical elements in the neutralist government of Mali is reported threatening the position of President Modibo Keita, a relative moderate who is trying to reconcile the two sides. At present, the principal issues in dispute concern Mali's future relations with France and the French Community and the amount of aid Mali should accept from the Sino-Soviet bloc. Should the extremists succeed in establishing predominant influence, Mali would probably move rapidly toward a close alignment with Guinea and welcome large-scale bloc assistance.
