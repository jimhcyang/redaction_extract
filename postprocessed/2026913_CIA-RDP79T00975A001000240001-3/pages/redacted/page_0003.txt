GENERAL

2. Tito hints acceptance of Trieste compromise and Italian participation in Balkan pact:

Turkish Foreign Minister Koprulu told Ambassador Peurifoy in Athens that, in reply to his suggestion that provision be made for eventual Italian adherence to a Balkan defense pact, Tito said that this could probably be arranged and that the Trieste issue itself was capable of "reasonably prompt solution."

Kopru[u] deplores Premier de Gasperi's attempts to have the West exert pressure on Belgrade in order to achieve a favorable Trieste solution. The Turkish Foreign Minister hopes that after the Italian elections it will be possible to obtain an early solution of the Trieste issue and secure Italian adherence to a Greek-Turkish-Yugoslav protocol.
