Anti-missile research activity has been under way in the Sary Shagan area since 1957. Missiles fired from the Kapustin Yar rangehead, (1,100 n.m. distant) SP-2 (350 n.m. away), and SP-5 (500 n.m.) have been used as targets for the collection of re-entry data and the testing of equipment.
