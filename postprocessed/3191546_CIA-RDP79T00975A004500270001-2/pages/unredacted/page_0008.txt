Chinese Communist Arms for Algerian Rebels

The Soviet ship Stavropol, which has frequently engaged in Soviet arms deliveries to free-world countries, may be carrying Chinese Communist arms to the Algerian rebels. The vessel, now in the Gulf of Aden, received instructions in early May at Rangoon to pick up a cargo for "Casablanca" in Shanghai. "The quantity and kind" of goods were to be designated at a later date. Until 6 June, when the vessel reported its position in the Gulf of Aden, it failed to make normal position reports, but instead followed communications procedures associated with previous Soviet arms deliveries to the Near East and Indonesia.

In December of last year Chou En-lai informed an FLN delegation in Peiping that China was willing to supply the Algerian provisional government with arms. In February Peiping decided to grant $4,000,000 worth of military aid to the FLN. Peiping indicated that it would ship "light weapons and ammunition" to the UAR, but Cairo and the FLN would have to arrange for delivery to Algeria. Cairo in the past has frequently acted as the middleman in transshipping arms to the Algerian rebels.
