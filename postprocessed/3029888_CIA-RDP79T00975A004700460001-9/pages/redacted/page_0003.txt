CENTRAL INTELLIGENCE BULLETIN
23 October 1959

DAILY BRIEF

I. THE COMMUNIST BLOC

Communist China - USSR: Recently available articles from Communist China's provincial press contain the most explicit admissions to date that Soviet disapproval of the communes contributed to dissension among the rank and file in the Chinese Communist party. The articles attack as "right opportunists" party members who cite Soviet abandonment of communes in the 1920s and who say, "If other socialist countries do not have communes, we should not establish them!" Khrushchev's failure to endorse even the limited concept that communes are good "for China" probably has added to the difficulties the Chinese leaders are having with unconvinced party members.
