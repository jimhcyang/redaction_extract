GENERAL
1. Rumania reportedly to receive large shipment of Belgian steel:
Rumania is to receive over 9,000 tons of finished steel from Belgium. Shipment will reportedly take place this week. Customs officials are not to see the receipt certificates until after the material has been loaded aboard ship.

Comment: This is the largest known sale of Belgian steel to the Orbit this year. Belgium demanded advance payment because of Rumania's adverse trade balance.
Rumania reportedly began sending wheat to the Netherlands for Belgian francs in order to obtain the necessary exchange for this purchase.

2. Poland considers arranging bunkering facilities in India:
The Polish Government in early October was considering to import bunker fuel into India for Polish vessels,

Comment: The Polish Ocean Lines, which carries a large portion of the Orbit's goods to China, needs bunkering facilities along the route from Poland to the Far East. Such facilities have been available at Djibouti but may be withdrawn shortly.

The Polish Government will find it difficult to obtain bunker fuel in India since Western companies, which control the handling of petroleum supplies, have agreed to refuse to bunker Orbit vessels trading with Communist China. Indian petroleum companies so far have been unable to secure tankers to bring in oil.
