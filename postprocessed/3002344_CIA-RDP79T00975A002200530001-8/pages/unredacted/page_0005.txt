2. ISRAEL MAY FAVOR SYRIAN ADHERENCE TO BAGHDAD
PACT

Israel would now like to see Syria join
the Turkish-Iraqi pact;

Israel now feels the Baghdad pact
is becoming "more Western" and "less Arab" and, therefore, the threat to Israel is decreasing.

the pact
might be used as an instrument for dividing the Arab League.

Comment
Israel initially strongly opposed the
Turkish-Iraqi pact, signed on 24 February 1955, and said it was directed against Israel. In
reappraising its approach to Middle Eastern problems,
Israel is now apparently exploring ways and means of dividing the Arab world for its own protection.
