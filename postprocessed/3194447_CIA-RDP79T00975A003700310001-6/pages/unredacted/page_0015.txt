Venezuelan Communists Claim Cabinet Penetration

Venezuelan Communist Secretary General Jesus Faria and his aide Gustavo Machado are en route to Moscow, where they plan to report on favorable conditions for the party in Venezuela. They will tell Moscow that three Venezuelan cabinet ministers are dues-paying Communists and that the party has close contacts with two other ministers.

While the two leaders may be somewhat boastful in talking to foreign Communists, the party has improved its position in recent months. According to the new election law promulgated on 23 May, all political parties previously organized in accordance with legal requirements are considered legal--a provision which gives the Venezuelan Communist party legal status to all intents and purposes, according to the US Embassy in Caracas.

Recently appointed Finance Minister Mayobre was reported active in Communist party affairs in the 1930's; Foreign Minister de Sola was legal counsel for the Czech Legation in 1950 and deposited a Czech bank draft in 1954; Education Minister Pizani has no known political party affiliation but is acceptable to the leftist-inclined students. The political affiliation of Interior Minister Numa Quevedo is not known.

The two leaders plan to report to Moscow on the possibilities of restoring diplomatic relations and expanding trade with Venezuela, which broke relations with the USSR in 1952. Trade with the USSR made up less than 2 percent of total Venezuelan trade in 1957.
