EASTERN EUROPE

3. Preliminary Yugoslav-Soviet trade talks unproductive:

Only one meeting has been held so far between the Soviet trade delegation and Yugoslav officials in Belgrade, and it accomplished nothing, according to American embassy there. The USSR reportedly is prepared to offer 100,000 tons of wheat, and is asking in return lead, tin and copper, which the Yugoslavs have refused to supply.

Comment: The Yugoslav attitude in these negotiations, which began two weeks ago, supports Tito's public statement on 19 September that Yugoslavia would welcome any Orbit gestures toward "normalization" but that it would always receive them "with a grain of salt" and not allow them to change Yugoslavia's relations with the West.
