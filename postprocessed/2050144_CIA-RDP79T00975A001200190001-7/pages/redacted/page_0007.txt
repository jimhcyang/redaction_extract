8. Czechoslovak and Rumanian leaders rumored to be in Moscow:

Czechoslovak president Zapotocky and Rumanian premier Gheorghiu-Dej are rumored to be in Moscow, according to the American diplomatic missions in Prague and Bucharest. Czech deputy premier minister Dolansky and minister of national defense Cepicka accompanied Zapotocky, according to rumors circulating in Prague. The American legation in Bucharest reported on 12 July that Gheorghiu-Dej had been absent from his house for two weeks, and that the premier left Rumania for Moscow on 3 July.

Comment: These Satellite leaders may have been summoned to Moscow to receive instructions on the implementation of the current "liberal" Orbit policy in their respective countries and to be briefed on the Beria purge. Both Zapotocky and Gheorghiu-Dej have been proponents of all-out industrialization programs at the expense of labor.

The ministerial reorganization and liberalization policy announced in Hungary on 4 July followed the return from Moscow on 19 June of party leader Rakosi and then minister of war Farkas.
