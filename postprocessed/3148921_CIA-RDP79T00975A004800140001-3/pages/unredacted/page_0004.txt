CENTRAL INTELLIGENCE BULLETIN

17 November 1959

DAILY BRIEF

I THE COMMUNIST BLOC

II ASIA-AFRICA

UAR-Iraq. UAR clandestine activities in Iraq are continuing. According to UAR messages, preparations are being made to deliver additional arms to Iraqi nationalists in Baghdad. Two Iraqis believed to have been involved in the assassination attempt against Qasim were scheduled to arrive in Cairo on 13 November, and the UAR's agent who maintains contact with Iraqi conspirators in Baghdad is en route to Damascus. In Baghdad, UAR contact with members of the group which attempted the assassination has resumed despite tight surveillance by Iraqi security authorities.

Laos - Communist China: The Lao Army is preparing to counterattack a post near the China border in Phong Saly Province which was lost last week to Communist insurgents. The China border area, in contrast to the border with North Vietnam, has previously been quiet. There have been unconfirmed reports the mixed insurgent force which took the post contained Chinese Communist elements.

The arrival of UN official Sakari Tuomioja, a Finnish economist, in Laos on 16 November for a four-week economic and political survey will give UN Secretary General Hammarskjold time to find another more permanent representative and try to place him in position without the active opposition of the USSR.
