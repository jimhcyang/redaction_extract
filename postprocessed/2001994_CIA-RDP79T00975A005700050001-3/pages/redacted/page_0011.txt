Although National Assembly President Charles Mutaka announced that he is constitutionally entitled to assume temporary command of the government when the president's position is vacated during an assembly session, UN representatives and foreign consuls summoned to a meeting by the Council of Ministers on 29 April were informed that Tshombe was considered absent for a conference and that no governmental reorganization was envisaged.

Monetary problems in Stanleyville, which appeared serious in February, are now reported partially resolved. Soldiers and noncommissioned officers are paid regularly and fully; army officers and civil servants up to 75 percent. Reports are contradictory regarding the source of these funds.
