THE ARAB-ISRAELI SITUATION
(Information as of 1700, 29 July)

King Hussain told the American ambassador in Amman on 27 July that Jordan was continuing to do everything in its power to bring the "present cycle of incidents" to an end. The king said he regretted the wounding of UN observers by Jordanian villagers on 25 July but did not find it surprising since the villagers had been living under tension for years.

As a result of a ground reconnaissance he conducted in the Suez Canal zone on 25 and 26 July, the American military attaché in Cairo doubts that four infantry battalions, a medium tank regiment and a heavy tank unit have been moved into the zone. He believes elements of each may be there, but he saw only a small number of infantry and a total of 23 assorted tanks at various camps.
