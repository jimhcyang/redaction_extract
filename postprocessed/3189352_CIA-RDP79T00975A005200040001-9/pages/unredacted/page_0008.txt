US RB-47 Aircraft Missing Over Barents Sea

A US RB-47 which flew into the Barents Sea area on 1 July on a routine reconnaissance flight that was to have lasted approximately four hours failed to return from its mission.

the RB-47 was tracked until approximately 1533Z (1133 EDT) to an area midway between the Kola Peninsula and the southwest tip of Novaya Zemlya.

The cause of the plane's failure to return is unknown. There is no conclusive evidence that a hostile intercept was accomplished, although at least six Soviet interceptors were scrambled during the period that the mission aircraft was under Soviet radar surveillance. Two Soviet fighters from a Kola Peninsula base made an apparent visual identification pass at approximately 1500Z

the US aircraft did not approach closer than 30 nautical miles to Soviet territory, maintaining an average distance of 50 to 60 nautical miles from the Kola Peninsula at an altitude of more than 32,000 feet.

Press reports indicate that Soviet ships joined in the American-Norwegian air search for the missing aircraft. There is no firm communications intelligence reflecting Soviet air search activity.
