EASTERN EUROPE

6. Polish and Czechoslovak shortages delayed 1952 trade agreement:
Significant shortages of industrial goods and raw materials impeded negotiations between Poland and Czechoslovakia for the 1952 trade agreement which was finally signed on 29 February.

The Czechs stated that they were having considerable difficulty persuading the Poles to deliver non-ferrous metals to be processed by Czechoslovak industry for Poland. Although Czechoslovakia had the plant capacity, it did not have the raw materials to satisfy Polish requirements.
