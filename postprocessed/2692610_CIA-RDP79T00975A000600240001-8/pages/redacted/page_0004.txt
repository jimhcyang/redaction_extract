3. Pakistanis reported "not too happy" over their relations with Peiping:

Analysis of bi-weekly secret reports submitted by the Pakistani Embassy in Peiping during 1951 has convinced American Embassy officials in Karachi that the Pakistanis are "not too happy" over their relations with the Chinese Communists.

Pakistani representatives in Peiping reported that the Chinese Communist leadership is "arrogant and fanatic" and that the Chinese people are "living under fear." Anti-foreign feeling on the part of the Chinese Communists was said to be "incredible," and the Pakistani Charge concluded that "we will have to write off the Chinese as far as friendship is concerned."

Comment: These reports confirm earlier indications that Pakistani officials do not share Indian Ambassador Panikkar's view that the Chinese Communists are primarily nationalists who may be influenced by a conciliatory policy.
