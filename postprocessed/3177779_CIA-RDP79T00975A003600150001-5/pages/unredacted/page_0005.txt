Taiwan - Japan: Chiang Kai-shek emphatically stated on 16 March to Ambassador MacArthur, then in Taipei, that he would sever relations with Japan if Prime Minister Kishi allowed the Chinese Communists to fly the flag over their trade mission premises in Tokyo. The Chinese Nationalists have already broken off trade negotiations with the Japanese. Previously, they had indicated they would confine diplomatic action to withdrawal of their ambassador to Tokyo.
