4. Chou En-lai's intercession speeds Sino-Burmese trade negotiations:

Comment: China is a major exporter of rice and its willingness to buy Burmese rice for resale at a loss demonstrates its political motive in these negotiations.

Chou's concessions to Burma would appear to be part of Peiping's current campaign to cultivate the Colombo powers. He also reportedly interceded to expedite the rubber-rice negotiations concluded with Ceylon on 6 October. In the current talks, Burma has offered to sell 200,000 tons of rice, some of which Peiping may buy and exchange for Ceylonese rubber.
