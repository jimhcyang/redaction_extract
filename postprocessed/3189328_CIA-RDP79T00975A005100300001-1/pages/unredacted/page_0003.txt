4 JUNE 1960

I. THE COMMUNIST BLOC
Comment on Khrushchev's disarmament proposals.
Soviet party presidium may be dealing with controversial issue.
Khrushchev accepts invitation to visit Cuba; no date set.

II. ASIA-AFRICA
Japan--Major newspapers warn that violence in today's strikes will lessen public sympathy for protests against Kishi and US-Japanese security treaty.
Turkey--Increasing evidence of illegal activities by former regime inducing new government to broaden arrests and investigations of former officials.
Sudan--Deputy commander in chief reported planning to take over government; plan is said to have Prime Minister Abboud's concurrence.
Congo--African leader Lumumba demands he be appointed premier.

III. THE WEST
Castro regime propagandizing charges that Nicaragua's and Guatemala's suspension of diplomatic ties with Cuba is part of US plot.
Venezuelan President wants Cuban President to postpone visit.
Movement of Chinese Communist fighters and standdown of North Korean Air Force.
