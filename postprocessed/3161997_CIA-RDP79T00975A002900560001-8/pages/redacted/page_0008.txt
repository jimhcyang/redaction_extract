5. ALBANIAN CHIEF RENEWS 1948 CHARGES
AGAINST TITO

The report of Albanian party chief Hoxha to a plenary session of the central committee on 13 February renews virtually all of the accusations leveled against Yugoslavia in 1948 and indicates Moscow may now have removed any restraints it had put on Satellite anti-Tito moves. Hoxha explicitly stated that the 1948 charges were valid. He accused Belgrade of working against Albania with all the means at its disposal during the 1948-1953 period, and also accused the Tito regime of mistreating the Albanian minority in the Yugoslav Kosovo region. He equated Yugoslav ideological views with practically all the Communist heresies of the past.

This is the most violent official statement against Yugoslavia by a Soviet bloc state since Khrushchev's visit to Belgrade in 1955.
