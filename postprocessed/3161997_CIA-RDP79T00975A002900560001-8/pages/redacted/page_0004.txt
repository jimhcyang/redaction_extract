1. BEN-GURION ASKS FOR UN COMMITTEE TO SETTLE
GAZA AND SHARM AL-SHAIKH QUESTIONS

Prime Minister Ben-Gurion of Israel has requested a postponement of UN discussions and the formation of a committee of a "few impartial states" to go to Israel and, "if necessary," also to Egypt in order to try to reach a settlement on Sharm al-Shaikh and the Gaza strip.

Ben-Gurion said "withdrawal under present circumstances would spell disaster for us." He said sanctions by the UN, supported by the US, to compel withdrawal would be an historic injustice and "what may be a fatal blow" would have been struck at the moral foundations of the UN.

Comment
Ben-Gurion is probably attempting to promote delays in the belief that the longer a vote on sanctions against Israel is postponed, the fewer supporters such a move is likely to find. The Israeli prime minister's "eleventh hour" appeal contains no indications of willingness to compromise on any significant point.
