LATIN AMERICA

5. Liberal-Reformist coalition may provoke coup by ex-dictator in Honduras:

The presidential candidates of the Reformist and Liberal Parties in Honduras plan to resign and unite behind Foreign Minister Valenzuela as a compromise candidate against ex-dictator Carias of the Nationalist Party in the 10 October elections, in El Salvador. If this plan materializes Carias will probably seize the government.

Comment: None of the three present presidential candidates is believed capable of securing the necessary majority of votes in a free election. The formation of a Liberal-Reformist coalition might well cause Carias, to win control of the government, to attempt a coup. It is estimated that the Nationalist and Reformist Parties have about equal strength in the politically conscious army.
