3. ADENAUER-SMIRNOV TALKS TO BE RESUMED

Comment on:

Chancellor Adenauer plans to continue talks with the Soviet Union to "review and clarify" a whole complex of questions, according to Adenauer's press chief and close adviser, Felix von Eckardt. Soviet Ambassador Smirnov is reported to have canceled vacation plans in order to be available for a resumption of talks which were interrupted on 13 December. Adenauer has publicly ruled out the idea of an atom-free zone proposed by Bulganin and Polish Foreign Minister Rapacki.

By straddling at Paris the question of accepting IRBM bases, Adenauer has reserved a bargaining point for discussion with Smirnov, while leaving the door open for German acceptance if the NATO military experts make what Adenauer considers a "convincing case" for the necessity of stationing missiles on German territory. From the standpoint of German public opinion, Adenauer would be more justified in accepting such weapons if Moscow failed to provide evidence of a new approach to unification.
