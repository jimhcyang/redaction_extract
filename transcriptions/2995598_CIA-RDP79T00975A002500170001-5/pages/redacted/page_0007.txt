THE ARAB-ISRAELI SITUATION
(Information as of 1700, 12 April)

UN secretary general Hammarskjold reported to the Security Council on 12 April that both Israel and Egypt have agreed to comply with Article II of the General Armistice Agreement, which bars warlike or hostile acts. However, both reserved their right to act in self-defense.

Israeli officials, when notified of Egyptian prime minister Nasr's agreement, stated that Nasr's assurances were "utterly worthless and false," as Arab terrorist activities were continuing in Israel at that very moment.

The American army attaché in Tel Aviv reported that as of early 12 April there were preliminary indications of a "heavy" Israeli mobilization. Jordan also has moved all Arab Legion combat units into West Jordan except for a regiment or two.
