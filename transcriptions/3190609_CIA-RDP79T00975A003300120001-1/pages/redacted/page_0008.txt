5. GREEK FOREIGN MINISTER PROPOSES COMMONWEALTH
STATUS FOR CYPRUS

Greek Foreign Minister Averoff suggested
to NATO Secretary General Spaak on 11 Sep-
tember as a possible solution of the Cyprus
dispute that the island become an independent
member of the British Commonwealth for 20 years, after which
time its status might be reconsidered. This plan has some chance
of being accepted, although the Turks would probably resist it as
merely a first step toward enosis. Athens may be proposing this
solution to avoid a UN resolution that might preclude eventual
union of the island with Greece.

Athens now feels the Cyprus dispute has
weakened the eastern flank of NATO's defenses and sees the
need of improving relations with Britain and Turkey. The Greek
government may also fear an adverse effect on its domestic sup-
port unless some progress is made toward a settlement of the
Cyprus dilemma during the forthcoming session of the UN Gen-
eral Assembly.

Implementation of the commonwealth proposal
would offer a chance for passions to cool, permit British reten-
tion of base rights, and allow the British to keep the pro-Commu-
nist elements on the island under control.
