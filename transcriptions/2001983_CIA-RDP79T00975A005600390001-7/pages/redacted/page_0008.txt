Pyongyang, however, has been under pressure to resume the program both from the Japanese, who want to reduce the size of the Korean community in Japan, and from its own front group in Japan which has warned of a major prestige loss if repatriation was not resumed. About 10 percent of the 600,000 Korean residents in Japan have gone to North Korea since the program was initiated in December 1959.
