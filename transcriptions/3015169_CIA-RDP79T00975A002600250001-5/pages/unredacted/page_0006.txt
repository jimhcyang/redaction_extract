3. BURMESE PREMIER MAY ATTEND NEHRU-TITO-NASR
MEETING IN BELGRADE

Comment

Nehru, Tito and Nasr are unlikely to want to increase the number of participants at their conference, particularly with a leader considered of lesser stature. It may be difficult, however, for them to refuse to invite Ba Swe. The fact that the Burmese ambassador made his recommendation would indicate that the response of the Yugoslavs in Moscow did not give the impression they were opposed to Ba Swe's attendance.

Although Ba Swe would probably be reluctant, for domestic political reasons, to leave Burma at this time, he may decide that by going to Belgrade he would gain prestige, which would outweigh the disadvantages of his being away from Rangoon.
