5. IRAQ AND TURKEY REPORTED PLANNING COUP
IN SYRIA

Iraq, with the concurrence of Turkey, is developing plans for a coup in Syria to be led by General Adib Shishakli, dictator of Syria from 1950 to 1954,

Some of the local army cliques would probably support the return of Shishakli, but unless such action were well supported from the outside and carefully executed, a counter-coup with Egyptian support would probably be precipitated.

Britain would be inclined to support action in Syria which might expand Iraqi influence. France, on the other hand, has been consistently fearful that Iraq might come to dominate or absorb Syria.
