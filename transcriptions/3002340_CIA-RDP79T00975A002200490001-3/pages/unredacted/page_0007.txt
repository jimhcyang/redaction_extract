3. RHEE ASKS DEFENSE CHIEFS FOR PLANS TO RECOVER
TERRITORY SOUTH OF 38TH PARALLEL

General Yi Hyong-kun, chairman of
the South Korean Joint Chiefs of Staff,
has told the American army attache
that President Rhee recently held a
meeting of his top defense officials to
demand again that the Communist-held areas south of the
38th parallel be retaken by force. When advised that
such action was impracticable, Rhee stated "nothing is
impossible" and requested that plans be submitted to him
within a few days.

The attache comments that another
refusal by South Korean military leaders to support the
use of force may lead Rhee "to fabricate incidents to provoke hostilities or replace the military leaders involved."

Comment

Rhee has the capability to provoke
incidents with small unconventional
forces. His immediate interest, however, is probably
less in recovering the "lost territory" than in assuring
the continued presence of American troops, maintaining
the atmosphere of crisis which facilitates his exercise
of authority, and possibly distracting attention from the
dispute over taxation of foreigners.
