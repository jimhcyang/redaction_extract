1. USSR MAKES MAJOR ADVANCE IN PHYSICS
RESEARCH

Revelation by V. I. Veksler, a top Soviet physicist at the Geneva conference on peaceful uses of atomic energy, that the Soviet Union will soon complete new equipment for high-energy nuclear physics research indicates that the USSR will soon be in a pre-eminent position in this field. Soviet scientists will thus be enabled to perform experiments in high-energy nuclear physics which cannot now be performed in the West.

The USSR, according to the physicist, is about to complete a 10 billion electron volt (Bev) proton synchrotron (a particle accelerator) and is planning construction of a 50- to 100-Bev accelerator. The Soviet Union recently also disclosed existence of a 680 million electron volt (Mev) synchrocyclotron, another type accelerator--the highest energy synchrocyclotron in existence.

The United States, long a leader in this field, possesses two proton synchrotrons of three and six Bev respectively. While 25- and 30-Bev units are being planned in the United States and Western Europe, none of these will be available for at least five years.
