According to Gruber, Soviet ambassador Malik in London recently evaded a direct reply to his question as to the possibility that an exchange of notes would advance the treaty. Malik reportedly remarked, "Why not try it and see?"

Comment: Disputes among the Western powers over the text have held up both official statements and a new note to Moscow on the treaty since 27 May, when the Russians failed to attend the scheduled four-power meeting. At that time, the Soviet Union invited the West to examine the treaty question via "diplomatic means."
