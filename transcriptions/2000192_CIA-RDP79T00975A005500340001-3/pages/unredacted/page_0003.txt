10 February 1961
CENTRAL INTELLIGENCE BULLETIN

CONTENTS

1. USSR: Moscow may be considering release of U-2 pilot Powers. (Page i)

2. USSR - Communist China: Annual trade talks begin in Peiping; Sino-Soviet trade expected to drop. (Page ii)

3. USSR-Indonesia: Earlier reports of Soviet agreement to provide Djakarta with TU-16 jet bombers and MIG-21 jet fighters now confirmed. (Page ii)

4. France-Algeria: De Gaulle to meet Bourguiba for preparatory talks on Algeria. (Page iii)

5. Congo: Ghana now supports disarmament of all forces in Congo except UN troops. (Page iii)

6. Portugal: Riots of 4-5 February in Angola have aroused further criticism of Salazar regime. (Page iv)

7. USSR-France: Moscow protests intercept of Brezhnev's plane by French aircraft off Algiers. (Page iv)
