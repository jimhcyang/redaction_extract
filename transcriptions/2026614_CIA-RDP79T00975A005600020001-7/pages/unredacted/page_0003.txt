2 March 1961

CENTRAL INTELLIGENCE BULLETIN

CONTENTS

1. Congo: Luluabourg under UN control; Gizenga repeats demands for aid from UAR and bloc. (Page 1)

2. India-Congo: Nehru warns Khrushchev solution to Congo problem impossible unless the issues of Hammarskjold's status and the Congo are separated. (Page i)

3. Laos: Chiang Kai-shek orders rapid withdrawal of irregulars from Burma-Laos border area. (Page ii)

4. France: New nuclear test will coincide with resumed US-UK-USSR test ban talks in Geneva. (Page iii)

5. Communist China: Peiping revives "100 flowers" concept; calls for limited increase in scientific freedom of expression. (Page iii)

6. Brazil: Yugoslav government accepts invitation for Tito to visit Brazil; suggests August as date. (Page iv)

7. Ecuador: Foreign Minister Chiriboga threatens to resign; opposes President Velasco's increasingly favorable policy toward Cuba. (Page iv)

8. Watch Committee conclusions. (Page iv)
