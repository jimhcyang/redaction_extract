Apparently making a virtue of necessity, the regime has restored all civil and ecclesiastical rights to Cardinal Mindszenty, who had already been freed by the rebels. All proceedings and allegations against him were branded false. In addition, Nagy continues to shuffle government figures. Hungary's chief delegate to the UN, Peter Kos, was recalled on 30 October; Laszlo Hay, president of the National Bank, was fired, and Prosecutor General Gyorgy Non--allegedly guilty of criminal activity in the past few days--was ousted. Nagy may also yield to popular demands for the removal of Ferenc Muennich, newly appointed minister of interior.
