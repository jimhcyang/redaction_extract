9. BONN CONSIDERING EARLIER ELECTIONS BECAUSE OF CHRISTIAN DEMOCRATS' GROWING POPULARITY

The election for the West German Bundestag may be held in July rather than on 15 September, according to Christian Democratic Union (CDU) floor leader Rasner. He told the American embassy in Bonn that a continuation of the rise in CDU popularity in the next public opinion poll would favor the early date. The final decision will be made by Chancellor Adenauer on his return from his vacation about 17 March.

Comment
The Federal Republic's constitution empowers the government to hold these elections on any Sunday or holiday between 9 July and 9 October.

The Social Democratic Party (SPD) registered gains in opinion polls and local elections last fall, but has now fallen behind the CDU. SPD leaders fear the government's social legislation and Strauss' modified rearmament program will leave the party no distinctive policy with which to appeal to the voters.
