1. SYRIA REQUESTS VISIT BY SOVIET FLEET TO COUNTER WESTERN MOVES

Syria ordered its military attache in Moscow to request that the "Russian fleet" immediately pay a "friendly visit" to the Syrian port of Latakia "because it would be appreciated by the Syrian people and because it would strengthen Syria's position."

Comment
The Soviet Union may accept the Syrian invitation as an opportunity for making a gesture of support for Syria. A Soviet Baltic Fleet cruiser and a destroyer are now in the Mediterranean for a scheduled goodwill visit to Yugoslavia. These units could be sent on to Latakia. The size of the visiting group would be of little importance either to the Syrians or the Soviet Union, since the significance of such a visit would lie in the gesture itself.
