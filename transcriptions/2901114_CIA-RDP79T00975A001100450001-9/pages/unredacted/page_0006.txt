NEAR EAST - AFRICA

6. Egyptian army supplies of ammunition and gasoline limited:

The Egyptian Ministry of War has been informed by its German military advisers that the army has enough ammunition for a maximum of two weeks of sustained combat and, because of lack of storage facilities, only one week's supply of gasoline. Moreover, there are no immediate sources of additional ammunition supplies.

Egyptians are now allegedly training for underwater demolition activity. German ordnance experts are working on a frog mine and plan to begin limited production about 1 June.

Comment: All evidence indicates that the Egyptian army, despite Nagib's reorganization, does not pose a threat to British forces in Egypt. The threat of guerrilla and mob activity, however, remains serious.

WESTERN EUROPE

7. West German populace seen standing firm against Moscow's unity overtures:

According to American officials in Bonn, West German public opinion would support an Allied rejection of any Moscow offer for a neutralized unified Germany. These American officials declare that in the past year the West German populace has become convinced of the importance of ignoring Soviet unity offers which would leave Germany neutralized without general European disarmament. Even the opposition Social Democrats now admit that Germany must be armed.
