On 31 March, immediately following alleged attacks by natives on isolated posts in northern Angola, the Portuguese Government announced plans for creating in its African provinces a civil defense organization, comprising a corps of Portuguese volunteers in each province under the direction of the governor general and intended to help maintain order.

In Lisbon on 4 April a Portuguese Government spokesman used a NATO anniversary celebration to renew attacks on US policies in Africa, declaring that the US desire to maintain an alliance in Europe was inconsistent with US policy in Africa hostile to Portugal, particularly when Portugal's very existence in the world community depended on its survival in Africa.
