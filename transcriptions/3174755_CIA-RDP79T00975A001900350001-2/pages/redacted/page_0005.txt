WESTERN EUROPE

7. West German Socialists plan delaying tactics on defense legislation:

The West German Social Democratic Party will attempt to delay passage of defense legislation necessary to implement the Paris treaties "as long as possible," party leaders told American representatives in Bonn on 23 February.

The Social Democrats will press for an East-West conference on German unity prior to the passage of such legislation. They believe that Moscow will be willing to hold a conference on German unity following ratification of the Paris treaties, and they want West German participation in any talks, even if Moscow insists that East Germany also participate.

Comment: Although Chancellor Adenauer will probably oppose any delay in enactment of defense legislation, many of his supporters would probably favor deferring action until the West has tried to get Moscow into a meeting on Germany.

This is the first time any major West German party has indicated it would accept participation of the East German government in unity negotiations. The Social Democrats have heretofore maintained that Moscow would not negotiate on unity following ratification of the Paris accords.
