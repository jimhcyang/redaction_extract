*South Vietnam: Persistent rumors have been received of possible Communist violence in South Vietnam to occur either on 1 May or within a week of that date, possibly as the start of an intensified terrorism campaign. One report says the American, French, and Chinese Nationalist embassies will be targets. There is as yet no confirmation of these rumors, but South Vietnamese security authorities are taking additional precautions.

South Korea: President Rhee's action in closing down the pro-opposition newspaper Kyonhyang Sinmun, second largest in South Korea, for "consistently attacking government policy" appears to be another in a series of repressive measures taken recently to assure his re-election next year.

Jordan: Premier Rifai has decided that he must seek removal of Jordan's army chief of staff, Major General Sadiq Shara, whom he has supported against charges of plotting against the regime. The premier now believes that the evidence against Shara is convincing. Rifai apparently hopes by reversing his position to prevent his own enemies from using the Shara affair to oust him. If King Husayn wishes him to continue as prime minister, Rifai proposes to resign in late May and form a new government.

Tunisia-France: Incidents along the Algerian-Tunisian border, involving violations of Tunisian territory by French forces based in Algeria, have recently increased. This situation, attributable in part to more vigorous French activity on the Algerian-Tunisian frontier, is causing mounting uneasiness among Tunisian officials. President Bourguiba has made a new urgent request for the expeditious shipment of arms and military vehicles from the US. (Page 4)
