7 February 1961
CENTRAL INTELLIGENCE BULLETIN

CONTENTS

1. USSR: Criticism in Soviet press of certain passages in President's State of the Union message represents no significant departure from Soviet propaganda line since the inauguration. (Page 1)

2. USSR: Assignment of presidium member Averky Aristov as ambassador to Poland appears to be a demotion. (Page 1)

3. Laos: Government forces are preparing to put into effect plans for recapture of Plaine des Jarres. (Page 1)

4. Congo: Britain, France, and Belgium now express reservations concerning the neutralization proposals. (Page 1)

5. Burma: General Ne Win has apparently staved off a potential challenge to his control of the army. (Page 1)

6. Iran: The Shah has reportedly issued new instructions to his prime minister not to be "totally antagonistic" toward the USSR. (Page 1)

7. Angola: Portuguese authorities can at present probably contain any further disorders, but African nationalist strength is expected to increase. (Page 1)

8. Western Europe: The Common Market leaders meet on 10 February to discuss confederation. (Page 1)

9. Brazil: Quadros orders establishment of diplomatic relations with Hungary, Rumania, and Bulgaria, and calls for new evaluation of Brazil's relations with Taiwan. (Page 1)

10. Colombia: Pressure from right-wing political groups to break relations with Cuba. (Page 1)
