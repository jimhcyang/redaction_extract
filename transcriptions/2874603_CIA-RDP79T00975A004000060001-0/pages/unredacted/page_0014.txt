Afghan Political Developments

Afghan Prime Minister Daud may be considering a number of high-level personnel shifts within his government, possibly involving some cabinet ministers and some intermediate-level officials. Among the latter, the mayor of Kabul and the governors of Maimana and Kandahar provinces, who are followers of Daud, may be slated for promotion.

The Afghan royal family, probably shaken by the Iraqi coup, may favor some gradual changes in the structure of the government.

A rumor that Daud intends to abolish the monarchy and declare a republic with himself as president is unsubstantiated. Daud seems to have no compelling reason at this time for breaking up the ruling triumvirate, which includes the King, Foreign Minister Naim, and himself. Daud is firmly in control of the government and does not consider Afghanistan ready for extensive democratic reforms. Abolition of the monarchy might create more popular unrest than presently exists, and Daud seems more likely to continue attempts to achieve economic and social modernization by gradual means.
