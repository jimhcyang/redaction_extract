GENERAL
1. Balkan Pact powers to begin military talks on 24 March:
Comment: This, the fourth tripartite military conference, originally scheduled for February, was postponed at Turkey's request after high-level Turkish military representatives had become suspicious of Yugoslav motives and sincerity. The Turkish distrust was aroused by a report alleging that a secret Yugoslav mission had gone to Moscow, and by Yugoslav unwillingness to make certain intelligence on the Soviet army available to Turkey.

The scheduling of the forthcoming meeting indicates that tripartite military cooperation is continuing and that Turkish suspicions may be somewhat allayed.

While all three Balkan Pact powers apparently desire to give each other firm defense commitments, Greece and Turkey continue to be restrained by their NATO obligations.
