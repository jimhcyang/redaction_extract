II. ASIA-AFRICA

Indonesia: Forces of the central government are believed to hold the oil centers of Central Sumatra as well as Medan in North Sumatra. While the dissidents claim they will receive support from Atjeh, no Atjehnese units are known to have joined the dissident forces. In South Sumatra, Lt. Col. Barlian is said to have reaffirmed his neutral posture. There is no further available evidence about the planned central government landings on the west coast of Sumatra. Col. Kawilarang, recently military attache in Washington, is en route to Menado, North Celebes, to lend his prestige to the dissident cause.
