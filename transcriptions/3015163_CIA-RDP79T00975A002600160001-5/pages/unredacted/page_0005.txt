2. BRITAIN FAVORS EVENTUAL NEUTRALIZATION OF LAOS

The British Foreign Office has expressed
the view to the American embassy in
London that Laos should in the long run
be neutralized, since it would probably
be "more of a liability than an asset as an ally!" The Foreign
Office did not seem particularly concerned over the projected
visit of Laotian premier Souvanna to Peiping, but was concerned that Souvanna should not throw away his bargaining
position there.

Comment

Britain has never excluded the expedient
of neutralizing Laos, if necessary, to remove a
possible source of conflict with the Chinese Communists.

Souvanna and other Laotian leaders, feeling uncertain of Western military support in case of an emergency, are already tempted to accept Communist inducements
to become neutral. The British attitude, if conveyed to the
Laotians at this time, would weaken their determination to
remain firm in dealing with the Chinese Communists and might
well be decisive in swinging Laos to neutralism.
