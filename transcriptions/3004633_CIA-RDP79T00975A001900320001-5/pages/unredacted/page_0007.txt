Comment: The Dhahran agreement runs until June 1956 and is subject to renewal. However, Faisal has been quoted as having told the Arab ministers in Cairo that "we can terminate it at any time." 8. Comment on the struggle for control of Syria:

The domestic situation in Syria has been deteriorating rapidly. The leftist-tinged Asali government and its conservative foes each suspects the other of planning to stage a military coup with foreign assistance.

Asali's government--which is hostile to Iraq, Britain, and the United States--believes that the Iraqi-Turkish pact is an Iraqi device for effecting a political union with Syria.

Syrian conservatives, who tend to favor Iraq, Britain, and the United States, believe that France is helping Shuqayr plan an open seizure of power. Syrians have expressed alarm to American officials over the leftist, anti-Western orientation of Syrian foreign minister Azm and his extreme Socialist and Communist supporters.
