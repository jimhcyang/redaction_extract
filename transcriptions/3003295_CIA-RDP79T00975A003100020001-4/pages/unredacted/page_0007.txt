3. BRITISH SEEN FAVORABLE TO NATO COMMON STOCKPILE OF NUCLEAR WEAPONS

Britain might support the French suggestion for a NATO "common stockpile" of nuclear weapons under SACEUR control if the plan did not interfere with the favored British position in obtaining US nuclear components, in the view of the American embassy in London. Senior British officials, in their anxiety to deter additional countries from producing their own nuclear weapons, had earlier suggested that the United States might prevent this by supplying such weapons.

Comment

Any method of dissuading European nations from producing nuclear weapons would be attractive to London. London's concern is so great that it would probably consider contributing to such a stockpile, or even supplying nuclear weapons directly to France or Germany.
