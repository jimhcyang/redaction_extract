2. POSTELECTION VIOLENCE FEARED IN SOUTH KOREA

Indications of fraud in connection with South Korea's closely contested vice-presidential race may lead to violent demonstrations, according to the American embassy in Seoul. Tension is rising following government efforts to delay the vote count. A crowd of 10,000 has already demonstrated in Taegu.

Numerous reports have reached the embassy concerning violence planned for 19 May in connection with the funeral of the late Democratic presidential candidate, Sin Ik-hui. Chargé Strom observes that the violence may exceed that which followed Sin's death, when police fired into a demonstrating crowd in front of Rhee's residence.

Comment
The action of the government's Central Election Committee in ceasing to issue returns on 17 May has lent credibility to widespread rumors that an effort is being made to fix the vote in order to give victory to Rhee's running mate, Yi Ki-pung. Returns at that time indicated that opposition candidate Chang Myon was leading Yi by about 80,000 votes out of nearly 9,000,000 cast, after at one time enjoying a lead of 415,000 votes.
