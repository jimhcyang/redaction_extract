Several times, even during the past few days, Navarre has told American officials that enemy action has not been such as to prevent him from moving forward on schedule.

His apparent reversal, coming immediately after the big-power decision to meet with representatives of Communist China and "other interested states" may well have a devastating effect on Vietnamese morale, making the development of an effective national army virtually impossible.

Navarre's sudden pessimism apparently reflects the desire of the French government to attain a settlement through the opportunity offered by the Geneva conference.
