FAR EAST

2. Rhee acquiesces to Geneva conference:

President Rhee's reaction to the Berlin arrangements for the Geneva conference was one "more in sorrow than in anger," and he did not express opposition in principle, according to Ambassador Briggs.

The president expressed dismay, however, over the additional delay in the unification of Korea and repeated his long-standing conviction that any conference on Korea will fail. He stated that he would go along with it if his "American friends" insisted.
