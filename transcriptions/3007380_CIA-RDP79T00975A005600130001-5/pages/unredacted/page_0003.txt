15 March 1961
CENTRAL INTELLIGENCE BULLETIN

C O N T E N T S

1. Laos: Situation in Laos as of 0400 EST (Page 1)

2. USSR: Advance in Soviet anti-ballistic missile program. (Page 4)

3. Congo: Hammarskjold worried over possible incidents following arrival of Indian UN contingent in the Congo. (Page 11)

4. Burma: Prime minister tells Khrushchev situation in Congo "calls for greater rather than lesser efforts" by UN. (Page 6)

5. Belgium: Spaak critical of Belgian Government's policies in Congo. (Page iii)

6. Communist China: Oil shipments received from USSR during February; imports from nonbloc sources apparently tapering off. (Page iii)

7. India-Communist China: Minor border incident likely to generate further protests in New Delhi. (Page iv)

8. Morocco: Rabat to receive additional military equipment from USSR. (Page iv)

9. Saudi Arabia: King requests US agreement to abrogation of Dhahran Airfield pact. (Page 9)

10. Argentina: Army chief reported determined to oust President Frondizi within the next 20 days. (Page v)
