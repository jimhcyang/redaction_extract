Foreign Minister Roa, who landed in New York without requesting prior permission to come to the United States, on 11 July requested an urgent meeting of the United Nations Security Council to consider the worsening relations between the United States and Cuba.

Roa informed the UAR ambassador in Havana that Cuba would submit its case to the UN and asked UAR help in getting the support of the governments of Ceylon and Cambodia. Roa added that the Soviet Union had already offered its support.
