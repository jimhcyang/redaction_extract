FAR EAST

1. Comment on Peiping's acknowledgment of flood disaster:

Peiping on 8 August admitted that flood damage this summer amounts to a major disaster. Six percent of China's cultivated land--about 15,000,000 acres--is now said to have been inundated. This land, most of it in the rich rice area of Central China, normally grows over ten percent of China's annual food crops.

The Chinese Communists appear to have been genuinely surprised by the duration and extent of the flood. They may have been lulled in late July by a temporary drop in the Yangtze water level at Hankow. The river started rising again, however, in early August while rainfall continued heavy. By 9 August, the water level at Hankow had risen to a level 40 inches above the previous high mark reached in 1931.

So far, Moscow propaganda has been silent on the flood and no offer of relief is known to have been made by the USSR.
