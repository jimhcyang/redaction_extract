SOUTHEAST ASIA

3. Viet Minh continues large stockpiling program:

Heavy Chinese Communist aid to the Viet Minh continues, according to French information given the American army attaché in Saigon. The rate of aid has been accelerated since the cease-fire on 27 July in an attempt to stockpile supplies before the arrival of international inspection teams. Included in the shipments are large quantities of munitions, artillery pieces, and vehicles.

The army attaché comments that while inspection teams will perhaps be able to control to some extent supplies entering Indochina over main routes, the cessation of illegal traffic is not expected. Materiel currently entering the country is intended not only for resupply but also to equip new units.

Comment: This report apparently confirms the expectation that Chinese aid would continue after the cease-fire at a level comparable to the 3,000-tons-a-month figure reported by the French early this year.
