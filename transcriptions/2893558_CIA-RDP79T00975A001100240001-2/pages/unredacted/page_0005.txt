5. Egyptian Foreign Ministry reaffirms unyielding position on Suez Canal issue:
Egypt is not now and has at no time been contemplating a defense agreement with the West, the Egyptian Foreign Ministry advised its missions abroad.

Moreover, the aim of the forthcoming discussions with Britain is to "liquidate rapidly all stationing of British forces and personnel on Egyptian territory."

The Foreign Ministry believes that both the United States and Britain realize that a strong, "contented" Egypt is necessary for Near Eastern stability, and that a satisfactory solution of the Suez Canal dispute is accordingly imperative. Nevertheless, the Foreign Ministry does not wish to encourage its missions to "overestimate the progress made."
