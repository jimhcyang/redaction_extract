WESTERN EUROPE

6. Comment on announced lifting of restrictions in Austria:

The Kremlin's reported intention to remove all major obstacles to the free movement of persons and goods across the frontiers of the Soviet zone of Austria, as announced by the Austrian government, is in line with other gestures designed to give the appearance of a desire to improve the international atmosphere. Together with the change in the Soviet administrative machinery in Austria, it is probably designed to persuade the Austrians of a new conciliatory Soviet policy and offset the recent Soviet refusal to discuss the Austrian peace treaty.

The USSR has little to lose by the lifting of restrictions in proportion to the propaganda gains which it may anticipate.

The announcement makes no reference to Allied personnel, and presumably restrictions on their movements remain.
