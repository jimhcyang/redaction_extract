1. AIR RECONNAISSANCE FINDS NO CHINESE COMMUNIST  
TROOPS ON PARACELS

Contrary to earlier Vietnamese reports of Chinese Communist troops on Robert Island in the Paracel group, aerial reconnaissance of 9 and 10 June revealed no Communist military activity in the area.

4. MALENKOV REPORTEDLY INVITED TO PARIS

The head of the Eastern European section of the French Foreign Ministry informed American representatives in Paris on 8 June that he is preparing, at the request of Foreign Minister Pineau, an invitation for eight or nine Soviet state planning officials to visit France. He thinks former premier Georgi Malenkov may be invited along with this group.

According to a semiofficial French Press Agency report of 7 June, Malenkov, who is now minister of Electric Power Stations, will soon go to Paris in response to an invitation extended by the French Economic Council delegation which visited the USSR last September. The report said his visit would precede that of Khrushchev and Bulganin.

Comment    Premier Mollet and Pineau have insisted on the importance of increased personal contacts between East and West. No date has been mentioned for the visit either of Malenkov or of the other Soviet leaders.
