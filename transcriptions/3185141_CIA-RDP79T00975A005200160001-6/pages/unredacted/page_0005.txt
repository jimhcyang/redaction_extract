UAR (Syria): The economic situation in Syria is worsening and may become disastrous, according to the American Consulate General in Damascus. Discontent is mounting as a result of the economic deterioration, suspicions that Nasir intends to relegate Syria to the position of a colony, and fears that Cairo will soon act to nationalize Syrian industries and unify Egyptian and Syrian currencies. Open criticism of Nasir is now being heard.

The value of the Syrian pound has gradually declined in recent weeks.

Turkey: The provisional government of Turkey plans to retire many senior officers of the armed forces and has requested US financial support for the program. While reform in Turkey's military personnel management practices is badly needed and should remove much "deadwood," summary retirement of large numbers of senior officers could increase political ferment in the country and result in some damage to the military establishment.
