The government-controlled news media, whose output may be influenced by bloc "advisers" in Guinea, already have embarked on a campaign which appears designed to arouse the masses against the West in general, including the United States.

In Mali, meanwhile, officials are likely to regard the reported arms network as confirmation of Touré's charges against the French. As a result, Senegalese leaders, most of whom have heretofore favored close ties with France after Mali becomes independent, now may be more inclined to yield to influential Soudanese elements who already have a predilection for ties with the bloc.
