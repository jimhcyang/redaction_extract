20 APRIL 1960

I. THE COMMUNIST BLOC

II. ASIA-AFRICA

Kishi planning to push for Diet vote on US-Japan security treaty between 22 and 26 April; demand of opposition Socialists for more time for debate gets some influential press support.

South Korean army chief of staff claims situation now under control in Seoul and other population centers; popular resentment and possibility of further disorders remain.

Senanayake government in Ceylon given only slim chance of surviving test vote in Parliament this week; governor general may order new elections if Senanayake is defeated.

Increased tensions in Turkey expected to follow Menderes government's announcement of three-month ban on all political activity.

Hammarskjold may visit Union of South Africa in June or July; failure of Black Africans to respond to work-stoppage calls indicates temporary drop in their ability to organize campaigns against Union government.

III. THE WEST

Castro, angered by some recent Cuban Supreme Court decisions, may replace court head; other reports indicate Foreign Minister Raul Roa may resign or be "retired" to presidency.
