SOVIET UNION

4. Route of Soviet ships in Far Eastern waters reduces likelihood of escort:

Comment: Although it is still possible that the ships may rendezvous northeast of the Philippines with naval escort craft, the circuitous route used by at least three of them should greatly reduce the need for such protection.

Moscow's third note to the United States on the seizure of the Tuapse, which was delivered on 19 July, was moderate in tone and suggests that the USSR is not planning any drastic steps in this matter pending further Chinese Nationalist action.

Information from Taipei indicated the Nationalists did not plan to release the Tuapse before 24 July, and reports of continuing Nationalist efforts to discover a pretext for confiscating the Soviet vessel suggest that release of the ship may be delayed beyond that date.
