Cuban Arms Purchases

Expanding economic and political ties between Cuba and the UAR may facilitate the Castro government's efforts to obtain military aircraft and increase its already substantial purchases of arms and ammunition.

A recent $6,000,000 barter transaction between the two countries may be used to cover purchase of Czech arms and ammunition. A Cuban arms technician arrived there from Prague on 8 February to make final arrangements on a three-way trade. Under its reported terms, a large part of the Egyptian cotton bartered for Cuban sugar would be sent to Czechoslovakia in payment for arms to be delivered by the Czechs to Castro.

Other widely varying reports continue to be received that Havana is attempting to buy Czech planes, tanks, and arms.
