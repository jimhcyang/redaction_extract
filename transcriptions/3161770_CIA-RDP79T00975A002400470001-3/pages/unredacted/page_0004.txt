1. CHINESE NATIONALIST HARASSMENT OF
MAINLAND REPORTED

A Chinese Nationalist frigate and
minesweeper fired about 100 shells
shortly after midnight on 11 March
at Communist radar and artillery
positions near Tinghai, on the
Peiling Peninsula north of Matsu.

The American Pacific Fleet reports that the Nationalists meanwhile are continuing their "harassing" artillery fire in
the Quemoy area, with the Communists apparently firing only in retaliation.

Comment
The Nationalist navy also shelled Communist positions on the Peiling Peninsula in mid-February in one of its rare attacks on
mainland targets. The Communists have termed such attacks "provocative." Retaliatory action could include
sneak attacks on Nationalist naval patrols in the Matsu
area and heavy artillery fire against the Quemoys.
