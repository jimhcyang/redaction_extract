6. POSSIBLE SOVIET JET HEAVY BOMBER SIGHTED IN FAR EAST

The daytime sighting of a possible Soviet BISON jet heavy bomber in the Far East was reported by the tail gunner of an American reconnaissance B-45 flying approximately 140 nautical miles south of Vladivostok on 9 December. The gunner reported seeing a large aircraft with a swept-wing configuration and a right tip tank. It approached from behind and paced the American aircraft at approximately 38,000 feet for about five minutes at a distance of from 5 to 30 miles, then dropped back and disappeared.

Comment

This is the first indication that there may be a BISON jet heavy bomber in the Soviet Far East. The description of a swept-wing aircraft could also apply to the BADGER (TU-16) jet medium bomber and to the BEAR turbo-prop heavy bomber. Of these three aircraft, only the BISON, however, has a pod on each wing tip which could be described as a "tip tank."
