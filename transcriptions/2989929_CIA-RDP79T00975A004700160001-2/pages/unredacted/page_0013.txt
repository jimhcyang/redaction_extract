UAR Orders Reduction of Military Mission in Yemen

The head of the UAR military mission in Yemen is carrying out orders from Cairo to reduce the size of the mission.
do not reveal how much of a reduction is contemplated or when it will be effected. The mission, which first arrived in 1957, probably numbers around 100 men.

Cairo is undoubtedly aware of the Imam's displeasure over the size of the mission and the fact that it has at times interfered in Yemen's internal affairs.

He suspects that Crown Prince Badr and the UAR are collaborating. Badr used members of the mission against dissident elements responsible for disturbances at Al-Hadaydah and Taiz during the Imam's absence.

the mission has been in contact with and may have given funds to elements probably opposed to the Imam. Upon the Imam's return last month, the head of the mission anticipated the possibility that a reduction would be demanded, but told Cairo he thought it would be necessary to "withdraw only those who are without actual work."
