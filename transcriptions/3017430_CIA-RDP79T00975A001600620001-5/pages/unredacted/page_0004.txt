GENERAL

1. Norway and Denmark protest agreement between Saudi Arabia and Onassis:

Norwegian and Danish ambassadors protesting the Saudi signature of the Onassis tanker agreement.

Comment: Aside from American representations, these are the first formal protests addressed to the Saudi government regarding the deal which gives the Onassis company virtually exclusive rights to transport Saudi Arabian oil. Italy is believed prepared to protest the agreement, and British foreign secretary Eden voiced his concern to the Saudi ambassador in late June. Other European countries with large shipping interests are also disturbed by the effect this agreement will have on world shipping.

These protests are unlikely to deter Saudi Arabia from implementing the Onassis agreement.

2. Indonesia plans to place New Guinea question on UN General Assembly agenda:

Indonesia's permanent delegate to the United Nations informed the American delegation on 3 August of Indonesia's intention to place the New Guinea question on the agenda of the Ninth General Assembly. He intimated he had support from the Asian-African group for including the item on the agenda. The delegate made it clear that raising the question in the General Assembly was a domestic political necessity.

Comment: Since Indonesia gained independence in 1949, President Sukarno and almost all other Indonesian leaders have tried to divert attention from their country's internal problems by inflammatory speeches demanding that the Dutch cede sovereignty over West New Guinea to Indonesia.
