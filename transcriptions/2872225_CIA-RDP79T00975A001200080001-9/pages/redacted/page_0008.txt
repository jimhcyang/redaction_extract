10. Comment on resignation of Finnish cabinet:

The resignation of Premier Kekkonen and his coalition cabinet on 29 June because of the inability of the Social Democrats and Agrarians to agree upon urgent economic reforms will increase Finland's problems.

The economic crisis has developed because rising industrial production costs have priced Finnish goods out of the world market, thus creating a threat of widespread unemployment this winter. Failure to resolve the crisis within the next two to three months would probably result in a Communist gain in the next communal elections on 4 October and weaken Finland's ability to resist possible Soviet overtures to increase the volume of trade exchanges. Approximately one third of Finland's foreign trade probably will be with the Soviet bloc in 1953.
