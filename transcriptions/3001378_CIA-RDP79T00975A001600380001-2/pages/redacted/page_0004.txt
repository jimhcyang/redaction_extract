3. Attack on Hung Yen indicated as enemy consolidates control over southern delta:

The Viet Minh has ordered all civilians in the Hung Yen area, about 30 miles southeast of Hanoi, to evacuate their villages, according to the American army attaché in Saigon (see map, p. 5). He notes that in the past such an order has indicated an impending attack.

The attaché also reports that the 304th Division, now in the Phat Diem-Ninh Binh-Nam Dinh area of the southern delta, is moving northward and spreading out both to the east and west. There is also "an indication" that the 316th Division is assembling near Thanh Hoa preparatory to moving north toward the delta.

Comment: Hung Yen is just inside the southern edge of the new French defense line. An attack against it by units already within the delta would be a step in a movement to seal off communications between Hanoi and Haiphong.

The movement of the 304th and 316th Divisions into the abandoned southern delta area, in order to consolidate enemy control over this zone, has been anticipated.
