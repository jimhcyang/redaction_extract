EASTERN EUROPE

4. Comment on replacement of Hungarian interior minister:

Hungarian Communist Party official Erno Gero's replacement as minister of interior on 6 July is not a demotion.

Gero retains his post as first deputy premier, and is believed still to be the third-ranking Communist leader in Hungary.

The selection of security police chief Laszlo Piros, rather than one of the other three deputies, to replace Gero indicates the regime's concern with internal discipline, which has deteriorated during this period of relaxed political pressure on the population.

Gero is the fourth Hungarian government leader to be released from his duties in recent weeks to assume an unnamed "important assignment." This reshuffling suggests the creation of a supercabinet to deal with Hungary's most pressing "new course" problems.
