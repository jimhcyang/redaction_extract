FAR EAST
3. India proposes peace treaty with Japan:
India has made a direct proposal to the Japanese Government that the state of war between India and Japan be terminated by unilateral declaration of the Indian Government. The declaration would be issued in agreement with the Japanese Government on or about the date of signing of the multilateral treaty. The Japanese Foreign Office advised the Indian representative that it could not, under present regulations, engage in direct discussions, and suggested that an approach be made through the diplomatic section of SCAP.

Comment: India is well aware of the fact that Japan, under present Occupation directives, cannot conduct direct diplomatic relations without approval of SCAP. Consequently, this direct approach may be intended to re-emphasize Indian disapproval of Western controls over Asiatic peoples and also to reassure Japan of India's intentions despite its refusal to attend the San Francisco conference.
