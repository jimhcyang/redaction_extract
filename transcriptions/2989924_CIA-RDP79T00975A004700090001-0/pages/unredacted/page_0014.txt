Chinese Nationalists Planning Additional Support For Kuomintang Irregulars

The Chinese Nationalists are planning to airdrop a large quantity of arms to the Kuomintang irregulars in the Burmese-Thai border area within the next two or three months.

Orders also have been given for the packaging of 400 machine guns and 5,600 rifles for airdrop. The drops are to be made by P4Y aircraft operating from airfields on Taiwan.

The Kuomintang irregulars are short of heavy arms needed for use against Burmese Army strong points. However, Chiang Ching-kuo, the chief of the Chinese Nationalist security services, would like to use some of the irregulars to assist Laos in the fight against Communist invaders in the Laos border area. about 3,000 of them were available for this use, but indicated that the views of the United States would be sought before any decision on action was taken. In addition, Li Mi, the former commander of the irregulars, about 1,000 irregular troops are based along the Mekong River within the borders of Laos, and that these troops are the poorest armed of all the irregular contingents. It is therefore possible that some of the arms listed above may be for the use of these units.

The decision to supply additional arms to the irregulars evidently was made after the return of a high-ranking intelligence official from a visit to the area.
