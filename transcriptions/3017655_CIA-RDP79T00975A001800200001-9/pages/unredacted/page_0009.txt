LATIN AMERICA

Comment on Chilean cabinet crisis:

The entire Chilean cabinet offered their resignations on 17 November, anticipating rejection by congress of the state of siege declared by President Ibanez on 20 September. The only two ministers actually replaced by Ibanez, however, were the ministers of interior and agriculture.

The new interior minister, Arturo Olavarria Bravo, is perhaps the worst enemy of the United States in Chile. He is also a rabid hater of Communism. As minister of the interior, he is next in line for the presidency and commands the national police of some 24,000 men.

Congress' expected rejection of the state of siege would not offset Ibanez' immediate control of the situation; it might result in his dissolving congress and ruling by decree.
