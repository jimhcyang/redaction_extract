3. Foreign shipping in Chinese ports continues at high level:

During May, 60 foreign-registry vessels totaling nearly 300,000 gross tons entered Chinese Communist ports. Of these, half--totaling about 100,000 tons--came from contiguous areas, while of the remaining 30, 21 were of non-Communist European registry, eight were Polish and Russian, and one was Indian.

Direct trade with Japan rose with the arrival of four Japanese vessels which loaded full cargoes of coal at Chinwangtao. Six more Japanese vessels are scheduled to load coal at the same port for delivery to Japan and Pakistan.

Comment: The level of foreign shipping in Chinese ports has been about 50 percent higher in 1953 than in 1952 and reflects a sharp rise in direct trade with both Western and Communist Europe.
