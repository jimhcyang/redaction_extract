8. USSR MAINTAINS PRESSURE FOR RELATIONS
WITH COLOMBIA

Colombia broke relations with the USSR
in 1948 following the Bogota riot in which Communists participated. Over the past year the local Communists, estimated at about 5,000, have apparently become increasingly
involved in the guerrilla unrest which has troubled Colombia
for several years.

Colombia has no diplomatic relations
with any member of the Sino-Soviet bloc but earlier this year
exchanged consuls with Prague.

27 May 56 Current Intelligence Bulletin Page 10
