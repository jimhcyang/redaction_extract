4. SIX SOVIET WARSHIPS ENTER MEDITERRANEAN

American naval sources report that the USSR has notified the Turkish government that six Soviet warships will leave the Black Sea via the Turkish Straits. One Sverdlov-class cruiser and two destroyer escorts will go through the straits on 20 June, and three destroyers will follow on 21 June.

The stated purpose of the movement is "navigational training." No date of return to the Black Sea was specified.

Comment
Scheduling the movement on two successive days complies with the Montreux Convention, which provides that a capital ship may be accompanied by no more than two destroyers. Three Soviet submarines and one minesweeper are already en route to the eastern Mediterranean, having passed through the Straits of Gibraltar on 9 June.

These naval units may rendezvous to conduct maneuvers in the Mediterranean or possibly make goodwill visits.
