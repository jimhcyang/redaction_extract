4. Indonesian Communist Party:

Comment: The growth of Communist influence in Indonesia during the past two years can be attributed primarily to the tolerance of the Ali regime, which was heavily dependent on the party's parliamentary support. The incoming government, supported by the army, is likely to be much less tolerant.

The Communists are capable of harassing the new government to a considerable degree, however, because they control Indonesia's strongest labor and peasant organizations.

EASTERN EUROPE

5. Comment on planned expansion of Yugoslav-Soviet trade:

Yugoslavia announced on 30 July a supplementary trade agreement with the USSR which will increase trade between the two countries in 1955 from $10,000,000 to $16,000,000 each way. This increase will bring Yugoslavia's planned over-all trade with the Soviet bloc to $52,250,000 each way, or to approximately 17 percent of its total annual foreign trade.
