Adenauer Plans All-Out Attack on Socialists

West German Chancellor Adenauer reportedly plans an all-out attack on the opposition Social Democratic party (SPD) during the Bundestag foreign policy debate on 30 June in an effort to provoke admissions useful to the Christian Democratic Union (CDU) cause in the 1961 national elections. The attack will be led by Foreign Minister von Brentano and Defense Minister Strauss, who will stress the SPD’s opposition to NATO and the party’s neutralist “Germany plan,” but will avoid emphasis on Berlin in order to keep West Berlin Mayor Brandt--who is expected to be the SPD candidate for chancellor in 1961--out of the limelight.

The CDU may also attempt to draw a parallel between the SPD and the Japanese Socialists by charging that the party is infiltrated by Communists and by playing up the Communist past of Deputy Chairman Herbert Wehner.

Realizing their vulnerability on foreign policy, the SPD leaders have called repeatedly since the summit collapse for a re-examination of foreign policy by both the CDU and the SPD in an endeavor to find areas of common agreement, such as Berlin. Brandt has stated that the SPD should avoid overdramatizing the present situation and remain as unobtrusive as possible. While convinced that Adenauer’s policy on German reunification is wrong and harmful to Berlin, he cautions against saying so publicly since “people simply won’t believe it.” He also warned, however, that the SPD must not change its basic foreign policy stand lest it lose five million votes won in 1957 on the basis of the party’s alternative to Adenauer’s policy.
