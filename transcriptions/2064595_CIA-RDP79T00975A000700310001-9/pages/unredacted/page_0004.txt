FAR EAST

3. High Commissioner of Malaya may recommend more American aid:
The American Consul in Kuala Lumpur believes that General Templer, High Commissioner of the Federation of Malaya, during his imminent visit to London may advocate greater American participation in the Malayan struggle. He may propose Mutual Security aid to obtain military supplies or American subsidization of natural rubber.

The High Commissioner is gravely concerned over the rubber price situation, and he may argue in London that the success of the anti-Communist campaign in Malaya should not be jeopardized by a drop in the price of rubber.

Comment: During the past two years, funds for the war in Malaya have been supplied chiefly by the Federation of Malaya, which has maintained a budget surplus largely through revenue from rubber exports.
