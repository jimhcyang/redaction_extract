General Mobilization Announced in Dominican Republic

Mobilization of all trained reserves under age 40 was announced on 22 June by the Dominican Government as rumors of fighting and heavy casualties circulated in the capital. Appeals for blood donors have been broadcast. 400 armed forces personnel are dead or wounded.

A new landing by insurgents near Azua on the southern coast was being opposed by land and sea units during the early hours of 23 June.

A boat loaded with about 100 men to leave Venezuela on the same day. The government also said that two groups in Cuba were expected to land on Haiti's northern coast and then attack the Dominican Republic. Two forces that landed on the Dominican northern coast near Luperon on 20 June and a group that was flown into the mountainous interior at Constanza on 14 June have not yet been defeated.

While the situation in Ciudad Trujillo is outwardly calm, rumors are rife. The chief of staff of the national army is said to be in jail. Anti-Trujillo talk continues to be guarded but more threatening in tone, according to the American Embassy. Ambassador Farland feels that a hard core of about 25 percent of the population will remain loyal to Trujillo and will "resist bitterly" even if an insurgent victory appears imminent.

The Venezuelan foreign minister, in a 22 June talk with the American ambassador which revealed that he had detailed reports from Dominican rebel sources, predicted that Trujillo will fall within 40 days. He reiterated that the Venezuelan Government is not aiding the rebels. However, it is probable that Venezuela, as well as Cuba, has given substantial clandestine aid to the insurgents.
