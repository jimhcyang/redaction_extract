2. COMMUNIST INFILTRATION OF INDONESIAN ARMY

Comment on:

Indonesian Communists appear to be making progress in penetrating army units in Java, particularly in the highly strategic Djakarta and West Java commands. the chief of staff in the Djakarta command is pro-Communist, and a Communist regimental commander in West Java is said to be exerting considerable influence on the territorial commander. Although right-wing officers in West Java still outnumber the Communists, the daily arrest or reassignment of anti-Communist officers for involvement in the "Lubis plot" is giving the Communists additional opportunities.

Control of the army has been a priority Communist goal in Indonesia having equal importance with the subversion of the peasant and laboring classes. Until 1956, the army--and particularly the West Java and North Sumatra commands--provided the principal brake on Communist influence in Indonesia.
