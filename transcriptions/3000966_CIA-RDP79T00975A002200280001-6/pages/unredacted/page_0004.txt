1. NASR UNYIELDING ON EGYPTIAN-SOVIET BLOC ARMS DEAL

In a conversation with Assistant Secretary Allen and Ambassador Byroade on 1 October, Egyptian prime minister Nasr reiterated his determination to go through with his arms deal with the Soviet bloc.

The prime minister emphasized that the agreement with Czechoslovakia was without conditions and that any unusual features were in Egypt's favor. He insisted that he was as anxious to keep communism out of Egypt as anyone and that having got rid of the British and refused to let the Americans replace them, he was not likely to let the Communists move in.

Nasr again spoke at length of the threat of Israeli aggression, eventual if not immediate, as the motive for making a deal with the Soviet bloc, and said he was convinced the United States was trying to keep Egypt weak.
