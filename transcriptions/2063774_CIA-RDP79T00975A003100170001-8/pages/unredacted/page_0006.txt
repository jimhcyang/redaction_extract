3. SOVIET SUBMARINE APPARENTLY TESTS UNDERWATER DEFENSES OF BOSPORUS

On 9 May the Turkish underwater magnetic detection system in the Black Sea approaches to the Bosporus detected a "probable" submarine. Turkish naval units were able to hold sonar contact for about one hour, at which time the submarine returned to the deep water of the Black Sea.

Comment

An attempt by a Soviet submarine to reconnoiter the approaches to the Bosporus would suggest that for war planning purposes the USSR is testing the feasibility of using the Turkish Straits in deploying submarines to or through the Mediterranean.

At the present time there are believed to be about 60 new long-range submarines in the Soviet Black Sea Fleet, many of which probably will ultimately be transferred to areas with access to the Atlantic or Pacific. Under terms of the Montreux Convention, Soviet submarines are not permitted to exit the Black Sea through the straits except singly by day on the surface for passage to repair yards. Detailed information of such passage must be provided the Turkish government in advance.
