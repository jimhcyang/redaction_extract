III. THE WEST

West Germany: The most actively discussed issue today in West Berlin is whether Mayor Willy Brandt can lead the attack of his Social Democratic party (SPD) on the Christian Democratic Union (CDU) without seriously weakening the city's coalition government. Brandt is well aware of the problem and has avoided a too-partisan position, at least in West Berlin. Nevertheless, the highhanded SPD tactics in the Berlin government have increased the dissatisfaction of many CDU members and--with the strain of the national campaigns--could lead to the collapse of the coalition despite the intentions of the top leaders of both parties. A strong stabilizing factor is the Berlin situation itself, which will probably cause these leaders to think a long time before allowing matters to reach the breaking point.

Austria - West Germany: Foreign Minister Kreisky and other top Austrian officials have welcomed the formal unpublicized protest of the West German ambassador against Khrushchev's abuse of Austrian hospitality in attacking Chancellor Adenauer. They feel it supports their own objections to Austrian Chancellor Raab's indiscreet conduct in associating himself with Khrushchev's activities. American officials in Vienna are disturbed, however, at the extent to which the Austrian Government is attempting to balance its neutrality policy by strengthening ties with the East for political and trade reasons.

Paraguay: A group of prominent Paraguayans, including some clergymen, military officers, and students, gave President Stroessner a memorandum on 22 June urging him to turn over power to a transitional junta composed of representatives of the political parties and the armed forces. The junta would guarantee Stroessner's personal safety. The group planned to wait until early July for a reply and if a reply were not forthcoming would begin a "campaign of resistance." Other pressures to oust the Stroessner dictatorship are growing.
