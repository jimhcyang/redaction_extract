Should Grivas come to power he would intensify government repressive measures against the Communists. Despite his strongly anti-Soviet orientation as a supernationalist, he might drive a hard bargain with the United States on questions pertaining to military cooperation. Because of his dissatisfaction with developments on Cyprus, he probably would end the Greek Government's policy of putting pressure on the Greek Cypriots to cooperate in the interest of a new independent Cyprus, thus reducing the chances for a successful settlement next spring.
