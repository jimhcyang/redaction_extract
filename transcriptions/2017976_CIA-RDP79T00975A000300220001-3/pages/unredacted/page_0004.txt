Comment: The UK's vigorous opposition stems both from its recognition of the futility of any effort to expand East-West trade while the US position remains unchanged and from the government's fear that the dissident Bevan group would eagerly exploit the anticipated Soviet propaganda.

French cooperation with US East-West trade control policies has been more willing in the past year. Recently, France has resisted strong Polish demands for molybdenum and nickel, and has agreed only tentatively to give the Poles a limited quantity of aluminum in an effort to obtain desperately needed coal. Some French Government economists will be strongly tempted, however, to encourage increased imports of food and raw materials from the East. They would consider these as complementary to a larger volume of US-financed imports of consumer goods, an anti-inflationary measure now advocated by some French officials.
