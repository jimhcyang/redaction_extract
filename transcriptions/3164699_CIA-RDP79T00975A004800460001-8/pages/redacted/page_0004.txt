III. THE WEST

Finland-USSR: Finland has officially accepted a Soviet 12-year $125,000,000 credit in rubles to purchase capital goods from the Soviet Union. The Finns, who have not been enthusiastic about the loan, may be reluctant to draw upon it but probably hope that the agreement will lessen Moscow's opposition to some kind of Finnish association with the European Free Trade Area. Finland and the USSR also reached an accord on the 1960 exchange of commodities under their long-term trade agreement.
