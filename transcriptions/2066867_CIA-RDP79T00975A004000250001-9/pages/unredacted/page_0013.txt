Peiping Concerned Over New Rangoon Government

Chinese Communist Premier Chou
expressed the hope that Nu would continue to promote a
"reasonable" settlement of the Sino-Burmese border ques-
tion.
Chou said he hoped Nu's successor,
Army General Ne Win, would continue Rangoon's relatively
conciliatory stand on the border issue. Chou charged that
the United States has been taking advantage of Burma's inter-
nal situation "to carry out interventionist activities and fur-
ther to damage the friendly relations between China and Burma."
Ne Win is scheduled to take over on 28 October.

Peiping's policy on defining the Sino-Burmese border has
been to maintain discussions but forestall final agreement.
By these tactics, designed to exploit Burma's eagerness for
a settlement, Communist China may have hoped to obtain
more favorable terms and to use the border issue as a lever for
extracting concessions on other matters.

Peiping fears the attitude
of a new Burmese government may preclude discussion of
the border dispute on terms acceptable to Communist China.
Chou's reference to "further" damage to Sino-Burmese rela-
tions implies that some damage has already been done by
recent events in Burma.
