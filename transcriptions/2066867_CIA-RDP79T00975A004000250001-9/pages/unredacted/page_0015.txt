If the colonels are able to persuade Ne Win to serve as
premier for more than six months, constitutional provisions
will require that he become a member of parliament, break his
tenure of office by at least one day, or rule under martial law.
At the outset, at least, Ne Win appears assured of almost
unanimous support in parliament as premier. U Nu is reported
to have offered to resign his parliamentary seat to make way
for a special election if Ne Win is willing to stand for parlia-
ment.

The chief obstacles to the colonels' hopes are Ne Win's
distaste for political office and his disapproval of military par-
ticipation in politics. He has publicized his intention to exclude
military men from the cabinet and has personally guaranteed
that elections will be held in April. He asserts that he will
serve as premier in his individual capacity and not as the rep-
resentative of the army.
