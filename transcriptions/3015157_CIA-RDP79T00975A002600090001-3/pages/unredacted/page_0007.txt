4. YUGOSLAV-SOVIET AGREEMENT TO INCREASE TRADE

The protocol signed in Belgrade on 6 June calls for an expansion of $20,000,000 each way in this year's Yugoslav-Soviet trade. A Yugoslav request for 100,000 tons of wheat from the USSR presumably accounts for slightly less than half of the increase. According to the announcement, other Soviet exports will be oil, aluminum and machinery, in exchange for Yugoslav steel, agricultural products, textiles and footwear.

With this agreement, Yugoslav-Soviet trade is scheduled to amount to $55,000,000 each way, which is slightly more than Yugoslavia's trade with China and all the Satellites. About one third of Yugoslavia's total scheduled trade will be with the Sino-Soviet bloc. (Concurred in by ORR)
