2. ALGERIAN NATIONALISTS REPORTED SEEKING ARMS
FROM CZECHS

The Algerian nationalists are arranging to get arms from Czechoslovakia. The arms would reportedly be channeled through Syria and be furnished free.

Comment

Unconfirmed that the USSR had agreed to supply free, via Egypt, any arms needed by the Algerian nationalists. Moscow's overt involvement in any such deal would mark a shift in the careful balance the USSR has been maintaining between sympathy for French interests and Arab nationalism on the Algerian question. If a Czech deal should be made, Moscow probably would insist that any arms reaching Algeria were sent by Arab nations.

At least one 12-ton consignment of arms and ammunition intended for Algeria is being readied in Damascus for shipment by air to an unspecified destination.

Algerian nationalist leader Ben Bella publicly stated in Cairo in late February that the rebels would accept arms from the Soviet bloc. Thus far, however, no arms identifiable as of Communist manufacture have been found in rebel hands. (Concurred in by ORR)
