The large withdrawals from Tibet seem to arise from the regime's unwillingness to take strong measures in quelling resistance and instituting all-out control of the country. Several leading Chinese Communist officials, including Mao Tse-tung, have admitted their decision to postpone indefinitely plans for "democratic reform" in Tibet.
