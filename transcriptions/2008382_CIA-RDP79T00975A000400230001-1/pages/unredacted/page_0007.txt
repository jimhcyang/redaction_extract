SOUTH ASIA

8. Comment on new appointments in the Pakistani Government:
The appointment of Finance Minister Ghulam Mohammad as Governor-General of Pakistan to succeed Khwaja Nazimuddin, who resigned on 16 October to become acting Prime Minister, indicates that the reorganization of the Pakistani Government is proceeding along conservative lines inas satisfactory a manner as can be expected. It does not, however, assure eventual political stability in Pakistan.

Ghulam Mohammad has been generally acknowledged to be the most competent cabinet minister after Liaquat Ali Khan, and he might have become Prime Minister had he not been hampered by serious, chronic illness, His appointment as Governor-General, a post which requires less exertion than that of the Prime Minister, should increase public confidence in the government.

Khwaja Nazimuddin, a prominent and capable member of the ruling Moslem League party, presumably took over the Prime Ministership on a temporary basis, pending deliberation by the Moslem League Council and the crystallization of public opinion as to the best permanent appointee.
