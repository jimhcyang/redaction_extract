5. USSR OFFERS TO INCREASE ITS MILITARY MAINTENANCE PERSONNEL IN EGYPT

Comment on:

The Soviet Union proposes to send 20 technicians to repair damages resulting from misuse of thermostat equipment on Egypt's three submarines and give training in maintenance. Egypt presumably will welcome these technicians. Cairo had previously requested Moscow to provide personnel to construct dry-dock facilities and to supervise the dry-docking of the submarines.

Moscow also has recommended that Egypt accept 80 specialists to staff military "field and unit workshops." Egypt replied that this number of workshop specialists is "larger than can be accepted immediately" and that the "expenses are huge," the cost in 1957 of the program recommended by the USSR would be $600,000. Cairo suggested that only 30 of these specialists should be sent this year and that the number "be increased gradually in the future."
