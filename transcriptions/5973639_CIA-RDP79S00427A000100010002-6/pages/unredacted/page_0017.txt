Belgrade has responded with elation to the choice of Yugoslavia as the site for the summit meeting. Tito views his association with the neutrals as a means of commanding more attention for his own foreign and domestic policies; he probably believes that the role of host will enhance his ability to influence the uncommitted states, as well as increase Yugoslavia's prestige generally.

The Sino-Soviet bloc has made no public comment on the proposed summit meeting. TASS factually reported on the preparatory meeting of what it termed "so-called noncommitted states" for the first time on 12 June.
