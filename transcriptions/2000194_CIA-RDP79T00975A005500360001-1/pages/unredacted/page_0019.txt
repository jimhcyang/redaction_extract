Qasim, who is acutely aware of Iraq's position as a nation of religious and linguistic minorities--Kurds, Arabs, Turkomans, and Sunni and Shia Moslems--became suspicious of Barzani's Kurdish nationalist aspirations and threw his support to rival Kurds. Upon Barzani's return from a trip to Moscow in January, he was stripped of many governmental privileges, and several of his followers were arrested. Sporadic fighting between Barzani's tribes and other Kurdish tribes last fall caused the regime considerable concern. Qasim probably fears that any Soviet support for Kurdish nationalists could threaten his regime.
