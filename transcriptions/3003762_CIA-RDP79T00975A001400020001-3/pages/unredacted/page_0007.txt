Comment: A principal deterrent to collaboration between Iraq and Iran disappeared with the removal of Mossadeq, but neither country will be able to build up an effective defense without American military assistance. Iran may delay such talks until it has assurances of receiving American support. It is not likely, however, that any mutual defense agreement between Iran and its neighbors would be possible until at least the oil dispute has been settled and a Majlis is elected which is capable of carrying out an effective domestic program.
