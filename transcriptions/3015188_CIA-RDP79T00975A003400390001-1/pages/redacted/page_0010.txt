6. RUMANIAN LEADERSHIP MAY BE SPLIT OVER
POLICY TOWARD UNITED STATES

Disagreement appears to exist within the Rumanian leadership over the foreign minister's desire to improve relations with the United States. Policy vacillation has been evident during Rumanian Party First Secretary Gheorghiu-Dej's illness of the past two months.

The chief of protocol, in commenting on the failure of the government to have a representative present at the airport on 12 December for the departure of American Minister Thayer, said that he and the foreign minister had wanted to be present, but gave the impression that the Foreign Ministry had been instructed to send no representative. The slight occurred on the day Gheorghiu-Dej made his first public appearance in over two months at the reception for Yemeni Crown Prince Badr.

the foreign minister had hoped to convince Dej that the regime must make some political concessions before American-Rumanian relations can be improved. The legation has noted a marked increase of emigration visas, particularly to West Germany, and reported that Premier Chivu Stoica had promised to facilitate the emigration of dual American-Rumanian citizens. Rumanian efforts to improve relations with the United States last year were interrupted by the Hungarian revolution.
