GENERAL

1. Comment on Soviet position at UN disarmament talks:

At the big-power disarmament talks at the UN General Assembly the USSR is evidently seeking an immediate ban on the atomic bomb in exchange for the questionable possibility of a later control agreement covering the entire atomic energy and armaments fields. Once the West agreed to such a ban, the Soviet Union would probably have little interest in arriving at an agreement on control of atomic energy, since the West would already be morally committed not to produce or use the bomb.

The USSR also continues to seek agreement in principle on a one-third reduction of conventional armaments and armed forces of the Big Five. Acceptance of this principle would tend to hinder the build-up of NATO defense without providing any mechanism for reducing Soviet and Satellite armed forces, and without taking into account the existing level and type of armaments.
