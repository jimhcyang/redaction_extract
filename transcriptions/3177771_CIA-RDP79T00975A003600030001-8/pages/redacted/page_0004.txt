CENTRAL INTELLIGENCE BULLETIN
4 March 1958

DAILY BRIEF

I. THE COMMUNIST BLOC

North Korea: The Pyongyang regime still insists that the United Nations Command in the Military Armistice Commission is not competent to negotiate the hijacked airliner issue, but the Communists have dropped their demand for "direct negotiations" with South Korea as a prerequisite to releasing the passengers. The Communists seem to have moved to a position which makes release contingent on some form of government-to-government approach by the United States, West Germany, and South Korea.
