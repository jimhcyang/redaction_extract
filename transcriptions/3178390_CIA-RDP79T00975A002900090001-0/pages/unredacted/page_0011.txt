7. PRAVDA ATTACKS POLISH CONCEPT OF NATIONAL COMMUNISM

Comment on:

Pravda's attack of 23 December on the "individual roads to socialism" followed in Poland and Yugoslavia shows Moscow's continued uneasiness over developments in these countries, particularly in Poland.

The Pravda article rejected the concept of the "correct relations" between Communist parties which a Polish writer recently said were based on "the principles of co-existence with the absence of any tendencies toward hegemony." It quoted with approval the statement in a joint Czech-East German communiqué that "all this talk about alleged national Communism contradicts Marxism-Leninism."

Though the article paid deference to the principle of full equality among Communist nations, it defended the concept of Soviet leadership with the statement that "the policy of proletarian internationalism presupposes on the part of the workers of all lands the support and defense of countries which have already entered the path of socialism."

Poland and Yugoslavia can be expected to react with firm reassertions of their view that they are entitled to pursue individual courses of development without interference and that co-operation among Communist countries does not presuppose domination of one by the other.
