1 AUGUST 1959

I. THE COMMUNIST BLOC

Peiping gets Crown Prince Badr's
permission to send more techni-
cians to Yemen.

II. ASIA-AFRICA

Iraq--Army instructors ordered
withdrawn from Popular Resistance
Force, Communists reported con-
sidering strikes and sabotage in oil
fields.

World Bank loan to Nasir for Suez
Canal improvement may be blocked
by Israel and UK. Israelis want
loan tied to commitment on freedom
of transit.

King Saud agrees to meet with Nasir.

Japan indicates it will "sign repatria-
tion agreement with North Korea;
may cause Seoul to withdraw offer
to resume talks with Tokyo.

South Korea--Rhee's personal control
of government may be weakening as
result of mental deterioration.

Afghan propaganda charges US collu-
sion in alleged Pakistani aggression
against 'Pushtoonistan.

Somalia requests arms from Italy;
could aggravate tensions with Ethi-
opia.

III. THE WEST

Reported plots against Castro re-
gime seen as unlikely to succeed if
attempted at this time.
