Probable MIG-19s in China

10 jet fighter aircraft, probably MIG-19s, at Tsanghsien Airfield 110 miles south of Peiping. These fighters were definitely larger than MIG-17s which, along with IL-28 jet light bombers, were also on the field.

The numerous reports, received since last fall, of high-altitude flights over the Chinese mainland may have been reflections of familiarization and training operations with this aircraft. Most of the extreme altitudes reported are within the capability of the MIG-19, which has a service ceiling of 62,200 feet.

This supersonic rapid-climbing aircraft enhances the overall capability of the Chinese Communist Air Force as it can be used for low-altitude and toss bombing in addition to intercept operations. The MIG-19 carries one or two small cannons, unguided rockets, and could easily be adapted to use homing missiles. One model, the MIG-19B, has an all-weather capability.

Communist China, with Soviet assistance, is preparing to produce this aircraft.
