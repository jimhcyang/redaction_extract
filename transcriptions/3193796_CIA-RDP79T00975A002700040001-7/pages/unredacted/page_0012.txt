Cairo radio's Hebrew language broadcasts have been officially regarded in Israel as taking a softer tone toward Israel as a direct result of its abstention from open adherence to the Western position in the Suez controversy. The nationalization of the Suez remains the principal preoccupation in foreign affairs with both the Israeli government and the press. The embassy in Tel Aviv reports that while no clear line has emerged, there is increasing evidence that the government's silence is based on a firm decision to "wait and see," until the Eastern and Western positions in the dispute are clarified.
