Adenauer's optimism about the talks apparently reflects in part De Gaulle's agreement not to push the idea of defense coordination at this time as well as pledges of "loyalty to NATO"

The chancellor may also be anticipating that in the high-level committee which will be discussing concrete measures before the next "little summit" meeting on 19 May, De Gaulle can be induced to make further concessions toward a more supranational arrangement. The communiqué itself refers to the necessity of "laying the basis of a union that will develop progressively," and pro-integration leaders like Jean Monnet have argued that confederation should be considered the first step toward federation.
