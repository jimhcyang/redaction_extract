7. ADENAUER'S REPLY TO BULGANIN MAKES SUGGESTIONS
FOR IMPROVING RELATIONS

Chancellor Adenauer's reply to Premier Bulganin's 8 February letter will call for talks with the USSR as soon as possible on trade relations and subsequent talks on scientific and technical co-operation.

The message, which is scheduled for delivery on 26 February and for publication later, notes the increase in German-Soviet trade and agrees that further expansion would improve political relations, but also cites the unification problem and the continued detention of German citizens in the USSR as two obstacles to friendly relations.

Comment

The unusually friendly tone of the letter is in evident recognition of the widespread sentiment in West Germany for improvement in relations with the Soviet Union. The opposition has exploited the poor state of Bonn-Moscow relations in its early campaigning for the September Bundestag elections.
