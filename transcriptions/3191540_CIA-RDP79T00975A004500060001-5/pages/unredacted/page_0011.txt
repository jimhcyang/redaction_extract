Sudanese Situation

Khartoum remained calm during the extraordinary 10-12 May meeting of the Supreme Military Council and commanders of all the major army commands. Maneuvers for an immediate shift in the power balance appear to have produced a temporary deadlock.

Abboud, whose policies of compromise have permitted the steady growth in Shannan's power, is reported still anxious to retire. Several senior officers outside the Supreme Council have proffered their resignations because of disgust over what they regard as appeasement of the junior officers movement and the consequent breakdown of army discipline.

Sudanese-Egyptian relations will be subject to further strain in the first part of July when the Sudanese plan to increase their diversion of Nile waters to about 20 percent more than the amount permitted by the 1929 Sudanese-Egyptian agreement.
