7. Madrid students protest streetcar fare policy:

Some 1,000 University of Madrid students are reported to have attempted to march to the City Hall on 2 April ostensibly in order to protest against restrictions on the use of their streetcar passes. Some of the demonstrators voiced their discontent with the high cost of living. Following the arrest of three leaders, the students dispersed quietly and no serious injuries were reported.

Comment: On 23 and 24 February, a similar protest by University of Barcelona students heralded the city-wide streetcar boycott and general strike which had Barcelona in a turmoil during the first two weeks of March. At this time, it was predicted that the success of the Barcelona demonstrations and the apparently conciliatory attitude of the government might encourage Spaniards elsewhere to voice their discontent against increasing economic hardships. The calling of a cabinet meeting to consider the cost of living problem reveals the government’s growing apprehension that such incidents might spread.
