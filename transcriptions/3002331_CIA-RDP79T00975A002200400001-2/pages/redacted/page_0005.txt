3. ITALY SEEN SOFTENING ON RECOGNITION OF
COMMUNIST CHINA

The American embassy in Rome re-
ports a marked softening in Italian
government circles regarding recog-
nition of Communist China, although
the statements of government leaders remain cautious.

Foreign Minister Martino told Ambas-
sador Luce on 15 October that Italy had little to gain by
recognition, since China's trading interest focuses on pro-
hibited strategic items, but that Italy was being increas-
ingly pressed by other NATO countries to recognize Com-
munist China.

Mean-
while, efforts by the Italian Foreign Ministry to formalize
trade relations have apparently ended in a stalemate.

Comment
The campaign for recognition of Com-
munist China
may be supported
not only by the leftist parties, but by industrialists seek-
ing new export outlets.

Peiping, however, has never previously
sought pledges of diplomatic recognition as a price for ex-
panding economic relations with non-Communist countries.
