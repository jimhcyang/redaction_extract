Algeria-France:

The question of using force had split the French cabinet on 25 January and several ministers who opposed such action, including Deputy Premier Soustelle, have threatened to resign. De Gaulle presumably will have to deal with these threats at the cabinet meeting scheduled for today. Soustelle, who had planned to resign yesterday, postponed action on assurance from Debré that De Gaulle will state publicly--without revoking his declared self-determination policy for Algeria--that the government will work for the "Frenchification" of Algeria.

The European extremists barricaded in downtown Algiers, meanwhile, have built up their numbers from 1,000 on 24 January to about 4,000, and apparently are counting on the reluctance of the French army to attack them to induce a statement from De Gaulle pledging a "French Algeria."

27 Jan 60
