If, as expected, the USSR turns down the British plan to send a UN observer to Laos, London may suggest a joint UK-Soviet proposal to the Laotian Government to recall the ICC. Britain, anticipating Laos' refusal, believes this move would make clear that the only possible peaceful solution is through the UN. As an alternative, London favors enlisting the "good offices" of Nehru. UN Secretary General Hammarskjold wrote to the Indian prime minister on 22 August suggesting that he approach Hanoi and Vientiane in an effort to work out a formula paving the way for the appointment of a "neutral" mediator who, Hammarskjold feels, should probably not be Asian. Hammarskjold's letter was the result of definite indications from the Laotian emissary that discussions with Nehru would have more chance of success than an approach through the Geneva cochairmen.
