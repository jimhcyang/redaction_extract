Morocco: The crown prince is reported to be displeased with the size of the initial American arms shipment scheduled to reach Morocco for display during the mid-November national holiday. He characterized the proposed shipment as "ridiculously small and entirely inadequate" for the spectacular demonstration he wants of American support for the palace and Royal Army. This adverse reaction appears to be a gambit to obtain a larger shipment. Meanwhile, the Moroccans seem to be resisting French pressure to fill Royal Army requirements for the November parade, and the USSR and Czechoslovakia are reported to be offering "everything and anything" the Moroccan Government wants.

Indonesia: Rumors of an impending army coup appear to arise in part from general apprehension over increased army influence in the government. The Indonesian Communist party may be exploiting such rumors in an effort to aggravate President Sukarno's apparently growing uneasiness over the army's political role.
