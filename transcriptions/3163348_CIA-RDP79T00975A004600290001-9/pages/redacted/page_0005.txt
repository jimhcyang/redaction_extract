Britain-Central Africa: Criticism by a special commission of the handling of last winter's disorders in Nyasaland may indirectly increase African unrest there. London is unlikely to initiate any significant corrective action which could be construed as an admission of errors before the British general elections expected in October, and the Macmillan government will therefore remain vulnerable to Labor charges that "police state" conditions are allowed to continue. In Nyasaland itself, the criticism has strengthened the appeal of the nationalist movement led by the imprisoned Dr. Hastings Banda.
