GENERAL

1. Soviet fighters ordered to attack aircraft in Koethen area:

Four Soviet fighter aircraft were ordered to attack an unidentified aircraft, called "a big one" near Koethen in the Soviet Zone of Germany. It stated that the aircraft was "not flying in any corridor."

Comment: observers in Berlin indicated that the aircraft which was attacked on 29 April was outside the authorized corridor when attacked.

FAR EAST

2. Recent enemy messages in Korea suggest no major operations planned in May:

An unidentified North Korean unit, possibly subordinate to the V Corps in the Wonsan-Hamhung area, on 29 April stated that "the training course at the Corps will be held from the 1st of May to the 30th of May."

Comment: The continuance of April procedures during May and continued training in a major North Korean command may be interpreted as an indication that no major enemy operation is contemplated in the immediate future.
