CENTRAL INTELLIGENCE BULLETIN
14 May 1959
DAILY BRIEF

I. THE COMMUNIST BLOC

USSR-Burma: Apparently as a result of the severe damage done recently to the USSR's prestige in Burma, Moscow is replacing its ambassador, A. D. Schiborin, who would have become dean of the diplomatic corps next month. Hostile public reaction to the Soviet Embassy's high-handed methods in handling the case of the Soviet military attache and Schiborin's failure to quash a libel suit against a TASS representative probably prompted Moscow's decision.

Watch Committee conclusion--Berlin: No significant indications bearing on the possibility of hostilities.
