3. ALGERIAN STRIKE APPARENTLY SUSPENDED

Comment on:
A general return to work of Algerian workers who began an eight-day strike on 28 January probably indicates a suspension rather than an end of the strike threat. A resumption of the strike, possibly accompanied by serious disorders, seems probable when the UN General Assembly begins its debate on Algeria.

The American consul general in Algiers reports that almost all Moslem workers and merchants in that city complied with the strike call on Monday. The strike did not, however, disrupt all activities, as essential services were maintained on a reduced scale by Europeans with the help of the military and, for the first time, convicts.
