2. Comment on softening of Greek attitude on Cyprus:

A significant softening of the Greek position on the Cyprus issue is indicated by a recent statement of Alexis Kyrou, acting chief of the Greek UN delegation. Kyrou told Ambassador Lodge that Greece wanted the General Assembly to pass a "very mild resolution" affirming the Cypriot people's right to self-determination.

Athens, despite the continuing bitter official and editorial comment, apparently is reappraising its position in the light of the American attitude. The Greek government evidently considers that Washington's attitude will be the determining factor as far as UN action on the Cyprus item is concerned.
