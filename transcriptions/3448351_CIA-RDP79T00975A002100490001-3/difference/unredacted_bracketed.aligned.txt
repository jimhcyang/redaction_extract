1. SOVIET AMBASSADOR GAINS AUDIENCE WITH SAUDI KING

King Saud received Soviet ambassador Lavrentiev in Tehran on 16 August, since the ambassador's repeated [[PRED_REDACTION_1]]efforts[[/PRED_REDACTION_1]] to see him were becoming "[[PRED_REDACTION_2]]embarrassing[[/PRED_REDACTION_2]]." According to a Saudi Foreign Ministry [[PRED_REDACTION_3]]official[[/PRED_REDACTION_3]], Lavrentiev spoke insistently of the desirability of exchanging diplomatic representatives. He said the USSR is greatly interested in arming the Saudi forces and [[PRED_REDACTION_4]]requested[[/PRED_REDACTION_4]] a statement of the kinds and quantities of weapons the Saudis desired.

The king promised to study the [[PRED_REDACTION_5]]questions[[/PRED_REDACTION_5]] raised by the ambassador, but spoke frankly of the religious and political differences between Saudi Arabia and the Soviet Union.

Comment
The Soviet ambassador's [[PRED_REDACTION_6]]extraordinary[[/PRED_REDACTION_6]] efforts to see Saud, in addition to recent Soviet overtures of a similar nature to Egypt, indicate the extent of Soviet efforts to exploit the [[PRED_REDACTION_7]]differences[[/PRED_REDACTION_7]] between these Arab states and the West on Middle East defense planning.

3. HUNGARIAN INTERNAL POLICIES MAY BE MODERATED

The Hungarian central committee [[PRED_REDACTION_8]]decided[[/PRED_REDACTION_8]] at a meeting on 5 or 6 August to shift to a more moderate line in Hungary's internal policies, [[PRED_REDACTION_9]]according[[/PRED_REDACTION_9]] to an unconfirmed report received by the American legation in Budapest.

The legation comments that if this [[PRED_REDACTION_10]]report[[/PRED_REDACTION_10]] is true, it may account for the marked slowing down in deportations from Budapest during the past few days, and may explain the hurried and incomplete preparations which were apparent behind Rakosi's 8 August address at Csepel. Rakosi's speech dealt with internal matters in a tone more moderate than has been observed in recent months.

Comment
The tone of Rakosi's 8 August speech probably reflects his effort to get back in line with Soviet policy, which is to make [[PRED_REDACTION_11]]Communist[[/PRED_REDACTION_11]] political control in the Satellites more palatable. During the past six months, political repression and [[PRED_REDACTION_12]]coercion[[/PRED_REDACTION_12]] have characterized the Hungarian Communists' [[PRED_REDACTION_13]]efforts[[/PRED_REDACTION_13]] to re-establish party discipline and tighten their control of the country.
