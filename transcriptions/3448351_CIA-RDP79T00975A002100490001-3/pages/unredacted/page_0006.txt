3. HUNGARIAN INTERNAL POLICIES MAY BE MODERATED

The Hungarian central committee decided at a meeting on 5 or 6 August to shift to a more moderate line in Hungary's internal policies, according to an unconfirmed report received by the American legation in Budapest.

The legation comments that if this report is true, it may account for the marked slowing down in deportations from Budapest during the past few days, and may explain the hurried and incomplete preparations which were apparent behind Rakosi's 8 August address at Csepel. Rakosi's speech dealt with internal matters in a tone more moderate than has been observed in recent months.

Comment
The tone of Rakosi's 8 August speech probably reflects his effort to get back in line with Soviet policy, which is to make Communist political control in the Satellites more palatable. During the past six months, political repression and coercion have characterized the Hungarian Communists' efforts to re-establish party discipline and tighten their control of the country.
