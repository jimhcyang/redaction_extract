SOUTH ASIA

4. Defense minister suggests Pakistan may grant air bases to US:

Pakistan's defense minister Ayub told American officials on 26 October that his government would be receptive to an American proposal to establish air bases in Pakistan capable of supporting B-47 medium jet bomber operations.

The American embassy notes that Ayub speaks with considerably more authority as defense minister than he previously did as army commander in chief.

Comment: Despite Ayub's position of authority he has been defense minister only since 24 October, and the new cabinet probably has not discussed its position on the matter of American bases.

Ayub is able and determined and may eventually win the government to his way of thinking, but he was probably speaking for himself alone at this time. He has in the past been ahead of the government on the question of military co-operation with the West.

NEAR EAST - AFRICA

5. Anti-American sentiment rising in Greece:

Ambassador Cannon reports that sentiments unfriendly to the United States are becoming a regular feature of the Greek press, even in newspapers usually most friendly, inspired particularly by confusion in Greece regarding the American position on the Cyprus question and popular resentment against extraterritorial provisions in the American-Greek Military Facilities Agreement.
