5. EGYPTIAN NEGOTIATIONS WITH CZECHOSLOVAKIA

discussions concerning Czech equipment and specialists for military maintenance depots would take place in Czechoslovakia next February. This timing suggests that no great urgency is attached to the new arms negotiations between Egypt and the Soviet bloc. Egypt submitted a list of military requirements to the Soviet military attaché in Cairo a month ago, but there has been little information on further negotiations.

Cairo was requested to send two representatives to inspect "mobile workshops" which would be exhibited on 2 January in Prague. These representatives would then return to Egypt with Czech specialists to inspect repair and maintenance facilities for aircraft, tanks, vehicles and artillery. Egypt would not order additional workshops until these inspections had been completed.

Egypt is apparently implementing its plan to procure Czech personnel for repair and maintenance depots.

Egypt intended to use Czechs as the key personnel in its military training programs.
