5. French urge caution in approach to Egypt on Middle East Command:

Comment: Egyptian sentiment is anti-British,
rather than neutralist, as a result of the serious Anglo-Egyptian Treaty dispute. Unless, as the French imply, the details of the proposed Middle East
Command scheme are made sufficiently attractive to Egypt, Egyptian Government leaders may not approve of the Middle East defense scheme.

6. European fascist meeting reportedly being held in Spain:

European Social Movement was scheduled to
meet in Spain on 28 September to organize a Pan-
European Movement or at least an international
body of fascist war veterans. Among the groups
which reportedly were to be represented at the
meeting were the neo-fascist Italian Social
Movement, Nazi SS legions, and the Falange. Among the individuals named as participants were the German neo-Nazi Priester, the Swedish neo-Nazi Engdahl, former SS General Korsemann and
the notorious Nazi intelligence agent Skorzeny. The Executive Council of the
European Social Movement hoped to secure a pledge of Falange cooperation
from Franco.
