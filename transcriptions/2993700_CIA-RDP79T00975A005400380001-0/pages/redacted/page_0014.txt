Brandt stressed that "prestige" issues such as a formal "Berlin clause" in the treaty itself are not important as long as the present method of handling the city's trade is not changed. Bonn has announced that lack of an agreement would not preclude trade between the two countries, which can continue on the basis of individual orders.

Meanwhile, Bonn has also been insisting that West Berlin and West Germany be treated as one "currency area" in present negotiations with East Germany on a new interzonal trade agreement, and the East Germans have not to date refused to continue the talks within this framework.
