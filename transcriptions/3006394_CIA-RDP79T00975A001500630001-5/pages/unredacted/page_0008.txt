Carrigan reports that although ARAMCO does not believe immediate action is necessary to forestall a strike, the company is worried because of the absence of Ibn Jiluwi, the strong local governor.

Comment: Saudi forces seem capable of controlling any serious disturbances. Troops under Colonel Awartani were able to maintain order during last October's disorders, when Ibn Jiluwi was absent from the country. Labor difficulties are aggravated by the government's continued repressive policy which was the real cause of last fall's troubles.

In face of increasing Saudi harassment of ARAMCO, another major strike, which the government would again blame on the company, would further complicate the company's relations with the government as well as with local employees.
