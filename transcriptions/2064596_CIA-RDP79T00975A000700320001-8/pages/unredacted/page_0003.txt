FAR EAST

1. Soviet radar plotted US aircraft missing in Japan Sea:
an unidentified aircraft, probably the B-29 which has been missing since that date, was plotted along with two other aircraft, presumably Soviet, 20 to 25 miles off the Soviet coast near Vladivostok.

Comment: The last US radar check gave the B-29's position as approximately 25 miles west of the tip of Hokkaido. Aircraft wreckage was discovered on 14 June about 130 miles southeast of the Vladivostok area, in the vicinity of the last Soviet radar plot. Equipment near the wreckage indicates that there may have been survivors.

Ambassador Kennan in Moscow has been instructed to request an urgent Soviet investigation.

2. Ranking members of Soviet mission in Japan returning to Moscow:
Representatives of the Soviet mission in Tokyo called at the British Embassy on 14 June and requested transit visas to Hong Kong for five members of the mission, including the chief, Major General Aleksei Kislenko and a political adviser. The party desires to go to Moscow this week via Hong Kong and China.

Comment: The departure of some ranking members may be a Soviet gesture in response to recent Japanese demands for the mission's withdrawal. Retention of a working staff of approximately 65 in Tokyo maintains the Soviet position that the dissolution of the Allied Council for Japan is "illegal."
