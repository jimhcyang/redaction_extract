EASTERN EUROPE

5. Comment on Black Sea canal work suspension:
The tacit admission by a Rumanian official on 10 August that work on the Danube-Black Sea canal has been suspended supports earlier reports that construction was stopped on 20 July. The apparent cessation of work on the project is in sharp contrast to the Rumanian propaganda line that the canal was to be the greatest of the country's socialist construction projects.

The Rumanian official implied that labor, equipment, and investment resources will be shifted from the canal to consumer goods production. This is in line with the new "liberal" policy in the Orbit. The work stoppage also parallels the recent abandonment of at least two such major construction projects in the Soviet Union.
