1. PARAGUAYAN MILITARY REBELLION REPORTED QUELLED

President Stroessner announced on 22 December that the "command crisis" which broke out on 21 [[PRED_REDACTION_6]]December[[/PRED_REDACTION_6]] in the First Cavalry Division "is headed toward a satisfactory solution." The rebellion in this key unit was touched off by Stroessner's order for the arrest of Epifanio Mendez Fleitas, Central Bank president and a [[PRED_REDACTION_7]]controversial[[/PRED_REDACTION_7]] leader of the ruling Colorado Party.

Stroessner and other conservative elements in the Colorado Party wish to remove Mendez from his politically strategic job, not only because of [[PRED_REDACTION_8]]political[[/PRED_REDACTION_8]] rivalry, but because they variously consider him to be pro-Peron, corrupt and leftist. Mendez and his [[PRED_REDACTION_9]]followers[[/PRED_REDACTION_9]], on the other hand, would like to reduce the [[PRED_REDACTION_10]]traditionally[[/PRED_REDACTION_10]] dominant political power of the military.

The Argentine government is reported to have made [[PRED_REDACTION_11]]the[[/PRED_REDACTION_11]] removal of Mendez a prerequisite for [[PRED_REDACTION_12]]granting[[/PRED_REDACTION_12]] the economic co-operation which Paraguay urgently needs--trade having virtually ceased since Peron was ousted last September. Buenos Aires is probably concerned over [[PRED_REDACTION_13]]plotting[[/PRED_REDACTION_13]] by Peronista exiles and the extent to which pro-Peron Paraguayan officials would be willing to limit such activity.

While Stroessner, as commander in chief of the armed forces, still appears to hold the stronger [[PRED_REDACTION_14]]position[[/PRED_REDACTION_14]], Mendez has made some progress in undermining [[PRED_REDACTION_15]]Stroessner's[[/PRED_REDACTION_15]] military support by granting special favors to younger officers, including the commander of the First Cavalry [[PRED_REDACTION_16]]Division[[/PRED_REDACTION_16]]. In addition, Mendez is said to have the support of the police and of the head of the Colorado Party.

It is evident that Stroessner has not yet resolved the crisis.

2. AFGHANISTAN RELEASES MORE FUNDS TO MORRISON-KNUDSEN COMPANY

The Afghan government has ordered $800,000 released to the [[PRED_REDACTION_17]]Morrison-Knudsen[[/PRED_REDACTION_17]] Company for current expenses, according to the company's manager in Afghanistan. The company has been authorized to resume purchasing and shipping through Pakistan, and its manager is [[PRED_REDACTION_18]]recommending[[/PRED_REDACTION_18]] that the sending of personnel to Afghanistan be resumed.

Comment
This sum is apparently in addition to the $700,000 released in recent weeks and previously reported.

Kabul's actions in favor of [[PRED_REDACTION_19]]Morrison-Knudsen[[/PRED_REDACTION_19]] suggest that the Afghans, in an effort to [[PRED_REDACTION_20]]counterbalance[[/PRED_REDACTION_20]] the effect of the Bulganin-Khrushchev visit, mean to keep the Helmand Valley project under American [[PRED_REDACTION_21]]auspices[[/PRED_REDACTION_21]].

3. POLES MAKE DETERMINED EFFORT TO GAIN ARABIAN RAILROAD SURVEY CONTRACT

The Polish organization responsible for Polish industrial construction in other countries has submitted the low bid for a survey of the Hejaz rail line [[PRED_REDACTION_22]]reconstruction[[/PRED_REDACTION_22]] project which would extend the line from Maan to Medina.

The difference between the Polish bid of $440,000 and the next lowest bids, $780,000 by an American firm and $865,000 by a Japanese firm, demonstrates that Warsaw is determined to win the subsequent contract for actual construction of the line.

The Hejaz commission will recommend acceptance of the Polish offer, although it realizes that the bid is far below the estimated cost of the survey, and [[PRED_REDACTION_23]]recognizes[[/PRED_REDACTION_23]] it as a Soviet-backed penetration effort. The final [[PRED_REDACTION_24]]decision[[/PRED_REDACTION_24]] as to which firm will receive the survey contract is, however, to be made by King Saud. ([[PRED_REDACTION_25]]Prepared by ORR[[/PRED_REDACTION_25]])

4. DISORDER INCREASING IN ALGERIA

Jacques Chevallier, the French mayor of Algiers, has told the American consul general that the Algerian Assembly has been paralyzed by the mass resignation of Moslem members. Moslem [[PRED_REDACTION_26]]administrative[[/PRED_REDACTION_26]] officials throughout Algeria, threatened with [[PRED_REDACTION_27]]assassination[[/PRED_REDACTION_27]] if they do not resign by the time of the 2 January elections in France, are leaving office in increasing [[PRED_REDACTION_28]]numbers[[/PRED_REDACTION_28]]. Clashes between the nationalists and the French [[PRED_REDACTION_29]]reportedly[[/PRED_REDACTION_29]] have resulted in about 90 deaths in three days. Terrorist activities will probably be stepped up over the holiday season, and propaganda leaflets calling for greater violence at that time are being circulated in the cities. The 175,000 French ground and air forces in Algeria are being reinforced.

Chevallier believes that Governor [[PRED_REDACTION_30]]General[[/PRED_REDACTION_30]] Soustelle must be replaced, as he has lost the [[PRED_REDACTION_31]]confidence[[/PRED_REDACTION_31]] of the Moslems. The mayor thinks the Moslems would still support a federal arrangement between France and Algeria, but in the opinion of the consul general, the French will have to spell out the details and timing of an [[PRED_REDACTION_32]]acceptable[[/PRED_REDACTION_32]] solution before the nationalists will lay down their arms.

The anticipated early consideration of the Algerian issue by the French National Assembly after the 2 January elections will be handicapped by the lack of Algerian representation.

5. INCREASED COMMUNIST ACTIVITY IN CENTRAL LAOS REPORTED

Pathet Lao-Viet Minh activity has [[PRED_REDACTION_33]]increased[[/PRED_REDACTION_33]] radically in Khammouane Province, central Laos, during the last two months, [[PRED_REDACTION_34]]sighting Lao-Viet[[/PRED_REDACTION_34]] units, ranging in size from two to 60 men, on 38 [[PRED_REDACTION_35]]separate[[/PRED_REDACTION_35]] occasions during this period. Not all of these groups were armed. [[PRED_REDACTION_36]]Security[[/PRED_REDACTION_36]] in the province is the worst in any of the ten under the government's [[PRED_REDACTION_37]]control[[/PRED_REDACTION_37]]. Police and army [[PRED_REDACTION_38]]reinforcements[[/PRED_REDACTION_38]] were recently sent there, presumably in anticipation of disorders during the 25 December elections.

Comment
This report indicates a considerable Communist capability for subversion and sabotage in central Laos. Although Khammouane [[PRED_REDACTION_39]]Province[[/PRED_REDACTION_39]], on a main [[PRED_REDACTION_40]]communications[[/PRED_REDACTION_40]] line from Viet [[PRED_REDACTION_41]]Minh-held[[/PRED_REDACTION_41]] central Vietnam, is apparently the most heavily infiltrated, the Communists are believed to be increasing their capabilities in other central and southern provinces as well. [[PRED_REDACTION_42]]A recent Pathet Lao defector said that guerrilla operations by small units throughout the country are planned[[/PRED_REDACTION_42]].

The government's military problem in the north has forced the concentration there of most of its military units. A withdrawal to meet security threats elsewhere would

[[PRED_REDACTION_43]]6. NASR CALLS CONFERENCE OF ARAB LEADERS ON JORDAN

Comment
The Saudis have previously suggested that Arab states should replace the British subsidy to Jordan, which amounts to about $33,000,000 annually, but have never made a concrete offer.

It is unlikely that the Saudis would live up to such a commitment. However, if the present political unrest[[/PRED_REDACTION_43]] in [[PRED_REDACTION_44]]Jordan continues, Hussain may feel that the safety of his throne depends on weakening his ties with Britain.

[[PRED_REDACTION_45]]THE ARAB-ISRAELI SITUATION
(Information as of 1700, 22 December)

No significant military activity has been reported.

On 30 November the secretary general of the Italian Foreign Ministry told the American embassy that it would become increasingly difficult to resist domestic pressure for the sales of arms to the Middle East as a result of delays in the Washington talks and reports that the French were continuing to sell arms to that area.
