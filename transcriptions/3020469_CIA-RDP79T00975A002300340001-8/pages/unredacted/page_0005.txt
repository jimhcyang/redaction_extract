2. AFGHANISTAN RELEASES MORE FUNDS TO MORRISON-KNUDSEN COMPANY

The Afghan government has ordered $800,000 released to the Morrison-Knudsen Company for current expenses, according to the company's manager in Afghanistan. The company has been authorized to resume purchasing and shipping through Pakistan, and its manager is recommending that the sending of personnel to Afghanistan be resumed.

Comment
This sum is apparently in addition to the $700,000 released in recent weeks and previously reported.

Kabul's actions in favor of Morrison-Knudsen suggest that the Afghans, in an effort to counterbalance the effect of the Bulganin-Khrushchev visit, mean to keep the Helmand Valley project under American auspices.
