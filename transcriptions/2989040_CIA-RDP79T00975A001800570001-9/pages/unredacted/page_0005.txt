The self-styled "South Moluccan Republic," centered on Ambon Island, staged an unsuccessful military effort for independence in 1950 and 1951, and is generally supported by the 100,000 Ambonese in the area who are anti-Javanese and pro-Dutch. The movement has never been entirely suppressed, and Indonesian authorities consider it Dutch-supported.

Government forces are believed capable of containing the dissidents. In view of recent statements by President Sukarno and high-ranking National Party leaders, however, recommending the use of force to win Netherlands New Guinea, the Indonesian government may use the Moluccan situation to justify increasing its clandestine military activity in New Guinea. Indonesian infiltration parties have been apprehended there by the Dutch during the past 18 months, most recently in October.
