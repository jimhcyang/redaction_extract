Chough's strategy is to form a new political party, without the anti-Rhee emphasis, and build it up as a counterbalance to Rhee's Liberal Party and particularly to the influence of Yi Pom-sok, Rhee's most likely successor.

Comment: Chough's decision follows press reports that at least 23 Democratic National Party legislators have or will soon join Rhee's party, reducing the Democratic Nationals' strength below the minimum required for an assembly opposition group.

With the disappearance of this party, Rhee will have systematically shattered the last remaining effective opposition. Reports indicate that he may foster a "democratic national front," using as an excuse the problem of unification.

3. Peiping announces increase in Sino-Satellite trade for 1953:

Trade between Communist China and the six East European Satellites in 1953 will be about one third more than last year, according to Peiping's vice minister of foreign trade. The volume of machinery and vehicles imported from Eastern Europe this year will be three times greater than in 1952, and will account for one half of Peiping's 1953 imports from that area.

Comment: A substantial increase in shipping between Communist China and the European Satellites was apparent early this year.

China's total foreign trade is also expected to be higher this year than last, as estimated imports from non-Communist countries in the first half of 1953 have reached 65 percent of last year's total and China's trade with the USSR is not believed to be declining.
