4. SHAH OF IRAN SUPPORTS US ON SUEZ CRISIS

The Shah of Iran feels that Egyptian president Nasr must go, but opposes the use of force on the Suez issue. The Shah apparently favors the stand taken by the United States, and believes it advisable to give Nasr "enough rope to hang himself."

The Shah said he would try "to open King Saud's eyes" to the threat Nasr poses to other Arab countries on his visit to Riyadh in the winter of 1956-57.

Comment

The Shah urged Saud to "give the necessary advice... to the president of the Egyptian Republic" to prevent Communist exploitation of the Suez crisis; Saud answered only that he was doing everything within his power "for reconciliation and peace."
