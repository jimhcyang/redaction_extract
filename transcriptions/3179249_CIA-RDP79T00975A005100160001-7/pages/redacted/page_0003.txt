CENTRAL INTELLIGENCE BULLETIN
19 May 1960

DAILY BRIEF

I. THE COMMUNIST BLOC

USSR: Khrushchev's final press conference on 18 May and the line being taken both publicly and privately by other Soviet spokesmen in Paris are designed to give the impression that while the Soviet premier, for the foreseeable future, will vigorously press his bitter anti-American campaign on the U-2 issue, he does not intend to make any sharp reversals in the other major lines of his foreign policy.

*This point was echoed by Yuriy Zhukov, chairman of the State Committee for Foreign Cultural Relations. Khrushchev, Gromyko, and Zhukov all seemed to assume that negotiations on disarmament and a nuclear test ban would continue. Zhukov indicated that Khrushchev had played "this one by ear," that even those in his own retinue did not know what he might do from one minute to the next, and that a central committee meeting and a subsequent bloc conference were virtual certainties.
