8 JUNE 1960

I. THE COMMUNIST BLOC

1. Peiping's People's Daily, while giving general support to Soviet disarmament proposals of 2 June, dissents from Soviet view that war now can be "fundamentally eliminated."

2. Moscow encourages Ankara to continue steps begun during Menderes regime for improving Soviet-Turkish relations.

3. Communist China sends "agriculturists" to help improve rice cultivation in Guinea, agrees to provide scholarships for Guinean students.

II. ASIA-AFRICA

4. Japanese leftists plan new and bigger demonstrations; although Kishi probably can get US-Japan treaty ratified as planned, his prospects remain uncertain for retaining office for long thereafter.

5. Laos: calls for insurgents to step up activities and frustrate Laotian Government's efforts to recapture Souphannouvong and other NLHS leaders.

6. UAR seeking private West German financing for Syrian Region's five year industrialization plan.
