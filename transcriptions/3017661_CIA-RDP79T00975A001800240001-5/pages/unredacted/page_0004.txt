FAR EAST

3. Japanese draft budget cuts defense expenditures:
The draft supplementary budget submitted to the Japanese cabinet by the Finance Ministry on 19 November included a cut of 4.55 billion yen ($12,640,000) in funds for the National Defense Agency, according to the Far East Command. The cut, amounting to 5.7 percent of planned expenditures is being considered by the cabinet and, if approved, will be presented to the Diet at the end of this month.

The Far East Command comments that it is possible the above action is intended as a "feeler" for the American reaction prior to final action.

Comment: The American Military Assistance and Advisory Group in Japan believes the proposed cuts would seriously hurt the morale, training, and effectiveness of the Japanese defense forces and preclude for the present the activation of any new units.
