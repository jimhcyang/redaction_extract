The Front candidate in Salonika has a sizable electoral advantage and it seems likely that, regardless of current efforts by the government, some of the other Front candidates will also win run-off elections.

The prestige of the Papagos government has received a severe blow and the widespread discontent revealed by the voting will strengthen the opposition. The success of Popular Front techniques on the municipal level suggests that the same techniques will be used both in parliament and in the next parliamentary elections. Fragmentation of the Rally through internal splits has already appeared in parliament.

WESTERN EUROPE

7. French Communists reported joining forces with rightists against Mendes-France:
The French Communist Party plans to collaborate with the Gaullists and other parliamentary groups hostile to Premier Mendes-France to overthrow him at the first opportunity, according to a confidential source of the American embassy in Paris. André Ullman, the Communist editor of the crypto-Communist weekly Tribune des Nations, stated on 20 November, when he revealed this plan, that "henceforth all restraints in dealing with Mendes-France can be abandoned."

Comment: The increasingly hostile tone toward Mendes-France in the Soviet and French Communist press is another indication that the Communists have reached a decision to try to unseat the premier as a result of Soviet failure to block West German rearmament.
