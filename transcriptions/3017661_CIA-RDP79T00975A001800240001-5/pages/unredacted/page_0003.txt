SOVIET UNION

2. Comment on appointment of Jacob Malik to head Soviet delegation to UN:
The appointment of Jacob Malik to be temporary head of the Soviet UN delegation--a post he occupied from 1948 to 1952--will not alter Soviet policy in the UN, but its presentation will certainly lack the color and some of the effectiveness of the Vyshinsky approach.

Malik's diplomatic career began in 1937 as a member of the press section in the Commissariat for Foreign Affairs. Two years later he was sent to Tokyo as embassy counselor and in 1942 was made ambassador.
