3. EXPULSION OF LIBERAL PARTY LEADERS MAY BRING
DISTURBANCES IN HONDURAS

Acting with the approval of his cabinet, Honduran chief of state Lozano on 9 July exiled to Guatemala Liberal Party chief Villeda Morales and two other Liberal leaders, Francisco Milla Bermudez and Oscar Flores. This action reportedly followed Milla Bermudez's attempt to promote a general strike for 25 July. A number of student arrests reportedly were ordered after students voted to begin a three-day strike on 9 July.

Comment  The Liberal Party, with Villeda as its presidential candidate, polled 48 percent of the vote in the 1954 elections. Since May 1956 Villeda's Communist-influenced faction of the party has been increasingly aggressive in attacks on the government. A threatened general strike in mid-June was probably promoted by Villeda and Gonzalo Carias, son of an ex-dictator, who apparently have a working agreement to oust Lozano.

Key armed forces officials declared their loyalty to the Lozano government on 13 June.
