1. EGYPT ALERTS FORCES ON ISRAELI FRONTIER

Cairo is reinforcing its forces in the Gaza strip and is issuing additional military equipment such as live grenades and barbed wire.

Egypt's military command has restricted leaves, and raised its state of readiness to "number one."

Comment  Egypt's actions, which are probably defensive in intent, spring largely from immediate concern over Tel Aviv's delay in implementing the Israeli-Egyptian agreement that both sides withdraw from the El Auja demilitarized zone. Egypt considers General Burns' request for a further delay to be an indication that Israel is maneuvering to gain time to prepare for further aggressive action.

Defense Minister and Prime Minister-designate Ben-Gurion has recently issued several blunt statements to the effect that he would break the Egyptian blockade of the Israeli port of Elat at the head of the Gulf of Aqaba.

Egypt is also aware that its arms deal with the Soviet bloc has roused strong Israeli concern and may anticipate Israeli border action on that account.
