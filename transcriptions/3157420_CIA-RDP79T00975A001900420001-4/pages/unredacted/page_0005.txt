Comment: This report appears to reflect a belated recognition by Rhee of his country's increasing isolation in matters bearing upon future developments in the Far East, which may also account for his recent initiative toward resolving his disputes with Japan.

Rhee may feel new concern that his country's interests will be sacrificed in the general impulse to find peace in Asia. He may therefore believe that he must take steps to convince United States public and congressional opinion that there should be no world settlement which does not achieve Korean unification on terms acceptable to him.
