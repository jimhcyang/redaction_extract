McCloy believes that Adenauer would accept an arrangement whereby the Western Allies would give their assurances that at some "appropriate time" not too far distant they would see that Germany had the same relationship with NATO as other participants in the European Defense Community. As Adenauer would have to use such a promise in his dealings with German parliamentary leaders, it could not be kept secret.

Comment: It has been apparent for some weeks that the problem of Germany's relationship to NATO was worrying Adenauer. Although the matter is not an urgent one at the moment, the Chancellor will certainly require satisfactory answers when the European army plan comes before the Bundestag, where the prevailing view is that Germany must receive equal treatment.
