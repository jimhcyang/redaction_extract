Any newly elected Parliament, however, would probably be controlled by the Wafd; consequently, the government would have difficulty in escaping that party's influence. Ruling without Parliament could probably be accomplished only with the aid of martial law.

Shadowy connections exist between the Wafd and the Communists. Some Wafd leaders, such as former Foreign Minister Salaheddin, were opportunistically inclined to do business directly with the Soviet Union.
