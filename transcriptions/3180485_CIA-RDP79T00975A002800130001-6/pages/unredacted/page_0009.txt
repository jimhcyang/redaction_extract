3. USSR TELLS EGYPT FURTHER AID DELIVERY
IS IMPOSSIBLE

it was impossible for the USSR in present circumstances to send any further arms, fuel, or experts to Egypt because of the Anglo-French air-sea blockade.

The USSR apparently desires to minimize the risk to itself of direct military involvement in the Middle East. Despite rumors of the imminent arrival of Soviet war materiel and "volunteers," there has been no firm evidence that the USSR is preparing to take an active part in the fighting, and Soviet statements of support for Egypt have been noncommittal.
