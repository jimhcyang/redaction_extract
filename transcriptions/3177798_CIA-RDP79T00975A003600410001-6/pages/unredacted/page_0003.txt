17 APRIL 1958

I. THE COMMUNIST BLOC
Mikoyan states Soviet leaders overestimated pressure of world opinion to force summit meeting on Soviet terms.
Poland - Gomulka proposes tighter party control over workers.

II. ASIA-AFRICA
Labor unrest brewing in US-owned oil operations in eastern Saudi Arabia.
Nasir offers about $65,000,000 to settle Suez compensation issue; British look favorably on offer; company not sure.
Israeli armor in Jerusalem for 24 April 10th anniversary celebration will probably evoke protest by Jordan; incidents possible.
Nasir worried by lack of organization in Syrian sector of UAR; considering visit to Damascus.
Morocco - Istiqlal party government officials resign in effort to force King to accept Istiqlal monopoly of cabinet.

III. THE WEST
Britain and West Germany propose settlement of support-cost dispute; bigger problem of UK military ties with Continent remains.
Venezuela - Jockeying by military and civilian junta elements continues to threaten transition toward constitutional government.

LATE ITEM
Indonesia - Djakarta announces amphibious invasion of Central Sumatra's west coast began at dawn 17 April. Operation apparently proceeding successfully in accordance with schedule.
