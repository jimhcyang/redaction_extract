SOUTHEAST ASIA

2. Comment on Burmese withdrawal from four-power committee:

The withdrawal of the Burmese delegation on 17 September from the four-power committee in Bangkok appears to eliminate all possibility of a negotiated evacuation of Chinese Nationalist forces from Burma. After nearly four months of talks marked by increasing Burmese impatience with Taipei's procrastination, the walkout was precipitated by Chinese rejection of a demand that 5,000 troops be evacuated within 35 days of the signing of the evacuation plan and the rest within three months.

The Burmese government will now feel compelled to take strong action against the Nationalists. It is practically certain that it will seek UN condemnation of Taipei
