3. India increasingly concerned over Communist gains in Indochina:

Indian thinking is moving slowly away from the idea that the Indochina war is purely colonial and is becoming more aware of the danger of further Communist approaches to India's borders, according to Ambassador Allen in New Delhi. Allen believes three factors are responsible for this shift: (a) the force of events; (b) mild British coaxing; and (c) the concern voiced at the Colombo conference by other Asian states--notably Burma.
