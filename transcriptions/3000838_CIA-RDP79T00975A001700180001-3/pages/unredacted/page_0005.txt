Comment: The idea of uniting Afghanistan and Pakistan was broached at least as early as February 1954.

It has also recently been the subject of high-level discussion within the Pakistani government. There appears to be no immediate prospect of federation, however, because agreement on the details of such a union would probably overtax the capabilities and patience of both sides, even if the basic principle were accepted.

The prospect of federation--particularly if it involved American good offices--would evoke a bitter Soviet reaction, although this would probably fall short of direct military intervention. India also would probably react strongly against such a proposal. Neither Afghanistan nor Pakistan would be likely to agree to federation without a military build-up and a Western guarantee of their borders.
