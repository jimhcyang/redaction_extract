FAR EAST

Communist China about to establish diplomatic relations with
Nepal:

The Chinese Communist ambassador to
India is reported to have left for [[PRED_REDACTION_3]]Kat-
mandu[[/PRED_REDACTION_3]] on 25 July to arrange for the [[PRED_REDACTION_4]]es-
tablishment[[/PRED_REDACTION_4]] of diplomatic relations [[PRED_REDACTION_5]]be-
tween[[/PRED_REDACTION_5]] Communist China and Nepal. The Nepalese [[PRED_REDACTION_6]]charg[[/PRED_REDACTION_6]]é in New
Delhi believes that the question of [[PRED_REDACTION_7]]chanceries[[/PRED_REDACTION_7]] in [[PRED_REDACTION_8]]Katmandu[[/PRED_REDACTION_8]] and
Peiping will not be raised in these negotiations, which he expects
to last only a few days.

Comment: In the past few months the
Chinese Communists have again been pressing for the [[PRED_REDACTION_9]]establish-
ment[[/PRED_REDACTION_9]] of diplomatic relations with Nepal, and they are unlikely to
be satisfied for long without installing a mission in [[PRED_REDACTION_10]]Katmandu[[/PRED_REDACTION_10]].

India, which dominates Nepal's foreign
relations, has in the past been reluctant to permit the Nepalese
to enter into relations with China. Nehru recently indicated to
the Nepalese ambassador that he does not want a Chinese mission
in [[PRED_REDACTION_11]]Katmandu[[/PRED_REDACTION_11]]. In the current atmosphere of decreasing world [[PRED_REDACTION_12]]ten-
sion[[/PRED_REDACTION_12]], however, Nehru probably feels he cannot fend off Chinese
requests any longer.

SOUTHEAST ASIA

Fall of Indonesian government analyzed:

The American ambassador in Djakarta
sees three major factors in the [[PRED_REDACTION_13]]resig-
nation[[/PRED_REDACTION_13]] of Premier Ali Sastroamidjojo's
government:
(a) the army-cabinet impasse over the
chief-of-staff problem;
(b) the increasing concern over the [[PRED_REDACTION_14]]gov-
ernment's[[/PRED_REDACTION_14]] neglect of the country's deteriorating economy; and
[PAGE 05]
(c) the growing dissatisfaction with the
drift toward Communism as seen in Ali's and President Sukarno's
opportunistic collaboration with the Communists.

The ambassador believes the fall of the
Ali cabinet and the lessons of the army crisis will bring Indonesia
back to an "even keel."

Comment: While there had been signs of
growing discontent with the Ali government, it would probably not
have fallen if there had been no chief-of-staff problem.

The Ali cabinet will carry on in a [[PRED_REDACTION_15]]care-
taker[[/PRED_REDACTION_15]] capacity until a new cabinet is formed. In the past, [[PRED_REDACTION_16]]forma-
tion[[/PRED_REDACTION_16]] of a new cabinet has required from five to ten weeks.

Singapore chief minister threatens to resign:

The chief minister of Singapore's first [[PRED_REDACTION_17]]lo-
cally[[/PRED_REDACTION_17]] elected government, David Marshall,
has threatened to resign unless London [[PRED_REDACTION_18]]or-
ders[[/PRED_REDACTION_18]] the governor of Singapore to accept
Marshall's advice rather than merely "[[PRED_REDACTION_19]]con-
sult[[/PRED_REDACTION_19]]" him, as required by the constitution. A motion to this effect
is now before the Singapore assembly.

The American consul general believes
that if Marshall resigns and a new election is held, the resulting
government would be further to the left than the present one.

Marshall told the consul he advanced the
motion in an effort to seize the initiative from the Communist-front
People's Action Party, which is capitalizing on the frustrations of
the government ministers.

Comment: The avowed intention of the People's Action Party is to unseat Marshall's Labor Front [[PRED_REDACTION_20]]govern-
ment[[/PRED_REDACTION_20]] and make new elections necessary. The party has provoked
a series of strikes and has used student unrest to keep Marshall off
balance almost continuously since his government was elected last
April.
[PAGE 06]
Marshall's drastic action apparently is
an effort to maintain his popular appeal and to counter pressure
from the People's Action Party--whether he continues in office
or stands again for election.

SOUTH ASIA

Comment on India's request to Portugal to close its legation in
New Delhi:

India's request to Portugal to close its
legation in New Delhi by 8 August [[PRED_REDACTION_21]]be-
cause[[/PRED_REDACTION_21]] of the Portuguese refusal to [[PRED_REDACTION_22]]nego-
tiate[[/PRED_REDACTION_22]] over Goa probably does not [[PRED_REDACTION_23]]fore-
shadow[[/PRED_REDACTION_23]] overt aggression by India against Portuguese enclaves.
Nehru is anxious to maintain his role as peacemaker in the [[PRED_REDACTION_24]]For-
mosa[[/PRED_REDACTION_24]] Straits and Indochina.

India's action is aimed at inducing [[PRED_REDACTION_25]]Portu-
gal[[/PRED_REDACTION_25]] to cede its territories on the subcontinent, but the closing of
the legation does not mean a break in diplomatic relations. India
closed its legation in Lisbon in June 1953 over this same issue.

In spite of political agitation in India for
more energetic action against Goa, the Congress Party Working
Committee passed a resolution on 23 July emphasizing that all
efforts to bring Goa into the Indian union must be peaceful and
that there should be no mass entry into Goa. This suggests that
the march of thousands of Indians proposed for 15 August may
actually turn out to be an insignificant demonstration.

6. West Germans pleased with summit conference:

West German representatives at Geneva were
in a "very happy mood" over the results
of the summit conference. Although
Bulganin's closing statement offered lit-
tle hope for German unification in the
near future, the important considera-
tion was that the subject would be dis-
cussed again at the foreign ministers[[/PRED_REDACTION_27]]'
[PAGE 08]
[[PRED_REDACTION_28]]meeting. Moreover, the summit conference had proved the un-
wavering unity of the three Western representatives in support of
German unification.

Comment: Chancellor Adenauer, at a
press conference on 25 July, called the summit conference a suc-
cess and praised the Western powers' flexibility and imagination
and support for German unification. Although West Germans gen-
erally did not expect the summit conference to make progress on
the unity issue, they do expect Adenauer to get some results on
his projected visit to Moscow[[/PRED_REDACTION_28]].
