It gives agricultural goals for the Sixth Five-Year Plan (1956-1960) which are very ambitious and probably unattainable, and which indicate that agriculture, on which the "new course" has concentrated, will continue to receive heavy emphasis.

It seems unlikely, however, that a plenum would have been called at this time solely to discuss agricultural and livestock production. The meeting took place shortly after the regime had apparently reached a decision to reaffirm the emphasis on heavy industrial production and at a time when decisions connected with projected West German rearmament and the increasingly sensitive Formosa situation may have been reached. The recall to Moscow of a number of Soviet ambassadors supports the possibility that foreign policy was one of the subjects discussed.

The plenum was apparently timed to precede the Supreme Soviet session, called for 3 February, at which any shifts in policy may become evident.
