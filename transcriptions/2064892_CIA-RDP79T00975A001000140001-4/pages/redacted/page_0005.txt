5. Greece again seeks participation in MEDO to protect interests in Cyprus:

Greek Prime Minister Papagos recently told Ambassador Peurifoy that he was disturbed over reports that a Middle East defense planning board will be established at Cyprus. He insisted that if this takes place without Greek representation, his government will be placed in an embarrassing position and the opposition will make it a major issue.

Papagos also stated that the participation of even one Greek officer as an observer or liaison representative would offset the criticism of the opposition.

Comment: The Greek Government has previously tried unsuccessfully to obtain full membership in the proposed Middle East defense organization. This is the first suggestion that Greece might be satisfied with anything less.
