2. MOROCCAN FINANCE MINISTER ESTIMATES ANTICIPATED
INCOME FROM US BASES

Moroccan minister of finance Benjelloun recently informed a member of the French National Assembly's Foreign Affairs Committee he considered an annual rental of 150 billion francs (about $430,000,000) a reasonable rental, considering the indispensability of the bases to United States security. Benjelloun reportedly indicated that he is planning to meet many of Morocco's obligations, particularly in the field of defense, with revenues from the rental of base sites to the United States.

Comment

Benjelloun is a member of the minority party participating in the present care-taker Moroccan government and does not necessarily represent the views of the sultan or the majority Istiqlal party. Nevertheless his suggestion is in line with other reports that the new Moroccan government will be bargaining sharply on the bases.

French officials have thought that Morocco would seek a rental large enough to cover its expected annual fiscal deficit--estimated by the French at $200,000,000.
