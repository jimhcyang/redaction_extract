5 APRIL 1958

I. THE COMMUNIST BLOC

USSR launches second ICBM test vehicle at Tyura Tam/Klyuchi ballistic missile range within week. Missile covered at least 3500 nautical miles.

Some reports of opposition to Khrushchev received. However, there is no indication at this time of an organized effort against him.

While Khrushchev says USSR will accept controls over nuclear test suspension, more likely Moscow will continue to insist on test suspension before control details are negotiated.

Hungarians give Khrushchev cool reception.

Technical and substantive problems may delay publication of new international Communist journal until July or September.

Cuba - Castro will probably attempt general strike about 7 April.

Bolivia - Conflict between Bolivian President and leftist labor leader continues.

Canada - Election results foreshadow some modification of Canadian policies.

Peiping receives somewhat negative reaction to suggestion that Asian diplomats volunteer for token physical labor on Chinese projects.

II. ASIA-AFRICA

Indonesia - Hints by government officials that Djakarta will shortly take some anti-Communist action probably are designed to curry favor with West.

Israelis likely to resume canal digging in Lake Hula area; further border clashes probable.

Gaza legislative council asks for membership in United Arab Republic at "right opportunity."
