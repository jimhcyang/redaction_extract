8. Finland and USSR to negotiate gold loan and settlement of trade credit:

A Finnish delegation headed by Trade Minister Aura left for Moscow on 25 January to negotiate a gold loan and a settlement of Finland's trade credit with the USSR.

Comment: Finland anticipates an export surplus of some $15,000,000 with the Soviet Union during 1954, and hopes to obtain a Soviet commitment to settle all or part of this credit in sterling or gold.

The Finns have shown some interest in the Soviet offer of a gold loan, which would permit them to expand their non-woodworking industries. These have not been covered by loans from the International Bank for Reconstruction and Development.
