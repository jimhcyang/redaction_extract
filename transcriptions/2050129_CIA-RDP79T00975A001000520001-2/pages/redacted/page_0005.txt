6. Anglo-Iranian Oil Company plans to ship Arab oil to Israel:

The British Foreign Office has recently learned that the Anglo-Iranian Oil Company plans to ship about 125,000 barrels of oil from Kuwait to Israel in 1953, according to American Minister Holmes in London. He gained the impression that no Kuwait oil had been shipped to Israel in 1952, and that although shipments were made in 1952 from the Sheikhdom of Qatar, none is planned in 1953.

Comment: The American Consul in Haifa previously reported that 1,800,000 barrels of Kuwait oil were shipped to Israel in 1952 via South Africa. If the Arabs learn of these transactions, more anti-Western sentiment will develop and some retaliatory action may be taken.

7. British military want to station armored brigade in Jordan:

The Chief of the British Imperial General Staff is pressing for his government's approval of a War Office plan to station an armored brigade in Jordan. The Jordanian Government agreed to this proposal during recent conversations with the Commander in Chief of the British Middle East land forces.

Comment: The proposed move is part of the War Office plans for evacuating troops from Suez. In addition, among other arrangements, the current plans include retaining 5,000 troops at the Canal base and dividing one infantry division between Cyprus and Libya.
