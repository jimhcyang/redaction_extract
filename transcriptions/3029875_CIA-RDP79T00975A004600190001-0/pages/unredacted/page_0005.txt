Burma: The army-controlled administration in Burma, now nine months old, has made a vigorous approach to economic and political reforms and has taken an increasingly open pro-Western stand. Dissidence by the Shan ethnic minority and disagreement among army leaders on how best to perpetuate army control, when and if Prime Minister Ne Win steps down, are the principal domestic political issues now facing the regime. Afghanistan--USSR: Afghanistan and the USSR on 18 July announced a new agreement under which Soviet technicians will assist in the construction of three motor-road-bridges in the eastern province of Ningrahar, near the Pakistani frontier. This activity would place Soviet personnel in sensitive tribal territory where disorders occurred last December in protest against government road-building operations. (Page 3) (Map)
