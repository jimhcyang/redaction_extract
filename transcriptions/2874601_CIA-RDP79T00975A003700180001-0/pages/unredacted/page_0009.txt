The prospects for a political compromise have been dimmed considerably. Opposition leaders, including Rashid Karamah, insist "Chamoun must go." The "Third Force," a group of moderate politicians who have been trying to mediate between the government and the opposition, denounced the Chamoun regime on 20 May for "ignoring any political solution which might restore peace in Lebanon." On the other hand, Prime Minister Sami Sulh, in a desk-thumping interview with the press, ruled out any compromise with the pro-Egyptian opposition and directly accused the UAR of supporting opposition elements by actively interfering in the internal crisis and furnishing weapons.
