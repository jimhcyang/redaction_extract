THE ARAB-ISRAELI SITUATION
(Information as of 1700, 8 January)

UN truce supervisor General Burns has concluded that developments are leading toward general hostilities between Israel and Egypt. Burns told the American embassy in Tel Aviv on 3 January he believed Nasr was too smart to launch a war against Israel until Egypt was prepared, probably in two or three years, although certain of his subordinates might not exercise the same restraint. Burns thought there was a good chance the Israelis would precipitate a war in the next several months. The embassy comments it does not believe any decision for preventive action has been taken by Israel, but that it is probable that if Israel's concern for its air defenses is not alleviated, Tel Aviv would be forced to consider the possibility of preventive action.
