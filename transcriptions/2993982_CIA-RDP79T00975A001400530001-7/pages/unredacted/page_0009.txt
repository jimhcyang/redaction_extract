LATIN AMERICA

10. Outlawed Venezuelan party may attempt revolution during Caracas conference:
The outlawed Venezuelan Democratic Action Party (AD) may be planning a revolution in the western oil city of Maracaibo next month.

They believe that the party will take advantage of the government's concentration on preventing disorders in Caracas while the inter-American conference is in session there. They note that revolutionary movements have usually started in Maracaibo and that considerable antigovernment sentiment exists there now. They doubt, however, that an AD attempt at this time would succeed.

Comment:
The party has been plotting to regain power ever since it was overthrown in 1948 but is not believed to be sufficiently recovered from the government's campaign of suppression to stage a successful revolt. There may be disturbances, however, fomented by the AD or either of two other disaffected groups.
