2. SOVIET MECHANIZED UNITS MOVE OUT OF BUDAPEST

During the evening of 8 November there
was a steady movement of Soviet tanks
and other motorized equipment both north
and west out of Budapest. This has given
rise to speculation about a move against
the West or an attempt to bolster the
Austrian border.

Comment The termination of large-scale resistance
in Budapest may be the occasion for this
move. One of the Soviet divisions probably involved in quelling the riots normally is located to the west on the Austrian-Hungarian border. There is no evidence of substantial resistance to the north of the city or in Czechoslovakia near the Hungarian border.

The USSR's reaction to Austria's strong
condemnation of Soviet aggression in Hungary and alleged
Austrian "interference" is not likely to go beyond a propaganda tirade or a Soviet show of force on the border.

Reports of other Soviet ground force movements--massing on the Polish border and tank units entering
Bulgaria--have not been confirmed.
