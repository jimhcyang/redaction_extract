4. CHOU EN-LAI MAKES NEW OFFER TO NEGOTIATE WITH NATIONALISTS

Chou En-lai, in a speech to the Communist Chinese National People's Congress on 28 June, reaffirmed Peiping's position that Taiwan must be "liberated" and claimed that the possibility of a peaceful solution "is increasing."

Expanding on his offer of July 1955 to negotiate with the "Taiwan authorities," Chou invited the Nationalists to name the time and place for talks about "specific steps and conditions" for "peaceful liberation." He repeated earlier promises of good treatment for "meritorious" Nationalists.

Chou's appeal is unlikely to influence the Nationalist government, which has vigorously rejected previous Communist overtures, but his mild tone will probably draw a favorable reaction from neutralist countries, many of which already support Peiping's claims to Taiwan.
