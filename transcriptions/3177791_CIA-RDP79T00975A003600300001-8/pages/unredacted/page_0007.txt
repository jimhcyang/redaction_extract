Paraguay: Although the minor revolt at Encarnacion on 1 April was easily crushed, the position of President Stroessner, the last remaining dictator in South America, seems weaker than at any time since he took office in 1954. Dissidence within the ruling Colorado party has increased, although under control for the time being; Stroessner seems to have endangered his vital military support; and the Catholic Church has for the first time criticized his regime.
