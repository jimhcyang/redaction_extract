CENTRAL INTELLIGENCE BULLETIN
4 April 1958

DAILY BRIEF

I. THE COMMUNIST BLOC

Communist China - Japan: The Chinese Communists have leveled bitter criticisms against Japanese Prime Minister Kishi in an effort to forestall Tokyo's yielding to Taipei on the Communist flag issue. Peiping regards provisions in the Sino-Japanese trade agreement ,which give a trade agency in Tokyo the right to fly the Chinese Communist flag as a major breakthrough in its campaign to win diplomatic recognition from Japan. The Japanese are implicitly warned that failure to implement the flag provision may void the agreement.

East Germany: Ulbricht continues to maintain firm control in East Germany despite opposition from a number of influential party figures. There are indications that security controls are being tightened, but in general he is moving cautiously; he does not appear to be pressing the economic provisions of his hard-line policy.
