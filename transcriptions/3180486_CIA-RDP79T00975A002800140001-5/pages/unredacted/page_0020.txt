9. SUBMARINE DESTINED FOR EGYPT RETURNING TO POLAND

the Polish submarine M-107 and its Polish merchant ship escort reversed course on 1 November after passing through the Straits of Gibraltar en route to Egypt. These vessels had left the Mediterranean by 2 November, presumably to return to Polish naval headquarters at Gdynia.

The M-107, a Soviet-built coastal submarine, left the Baltic and apparently was the first of two submarines scheduled for delivery to Egypt under the Soviet-Egyptian arms deal. It is apparently manned by Soviet and Polish crewmen.

it was impossible for the USSR to deliver further aid to Egypt at present because of the Anglo-French air-sea blockade.
