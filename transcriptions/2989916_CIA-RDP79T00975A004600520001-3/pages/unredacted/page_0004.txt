CENTRAL INTELLIGENCE BULLETIN
29 August 1959

DAILY BRIEF

I. THE COMMUNIST BLOC

Taiwan Strait--Special Watch Committee conclusion: On the basis of findings by a special meeting of its Watch Committee on 28 August concerning developments relating to the Taiwan Strait area, the United States Intelligence Board concludes that:

Available information on air, ground, and naval activities does not indicate that a major military undertaking by the Communists is in preparation for the immediate future. It is therefore considered likely that Chinese Communist activities noted in the area are related to one or more of the following:

(a) typhoons and flood damages;
(b) preparations for Chinese Communist military exercises;
(c) preparatory planning for some step-up of military activity in the Strait area, the nature or timing of which is not yet evident from available information.

Communist China - India: The border clashes between Chinese Communist and Indian troops publicized by Nehru in the Indian Parliament on 28 August occurred in areas of northeast Kashmir and Assam which Peiping claims are Chinese Communist territory. Nehru, who is publicly committed to defense of the frontier areas involved, has turned the Assam problem over to military commanders.
