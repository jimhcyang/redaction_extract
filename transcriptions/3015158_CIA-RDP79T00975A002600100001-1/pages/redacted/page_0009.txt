6. ASSASSINATION THREAT REPORTEDLY CAUSED RHEE
TO CANCEL ADDRESS

President Rhee failed to make his scheduled Memorial Day address on 6 June, reportedly because he had received information that he and Assembly Speaker Yi Ki-pung were in danger of assassination.

Comment

President Rhee's action reflects the atmosphere of tension which has prevailed in South Korea since the election. Vice-President-elect Chang Myon is also reported being heavily guarded.

Opposition assemblymen have demanded an investigation into the alleged use of bribes to secure the reelection on 8 June of Yi Ki-pung, Rhee's defeated vice-presidential running mate, as speaker of the assembly.
