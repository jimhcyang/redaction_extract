Following up the recent French agreement to resume shipments of jet planes to Egypt, France has reportedly signed contracts to sell Mystere Mark II aircraft to Israel, according to a Paris press report of 26 November. However, the French counselor in Tel Aviv told an American embassy official on 23 November that Israel is no longer interested in obtaining Mark II Mysteres because there is doubt that these planes can compete with MIG-15's. Israel, he said, would like to get Mark IV Mysteres but the French government has thus far made no commitment to deliver Mark IV's. It is part of the French plan to balance arms shipments to Egypt by authorizing delivery of Mystere jets to Israel. Israel is most anxious to obtain F-86 jet aircraft from the US and from Canada. Israeli officials have suggested to the American air attaché in Tel Aviv that if the F-86's were delivered, Israel might drop efforts to procure Mysteres.
