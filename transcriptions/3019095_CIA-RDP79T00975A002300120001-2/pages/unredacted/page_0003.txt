1. PRESIDENT DIEM'S BROTHER COMMENTS ON SEATO

President Diem's brother and principal adviser, Ngo Dinh Nhu, believes the military value of SEATO is greatly overrated by British Commissioner General for Southeast Asia Sir Robert Scott, who recently visited the Indochina states. Nhu thinks Scott's suggestion of the possible future adherence of Indonesia and Burma to SEATO is academic and observes that combining militarily weak states in Southeast Asia to the neutralist states would be adding "zero to zero."

Comment

President Diem has recently been showing interest in a more formal association with SEATO. However, his brother's opinions, which normally carry weight in the development of South Vietnamese policy, may cause Diem to temper his attitude toward the Manila pact organization.
