GENERAL
1. Disillusionment prevalent at Vienna Peace Congress:

Vienna Peace Congress attendance of delegates at congress meetings was poor, much to the consternation of the Communist organizers.
Delegates criticized the repetitious speeches, the lack of constructive results and the absence of discussion committees.

Comment: In attempting to pursue the Kremlin directive for a "mass organization," the organizers apparently kept the agenda vague in order to win support from the largest possible number of non-Communists. Consequently, the Congress suffered from lack of purpose. Satellite delegates were criticized for treating the occasion as a shopping opportunity, while certain non-Communists, whose attendance had been encouraged, were not deterred from making outspoken attacks on Soviet policies.
