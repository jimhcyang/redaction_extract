3. Viet Minh stresses guerrilla operations in Tonkin:
The Viet Minh high command is striving to keep its infiltrated regiments in the Tonkin delta until the rainy season, when it is expected that the French will be forced to relax their pressure, according to a reliable source.

Viet Minh units north of the delta have the mission of diverting French troops from current clearing operations and may undertake a limited offensive to reduce the size of the French-held area.

Comment: The flooding of the delta during the summer rains, while curtailing the operations of both the French and Viet Minh, is more harmful to the French in that it handicaps their air and armor capabilities more drastically than it does the Viet Minh's guerrilla warfare.

Recent reports state that the Viet Minh forces north of the delta, comprising more than two battle-worn divisions are regrouping.
