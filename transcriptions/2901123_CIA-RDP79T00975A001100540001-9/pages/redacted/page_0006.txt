SOUTHEAST ASIA

6. Bao Dai plans ultimatum to French on Vietnam army build-up:

Vietnam's new defense minister has informed the American consul in Hanoi that Bao Dai is currently working on a plan for a Vietnamese army of 500,000 within the next three years. Bao Dai reportedly stated that the present Vietnamese military establishment is stagnating and that the time has come to make a "last offer" to the French, indicating that unless they support his army plan, he will turn to the United States.

Comment: The French-Vietnamese program for the build-up of the Vietnam army is considerably behind schedule. Bao Dai's principal purpose in making demands on the French is probably to strengthen his political position in Vietnam.

Assuming adequate foreign aid, a Vietnam army of 500,000 is conceivable, but unless the government gains greater popularity the reliability of such an army would be questionable.
