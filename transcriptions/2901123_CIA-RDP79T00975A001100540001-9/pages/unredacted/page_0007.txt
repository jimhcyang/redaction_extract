EASTERN EUROPE

8. Czechoslovakia opens first aluminum plant:
Comment: This plant, the first of its kind in Czechoslovakia, had been scheduled to go into operation next October with an initial annual production of 3,000 to 4,000 tons based on imports of semi-processed Hungarian ore. By 1955 the plant is scheduled to reach its maximum production of approximately 40,000 tons a year, or about seven percent of the output forecast for the Orbit. A substantial part of this production will probably be used in Czechoslovakia's expanding aircraft and war industries.

The planned expansion of Satellite aluminum output should eliminate by 1955 the bloc's dependence on imports.
