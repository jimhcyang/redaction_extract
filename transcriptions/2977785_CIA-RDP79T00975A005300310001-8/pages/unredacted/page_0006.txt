I. THE WEST

Cuba: Fidel Castro's experience at the United Nations has apparently strengthened his view of himself as a leader of the peoples of all underdeveloped countries in their "struggle against imperialism." He evidently plans to return to the UN. Upon his 28 September departure from New York he said he would return in a few weeks; New York hotel of- ficials reported on 3 October that Cuba is seeking reservations beginning 23 October for a delegation that may be headed either by Fidel or Raul Castro. (Page 6)

Cuba: Instructions have been sent by the National Bank of Cuba to all New York banks concerned, to transfer to the United Kingdom or to Canada balances held for the account of Cuban banks. The amount of balances and securities held by Cuban banks in New York banks as of 31 August is reported to be slightly in excess of $1,300,000. Furthermore, the Inter- national Monetary Fund (IMF) was likewise instructed to dispose of and transfer to the Bank of England, the proceeds of $1,645,000 held in gold by the Fund for the Bank of Cuba. The transfer of Cuban assets out of the US is probably to safeguard them from freezing action by US. The transfer of Cuban gold held by the IMF may presage Cuba's withdrawal from the Fund.

On 3 October, the National Bank of Cuba purchased in New York the equivalent of nearly $4,500,000 in pounds sterling and ordered its deposit in London to the account of the Czech State Bank. This transaction was probably a payment for Bloc arms.
