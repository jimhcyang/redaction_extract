Cyprus: Tension on Cyprus continues with acts of violence and sabotage. The wounding of a British serviceman on 14 April was the first attack on a Briton in more than a year and may foreshadow more serious attacks. There have been riots in detention camps, a 24-hour general strike, and an expanding boycott of British goods accompanied by threats and warnings. The Turkish Cypriot underground has demanded the immediate withdrawal of Governor Foot because of his alleged pro-Greek activities. Active diplomatic efforts to solve the Cyprus problem are in abeyance pending the 11 May Greek elections.

Gaza strip: Demonstrations which took place on 14 April in the Gaza strip reflect a move on Nasir's part to turn Palestinian Arab opinion against King Husayn of Jordan and possibly to pave the way for the adherence of the Gaza strip to the United Arab Republic. There is no indication, however, that Nasir has yet decided on a date.

III. THE WEST

West Germany - Summit talks: Chancellor Adenauer evidently is so deeply concerned over the serious division within West Germany on the issue of equipping the Bundeswehr with nuclear weapons that he believes the main topic of a summit conference should be disarmament. Adenauer now holds that other topics, including the German reunification issue, are secondary to the importance of achieving some relaxation of tension, particularly through an agreement on nuclear weapons.
