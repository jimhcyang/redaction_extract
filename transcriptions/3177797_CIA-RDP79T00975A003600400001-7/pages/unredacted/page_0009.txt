II. ASIA-AFRICA

Indonesia

a naval task force has been organized for an early sea-borne attack on the west coast of Central Sumatra. the force "amphibious Task Force 17," while the other states that it is under the command of Lt. Col. John Lie, captain of the navy's flagship, the destroyer Gadja Mada, and indicates main naval units are actually on station off Padang.

The amphibious attack, originally scheduled for 15 April, was postponed, apparently for a few days, because of logistical and transportation problems. According to a report originating with a source in the army chief of staff's office, the assault from the sea will be coordinated with paratroop drops and will be timed to coincide with the capture of towns on the approaches to Padang and Bukittinggi by elements advancing overland from the east and the south.

Djakarta claims that one of the columns advancing overland has taken Muara Mahat, a village on the road to Bukittinggi, which had been strongly defended for several days by the dissidents. Another column to the south is said to be nearing the junction controlling the dissidents' supply route to South Sumatra. The central government has also claimed further advances by elements invading Tapanuli Province to the north of Padang and Bukittinggi.
