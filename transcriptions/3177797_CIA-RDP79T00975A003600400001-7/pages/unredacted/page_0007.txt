I. THE COMMUNIST BLOC

India Again Considering Civil Air Agreement With Communist China

Indian Prime Minister Nehru is again considering the advisability of concluding a civil air agreement with Communist China. the Chinese have complained to Nehru that India's recent air agreement with the USSR and New Delhi's favoritism toward air services to Europe have "slighted" Peiping.

Officials of the government-controlled Indian international airline seem to feel that air service to China would be uneconomical and that the two new Super-Constellations soon to be delivered to India will be required on the Soviet route. Economic objections raised by India in connection with the Indo-Soviet agreement were met by the Russians, who agreed to share their profits with India, and China might be willing to make similar concessions.

Peiping has in the past few weeks adopted an unusually friendly attitude toward talks on Himalayan border problems, a visit by Nehru to Tibet, and a civil air agreement. It is possible, however, that if Nehru feels he can define the Indo-Tibetan border and expand relations with Tibet, he might favor an air agreement with Peiping.
