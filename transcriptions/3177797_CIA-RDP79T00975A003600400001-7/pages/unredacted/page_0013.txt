CENTRAL INTELLIGENCE BULLETIN
16 April 1958

DAILY BRIEF

I. THE COMMUNIST BLOC

Communist China - India: Peiping has again raised with New Delhi the subject of a civil air agreement. At present, Burma is the only nonbloc Asian nation with which Communist China has an air agreement. New Delhi has just signed an air agreement with the USSR and may accede to Peiping's request. (Page 1)

USSR - Latin America: The highest ranking delegations from the USSR and the Eastern European satellites to visit Latin America in recent years will attend the inauguration of Argentine President-elect Frondizi on 1 May. The Soviet delegation will include First Deputy Foreign Minister Kuznetsov, who will also visit Mexico briefly. The bloc representation will include Rumania, Poland, Czechoslovakia, Hungary, and Bulgaria, and will add impetus to the bloc's intensified efforts to increase trade and diplomatic relations with Latin America.

Gaza strip: Demonstrations which took place on 14 April in the Gaza strip reflect a move on Nasir's part to turn Palestinian Arab opinion against King Husayn of Jordan and possibly to pave the way for the adherence of the Gaza strip to the United Arab Republic. There is no indication, however, that Nasir has yet decided on a date.
