FAR EAST

3. Comment on expected no-confidence motion against Japanese foreign minister:

Japanese foreign minister Okazaki, a key figure in current MSA negotiations, is faced in the Diet with a Socialist-inspired motion of no confidence. The vote will probably be scheduled for the next few days. It would seriously threaten Premier Yoshida, since it could logically be interpreted as a lack of confidence in the present cabinet. In the light of Yoshida's record, he might call for a new general election.

The attitude of the Progressive Party, a conservative opposition group, on the motion has not been made known. It is unwilling to face new elections in the immediate future and as the price for its support will probably seek concessions from Yoshida's Liberal Party in their negotiations looking toward a coalition.
