1. SOVIET JET HEAVY BOMBER SPECIALLY PREPARED
FOR HIGH-YIELD NUCLEAR WEAPON DELIVERY OR
OTHER TEST ACTIVITIES

Comment  This is the first report of a Soviet
aircraft with its underside painted
white. The US has successfully used white paint to re-
duce to safe limits the effects of thermal radiation on the
surfaces of aircraft associated with the delivery of high-
yield weapons.

On 2 November heavy bombers were
sighted on the airfield at Ramenskoye, a flight test center
of the Ministry of Aviation Industry. On 16 November a
high-performance aircraft was scheduled from this field
to Semipalatinsk, where tests of air-dropped, large-yield
nuclear weapons were in progress. The coincidence be-
tween the first sighting of a white-painted aircraft and the
first Soviet air drops of high-yield weapons suggests that
the aircraft apparently landing at Ramenskoye, southeast
of Moscow, may have been used in weapon drops or in
other activities connected with the recent Soviet nuclear
weapons tests. The sighting in any case indicates that at
least one BISON has been specially prepared for deliver-
ing or participating in tests of high-yield nuclear weapons.

(Prepared jointly by OCI and OSI)
