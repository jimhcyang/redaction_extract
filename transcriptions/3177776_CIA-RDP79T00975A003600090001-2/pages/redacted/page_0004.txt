India - USSR: The USSR may provide financial aid for one of the two oil refineries the Indian Government intends to build in eastern India. This was intimated privately by the Indian minister for mines and fuel early in 1958, and was publicly expressed on 8 March by the leader of a Soviet parliamentary delegation now touring India. Soviet interest probably will center on a refinery planned west of Calcutta in Bihar State, since Rumania is reported to have made a firm aid offer for a small one to be built in Assam. (Map)
