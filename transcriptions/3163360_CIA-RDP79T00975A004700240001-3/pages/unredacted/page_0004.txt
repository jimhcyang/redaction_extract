CENTRAL INTELLIGENCE BULLETIN
28 September 1959

DAILY BRIEF

I. THE COMMUNIST BLOC

Chinese Communist anniversary celebrations: The USSR is sending a particularly high-level delegation to the Chinese Communist tenth anniversary celebrations in Peiping. Under Khrushchev, the Soviet group includes party secretary Suslov, Foreign Minister Gromyko, and secret police chief Shelepin, but no top-ranking military officials. The Asian satellites are to be represented by their top party leaders. In contrast, most delegations from the Eastern European satellites are headed by their titular heads of state and include no top-level political figures, except for Albanian Premier Shehu and Czechoslovak President Novotny. Peiping is likely to interpret the composition of the Eastern European delegations as a snub, but Moscow may have desired this type of representation in order to facilitate private talks between the Soviet and Chinese leaders.

USSR-Algeria-France: Moscow is continuing its efforts to convince the Arab world that it is on the side of the Algerian rebels, but it is avoiding measures which would worsen relations with France. Soviet officials have informed Arab diplomats in Moscow that the USSR is opposed to De Gaulle's proposal for an Algerian settlement and have offered to respond to requests for assistance to the Algerians through Soviet bloc "social organizations." At the same time, the Soviet Government has reiterated its refusal to extend diplomatic recognition to the Algerian rebel government since it does not want "to provoke France while Khrushchev is striving to achieve world peace."
