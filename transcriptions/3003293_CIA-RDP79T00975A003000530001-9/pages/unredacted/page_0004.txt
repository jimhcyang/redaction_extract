1. THE JORDANIAN SITUATION

Comment on:

The position of the Khalidi cabinet appears very insecure as a result of West Jordanian demands that it resign and of an intensive campaign being waged against it by Egyptian and Syrian propaganda media. Reports that its members have actually submitted their resignations, however, have been officially denied.

In Damascus, former chiefs of staff Nuwar and Hiyari are reportedly meeting with Syrian leftist leader Hawrani, according to the American army attache there. The presence in Damascus of Major General Maayta, who has been reported as having left his post as chief of the Jordanian security force, is unconfirmed. The attache notes, however, that during a previous stay in Damascus, Maayta was considered a leftist sympathizer and friend of Syrian army intelligence chief Sarraj.
