3. COMMENT ON CRISIS IN GREEK-AMERICAN RELATIONS

The Greek press is launching a bitter attack on the United States as a result of the American vote on 21 September against inscription of the Cyprus issue on the agenda of the UN General Assembly.

Both Greek foreign minister Stephanopoulos in New York and Deputy Premier Kanellopoulos in Athens reacted strongly to the American decision and predicted dire developments when given advance information of it. Reports from Athens, including a statement by Ambassador Cannon, suggest, however, that local Greek security forces are probably capable and desirous of handling any demonstrations. British forces on Cyprus are likewise adequate for maintaining local order.

Aside from the possibility of isolated acts of terrorism, the more serious longer-range threat would appear to be the rising trend toward neutralism in Greece, which will be given strong encouragement by the sense of frustration and abandonment now rife in Greek political circles.
