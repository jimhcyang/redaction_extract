Most of the current trouble is in the mechanical, metallurgical, mining, chemical, and ceramics industries. The strikes seem to be following a carefully planned pattern, taking place in different industries on different days.

Comment: Labor unrest in Italy, besides slowing down the defense production program, is also likely to jeopardize the chances of the present government parties in the coming spring elections in the south.

The Communist-promoted "chain-reaction" pattern of strikes began over a month ago after the Fiat factory in Turin fired a number of employees for engaging in political activity while at work. The rising dissatisfaction among factory workers over the discrepancy between wages and prices has made it easy for Communist agitators to induce many non-Communists to join in the strikes.
