Khrushchev told French leaders last month
the USSR would rather have 50,000,000 Germans against it and
20,000,000 where they are now than have 70,000,000 Germans
united against it. Despite such recent assertions, the USSR,
with an eye on the 1957 elections in West Germany, apparently
intends to keep encouraging Social Democratic hopes that a
basis for agreement on unification and neutrality can be found.
In doing this, Moscow's objective is to strengthen the SPD's
efforts to develop a convincing alternative to Chancellor Ade-
nauer's policy on unification.
