23 February 1961
CENTRAL INTELLIGENCE BULLETIN

Note: Items 1, 2, 7, 8, and 10 appeared in the Daily Brief for 22 February. Backup material has been added in this issue.

1. USSR: New 2,000-nautical-mile IRBM has undergone extensive flight testing since last June. (Page 1)

2. USSR - West Germany: Moscow's 17 February memorandum to Bonn repeats call for free city in West Berlin and a German peace treaty. (Page 2)

3. Congo: Leopoldville army officers reported preparing anti-Ileo coup. (Page 5)

4. Laos: Souvanna Phouma's speech on arrival in Xieng Khouang blames US for preventing national harmony in Laos. (Page 6)

5. Burma: Leftist-led riots may lead to further demonstrations. (Page 7)

6. North Vietnam: Premier Pham Van Dong relinquishes Foreign Ministry portfolio. (Page 7)

7. Portugal: Defense minister demands Salazar effect reforms in Lisbon's African colonies. (Page 8)

8. Saudi Arabia: Rivalries within royal family weaken position of King Saud. (Page 9)

9. Ghana: President Nkrumah endorses Western aid for Volta River hydroelectric project. (Page 9)

10. Watch Committee conclusions. (Page 10)
