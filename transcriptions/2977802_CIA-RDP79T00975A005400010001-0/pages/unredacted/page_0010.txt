Emphasis has been placed on West German shipments of producer goods--including steel pipe, chemical plant equipment, and machinery--and Soviet deliveries of raw materials and foodstuffs. West German chief negotiator von Zahn-Stranik has assured American officials in Bonn that he will firmly resist expected Soviet demands for a five-year agreement, but he indicated that the USSR, as in 1958, would probably not accept anything less than three years.

American officials have observed that although Bonn has successfully withstood the more unreasonable Soviet demands for high trade quotas, each succeeding round of talks has produced substantial increases, and they expect that this will continue to be the case in the future. Under the present agreement, quotas have generally been about 75 percent fulfilled.
