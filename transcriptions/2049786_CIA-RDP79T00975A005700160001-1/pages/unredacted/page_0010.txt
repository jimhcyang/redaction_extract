North Vietnam has formally asked that the Geneva co-chairmen take "urgent measures" to prevent further US aid for South Vietnam's struggle against Communist guerrillas. A government statement, issued in Hanoi on 12 May but signed by North Vietnam's foreign minister who is now in Geneva, charged that Vice President Johnson, now in Saigon, was discussing use of US troops in South Vietnam and that the situation constitutes a "most dangerous menace to peace in Indochina . . . at the moment when the situation in Laos is temporarily easing." This statement, coupled with previous statements linking Laotian and Vietnamese issues, seems to foreshadow a North Vietnamese effort to expand the present Geneva Conference to include discussion of Vietnamese reunification.
