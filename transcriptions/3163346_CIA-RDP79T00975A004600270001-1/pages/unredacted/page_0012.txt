Peiping Expresses Concern Over Indonesian Restrictions on
Overseas Chinese

Communist China is "very disturbed" over the recent Indonesian
decree prohibiting alien-owned retail enterprises outside metropolitan areas after the
end of the year. The ban was directed at Overseas Chinese,
who control most of the retail trade in Indonesian villages.

Peiping's displeasure was underscored by the fact that
prior to the ambassador's departure for Djakarta in mid-
July, Chen called on him personally to ask reconsideration
of the decree. While Chen's tone during the conversation
was friendly, he suggested that the decree might jeopardize
good relations and further economic aid to Indonesia. Chen
served notice that, if the ban is enforced, Peiping expects
full compensation for losses incurred by Overseas Chinese.

Djakarta may proceed cautiously in implementing the
decree, since its enforcement could have severe repercus-
sions on the Indonesian economy. Chinese merchants may
be able to evade the decree by hiring Indonesians as nominal
heads of their businesses.
