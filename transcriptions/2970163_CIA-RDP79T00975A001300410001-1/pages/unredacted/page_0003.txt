GENERAL

1. Peiping meeting commitment to export rice to USSR:

Comment: Peiping had contracted to deliver to the USSR 50,000 tons of rice in 1953. Peiping's fulfillment of this agreement should probably be interpreted as further evidence of a determination to pay for imports by maintaining food exports, despite relatively poor harvests, rather than as evidence of Soviet pressure. This desire was shown in barter talks this summer with Finland and Ceylon in which the Chinese insisted that both countries accept more foodstuffs than they desired.
