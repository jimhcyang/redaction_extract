20 MAY 1958

I. THE COMMUNIST BLOC
Reports of some opposition to Khrushchev received.

II. ASIA-AFRICA
Lebanon - UAR support for opposition continues.
Jordan cut off from fuel sources in Lebanon, drawing on military stocks; UAR message notes shortage of 100-octane gasoline in Syria.
Indonesia - Djakarta steps up attack near Gorontalo; says Peiping has offered "thousands of volunteers."
Ceylon to request up to $50,000,000 in foreign aid from Western sources.
Algerian movement now considered "irreversible."
Tunisia - Loyalty of French troops in Tunisia wavering.

III. THE WEST
France - De Gaulle's statements strengthen extremist elements in French military.
Britain will offer protection to its fishing trawlers if Iceland extends territorial limits to 12 miles.
Venezuela - Extensive government changes under way, with chances of de facto military take-over increased.
