North Vietnam's poor agricultural year in 1960 was due primarily to weather, although stepped-up socialization drives in the rural areas contributed to the present food difficulties. Prolonged drought damaged last year's spring harvest, and floods and insects affected the fall harvest, causing a drop in food production of 14 percent from the previous year's level.
