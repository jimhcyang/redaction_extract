Turkey- USSR: Foreign Minister Zorlu in a review of the Middle East situation with Ambassador Warren on 6 June emphasized Turkish concern over anticipated Soviet advances in the Middle East. He stated that if Turkey and its Western allies "must reach a modus vivendi with the USSR, we must do so in such a way as to keep the USSR out of this area."

On 15 May the Turkish ambassador in Moscow, reporting to Ankara on Western attitudes toward the USSR, had noted that "our principal allies are virtually racing with each other to tighten their contacts and ties with Soviet Russia." (Page 3)

UAR: The introduction of a unified new currency in both regions of the UAR is reportedly scheduled for 1 July. This attempt of Egypt to strengthen its control over the Syrian economy is apt to result in new strains and an intensification of the flight of Syrian capital.

The existence of separate currency systems in the UAR has been a major roadblock to rapid economic integration. (Page 4)

Pakistan: The continued decline in Pakistan's exports during recent months is posing a threat to the new government's efforts to improve economic conditions and is causing Pakistan to seek expanded barter trade with the Sino-Soviet bloc. Exports for the first quarter of 1959 were 32 percent below the first quarter of 1958. The decline in exports is forcing Karachi to restrict imports of raw materials and spare parts thereby limiting the growth of industrial production. (Page 5)
