II. ASIA-AFRICA

Turks Fear East-West Modus Vivendi Would Ignore Their Interests

Turkish Foreign Minister Zorlu told US Ambassador Warren on 6 June that if Turkey and its Western allies must reach a modus vivendi with the USSR, they should do so in such a way as to keep the USSR out of the Middle East. This is the first indication in recent years that Turkey sees any possibility of reaching an accommodation with the Soviet Union.

The Turks seem primarily concerned lest, in a general settlement, Middle Eastern questions may be resolved in a manner counter to Turkey's interests. Zorlu reiterated Turkey's concern over a possible Communist take-over in Iraq, urging an early meeting of American, British, and Turkish officials for the purpose of contingency planning. Such a meeting, if it became known, could have serious repercussions in both Iraq and the USSR.

The Turks may be re-examining their over-all policy toward the USSR--possibly to include more extensive cultural and educational ties--in the light of what they consider changes in Western attitudes. The Turkish ambassador to Moscow observed on 15 May to Ankara that cultural ties between the USSR and the Western world had widened and that "our principal allies are virtually racing with each other to tighten their contacts and ties with Soviet Russia."
