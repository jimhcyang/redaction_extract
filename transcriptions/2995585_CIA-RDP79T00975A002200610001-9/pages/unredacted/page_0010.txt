7. RIGHT WING OF BOLIVIAN GOVERNMENT PARTY REPORTEDLY TO ATTEMPT COUP

The right wing of the governing Nationalist Revolutionary Movement (MNR) in Bolivia, aided by the army and national police, plans to seize control of the government. Possible dates for the coup were given as 10 and 15 November.

The rightists' aim is reported as an attempt to forestall a possible coup by MNR leftists who have lost ground in recent party elections.

Comment
Since President Paz Estenssoro has invariably been associated with the MNR's right wing, it is unlikely that the reported coup is aimed at removing him from power. It may possibly be planned with his connivance as a preventive move. Paz' popularity with the rank and file of all party factions is such that any attempt to force him from office would probably precipitate civil war.
