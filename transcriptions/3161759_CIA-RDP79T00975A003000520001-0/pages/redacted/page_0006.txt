2. SOVIET NOTES ON THE MIDDLE EAST

The 19 April Soviet notes to the United
States, Britain and France appear to be
primarily an attempt to exploit for its
propaganda value in the Arab world the
Western refusal of the USSR's six-point
draft declaration of 11 February on the Middle East.

The USSR, while restating the validity of
its original declaration, now calls for a four-power declara-
tion condemning the use of force in the Near and Middle East
as a first step toward "normalization." It accuses the West
of refusing to seek an acceptable basis for a regional settle-
ment. The three notes are: not identical in their lines of argu-
ment, but they all reflect the USSR's preoccupation with pre-
venting unilateral Western intervention in the area. For the
most part, the notes merely recapitulate the established
Soviet position.
