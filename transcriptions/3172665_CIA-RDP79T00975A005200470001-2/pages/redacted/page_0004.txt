South Korea: The new government which Prime Minister Chang Myon has formed, largely from his faction of the Democratic party, is not strongly based. Chang controls slightly less than half of the ruling party's members in the House of Representatives, and his opponents, having rejected his offer of cabinet posts, have announced their intentions to form an opposition party--presumably with the objective of forcing the new cabinet from power. The factional conflict is basically the result of personal rivalries rather than policy differences, and continued maneuvering for personal advantage could undermine confidence in the post-revolution leadership.
