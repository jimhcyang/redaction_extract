2. WEST GERMAN PILOTS MAY ACCEPT SUEZ CANAL JOBS

estimates that 25 to 30 Kiel Canal pilots will accept Egyptian offers of Suez Canal jobs if their present wages are not substantially increased. The departure of a number would seriously handicap Kiel traffic, but only one has already signed up. The Egyptians have also approached river pilots, although Kiel Canal pilots are preferred since they would require less training on Suez operating procedures. Strong inducements have been offered the Germans, including a salary increase within two years to three times the present average Kiel salary.

Comment
The Egyptians, who have been trying to recruit foreign pilots, probably need at least 100 pilots to replace British and French pilots likely to resign if a negotiated settlement is not reached.
