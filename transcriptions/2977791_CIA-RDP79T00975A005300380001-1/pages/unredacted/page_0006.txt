Republic of the Congo: Colonel Mobutu's 13 October statement that he will not attempt to arrest Lumumba so long as he remains in his residence may preserve the uneasy political equilibrium in Leopoldville. There is speculation in Leopoldville that Mobutu may reconvene parliament with a view to stripping Lumumba of his parliamentary immunity. UN officials have stated that Lumumba cannot be legally arrested until such action is taken.

UAR Ambassador Ghaleb in Leopoldville continues to urge on UN representative Dayal policies designed to restore Lumumba as premier.

Laos: The Souvanna Phouma cabinet decided on 12 October to move the government to Luang Prabang, reportedly within the next ten days. The cabinet also reportedly determined that Laos would seek a loan from the USSR, if the United States did not give aid for the armed forces and the police. Soviet Ambassador-designate Abramov arrived in Vientiane on 13 October as scheduled. (Page 5)
