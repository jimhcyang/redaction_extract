This is the first time any responsible West German politician has supported the idea, occasionally discussed in the press, of all-German elections on a zonal basis, a procedure which some believe would be acceptable to Moscow. The Bundestag officially favors a single election district covering all of Germany.

Dehler, who has endorsed the decisions of the London conference, is evidently intent on gaining popular support for his party by putting it in the vanguard of renewed efforts at German unification. His plan will probably provoke a sharp debate and will be opposed by Chancellor Adenauer.
