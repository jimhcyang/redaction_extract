8 MARCH 1958

I. THE COMMUNIST BLOC
Khrushchev still faces opposition on agricultural reorganization.
Bulganin repeats call for early foreign ministers’ conference.
USSR agrees conditionally to procedural discussion of disarmament by Security Council.

II. ASIA-AFRICA
Djakarta continues deployments for attack on dissidents; Sukarno-Hatta talks postponed.
Egypt may plan build-up of forces on Sinai and Red Sea coast.
Lebanon - Chamoun's plan to seek re-election likely to precipitate serious disorder.
Okinawa - Leftists will probably make significant gains in 16 March legislative election.

III. THE WEST
France - Gaillard's proposal for Mediterranean pact hints some form of independent status for Algeria.
French decide "in principle" to undertake early reduction of forces in Morocco.
Announcement of Sandys' proposed visit to Moscow designed to meet British public demand for moves toward East-West detente.
West German leaders split on policy toward possible summit agenda.
