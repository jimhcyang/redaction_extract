Angolan natives now are determined to continue the fighting until independence is achieved even if they have to rely principally on arms captured from Portuguese military personnel. The Portuguese press claims that hundreds of rebel reinforcements are funneled into northern Angola from the Congo through a rebel-held corridor.

The Leopoldville headquarters of the Angola Peoples' Union (UPA) reportedly has little information on the fighting because it lacks communications facilities to coordinate the action. UPA lacked the means to extend the movement into Luanda and found no liberal European element with which African nationalists could combine forces.
