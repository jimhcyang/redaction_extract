DAILY BRIEF

I. THE COMMUNIST BLOC

II. ASIA-AFRICA

Japan: Leftist-led demonstrations in opposition to Prime Minister Kishi and the US-Japan security treaty have created an emotionally charged atmosphere which the director general of the Japanese National Police said on 8 June has seriously aggravated security problems related to the President's visit. He believes that the less extreme elements have lost their control of the opposition movement, which now has come to tolerate violence. While the great majority of the Japanese people welcome the President's visit, the director general believes there is a real possibility that extremists, despite security precautions, may subject the President to indignities.

Lebanon: The commander in chief of the Lebanese Army is disturbed at reports of army interference in the elections set for 12 June. He fears that if this is taking place extensively it will cause the army to split along religious lines, impairing its effectiveness and ability to maintain order. Disorders are expected during the elections.
