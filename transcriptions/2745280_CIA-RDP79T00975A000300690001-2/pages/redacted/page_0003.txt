GENERAL

1. British and French tone down opposition to US-Spanish ties:

During the tripartite meetings on 14 September, British Foreign Secretary Morrison urged the US to go no further than necessary in its relations with Spain, and under no circumstances to raise the question of bringing Spain into NATO. Morrison indicated that there was strong feeling in the UK on this issue.

French Foreign Minister Schuman said he believed that French opinion would be relieved by the US assurance that no attempt was being made to bring Spain into NATO. The main French fear, he said, would be a Spanish army equipped by the US. Such an army would be competing with France for supplies, and would make it appear that the US was basing Western defense on the Pyrenees.

Comment: Last July the British and French openly opposed any bilateral US-Spanish defense arrangements.

FAR EAST
