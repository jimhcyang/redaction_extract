5. Greece and Turkey strengthen fleets with British aid:

The Turkish government has reportedly accepted a British offer of a cruiser, according to Ambassador Warren.

Meanwhile British officials in Athens had announced that, in response to a Greek request, London had extended the period of the loan of eight British destroyers and four submarines for ten years. One of the provisos of the extension was that the United States pay the cost of modernization and maintenance.

This statement was released without prior discussion with the American embassy and despite the specific request of the Greek defense minister that public reference be deferred until the American position could be learned.
