Iraq: Members of Moslem extremist groups are said to be planning to assassinate Prime Minister Qasim sometime this month. Among Qasim's most vocal critics have been Iraq religious leaders, who have consistently opposed his past reliance on the Communists for support and the suppression of rightist religious political parties.

Moslem religious fanatics are more prone to desperate action to achieve their ends than are Arab nationalist elements.

Libya: The reorganization of Prime Minister Uthman's cabinet on 4 May appears aimed at heading off a new governmental crisis over graft and corruption among top officials. Members of the Uthman government have in their six months of power managed to find "legal" as well as sub rosa means for extracting large sums from several Western-owned oil companies.
