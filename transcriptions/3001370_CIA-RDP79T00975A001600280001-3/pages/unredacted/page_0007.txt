SOUTH ASIA

7. Pakistan faces foreign exchange crisis:

Senior Pakistani officials concerned with economic development have told American officials that they believe a critical shortage of foreign exchange threatens Pakistan's political and economic stability. Karachi estimates the gap between available dollar and sterling resources and requirements for essential imports during the coming fiscal year at more than $90,000,000.

The officials expressed the hope that the United States could assist in partially bridging the gap. They also hope to divert about $15,000,000 worth of American and Colombo plan aid to pay for industrial imports.

Comment: The Pakistani regime's ability to re-establish its political prestige now depends largely on easing quickly the stringent economic position of consumers--particularly since the 30 May imposition of governor's rule in East Pakistan and the concomitant promise of economic relief.

Diversion of present resources to consumer items would force the abandonment of the country's industrial and agricultural development plans and thus make for recurring financial crises.
