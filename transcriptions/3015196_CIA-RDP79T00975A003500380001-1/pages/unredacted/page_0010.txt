USSR Refuses to Change Payment Terms of Afghan
Oil Exploration Agreement

The USSR has turned down an Afghan request that financing of the joint Soviet-Afghan agreement for exploration and development of oil resources be changed from a loan to a grant basis.

The Afghan Embassy in Moscow reported on 5 January that in accordance with the original proposal the $15,000,000 cost of the project must be treated as a loan repayable in five annual installments starting in 1962, probably at 2.5-percent interest.

The agreement, announced last summer and signed in Kabul on 7 January 1958, stipulates that Moscow is to furnish technicians and equipment from the USSR, Czechoslovakia, and Rumania to explore oil resources in northern Afghanistan.

Afghanistan had informed the USSR and the US in December that economic difficulties forced it to seek grant aid instead of loans, excepting projects already agreed upon.
