West German Foreign Ministry officials favor offering India new credits of $72,000,000 for economic development programs, besides postponing payments on the principal of India's $144,000,000 debt. For political reasons Bonn would like to encourage further private investment in India by German firms. Bonn states that German firms are short of funds, and urges joint Western financial aid. (Page 7)

Britain will resume financial negotiations with Egypt on 30 January in Rome. Although the British representatives have considerable flexibility in their proposals, the negotiations will take some time, and the British are going to insist on prior concessions. Egypt appears ready for an agreement, and the British are looking forward to an eventual resumption of diplomatic relations with Cairo, which they believe will improve their relations with other Arab states.
