He noted that Nagib, who once gave the impression of being a thoughtful, well-read man, is now primarily concerned with his own popularity and with favorable publicity. The chargé added that Nagib's health has also suffered as a result of his arduous schedule in the role of figurehead for the regime, and that he has become more and more superficial and garrulous.

The chargé doubted that Nagib "now possesses the moral integrity to support an unpopular move" such as a compromise on the Suez base.

Comment: This characterization of Nagib is supported by some other sources. If correct, it raises doubts that Nagib could dominate or even control any government which he might head, especially if it included old line politicians.
