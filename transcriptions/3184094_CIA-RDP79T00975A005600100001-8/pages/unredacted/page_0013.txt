North Vietnam Orders Efforts to Break Up South
Vietnamese Elections

President Diem has predicted that the Communists will step up bombings and assassinations in South Vietnamese cities in an effort to win some control in urban as well as rural areas. There has recently been an upsurge in guerrilla activity, following a comparative lull since late December. For several months, however, there has been increasing emphasis by the Communists on political activity and agitation, including anti-Diem peasant demonstrations.

As part of an effort to counteract popular discontent, Diem is reorganizing his government and has pledged to add an elected representative from his loyal Republican Youth organization to the village councils, now entirely appointive. One of Diem's cabinet ministers has stated that the government's "only hope" is to end its "mandarin spirit" and effect a drastic improvement of officials at the highest and lowest levels.
