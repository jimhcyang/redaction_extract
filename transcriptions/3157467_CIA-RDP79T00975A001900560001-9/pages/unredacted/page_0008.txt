Comment: Sugihara has inspired Japan's current program for a quick rapprochement with the Orbit and is believed responsible for Prime Minister Hatoyama's campaign statements on foreign policy. The Foreign Ministry believes Sugihara will head the Japanese delegation to negotiate restoration of diplomatic relations with Moscow, and he reportedly is slated to become foreign minister after the elections.

He is the most prominent of an influential group of men around Hatoyama who had important roles in Japan's pre-World War II activities on the mainland. The thinking of this group is still in terms of a captive China market which no longer exists. Sugihara's ardor might be cooled, however, by the report of a recent Japanese trade mission to Peiping which was convinced that it would be difficult to expand trade with China at this time.

7. Comment on Chinese Communist threat to Nanchishan:

The Chinese Nationalists apparently intend to try to hold the small offshore island of Nanchishan, which was formerly part of the Tachen commando.

Taipei is planning to remove civilians from Nanchishan. The garrison of 2,500 regular troops and 700 guerrillas is to remain, however, and may be augmented by guerrillas evacuated from the Tachens.

Nanchishan, 85 miles southwest of the Tachens and only 16 miles from the mainland, can be dominated by Communist air power from the Shanghai-Ningpo complex just as the Tachens were. The Communists could be ready in a short time to undertake an amphibious operation against the island as well.
