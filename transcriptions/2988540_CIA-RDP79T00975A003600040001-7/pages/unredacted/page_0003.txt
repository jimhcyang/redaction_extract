5 MARCH 1958

I. THE COMMUNIST BLOC

Soviet Air Force activity returns to normal.

North Korea says it will deliver passengers of hijacked South Korean air liner at Panmunjom on 6 March.

Communist China extends Ceylon $10,500,000 flood rehabilitation loan.

II. ASIA-AFRICA

Indonesia - Djakarta warns it will bomb American- and Dutch-owned oil storage tanks in dissident-held areas. US charge warns of consequences. Djakarta expects to receive Soviet bloc jet fighters in April.

Jordan - Nasir sympathizers blow up presses of progovernment newspaper. Further violence likely.

King Saud says he will join neither the United Arab Republic nor the Iraqi-Jordanian federation.

Tunisian newspapers indicate impatience and suspicion of good offices efforts.

Moroccan Government requests US to prevent French materiel shipments into Morocco through US air bases.

India may accept Rumanian offer to construct oil refinery.

III. THE WEST

Norwegian cabinet group recommends thinning out East-West forces in Germany along lines of Rapacki plan.

Bolivia - President Siles Zuazo threatens to resign over opposition within his own party to US-backed economic stabilization program.
