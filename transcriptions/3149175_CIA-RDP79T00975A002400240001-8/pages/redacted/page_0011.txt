THE ARAB-ISRAELI SITUATION
(Information as of 1700, 15 February)

The American military attaché in Tel Aviv reports a minor selective mobilization of personnel and equipment with some movement to the south, including Eilat, at the mouth of the Gulf of Aqaba. He believes that this is a precautionary reinforcement in view of the recent increase of Egyptian forces in the Sinai.

Israeli chief of staff Dayan has told a visiting American officer that a policy of "activism" is the only one Israel can rely on. Dayan saw three alternatives in the present situation: (1) that Israel will be armed, thus forestalling Arab aggression; (2) that Egypt will attack soon; (3) that Egypt will defer action until the Arabs are stronger and better organized.

The Soviet freighter Admiral Ushakov arrived at Alexandria on 10 February and berthed in the port's security area. Cargo reportedly observed on the Ushakov's deck included several tanks and trucks and two torpedo boats. This ship has previously transported military equipment to Alexandria.
