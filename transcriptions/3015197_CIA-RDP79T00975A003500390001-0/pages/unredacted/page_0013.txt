Poland Continues to Push Rapacki Plan

The Polish ambassador to the United States has stated that Poland would be willing to accept "the most rigorous possible inspection procedures" under the Rapacki plan for a nuclear-free zone in central Europe, fully recognizing that many atomic weapons are quite small and easily concealed. The Polish Foreign Ministry has thus far refused to discuss control details but plans to elaborate further on control measures in order to keep the plan alive. The Poles have also explained that it does not necessarily entail West German dealings with East Germany.

Foreign Minister Rapacki has stated that withdrawal of all troops from Europe and the concept of a neutralized, reunified Germany are utopian ideas and has indicated that he did not expect his own plan to be acceptable to the West. The Polish ambassador in Washington indicated that Poland advanced the plan to prevent West Germany from gaining nuclear arms. The Poles also probably hope to gain prestige by authoring a first step to broader disarmament schemes, and to avoid Soviet demands for basing nuclear weapons in Poland.
