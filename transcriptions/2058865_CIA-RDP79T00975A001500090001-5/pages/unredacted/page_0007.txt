EASTERN EUROPE
7. Bulgaria complains to Moscow of Rumanian rail restrictions:
Recent restrictions imposed by Rumania on international rail traffic destined for Bulgaria "are causing serious difficulties" for the Bulgarian economy. Bulgarian railways "insist" on the abolition of the Rumanian restrictions.
The Rumanian regulations, announced on 7 January, limited to 50 a day the number of cars for Bulgaria accepted at the Hungarian border. According to Rumania, the severe limitation was imposed in order to reduce a backlog of 2,100 cars already being held for Bulgaria.
Comment: The Rumanian restrictions probably resulted from the temporary blocking of rail routes by heavy snowfalls. Bulgaria's direct appeal to Moscow indicates that friction continues to exist between the two Satellites. Further friction between Satellites was revealed threatening Hungary and Rumania with retaliation for nonfulfillment of meat shipments.
