LATIN AMERICA
7. Argentina appears interested in trade agreement with the USSR:
Argentine delegates to the Moscow Economic Conference discussed with Soviet officials a possible trade agreement which would provide for the exchange of leather, wool, casein, tungsten, and beryl for Russian machinery.

Comment: It was reliably reported last December that Argentina and the USSR were planning to conclude secret trade negotiations "in the near future." Delegates returning from the Moscow Economic Conference stated that the USSR was primarily interested in Argentine wool.

Direct trade between the two countries has heretofore been negligible.
