2. GUATEMALAN OPPOSITION PLANNING REVOLUTION

A developing plot against the government of Guatemalan president Castillo Armas has the support of exiles in Mexico, Costa Rica, and El Salvador, some of whom are now returning to Guatemala. Opposition elements inside Guatemala are also involved, and a group in the railroad workers' union is reportedly the best organized. The Communists have no direct part in the planning, but are to be assigned to assassinate Castillo and other members of the government.

Comment

President Castillo is being kept closely advised on the current plotting. He intends to wait until after the national holidays early in July before arresting the plotters. He believes he can control the planned revolutionary attempt and expects it to expose enemies whom he can then jail or exile.

Castillo believes that Colonel Enrique Oliva, a close army associate and brother of the defense minister, is involved in the plotting against him. Castillo appeared not to be worried over possible repercussions in the army over any action against Oliva. Most army officers are believed to be still loyal to the president.
