These men played the leading roles in exhorting the "leap" and in threatening those opposed to a headlong course. Party leaders who had seemed to have reservations about the "leap" were senior administrators and economic specialists, possibly including Premier Chou En-lai.

Mao appears still to dominate the party; the communique notes that the party plenum was held under Mao's "guidance," and it concludes by urging the party and people to unite under Mao's leadership. It is not yet clear whether Mao intends to fix any blame for the leap's shortfall on other party leaders rather than low-level cadres.
