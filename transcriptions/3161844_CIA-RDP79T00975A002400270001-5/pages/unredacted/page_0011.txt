7. CHINESE COMMUNIST AMBASSADOR LEAVES PAKISTAN

Comment - Following Pakistan's prompt response to Soviet premier Bulganin's recent offer of a trade pact and coming before the proposed visit of Pakistan's premier and foreign minister to Peiping, these departures may presage further Sino-Soviet bloc political and economic overtures to Pakistan. The Chinese Communists apparently hope to dramatize their desire for more cordial relations with Pakistan and may send new staff members more competent in economic and technical aid matters.

Han has served in Karachi since September 1951. He will probably be replaced by an official of higher rank.
