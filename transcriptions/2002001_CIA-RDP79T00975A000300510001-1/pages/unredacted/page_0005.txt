Comment: The USSR's delegate to this meeting has already stated that grain would be available to Western European countries in exchange for strategic commodities which were exported by them in 1949-50.

Soviet merchant shipping tonnage has been inadequate to meet the USSR's requirements, and Moscow has resorted to chartering Western vessels for many purposes. This desire for ships and tankers from Denmark may be partly explained by the USSR's current effort to utilize ocean transport from European ports in supplying Communist China, thus easing pressure on the Trans-Siberian Railroad and sources of supply in the Soviet Far East.
