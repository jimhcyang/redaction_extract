4. Kachin force trained by Chinese Communists may have entered Burma:

Chinese Communist-trained forces under the Burmese Kachin renegade Naw Seng had advanced to the vicinity of the "headwaters of the Irrawaddy River" in northern Burma. There they are reported to have established base camps and "joined up with the guerrillas," apparently to engage in political activities.

Comment: Naw Seng is a former Burmese army captain who fled to Communist China in 1950.

The Chinese Communists in Yunnan have been training a Kachin force under his command estimated to number approximately 1,500.
