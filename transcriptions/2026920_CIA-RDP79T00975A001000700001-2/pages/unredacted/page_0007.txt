6. Ibn Saud grants Farouk $500,000 annually:
King Ibn Saud sent $500,000 to former King Farouk some months ago and has allotted him a similar amount annually. He has also directed that Farouk be urged to continue to "have confidence" in him.

Comment: In allotting this aid, Ibn Saud runs a serious risk of damaging the good relations he has promoted with the Nagib government in Cairo.

7. Israel asks United States for $70,000,000 grant:
Ambassador Eban and three other Israeli officials in Washington have asked the State Department for a grant of approximately $70,000,000 to meet debts falling due between April 1953 and May 1954. The Israelis stated that the national debt would be permanently reduced by that amount.

Comment: On 10 March Ambassador Davis reported from Tel Aviv that Israel was planning to request "one-time" special assistance from the United States and would require $20,000,000 in outside aid for the quarter ending 30 June 1953.
