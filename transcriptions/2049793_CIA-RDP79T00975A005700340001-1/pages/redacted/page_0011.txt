India - Communist China: General Thapar, the Indian Army's new chief of staff, who recently inspected the northeast frontier area opposite Tibet, was reportedly disturbed by the inadequacies he found in India's defensive posture there. He has recommended to Nehru further strengthening of combat and support units, especially in Sikkim, a major effort to improve military communications, the construction of new air strips to facilitate aerial patrolling, and urgent measures to improve morale and combat subversion in the frontier areas. Thapar's report reflects the army's concern over its capability to secure eastern India against the substantial Chinese Communist forces in the border area opposite Sikkim. (Map)
