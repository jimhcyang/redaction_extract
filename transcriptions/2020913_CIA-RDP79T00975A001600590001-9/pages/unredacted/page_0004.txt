3. Peiping offers to buy 50,000 tons of Burmese rice:

Peiping has expressed interest in buying 50,000 tons of rice from Burma during 1954, that this quantity might subsequently be increased.

Comment: This appears to be the first transaction under the Sino-Burmese trade agreement signed last November.

China, which under the Communist regime has become a major exporter of rice, is probably buying for resale elsewhere in the Communist bloc. It already ships 200,000 tons a year to the USSR.

Such a rice purchase may have similar motivations as Chinese rubber buying activities in Asia. Much of the rubber also goes to the USSR. Transactions of this type permit Peiping to take the lead in furthering both Communist economic relations and political objectives in Asia.
