CENTRAL INTELLIGENCE BULLETIN
23 March 1960

DAILY BRIEF

I. THE COMMUNIST BLOC

USSR: Soviet workers have recently protested wage reductions resulting from the shortened workday by staging meetings and slowdowns. In addition, the reported outbreak of worker disturbances in Kazakhstan last October has recently been confirmed. These protests are isolated but signify an emboldened worker attitude that contributes to pressure on the regime to improve living standards.

USSR: A Soviet lecturer, speaking before an elite Moscow audience on 16 March on recent developments in Asian and African Communist parties, singled out only Guinea and Sekou Touré for unqualified praise. He criticized other nationalist leaders, including Nehru, Sukarno, U Nu, Nasir, Abboud, and Qasim. The speaker's remarks probably reflect a feeling among Soviet policymakers that the Communist bloc's economic and military assistance programs have produced fewer political dividends than expected.
