GENERAL

1. Japanese anticipate move by USSR to terminate state of war:

Comment: Premier Malenkov may choose New Year's Eve to make a statement to the Japanese people such as Stalin did on 31 December 1951 in answer to a request of the Kyodo News Agency. This year Asahi, the leading independent Japanese newspaper, may have sent Malenkov a series of questions, just as it has submitted five questions to President Eisenhower, with a request for answers in time for publication in its New Year's edition.

While Poland, like the USSR, rejected the San Francisco treaty, it did not actively engage in hostilities with Japan during World War II, and the USSR may now be using Poland to test Japanese reactions prior to further Moscow overtures.
