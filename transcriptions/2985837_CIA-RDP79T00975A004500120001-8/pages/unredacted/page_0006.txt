Laos: The Pathet Lao battalion which defected on 19 May is still at large. Although Laotian officials have been optimistic that this battalion could be persuaded to return to its base, an unconfirmed press report states that fighting has broken out between Laotian Army troops and "rebel units."

Hanoi has publicized a letter from Premier Pham Van Dong to Nehru on 19 May calling for a resumption of the International Control Commission's activities in Laos. India favored reconvening the ICC earlier this year and, if the situation continues to be serious, probably will support resumption of its activities.

Singapore: Lee Kuan Yew, leader of the Communist-infiltrated People's Action party, is pressing the British to release from jail six pro-Communist leaders of the party. Lee has said that if the British refuse, he will not form a government following the anticipated victory of his party in the 30 May general elections. - The British apparently feel they must release the prisoners. Their release would almost certainly enhance Communist prospects for taking over the People's Action party. (Page 7)

III. THE WEST

European Community: Some important Ruhr industrialists are stepping up their campaign to undermine the European Coal-Steel Community in order to escape supervision, particularly on cartel matters. - They are finding support among French steel interests and Paris officials, including Premier Debré. Their efforts emphasize the growing nationalist trend in the six-nation community and are an obstacle to the further development of the integration movement. (Page 8)
