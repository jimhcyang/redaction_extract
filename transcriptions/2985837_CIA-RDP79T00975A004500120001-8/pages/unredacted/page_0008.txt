He had said earlier that Moscow is thinking in terms of mid-August for a summit meeting and is trying to arrange an alternative schedule for Khrushchev's Scandinavian trip.

The USSR's notes of 23 May to the United States and West Germany protesting the alleged acceleration of the nuclear rearmament of West German forces probably were timed to support Gromyko's conference line that if progress is to be made, the West must abandon its package plan and agree to discuss the urgent issues of a peace treaty and Berlin.
