SOUTHEAST ASIA

1. Thai premier lukewarm over close co-operation with Burma:

The Thai deputy foreign minister has informed the American embassy in Bangkok that Premier Phibun desires to maintain friendly relations with the Burmese people, but not necessarily with their present government. Moreover, the premier views sympathetically the pleas for assistance from both Karen insurgents and Chinese Nationalist guerrillas in Burma.

Comment: The Thais have been aiding both the Karens and the Chinese guerrillas in the belief that they would serve as a buffer between Communist China and Thailand.

There has been a Thai-Burma rapprochement during the past year, but this report indicates that Bangkok is not sanguine about this development. Thai suspicions of the Burmese may well be rekindled at the Afro-Asian conference, where Burma is likely to be ranged more often than not on the side of Peiping in opposition to Thailand.

WESTERN EUROPE

2. CSC president Monnet reported offering plan for European atomic energy "pool":

A proposal for the immediate formation of a "European Atomic Energy Community" will be included by Jean Monnet, president of the European Coal-Steel Community, in the "declaration of principles" he plans to issue when he leaves his present post.

The proposal will suggest that the six governments of the Community pool their atomic knowledge, research facilities, and materials under a supranational "atomic high authority."
