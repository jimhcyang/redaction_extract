GENERAL

1. Kennan believes Sino-Soviet talks involve real bargaining:

Ambassador Kennan believes that a tour by the Chinese delegation to points of interest in the USSR indicates that Sino-Soviet negotiations have reached a point where instructions from Peiping have become necessary. Kennan considers that the length of the talks shows that they are not "pro-forma" in character, but that real bargaining is taking place.

Comment: Communist cadres in China are being led to believe that increased Soviet aid will result from the talks.
