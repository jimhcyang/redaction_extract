5. Dejean favors expanding recruiting of Germans for Foreign Legion:

French commissioner general Dejean told Ambassador Heath in Saigon on 4 January that his solution for hastening victory in Indochina would be to expand the Foreign Legion forces there with six new regiments recruited almost entirely in Germany. He said there would be no difficulty in this if Chancellor Adenauer "would look the other way."

Comment: The West German populace would be greatly aroused, as it was in early 1953, by any resumption of overt legion recruiting. It is therefore improbable that the Bonn government would agree to such a proposal.
