There were a number of flights into Phong Saly on 3 April, but prior to that date such flights were rare.

A possible reinforcement of the airlift is suggested by the arrival on 4 and 5 April of at least ten Chinese Communist military transport aircraft at Hankow in central China. Most of these aircraft came from bases in north China and the movement resembled an influx of transports into Hankow in mid-December 1960 which was associated with support of the airlift into Laos. There is some possibility, however, that Mao Tse tung is in the vicinity of Hankow and that party leaders may be gathering there for a conference.
