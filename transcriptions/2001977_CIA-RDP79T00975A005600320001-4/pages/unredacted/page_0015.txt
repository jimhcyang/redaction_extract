The Leopoldville representative of the League of Red Cross Societies has been informed by his Geneva headquarters that the Red Cross will try to arrange a shipment of relief supplies from bloc countries into Orientale Province via the Sudan. It is not clear whether this shipment would be made under direct Red Cross control; if not, it could be used as cover for small amounts of clandestine assistance to the Gizenga regime from the bloc. The Sudan, which has repeatedly said that all aid should be channeled through the UN, turned down a unilateral Soviet Red Cross request in February for permission to send supplies into Stanleyville; however it might consider granting transit rights if approached by the International Red Cross organization.
