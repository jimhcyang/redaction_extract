1. THE TAIWAN STRAIT

Comment on:

Tatan and Erhtan, two small islands in the Quemoy group, were shelled by Communist batteries on 28 June for the third straight day, according to press accounts from Taipei. The islands are used by the Nationalists as observation points and as bases for mortar fire against vessels entering Amoy harbor. Nationalist spokesmen, in briefing the press, have described the situation as "very tense," although there has been no report from any other sources indicating that an attempt to seize the islands is imminent. The Chinese Communists may have stepped up artillery fire against Tatan and Erhtan in order to silence Nationalist mortars there, which have been harassing ships entering Amoy harbor.

The total force on both Tatan and Erhtan is only about 1,100 men.
