He has in the past had fairly good relations with the pro-UAR, ultranationalist National Socialist party, and this might improve the prospects for a normalization of relations with the UAR. Unless some accommodation to the nationalist view is made, Husayn's decision to go along with the Bedouin in this instance could narrow further the basis of the monarchy's support in Jordan. It might thus open the way for a resumption of instability in the government and army which was chronic before Rifai took over government leadership.
