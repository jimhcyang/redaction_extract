SOUTHEAST ASIA

5. France opposes meeting on Indochina during Bangkok conference:

Minister for the Associated States La Chambre and Premier Mendes-France are both opposed to holding the next meeting of British, French and American officials on Indochina in conjunction with the 23 February Manila pact conference in Bangkok. They fear that a meeting under such circumstances would be regarded in France as "bellicose" and that the Viet Minh would derive propaganda advantages from the presence of Generals Collins and Ely.

La Chambre continues to envisage a full-dress meeting--preferably in Washington--including military, economic and political experts which would "get at the bottom of things." He stated that the French are convinced that decisions on whether to continue to support Diem and on what basis, and what Bao Dai's future role is to be, should be reached before 15 March. According to La Chambre, the French are now convinced that popular support for Diem has increased, but fear that the Diem government has made no "administrative progress."

Comment: La Chambre has been one of the strongest advocates of Diem's removal. Despite this recognition of the Vietnamese premier's growing popularity, the French are still seeking methods to curtail his authority, perhaps through periodic reviews of his accomplishments. In addition, the French may increase pressure for Bao Dai's return to Vietnam in the hope that he will control Diem's anti-French proclivities.
