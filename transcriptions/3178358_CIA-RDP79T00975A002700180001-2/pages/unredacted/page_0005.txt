2. PANAMANIAN LEADERS TO ENCOURAGE STUDY OF CANAL NATIONALIZATION

Panama's President Ricardo Arias and President-elect Ernesto de la Guardia agreed at a recent meeting that the next administration will "harp constantly, through the press, radio, and official circles, on the theme of Panama's irrevocable sovereignty over the Panama Canal" and the similarities between the Panama and Suez Canals.

They reportedly also agreed to encourage the National Assembly to study "nationalization or internationalization" of the Panama Canal.

Comment - Such a campaign would be designed to step up Panama's long-time efforts to weaken United States treaty rights and gain ultimate participation in the operation and profits of the canal. It would also serve well as a nationalistic issue distracting the public from serious domestic problems and thus strengthening the next administration, which will inherit the corruption and internal factional struggles which have weakened the Arias administration.

De la Guardia is to be inaugurated on 1 October. He is basically friendly to the United States, but will be obliged to appear to the public as an energetic defender of Panama's "rights" with respect to the canal. One of his personal advisers is a pro-Communist who can be expected to encourage this nationalistic approach.
