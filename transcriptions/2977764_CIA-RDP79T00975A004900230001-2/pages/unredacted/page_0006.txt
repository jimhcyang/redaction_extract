III. THE WEST

Bolivia: President Siles has expressed to the US ambassador grave concern over the situation resulting from the Communist-led seizure last week of Huanuni, a key tin-mining town in central Bolivia. Siles said the way is now open for Communists to seize the major city of Oruro, 40 miles away on the railroad to La Paz, and stressed the possibility of civil war. He also emphasized that disruption of an important segment of the tin-mining industry would result in even greater Bolivian financial difficulties. The American Army attache characterizes the Huanuni episode as the most daring exploit of the Bolivian Communist party to date. Siles, who may be reluctant to cause further bloodshed, is said to question whether the army could dislodge the Communist-led miners' militia controlling the town. (Page 5) (Map)
