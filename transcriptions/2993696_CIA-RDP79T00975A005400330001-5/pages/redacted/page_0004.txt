II. ASIA-AFRICA

Algeria: The atmosphere in Algiers remains quiet but tense, and appears little changed by the flight of Pierre Lagaillarde from France, according to the American consul general. Comments and press articles in Algiers tend to place emphasis on the honor-breaking aspects of the flight, but there is speculation that Lagaillarde would not have taken this step unless he felt that the deadline for action was near. Security precautions continue to be discreet, with the security police remaining out of sight. Activists in Algeria have been reported planning a coup or a general strike ever since De Gaulle's 4 November speech but apparently have not been able to decide on the timing. The arrival of De Gaulle in Algeria on 9 December, or any announcement during his six-day tour of further steps toward a liberal solution, could spur his opponents to act.
