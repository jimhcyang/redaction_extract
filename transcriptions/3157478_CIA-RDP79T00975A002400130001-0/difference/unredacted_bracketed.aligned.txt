BURMA SOON

The high-level nature of this delegation

less than two months ago is probably designed not only to en-

tries in the area with Soviet ability to dispense economic aid

2. BURMA MAY ASK US FOR LARGE-SCALE ASSISTANCE

A spokesman for Minister of [[PRED_REDACTION_7]]Indus-
tries[[/PRED_REDACTION_7]] Kyaw Nyein has informally
sounded out the American [[PRED_REDACTION_8]]ambassa-
dor[[/PRED_REDACTION_8]] in Rangoon regarding the [[PRED_REDACTION_9]]possi-
bility[[/PRED_REDACTION_9]] of large-scale American financial assistance "for
political purposes." He suggested that this aid might take
the form of a loan, which he hoped could be substantially
written off. Kyaw Nyein was said to be thinking in terms
of $200,000,000, but his emissary indicated that $50,000,000
would be "very helpful."

Comment Although this approach is primarily
aimed at exploiting American [[PRED_REDACTION_10]]con-
cern[[/PRED_REDACTION_10]] over Burma's increasing economic ties with the [[PRED_REDACTION_11]]Sino-
Soviet[[/PRED_REDACTION_11]] bloc, it probably also reflects the desire of an [[PRED_REDACTION_12]]im-
portant[[/PRED_REDACTION_12]] element in the Burmese government to achieve some
balance between Soviet and Western participation in Burma's
development program.

Prior to Bulganin's and Khrushchev's
dramatic offers to Burma during their December visit to
Rangoon, the Burmese government had on several [[PRED_REDACTION_13]]occa-
sions[[/PRED_REDACTION_13]] hinted a desire for a resumption of American aid--
provided it could be made to appear as trade rather than
aid.

3. BONN TO PROTEST ACTIVITIES OF SOVIET
AMBASSADOR ZORIN

West German foreign minister Von
Brentano has drafted a sharp letter
to Soviet ambassador Zorin [[PRED_REDACTION_14]]com-
plaining[[/PRED_REDACTION_14]] that Zorin is exceeding the
bounds of normal diplomatic [[PRED_REDACTION_15]]prac-
tices[[/PRED_REDACTION_15]].

Cabinet minister Strauss, who gave
this information to American representatives in Bonn,
declared that Zorin has embarked on an extensive [[PRED_REDACTION_16]]pro-
gram[[/PRED_REDACTION_16]] of visiting industrial plants and establishing [[PRED_REDACTION_17]]con-
tacts[[/PRED_REDACTION_17]] with Bundestag deputies. Moreover, Zorin has
suggested to industrialists that they pressure the Bonn
government for a trade treaty with Moscow and has [[PRED_REDACTION_18]]crit-
icized[[/PRED_REDACTION_18]] the Bonn government to his diplomatic colleagues.

Comment It is probable that the early conclusion
of a Soviet-West German trade [[PRED_REDACTION_19]]agree-
ment[[/PRED_REDACTION_19]] is one of Zorin's principal objectives. Since his [[PRED_REDACTION_20]]ar-
rival[[/PRED_REDACTION_20]] in Bonn last December, Zorin has stressed in his
talks with Foreign Ministry officials and in public [[PRED_REDACTION_21]]state-
ments[[/PRED_REDACTION_21]] the favorable prospects for developing economic
and cultural relations.

The West German ambassador to Moscow
is not expected to take up his post before 1 March. On 3
February, a TASS broadcast complained of unfavorable West
German press articles about the Soviet embassy in Bonn
and alluded to a West German design to "destroy what the
chancellor himself found necessary to speak up for in [[PRED_REDACTION_22]]Mos-
cow[[/PRED_REDACTION_22]]."
