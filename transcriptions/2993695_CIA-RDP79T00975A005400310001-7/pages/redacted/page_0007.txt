III. THE WEST

Brazil: President-elect Janio Quadros apparently plans a more "independent" foreign policy for Brazil following his inauguration on 31 January. Changes will be especially marked in policy toward the European colonial powers and in the field of Latin American economic development, according to Joao Dantas, an anti-US Brazilian publisher who sometimes acts as an emissary for Quadros. Dantas believes Quadros will soon make public an exchange of correspondence with Ferhat Abbas in which Abbas calls on Brazil for help in "completing decolonialization." Quadros, who is now in Spain, has turned down an informal invitation to meet with President-elect Kennedy in the US during the preinaugural period. (Page 4)
