Communist China: The Chinese Communists are encountering "tremendous difficulties" in industry. The Chinese are unable to fill their urgent requests for deliveries of iron and manganese ore. Furthermore, export shipments of rice and soybeans are considerably below normal for this time of year, and China has invoked "act of God" clauses—which cover situations beyond the control of either party—for its refusal to ship other agricultural products. In addition to internal economic difficulties, China's trade problems may reflect stalling tactics while the recent Moscow meeting was in session. However, bloc suppliers have warned the Chinese that continued procrastination in establishing 1961 trade plans will lower trade in the first quarter of the coming year. (Page 3)

II. ASIA-AFRICA

UAR-Iraq: Cairo has ordered its information officer in Beirut to initiate a widespread Lebanese press attack on the policies of Iraqi Prime Minister Qasim. The attack is to stress alleged Israeli approval of Qasim and to associate him with Near Eastern elements loyal to "imperialism." This campaign suggests that Cairo—which has already pointed to the recent Iraqi-Jordanian rapprochement as "imperialist inspired"—believes Qasim's position in Iraq is deteriorating and is beginning a new effort to hasten his decline
