GENERAL

1. Comment on Polish offer of a mutual assistance pact to France:

The Polish offer to France of a treaty of alliance and mutual assistance, made in a note presented in Paris on 25 August, is in line with other recent Orbit attempts to defeat EDC and to woo France away from the Western alliance system.

Poland has been taking the lead in these maneuvers and has consistently sought to transform the existing agreement to resume cultural relations into a political instrument for influencing French public opinion.

Despite the French Foreign Ministry statement to the press on 27 August that the proposal will be rejected, it is unlikely that the French government will flatly reject the offer, particularly since Premier Mendes-France is eager for further talks with the USSR.

SOVIET UNION

2. MVD refusing to supply forced labor to Dalstroi:

Comment: Dalstroi, an important mining and construction complex in northeast Siberia, depends entirely on prisoners for nontechnical labor. The transparent objections raised by the MVD may merely cloak resentment vis-a-vis the Ministry of Nonferrous Metallurgy, which obtained control of Dalstroi from the MVD after Stalin's death. Earlier this year control of Dalstroi became the subject of prolonged interministerial controversy; the final decision in May was unfavorable to the MVD.
