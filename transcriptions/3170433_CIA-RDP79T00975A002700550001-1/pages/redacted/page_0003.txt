1. THE JORDAN-ISRAEL BORDER SITUATION

Comment on:

The heavy Israeli assault on Jordanian military positions in the Qalqiliya sector, 12 miles northeast of Tel Aviv, on 10 October creates another crisis for the faltering regime of King Hussain. Faced with a population embittered by Jordan's weakness, the 21-year-old king's position may be further threatened by a leftist, ultranationalist and antimonarchical trend in the elections for the lower house of Jordan's legislature, scheduled for 21 October.

It is unlikely that Jordan will feel capable of undertaking a military reprisal against Israel; Jordan has, however, indicated the intention of bringing up the Israeli attack in the UN.

In addition to retaliating against a known center of terrorist groups in Jordan, the Israeli assault destroyed fortifications and tested Jordanian defenses on a main route to Nablus and the Jordan River. Shortly before the action, Ambassador Lawson reported that both strategic and tactical considerations now appeared to be pulling Israel from a policy of moderation.
