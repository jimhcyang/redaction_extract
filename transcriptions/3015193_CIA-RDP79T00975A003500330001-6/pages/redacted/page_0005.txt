3. RIFT IN CHINESE NATIONALIST GOVERNMENT

A serious rift between the Control Yuan and the executive organs of the Chinese Nationalist Government may, if not resolved soon, lead to further Control Yuan attacks on government officials and possibly to some ministerial shake-ups. The Control Yuan, the "watchdog" branch of the government, on 23 December impeached Premier O. K. Yui on charges of dereliction of duty, and on 9 January, in the face of strong official disapproval, voted to continue the investigation.

In its resolution of 9 January, the Control Yuan affirmed its intention to continue to investigate Yui's connections with the government Central Bank.

The members of the Control Yuan have long been frustrated by their lack of authority. Repeated attempts in the past to impeach officials have failed.

A potentially serious aspect to the crisis is the demonstrated collapse of Kuomintang party discipline. Ninety percent of the Control Yuan members belong to the ruling party.

This lack of discipline also extends to the Legislative Yuan, which has recently opposed official policy on several important issues.
