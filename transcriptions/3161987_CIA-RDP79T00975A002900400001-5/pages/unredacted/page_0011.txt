7. PEIPING NEGOTIATING WITH TIBETAN REBELS

Peiping has asserted that Tibetan armed resistance is confined to Tibetans living in Western China, the disorders have spread deep into Tibet.

Chang Ching-wu, the ranking Chinese Communist in Lhasa, capital of Tibet, was told by Peiping to report on negotiations with Tibetan rebels. Instructed to make daily reports "until appeasement has been made," Chang was warned that too many concessions to the rebels would make them "too proud" and more difficult to handle.
