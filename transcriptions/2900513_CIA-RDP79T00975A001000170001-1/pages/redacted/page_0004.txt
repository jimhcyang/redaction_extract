3. Comment on Soviet attack on Israeli leaders:

In blasting Israel's Premier Ben-Gurion, Foreign Minister Moshe Sharett and the late President Weizmann as tools of American-British imperialism and "executors" of US State Department spy operations in the USSR, Soviet propaganda reverted to the anti-Zionist theme which had been muted since the initial accusations against the nine Soviet doctors.

An article in New Times further charged Aubrey Eban, Israeli delegate to the UN and Ambassador to the United States, "and others" with being British agents, and was the sharpest attack on Israeli officials yet to appear in an official Soviet publication.

The editorial also accused the Israeli nation of "waging a cruel war of destruction against the local Arab population in Palestine," thus attacking the Israeli nation as well as Zionism.
