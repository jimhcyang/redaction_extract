7. Egypt reportedly prepared to break off talks with Britain:

According to the Egyptian ambassador in Washington, Vice Premier Nasr told General Robertson, prior to the latter's departure for London, that Egypt would feel compelled to break off the talks with Britain unless a new British attitude were forthcoming which would permit an agreement between the two countries. Nasr warned that without such an agreement Cairo would no longer be bound by the concessions it had already made and would feel free to take whatever action it thought best. He added that Nasr was prepared to tell the British "take up your base and go."
