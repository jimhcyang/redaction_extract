LATIN AMERICA

3. Bolivia gains support in tin crisis:

Comment: The decision of the Reconstruction Finance Corporation in March to discontinue purchases, together with the drop of almost 25 percent in world tin prices during the past month, has greatly diminished the La Paz government's chances of survival. About half of Bolivia's tin has normally been marketed in the United States.

Bolivia maintains that tin is the "source of life" of its people, and that any tin questions cannot be dealt with on a purely commercial basis, but are also political. A prolonged delay in settling the problem could result in economic chaos, and lead to the accession of an even more extremist government.
