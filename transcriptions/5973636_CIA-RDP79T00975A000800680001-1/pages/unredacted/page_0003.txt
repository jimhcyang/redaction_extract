SOVIET UNION

1. Dairen-based aircraft apparently destined for Soviet Union:
Some of the Soviet aircraft scheduled to fly from Dairen to Tunhua, Manchuria, on 6 October were apparently destined to fly on to the Soviet Union on 7 October, the movement on 7 October of groups of TU-2's, IL-10's, and transports from Dairen and Mukden to Vozdvizhenka in the Southern Maritime Province, some via Tunhua.

Comment: The withdrawal of a large number of conventional ground attack and light bomber aircraft to the Soviet Union could be preparatory to the deployment of additional jets to the Dairen area.

2. Possible development of TV transmitter for Soviet aircraft:
This is the first indication that the USSR may possess television transmitters for use in aircraft. Possible military applications of significance are in aerial reconnaissance, flight testing, and terminal homing of drone aircraft or guided missiles.
