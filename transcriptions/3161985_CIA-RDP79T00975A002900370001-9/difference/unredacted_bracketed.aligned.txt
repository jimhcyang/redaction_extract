1. INDIA REACTS SHARPLY TO UN PLEBISCITE RESOLUTION ON KASHMIR

[[PRED_REDACTION_5]]Comment on:

The Indian army is "fully mobilized" against the threat of Pakistani military action in Kashmir, will strike only if Pakistan initiates hostilities[[/PRED_REDACTION_5]].

The state of tension in India, resulting from the UN Security Council's [[PRED_REDACTION_6]]resolution[[/PRED_REDACTION_6]] of 24 January which called for a [[PRED_REDACTION_7]]plebiscite[[/PRED_REDACTION_7]] in Kashmir, is also reflected in Krishna [[PRED_REDACTION_8]]Menon's[[/PRED_REDACTION_8]] statement to [[PRED_REDACTION_9]]Ambassador[[/PRED_REDACTION_9]] Lodge on 28 January that there is danger of Pakistani "fedayeen" attacks in Kashmir and that if they occur, he [[PRED_REDACTION_10]]favors[[/PRED_REDACTION_10]] taking over all of Pakistan.

The Indian press has lashed out at "the interested and mischievous role" played by Britain and the United States throughout the Kashmir dispute.

India's belligerent attitude will probably become even more extreme in reply to Pakistan's proposal for introduction of a UN force into Kashmir. However New Delhi, which controls most of the disputed state, is unlikely to provoke hostilities unless it decides that this is the only way to prevent a plebiscite--which it would probably lose to Pakistan.

Karachi is equally unlikely to precipitate [[PRED_REDACTION_11]]a conflict as long as it continues to receive UN support[[/PRED_REDACTION_11]].

[[PRED_REDACTION_12]]2. NEW EGYPTIAN ARMS SHIPMENT BELIEVED EN ROUTE
TO ALGERIANS

Libya has agreed that "the goods" may pass through his country. the shipment would be ready for delivery in "al-Salum"--presumably the port city on the Egyptian-Libyan border--on 31 January and that "a reliable group" should be sent from Tunisia to receive it there in order to guarantee its arrival in Tunis.

Comment

The "goods" are probably additional arms to support the military offensive being planned by the Algerian rebels.

Arms consignments to the Algerians via Libya reportedly have been stepped up since 1 December with the Libyan government facilitating their transshipment. These reports suggest that Tunisian authorities usually take charge of consignments at the Libyan-Tunisian border and make the actual deliveries[[/PRED_REDACTION_12]].

3. HUNGARY REPORTEDLY EXTORTING "CONFESSIONS"
IMPLICATING WESTERN NATIONS

A reliable source of the American [[PRED_REDACTION_13]]legation[[/PRED_REDACTION_13]] in Budapest states that many [[PRED_REDACTION_14]]Hungarians[[/PRED_REDACTION_14]] deported to the USSR after the revolution have been returned and are now in a Budapest prison. The returnees are being [[PRED_REDACTION_15]]released[[/PRED_REDACTION_15]] only if they sign a "confession" admitting "contact" with Western powers.

Comment

Such confessions may be used in the forthcoming political trials to implicate Western states and their diplomatic missions in the [[PRED_REDACTION_16]]Hungarian[[/PRED_REDACTION_16]] uprising.

Hungary has accelerated its anti-Western campaign during the past week, and the legation believes that efforts to hamper the activities of Western missions in Budapest are already under way.

French chargé Soulié has joined with the Indian truce commissioner in recommending the urgent return to Vientiane of Pathet chief Souphannouvong in order to head off growing cabinet criticism of the 28 December agreement with the Pathet Lao.

By such tactics Soulié apparently hopes to increase French influence in Laos. 5. FRENCH WEST AFRICAN OFFICIALS ALARMED OVER SELF-GOVERNMENT DEMANDS

Local French officials are deeply disturbed over current political developments in French West Africa, particularly by the growing [[PRED_REDACTION_18]]demands[[/PRED_REDACTION_18]] for early self-government. The [[PRED_REDACTION_19]]American[[/PRED_REDACTION_19]] consul general at Dakar notes that they are most concerned over the rapid pace of these developments, and fear that Paris will not realize the [[PRED_REDACTION_20]]necessity[[/PRED_REDACTION_20]] of major policy [[PRED_REDACTION_21]]re-adjustments[[/PRED_REDACTION_21]] to meet the new situation.

According to the deputy high [[PRED_REDACTION_22]]commissioner[[/PRED_REDACTION_22]] of French West [[PRED_REDACTION_23]]Africa[[/PRED_REDACTION_23]], the Africans now say they plan to turn the federations of West and Equatorial Africa into two independent states associated with France rather than accepting the French plan for 12 autonomous territories.

Comment

Most of this change in African attitude has taken place since French Togoland was granted autonomy in late 1956, and has been influenced by [[PRED_REDACTION_24]]developments[[/PRED_REDACTION_24]] in North Africa and the Gold Coast.

West African territorial leaders recently supported a refusal of the Grand Council at Dakar to discuss the budget because of dissatisfaction with the implementation of the new basic reform law. The African majority of the [[PRED_REDACTION_25]]representatives[[/PRED_REDACTION_25]] on a committee of the French National Assembly voted [[PRED_REDACTION_26]]unanimously[[/PRED_REDACTION_26]] on 24 January to grant larger powers to African councils.
