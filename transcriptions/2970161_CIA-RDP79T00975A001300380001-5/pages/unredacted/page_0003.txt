FAR EAST

1. Macao removes official restriction on trade with Communist China:

The governor of Macao recently admitted to an American consul from Hong Kong that he was no longer restricting the re-export of strategic goods to Communist China. In fact, he said, the colony's depressed finances forced him to encourage trade.

The governor also stated that Chinese pressure to buy petroleum and other goods is now negligible and implied that Macao will have to promote sales to keep from being bypassed entirely.

Comment: The Chinese Communists, who have been increasingly successful in procuring their strategic imports directly from Western countries of origin or from the Soviet bloc, are also bypassing Hong Kong. Despite the reduced importance of the colonies to Peiping as sources of strategic supplies, there is no indication of military moves against either.
