FAR EAST

1. Change in Thai Government again indicated:
Ambassador Stanton has been told that conflict within the ruling clique is reaching a critical point and that the actions of the chief rivals for power, Generals Sarit and Phao, indicate they are preparing for an armed showdown.

Citing considerable evidence of increasing tension, Stanton states that a major change in the Thai Government may occur within the next five weeks.

2. Chinese Nationalist force penetrating Karen territory:
a Chinese Nationalist force of 700 men has moved south from northeastern Burma into the base area of Karen operations (see map).

The American Embassy comments that continued Nationalist involvement with the Karens might cause the Burmese Government to make the Nationalists their primary target instead of the insurgent Communists.

Comment: Open Nationalist cooperation with the Karens could influence the Burmese to appeal the issue to the United Nations or to request military assistance from the Chinese Communists.
