GENERAL

1. Viet Minh discusses role at Geneva conference:

Comment: Moscow has not explicitly stated that it will sponsor Viet Minh attendance at Geneva, but shortly after the Berlin conference the Communists began preparing their case on behalf of the Viet Minh at the Geneva conference.

The interest in land reform documents indicates that Moscow will present detailed evidence at Geneva to bolster the Viet Minh claim of being the only effective government in Vietnam. Viet Minh propaganda cites Ho's success in land reform as proof of this claim.

2. Impasse expected on selection of chairman at Geneva:

UN secretary general Hammarskjold told Ambassador Lodge on 20 April that he is "disinclined" to accept the chairmanship of the Geneva conference. He said he would reconsider, however, if this proved to be the only way to "get the conference going."

Comment: The USSR and Communist China could be expected to oppose Hammarskjold as chairman. Both have consistently sought to avoid any impression that the Geneva talks will be held under UN auspices.
