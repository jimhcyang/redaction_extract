2 APRIL 1958

I. THE COMMUNIST BLOC

USSR - Mikoyan and Frol Kozlov will have most of day-to-day responsibility in Khrushchev's new government.

II. ASIA-AFRICA

Tunisian officials warn that Bourguiba may feel compelled to withdraw concessions made to good offices mission.

Burma asks United Arab Republic to permit overflight of military planes being ferried to Burma, probably F-51 piston fighters purchased from Israel.

III. THE WEST

Cuba - Castro sets 12 April as "target date" for ousting Batista, but his capability for overthrowing government in next few weeks is not impressive.

LATE ITEM

Lebanese Army commander opposes President Chamoun's re-election.
