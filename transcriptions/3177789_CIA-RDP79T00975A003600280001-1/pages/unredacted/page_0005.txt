III. THE WEST

Cuba: In setting 12 April as the "target date" for ousting Batista, Castro appears to be relying not so much on his estimated 1,200 rebels deployed in Oriente Province as on spontaneous popular support for a general strike. Since the leadership of Cuba's powerful organized labor movement remains opposed to Castro's strike plan, his apparent capability for overthrowing the government during the next few weeks is not impressive. LATE ITEM

* Lebanon: Lebanese Foreign Minister Malik has stated that army commander General Shihab is opposed to President Chamoun's ambitions to succeed himself since he fears that Chamoun's efforts will lead to civil disorders. Shihab believes that "Nasirism" is the wave of the future and that Chamoun's strong pro-Western policy is compromising Lebanon's "traditional" position as a neutral. (Page 4)
