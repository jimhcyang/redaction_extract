5. FULFILLMENT OF POLISH-BURMESE TRADE
AGREEMENT THREATENED

A Polish trade representative in Rangoon expressed pessimism on 13 June regarding the prospects for the successful fulfillment of the barter agreement between Burma and Poland. He said Poland had been forced to accept a considerably lower grade of rice than called for in the trade agreement. He also stated the Burmese were apprehensive that the goods to be supplied by Poland would be inferior to similar products manufactured in the United Kingdom, the United States, and Japan. The Polish official conceded that these fears were well founded.

Comment
Burma has been increasingly dissatisfied with its barter agreements with bloc countries and has tended to discriminate against them in favor of cash customers. Poland is slated to take 50,000 tons of Burmese rice this year. Actual shipments, however, may not reach that total, as only 11,000 tons have been shipped to date.
