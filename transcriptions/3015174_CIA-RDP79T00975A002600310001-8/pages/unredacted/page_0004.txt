1. SYRIA RECOGNIZES COMMUNIST CHINA

Syria's recognition of Communist China on 2 July reflects strong anti-Western sentiment and is an expression of Syria's antagonism toward what it considers the "pro-Israeli, pro-Baghdad pact" policy of the United States. It is also an attempt to underscore Syria's endorsement of Egypt's policy of "independence." Egypt has been campaigning to get other Arab states to follow it in its recognition of Peiping on 16 May.

A high Foreign Ministry official told the American embassy in Damascus prior to the visit of Soviet foreign minister Shepilov that Syria did not expect to receive anything concrete as a result of the visit, but if it did, recognition of Communist China would be an "appropriate, easy, and inexpensive" quid pro quo.
