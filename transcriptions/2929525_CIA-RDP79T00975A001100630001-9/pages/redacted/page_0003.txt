GENERAL

1. USSR to appoint ambassador to Yugoslavia:

Ambassador Bohlen reports that Soviet foreign minister Molotov has asked Yugoslavia to accept an ambassador and has expressed the hope that Yugoslavia would reciprocate. The foreign minister requested agreement for the present chief of the Foreign Ministry's Balkan section.

Comment: There is no indication at the present time that the Kremlin will make an attempt to re-establish closer economic and cultural relations with Yugoslavia or that the latter would be receptive to such overtures.
