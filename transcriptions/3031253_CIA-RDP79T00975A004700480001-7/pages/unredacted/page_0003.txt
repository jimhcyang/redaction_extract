26 OCTOBER 1959

I. THE COMMUNIST BLOC

Another ballistic missile launched at Tyura Tam on 25 October.

Soviet defense chief backs Khrushchev's foreign policy.

Supreme Soviet, convening 27 October, likely to take up economic matters and get briefing on foreign policy.

North Korea--Replacement of Foreign Minister Nam Il unlikely to signify any change in foreign policy.

II. ASIA-AFRICA

Indian public reacts strongly to incident on Chinese border; New Delhi obliged to take firm stand.

Deterioration of security in southern Laos noted; if trend continues, government's authority may be restricted to larger towns.

III. THE WEST

Cuba--Castro's recent moves indicate real threat of extremist control over him.
