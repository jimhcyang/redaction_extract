Adenauer, however, has refused to consider Erhard's candidacy because he feels Erhard would be less inclined to follow his lead in foreign policy. Erhard has been associated with a CDU faction which favors more tactical flexibility in foreign policy, and has not been a strong supporter of European integration or the priority of good French-German relations.

Although last week Adenauer publicly denied speculation that he would withdraw from the presidential race, he has never been reconciled to the prospect of relinquishing the real power of the chancellorship and the day-to-day control over foreign policy. Since early May he has been considering reversing his acceptance of the nomination, after his close advisers had warned him he could not obtain Etzel's nomination even if, as president, Adenauer formally nominated him.
