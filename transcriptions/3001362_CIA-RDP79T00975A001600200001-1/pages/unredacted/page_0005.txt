LATIN AMERICA

5. Brazil sees no general agreement on US resolution prior to OAS meeting:

The ministry noted that a number of countries were offering reservations and that little time was available for negotiation. It added that the meeting itself is the "proper place" to reach agreement, and that insistence on prior agreement may "compromise the very principle of calling the meeting."

Comment: Brazil has offered to intercede with other governments on behalf of American objectives in this case, but is not itself committed to the text of the resolution proposed by Washington on 5 June.
