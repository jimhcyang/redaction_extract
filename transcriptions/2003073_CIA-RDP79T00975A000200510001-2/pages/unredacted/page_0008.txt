Comment: The Spanish Army's preference for a special bilateral pact probably stems chiefly from its reluctance to undertake the commitments involved in NATO membership and its desire to reserve any Spanish military effort solely for the immediate defense of Spain. While Spanish officers' expressions of distrust of French military capabilities are undoubtedly genuine enough in the light of World War II events, they may also be motivated in part by sensitivity regarding the Riffian wars, when French military intervention (in 1925) saved the Spanish Army from a complete debacle.
