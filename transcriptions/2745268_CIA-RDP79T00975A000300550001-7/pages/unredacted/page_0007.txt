WESTERN EUROPE

7. Italy objects to conditions for postponement of Trieste elections:
Italy objects to making the two-month postponement of the Trieste elections conditional on the assurance that Italy will take the initiative with Yugoslavia regarding the disposition of the Free Territory. This objection was voiced by the Italian Charge in Washington in response to the US and UK decision to permit the postponement until December. Italy feels that such a provision constitutes pressure on Italy with no equivalent pressure on Yugoslavia. Previously the British had urged even stronger measures, i.e., that postponement be permitted only after assurance that the Italian and Yugoslav Governments had already agreed to resume negotiations.

Comment: The Italians feel their bargaining position vis-a-vis Yugoslavia is deteriorating in the face of Yugoslavia's increased strategic importance to the Western powers. There has been a recent rise in the ever present anti-British feeling in Italy due to the impression that the UK is championing Tito at Italy's expense.
