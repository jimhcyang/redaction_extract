3. NEW CRISIS THREATENS IN JORDAN

Comment on:

The 4 January supreme court decision that King Hussain's 19 December dissolution of the Jordan parliament was illegal has infuriated Jordanian elements hostile to the Baghdad pact, which had hoped to dominate a newly elected parliament. The consulate general in Jerusalem expects renewed rioting.

The governmental problem remains unresolved, with rumors that the caretaker cabinet may resign. The authority and prestige of the throne have declined and the British position has been severely damaged. Egypt's prestige has risen sharply and it is unlikely that any Jordanian decisions in the near future involving Israel or Middle East problems will be taken without the approval of Cairo.
