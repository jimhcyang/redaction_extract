The Soviet demand for inclusion of China in a conference has become a definite position from which Moscow is now unwilling or unable to withdraw. If maintained, this demand means that no conference of any kind on a major question is possible in the foreseeable future.

Officials in both London and Paris agree on the "extreme rigidity" of the Kremlin approach although the Foreign Office was loath to comment fully in view of Foreign Secretary Eden's impending parliamentary speech on foreign affairs. A prominent official of the Quai d'Orsey regards the obvious appeals to France, such as references to Southeast Asia and the Franco-Soviet pact, as too vague to be of much use to French advocates of a five-power meeting.

3. Soviet IL-28's visit East Germany in apparent air exercise:

Comment: This is the second time since the withdrawal of IL-28 aircraft from East Germany in late June that the advanced bases there have been used in redeployment exercises by jet light bombers stationed within the USSR. The fact that the IL-28's did not return to the same bases in the USSR suggests a more extensive exercise in tactical mobility.

Annual air maneuvers involving all major components of the Soviet air force were apparently in progress during the latter part of October and revealed a higher level of training than noted in previous years.
