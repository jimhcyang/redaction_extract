7. OPPOSING LAOTIAN LEADERS MAY POSTPONE MEETING TO MID-JUNE

Comment
Indians were actively participating in secret arrangements to bring about a meeting between Souvanna Phouma and Souphannouvong on 22 May.

Souvanna Phouma is anxious to settle the Pathet Lao problem through private negotiations with Souphannouvong and his other half-brother, Prince Petsarath, a Pathet sympathizer now exiled in Thailand. The Laotian premier is believed inclined to accept Communist proposals which would include return of the two northern provinces to government control in exchange for adoption of a neutral policy by the Laotian government.

25 May 56 Current Intelligence Bulletin Page 9
