THE ARAB-ISRAELI SITUATION
(Information as of 1700, 24 May)

Foreign Ministry has informed Ambassador Moose of the Arab position on UN secretary general Hammarskjold's report formulated at the recent four-power Arab meeting in Damascus. If the Security Council merely notes Hammarskjold's report and thanks Hammarskjold, no Arab action is contemplated. If, however, the Security Council approves or adopts the report, the Arabs will have to "clarify their position" since Hammarskjold's report is "liable to misinterpretation." If the Security Council suggests further moves, such as a new Hammarskjold mission with expanded terms of reference, the Arabs will oppose them on the grounds that Hammarskjold has done all that can be accomplished now.
