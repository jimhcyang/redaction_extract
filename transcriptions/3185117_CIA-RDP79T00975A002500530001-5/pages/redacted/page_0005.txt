2. EGYPTIAN FOREIGN MINISTER PREDICTS "NEW PHASE" OF POLICY

Egypt's Foreign Minister Fawzi told Ambassador Byroade on 23 May that the United States would see in the next few weeks evidence of a new phase of Egyptian foreign policy which would please the United States. Fawzi said he would not be more specific, since it was premature to inform other governments about these plans. He made these statements in the context of remarks about Egypt's attitude toward the Baghdad pact.

Fawzi stated that the Aswan High Dam project remained of paramount importance and that Egypt wanted to accomplish it in exclusive co-operation with the International Bank, the United States and Britain.

Comment
Fawzi is usually more optimistic on the prospects for improving Egypt's relations with the West than are more influential members of the Nasr regime. However, it is possible that Nasr is considering what he conceives to be pro-American gestures to offset the bad effects he may feel were created by Egypt's recognition of Communist China.
