1. SOVIET NUCLEAR AGREEMENT WITH POLAND REPORTED

The Soviet Union announced publicly on 29 August that it will produce 2,000-kilowatt atomic reactors and cyclotrons with power up to 25 Mev for Poland, Czechoslovakia, Rumania, Hungary, Bulgaria and East Germany. For Communist China, it will produce a 6,500-kilowatt atomic reactor and a 25-Mev cyclotron.
