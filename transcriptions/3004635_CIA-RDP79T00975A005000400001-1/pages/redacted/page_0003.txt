CENTRAL INTELLIGENCE BULLETIN
15 April 1960

DAILY BRIEF

I. THE COMMUNIST BLOC

USSR: Changes in the party presidium will be made at a forthcoming meeting of the central committee, according to a current Soviet rumor. The committee will allegedly promote candidate members Dmitry Polyansky and Aleksey Kosygin to replace Aleksey Kirichenko and Nikolay Belyayev, who are in disfavor. A separate rumor alleges that security chief Aleksandr Shelepin is also in trouble. While the main purpose of any central committee meeting convoked in the near future would probably be a demonstration of party support for Khrushchev on the eve of the summit, the promotion of Polyansky and Kosygin at such a meeting is not unlikely.
