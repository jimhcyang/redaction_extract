3. BRITAIN MAY CHANGE ARMS POLICY TOWARD LIBYA

Arms assistance to Libya has become a political issue, according to the British ambassador in Tripoli, and Britain may be prepared to give Libya certain amounts and types of military equipment which in the opinion of the British military mission in Libya are beyond the army's needs and capability to absorb.

Preliminary to any action, the British ambassador plans to obtain from the Libyan prime minister an estimate of the country's present arms needs not supplied under the Anglo-Libyan treaty as well as an estimate of its future needs.
