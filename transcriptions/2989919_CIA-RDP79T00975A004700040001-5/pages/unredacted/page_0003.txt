4 SEPTEMBER 1959

I. THE COMMUNIST BLOC

Communist China--Peiping may be preparing domestic purge as loss of prestige over economic shortcomings becomes evident.

USSR begins to exploit atmosphere surrounding forthcoming Eisenhower-Khrushchev talks in urging US allies to loosen ties.

II. ASIA-AFRICA

Iran to make gesture toward resumption of negotiations with USSR.

Nasir may try to get arms from the West.

India--Army expected to restore conditions in Calcutta to normal.

Belgian Congo--Progress toward independence receives setback with resignation of Belgian minister of the Congo.

Algeria--Rebels attack near Tunisian border probably to relieve pressure from French in Kabylie Mountains; further clashes expected.

III. THE WEST

De Gaulle expected to proclaim Algeria's right to self-determination after "pacification"; Premier Debré reportedly organizing opposition.

Cuba--Some antigovernment activity apparently continues.

Iraq--Senior army officers reported planning early coup.

Argentina--New army crisis flares over command appointments.

LATE ITEMS

Laotian cabinet decision to request UN and SEATO intervention reported.
