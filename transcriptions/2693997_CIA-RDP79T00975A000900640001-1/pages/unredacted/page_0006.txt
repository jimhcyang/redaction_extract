6. Employees of Western missions face restrictions in Poland:

The Polish Government has modified its penal code in order to withdraw the immunity from criminal procedure of all non-diplomatic employees of foreign missions and their families, regardless of nationality. An official of the Polish Foreign Office has stated that no exceptions could be made on the basis of reciprocity.

The American Embassy believes the new law has serious implications from the standpoint of security and the morale of non-diplomatic employees of the Embassy and their families, especially those in the Military Attache's office.

Comment: The practical effect of this change in the penal code will be to curtail the Embassy's ability to observe developments in Poland.

Restrictive measures against the activities of Western diplomatic and non-diplomatic personnel in Poland previously have been much less severe in their practical application than those of other European Satellites.
